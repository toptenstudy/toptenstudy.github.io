Mezzo-soprano, (Italian: “half-soprano”), in vocal music the range between the soprano (q.v.) and the alto, usually encompassing the A below middle C and the second F or G above middle C. The term is often abbreviated to “mezzo.”

15. meaning of MEZZO – SOPRANO​


Answer:

A mezzo-soprano or mezzo (English: /ˈmɛtsoʊ/; Italian: [ˈmɛddzo soˈpraːno]; meaning "half soprano") is a type of classical female singing voice whose vocal range lies between the soprano and the contralto voice types.

Answer:

A mezzo-soprano or mezzo (English: /ˈmɛtsoʊ/; Italian: [ˈmɛddzo soˈpraːno]; meaning "half soprano") is a type of classical female singing voice whose vocal range lies between the soprano and the contralto voice types.

Mezzo - is the Italian word for "half", "middle" or "medium".

Soprano - is the highest among the four standard singing voices.
