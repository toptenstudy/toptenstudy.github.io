Explanation:

Hypothesis and Conclusions of Conditional Statements

What is a conditional statement?

Hypotheses followed by a conclusion is called a conditional statement. It is also known as If-then statement. Conditional statement is represented by p → q. This is read as if p then q.

Hypothesis

The hypothesis of a conditional statement is the phrase following the word if.

Conclusion

The conclusion of a conditional statement is the phrase following the word then.

Example:

If two angles are supplementary, then the sum of the measures of the angles is 180.

H: Two angles are supplementary.

C: The sum of the measures of the angles is 180.

Sometimes a conditional statement is false if hypotheses is true and the conclusion is false.

Example:

If two lines form right angles, then the lines are parallel.

This is a false conditional statement because parallel lines cant form angles.
