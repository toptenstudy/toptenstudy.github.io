Answer:

42.41 cm^2


Step-by-step explanation:

A SECTOR is an area inside a circle which is enclosed by an arc and two radii with a certain angle.


Recall that in finding the AREA OF A SECTOR, we use the formula


A = (1/2)(r^2)(Θ)


where A is the area;


r is the radius; and


Θ is the angle in radians


Note that the angle is in "radians", so we should convert the given angle into radians. RADIAN is the standard unit used in angular measurements, and is also the SI unit in measuring angles. CONVERTING DEGREES TO RADIANS, we use the conversion factor π radians is equal to 180 degrees.


We first convert 60 degrees to radians


Θrad = (60°)( π rad / 180°)


Θrad = π/3 rad


Substituting the values in the equation, we have


A = (1/2)(9cm)^2(π/3)


A = [tex]\frac{27}{2}[/tex]π

Therefore, the area of the sector is [tex]\frac{27}{2}[/tex]π or approximately 42.41 cm^2

Let [tex]\theta[/tex] be the angle of the sector. We use the formula for the area of a sector, as expressed below, to solve for [tex]\theta[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{A_{sector}=\dfrac{\theta}{360^{\circ}} \times \pi r^2}[/tex]

[tex]360^{\circ}(35.4)=\theta \times 113.04[/tex]

[tex]\therefore \theta =\dfrac{360^{\circ}(35.4)}{113.04} \approx \boxed{112.74^{\circ}}[/tex]

The angle of the sector is approximately 112.74 degrees.

Area of sector = (angle / 360) * pi * radius^2

To solve for the area of a segment, you will need to know the radius of the circle, the measure of the central angle of the sector in degrees, and the length of the chord that defines the segment. The formula for the area of a segment is:

Area of segment = [radius^2 * (angle / 360) * (1 - (chord length / (2 * radius)))] - (chord length * height / 2)

To solve for the arc length of a circle, you will need to know the radius of the circle and the measure of the central angle of the arc in degrees. The formula for the arc length is:

Arc length = (angle / 360) * 2 * pi * radius

Note that in all of these formulas, the measure of the central angle must be in degrees. If the measure is given in radians, you will need to convert it to degrees before using the formulas.
