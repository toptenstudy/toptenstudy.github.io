10. What is drama?What is ancient drama?What is Renaissance drama?What is medieval drama?What is modern drama?What is Oriental drama?What is Philippine drama?​


Answer:

Drama is also a type of a play written for theater, television, radio, and film. In simple words, a drama is a composition in verse or prose presenting a story in pantomime or dialogue. It contains conflict of characters, particularly the ones who perform in front of audience on the stage.

Ancient drama or Greek drama began in the 6th century BCE in Athens with the performance of tragedy plays at religious festivals. These, in turn, inspired the genre of Greek comedy plays. The two types of Greek drama would be hugely popular and performances spread around the Mediterranean and influenced Hellenistic and Roman theatre.

Renaissance drama refers to European drama from about the 15th to the early 17th centuries. During this period the rediscovery and imitation of classical works established the Renaissance drama foundations of the modern theatre. England’s major contribution was the lively Elizabethan stage that produced Shakespeare.

Renaissance drama began in Italy, with scholars initially attempting to recreate the original stagings of Greek and Roman plays, then adapting them to contemporary dress and speech. The new interest in classical drama was fired by the rediscovery of texts by Euripides, Seneca, Plautus, and Terence. Aristotle’s Poetics, which defined the classical genres of tragedy and comedy, came to light in the 15th century.

Modern Drama refers to plays written in the 19th and 20th centuries whereas contemporary drama refers to plays written today.

The Medieval drama was a source of entertainment and education for residents of the Middle Ages. Though initially tinged with religious zeal, Medieval theatre went through centuries of evolution and themes outside of the Bible were eventually accommodated.

Oriental Drama also called a movie or motion picture, is a series of still images which, when shown on a screen, creates the illusion of moving images due to phi phenomenon.

28. 1. What are the essential expressive skills that contribute to a great performance? 2. Why is it important to pronounce and enunciate words properly? 3. What is the link between drama techniques and stage movement? 4. How do these expressive skills contribute to a great dramatic performance? 5. What makes for powerful drama?


Explanation:

The essential expressive skills that contribute to a great performance include vocal control, facial expressions, body language, emotion, and timing. These skills allow actors to convey their characters' thoughts, feelings, and motivations convincingly and effectively.

Proper pronunciation and enunciation are important because they ensure that the audience can understand what the actors are saying. This is particularly important for live performances where there may not be the benefit of close-ups or subtitles. Proper pronunciation and enunciation also contribute to the overall clarity and coherence of the performance.

Drama techniques, such as blocking and choreography, are often used to enhance stage movement and make performances more visually engaging. Effective use of stage movement can help actors to convey their characters' emotions and motivations, as well as establish the setting and atmosphere of the play.

These expressive skills contribute to a great dramatic performance by allowing actors to fully embody their characters and engage the audience on an emotional level. When actors can convey the nuances of their characters' emotions through vocal and physical expression, the audience is more likely to connect with the characters and become invested in the story.

Powerful drama is characterized by compelling storytelling, dynamic characters, and emotional resonance. A great dramatic performance can elicit strong emotions in the audience, whether it be laughter, tears, or a sense of catharsis. It is also characterized by authenticity and believability, where the actors are fully committed to their characters and the story being told.
