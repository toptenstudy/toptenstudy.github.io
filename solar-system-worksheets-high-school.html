17. bakit po ba tinawag na solar system ang solar system​


Answer:

The Solar System is the Sun and all the objects that orbit around it. The Sun is orbited by planets, asteroids, comets and other things.

The Solar System is about 4.6 billion years old.[1] It formed by gravity in a large molecular cloud. Most of this matter gathered in the center, and the rest flattened into an orbiting disk that became the Solar System. It is thought that almost all stars form by this process.

The Sun is a star. It contains 99.9% of the Solar System's mass.[2] This means that it has strong gravity. The other objects are pulled into orbit around the Sun. The Sun is mostly made out of hydrogen, and some helium and higher elements.

There are eight planets in the Solar System. From closest to farthest from the Sun, they are: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune. The first four planets are called terrestrial planets. They are mostly made of rock and metal, and they are mostly solid. The last four planets are called gas giants. This is because they are much larger than other planets and are mostly made of gas.

The Solar System also contains other things. There are asteroid belts, mostly between Mars and Jupiter. Further out than Neptune, there is the Kuiper belt and the scattered disc. These areas have dwarf planets, including Pluto, Makemake, Haumea, Ceres and Eris. There are thousands of very small objects in these areas. There are also comets, centaurs, and there is interplanetary dust.

Six of the planets and three of the dwarf planets are orbited by moons. There are about 200 moons in the Solar System. Mercury and Venus have no moons, and Jupiter and Saturn have the largest number of moons. The largest moon is Ganymede which is a moon of Jupiter.

Furthermore, planetary dust orbits the gas giants. Many other systems like the Solar System have been found. Each of the billions of stars in the Milky Way galaxy might have a planetary system.

THE SOLAR SYSTEM

The Formation of Solar System

Our solar system formed about 4.5 billion years ago from a very dense cloud of cosmic gas and dust. After a long amount of time the cloud eventually collapsed, possibly due to the an immense force or shockwave of a nearby exploding star, also called a supernova. When this dust cloud collapsed, it formed a the very young solar nebula a spinning, accretion disk of material.

At the center of the nebula, the gravity gradually pulled more and more material in. Eventually the when the pressure in the core was so immense that hydrogen atoms began to merge and form helium, releasing a great amount of energy. With that, our very young Sun was born, and it eventually accumulate more than 99 percent of the remaining matter.

The available matter farther out in the disk was also bonding together. These clumps crashed into one another, forming larger and larger objects. Some of them grew big enough for their gravity to shape them into spheres, becoming planets, dwarf planets and large moons. In other cases, planets did not form: the asteroid belt is made of bits and pieces of the early solar system that could never quite come together into a planet. Other smaller leftover pieces became asteroids, comets, meteoroids, and small, irregular moons.

Structure

The solar system is the collection of large and small bodies that orbit our central star, the Sun. The contents of our solar system is numerous and stretch across a large area of space. The distance of Sun to Neptune is 4.5 billion kilometers!

PLANETS

TERRESTRIAL PLANETS namely Mercury, Venus, Earth and Mars has solid compositions, frozen innercores, mantles and crusts. Mercury has a huge core, while venus on the other hand is almost the same as earth, in terms of size and density. Usually made up of metal, silicate and a rocky surface.

GAS PLANETS OR GAS GIANTS these planets can be found beyond the asteroid belt, namely Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune. These planets are large and mostly composed of gases, such as helium and hydrogen, some of these planets have small rocky core.

COMETS

• Comets are interstellar objects that follows long, oval or elliptical orbits around the Sun. They come from deep space, swing around the Sun and head off again, to return years or centuries later. If Earth passes through a comet's tail, the particles of dust become meteoroids that burn up into our atmosphere, as a spectacular meteor shower. It has three main parts, The Nucleus inside its head, the head, and its Tails.

ASTEROIDS

• Asteroids have dark surfaces. Scientists think that this asteroids are rich in carbon. Other asteroid are thought to be rocky and to have a core made of iron or nickel.

OORT CLOUD

• The spherical shell of interstellar or cometary bodies believed to surround the Sun beyond the orbits of the outermost planets outside the actual solar system and from which some are forcefully removed when perturbed to fall toward the sun.

KUIPER BELT

• The clumps of small celestial bodies far beyond the orbit of the planet Neptune from which many short-period comets are believed to materialize a long time ago.

SCIENTISTS AND THEIR REVOLUTIONARY CONTRIBUTIONS

• Galileo Galilei (1564-1642)

was one of the greatest scientist who ever lived. He made many discoveries about gravity and acceleration. But he is especially famous, because he was the first to scan the heavens and make many important discoveries, described in his book The Starry Messenger (1610)

Discovered the moons of Jupiter, that is now called The Galileian Moons.

• Johannes Kepler (1571-1630)

Mathematician and Astronomer who supported the ideas of Copernicus. The Earth was not the center of the universe, with everything going around it. Kepler worked out the orbits of planets known at the time, in mathematical detail. The Kepler's three laws of planetary motion.

• Nicolaus Copernicus (1473-1543)

studied arts, medicine and law before becoming a church canon in Poland. Then turned his attention to Astronomy. He proposed that the Geocentric idea of the Earth as the center of everything, believed since ancient times, did not fit with astronomical observation. He suggested that the Heliocentric theory where the Earth and other planets went around the Sun. This caused great debate but also began a new phase of science.
