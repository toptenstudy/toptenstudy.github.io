29. what is the answer of. x:4=5:10 with solution pls.. math grade 9


Hi Rycidy!

Your problem is about ratios. Let me first explore a bit what are ratios.

A ratio gives us the relationship between the quantities of two different things or entities. For example, there are 3 hens and 9 chicks in a farm. This relationship can be express in a form of a ratio. You can use two different notations, i.e. 3 to 9 or 3 : 9, which is read as 3 to 9 as well. You can say that there are 3 hens for every 9 chicks. In some cases though we have to reduce ratios the same way we do for fractions. Notice that 3 and 9 are both divisible by 3, so by dividing both numbers by it you will get the lowest term for the ratio as 1 to 3, or 1 : 3. It means in simpler terms, there will be a hen for every 3 chicks. The idea for ratios is that we are expressing the relationship of one entity to another entity, i.e. in quantitative terms.

So for your question you have[tex] \frac{x}{4} = \frac{5}{10} [/tex]. In order to answer this, you have to cross multiply.

On the left hand side you have to multiply the numerator of the first fraction by the denominator of the second fraction.

Then on the right hand side you have to multiply the denominator of the first fraction by the numerator of the second fraction. Insert equal sign at the middle. The equation must look similar to this: [tex](x)(10) = (4)(5)[/tex].

Simplify the equation. It must be [tex]10x = 20[/tex].

Eliminate the numerical coefficient of x in the left hand side by dividing both sides by 10. So [tex] \frac{10x}{10} = \frac{20}{10}[/tex] is [tex]x = 2[/tex]!

Therefore, [tex]x = 2[/tex]!

The final form for the ratio will be [tex] \frac{2}{4} = \frac{5}{10}[/tex].
