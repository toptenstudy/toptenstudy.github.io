7. what is the positive effect of political dynasty​


Answer:

The positive effect of political dynasties is that there tends to be greater continuity and stability in political decision making. This results in forward-looking policy initiatives as leaders are comfortable exploring new avenues, confident that their legacy is secure and any mistakes they might commit will be buried in the shadow of dynasty led rule. Political dynasties are also important conduits for patronage networks which can play a significant role in income redistribution and reaching out to impoverished communities. As such, family-run parties often enjoy high levels of voter loyalty, something which can be both beneficial and detrimental depending on the party’s views.

11. issue of political dynasties and political clientelism in the philippines .​


Answer:

Political dynasties have long been a feature of the Philippine political landscape. They are typically characterized as families that have established their political or economic dominance in a province and have coordinated efforts to move on to involvement in national government or other positions of national politics that treats people prominence. Political dynasties usually have a strong, consolidated support base concentrated around the province in which they are dominant. Members of such dynasties usually do not limit their involvement to strictly political activities, and have been found participating in business or culture-related activities."

Political dynasties started emerging after the Philippine Revolution when the First Republic of the Philippines was established. With the decline of Spain's economic power and international prestige in the 19th century, the expansion of British and American influence around the world, and the political current of emergent nationalism among the children of the economically enfranchised bourgeois, the power of the peninsulares', or Spanish-born aristocracy declined propitiously. Following the defeat of the Spanish in the Spanish–American War, the surviving members of the Spanish or Spanish-sanctioned landholding elite and the newly ascendant merchant elite, who were mostly foreign expatriates or of Chinese origin, formed a de facto aristocracy to replace the power vacuum the Spanish had left. Aristocracy survived and prospered under the American colonial regime, and remained a permanent fixture in Philippine society even following the independence of the Philippines was finally confirmed following the devastation of the Philippines under the Japanese occupation of the Philippines during World War II. Over the years, political dynasties continued to adapt, as newer dynasties emerged to fill power vacuums left behind by the extinction of older dynasties. The majority of the available positions in Philippine government are currently held by members of these political dynasties. Notable Philippine political dynasties include the Aquino, Marcos, Arroyo, Binay, Duterte, and Roxas families." There has been a lot of debate regarding the effects political dynasties have on the political and economic status of Philippine society. Despite the negative reaction of the populace towards political dynasties and the association between dynastic activities and corruption,[1] it is only prohibited in the members of the youth-oriented Sangguniang Kabataan.


12. summary of political Dynasty​


Answer:

A political dynasty exists when a family whose members are related as a spouse, and up to the second degree of consanguinity or affinity, whether such relations are legitimate, illegitimate, half, or full blood, maintains or is capable of maintaining political control by succession or by simultaneously running for or holding elective positions.

Summary of political Dynasty​.

A political family (sometimes known as a political dynasty) is a group of people who are interested in politics, particularly electoral politics. Members may be related by blood or marriage; numerous generations or siblings are frequently involved.

In the Philippines, political dynasties are often defined as families that have established political or economic dominance in a province and have coordinated efforts to advance to national administration or other positions of national politics. Political dynasties are known for having a strong, entrenched support base in the provinces in which they rule. Members of such dynasties are often involved in more than just politics, and may also engage in economic or cultural pursuits.

Political dynasties have long been a part of the political scene in the Philippines. After the Philippine Revolution, when the First Republic of the Philippines was created, political dynasties began to emerge. With the decline of Spain's economic power and international prestige in the nineteenth century, the spread of British and American influence around the world, and the political current of emerging nationalism among the children of the economically enfranchised bourgeois, the power of the peninsulares or Spanish-born aristocracy, declined precipitously.

14. Mga political dynasty


Ang mga dynasty sa politika ay matagal nang naging eksena ng politika sa Pilipinas. Karaniwan silang nakikilala bilang mga pamilya na nagpapanatili ng kanilang posisyon bilang nangunguna sa pang-politika o pang-ekonomiyang pagpapatakbo sa mga probinsiya. Karaniwan na maingat na binalak ang mga plano upang magpatuloy sa pagtakbo sa pambansang posisyon sa politika na lalong nagpapakilala sa kanilang pamilya.  

Ang mga dinastiya sa politika ay karaniwang may isang malakas, sentralisadong "homebase" ng suporta na nakasentro sa loob ng lalawigan na kanilang kinabibilangan. Ang mga miyembro ng mga dynasty ay hindi lang kilala sa larangan ng pulitika sa kanilang probinsya or rehiyon. Sila rin ay sumasakop sa mga activities, at mga negosyo.

Ilan sa mga kilalang political dynasties sa Pilipinas ay ang mga:

Marcos (Mariano, Ferdinand, Imee, Bongbong, Imelda)Roxas (Manuel, Gerardo, Mar)Duterte (Rodrigo, Sarah, Paolo, Sebastian)Aquino (Servillano, Benigno Sr., Benigno Jr., Corazon, Paolo "Bam")

24. are you in favor of political dynasty?


No, because instead of serving the people, they focus on how to perpetuate themselves in power. I also believe that there are a lot of more talented people who want to serve the public that should also be given the opportunity to serve. The perpetuation of political dynasties is also against the Philippine Constitution and against moral ethics. Overall, they also hinder progress in the Philippines because they tend to consolidate power among themselves. This often leads to rampant graft and corruption.  

What does the law say?

There is no law, unfortunately. The Philippine Constitution inn Article II Section 26 says that: "The State shall guarantee equal access to opportunities for public service, and prohibit political dynasties as may be defined by law." Unfortunately, while there is this provision, there is no enabling law that defines what a political dynasty is and its limits. Of note are the Senate bills filed by Miriam Defensor-Santiago in 2013 and Ping Lacson's current Senate Bill 30 (in the 18th Congress). He noted, however, that it would be difficult to pass this law because the Congress, and the Senate, who will approve this law are full of people who have benefited from political dynasties. Known Philippine political dynasties are the Aquino, Marcos, Arroyo, Binay, Duterte, and Roxas families, among others.
