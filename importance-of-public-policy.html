Explanation:

Impact of Cultural, Social, and Community Environments on Home Care

Steven M. Albert

The home environment is critical for maintaining health and well-being among the medically ill and people living with disabilities. Access to appropriate supportive care technologies and home health care services depends in part on where homes are located, what sorts of spaces are available for care in the home, and whether basic services (such as utilities) are reliable. These aspects of home environments are difficult to measure, even when features of homes are narrowly defined and only a single attribute, such as safety, is considered (Gitlin, 2003). Measurement challenges become more complex when considering that each of these environmental features also has a cultural or social component. Homes are located in neighborhoods, where home health care providers may not feel welcome or safe because of crime in a low-income neighborhood and discrimination or suspicion in a higher income one. Homes differ in their spaces available for care but also in the willingness of families to make these spaces available, adapt them as needed, and work with home health staff to provide care. Also, utilities, telephone service, and access to services differ by community, with some communities well serviced and others shortchanged. Thus, the home environment is nested in social and cultural layers that may lead to different home care outcomes, even with similar patients and common home environments (Barris et al., 1985).

The cultural component is immediately visible in family adaptation to home care. Families differ in the degree to which they reorganize themselves and their living spaces to accommodate care for the disabled or medically unstable (Albert, 1990), with different tolerance for disorder and different strategies for reducing such disorder (Rubinstein, 1990).

Answer:

Disaster risk reduction and management (DRRM) refers to the efforts and measures taken to reduce the vulnerabilities and potential impacts of natural and man-made disasters on communities, as well as to improve the capacity of individuals and organizations to prepare for, respond to, and recover from such events. Public policies play a crucial role in facilitating and enforcing DRRM efforts, as they provide the legal and institutional framework for disaster management activities, as well as set out the roles and responsibilities of various stakeholders in this process.

There are several reasons why it is important to abide by public policies on DRRM:

Ensuring public safety: Public policies on DRRM help to ensure that communities are prepared and equipped to handle disasters, which in turn helps to reduce the risk of injury and loss of life. By following these policies, individuals can take steps to protect themselves and their families from harm, as well as help their communities to be more resilient in the face of disasters.

Protecting property and infrastructure: Disasters can cause significant damage to property and infrastructure, leading to costly repairs and reconstruction. Public policies on DRRM help to mitigate this damage by promoting the use of structural and non-structural measures to reduce the risk of disaster, such as building codes, land-use planning, and emergency preparedness plans.

Reducing economic impacts: Disasters can have significant economic impacts, including lost productivity, damage to businesses and infrastructure, and disrupted supply chains. Public policies on DRRM help to minimize these impacts by encouraging the use of risk assessment tools to identify and prioritize areas at risk, as well as by promoting the development of contingency plans to minimize the disruption of essential services.

Promoting sustainable development: DRRM is an important aspect of sustainable development, as it helps to reduce the risks and impacts of disasters on vulnerable communities and ecosystems. By abiding by public policies on DRRM, individuals and organizations can contribute to the development of more resilient and sustainable communities.

In conclusion, it is important to abide by public policies on DRRM in order to ensure public safety, protect property and infrastructure, reduce economic impacts, and promote sustainable development.
