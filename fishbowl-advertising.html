3. sun's rays are observed to focus at a point behind the fishbowl near the window the fishbowl act as what type of lens


Fishbowl act as what type of lens

The answer is Convex lens.

Fill your glass or bowl halfway with water. When light travels from one medium to another, it refracts and changes speed. A spherical vessel of water behaves as a convex lens because it bulges outward at the center, as seen in a fishbowl or wine glass. A converging lens is another name for a convex lens. A converging lens is one that focuses light rays that are going parallel to its main axis. Their shape, which is rather thick in the middle and thin at the upper and bottom margins, distinguishes them.

Light is refracted inward by convex lenses, which refract light inward toward a focal point. Light rays going through the corners of a convex lens bend the most, whereas light passing through the center of the lens remains straight. Farsightedness is corrected by convex lenses.

13. 10. Each letter of the word 'POLICY' is written on a strip of paper, roiled and plated in a fishbowl. If one letter is drawn and it is consonant, what is the probability that it is a Y?​


Step-by-step explanation:

The word POLICY has 6 letters, out of which 4 are consonants (P, L, C, Y) and 2 are vowels (O, I).

If one letter is drawn at random from the fishbowl, and we know that the letter drawn is a consonant, then there are 4 possible outcomes (P, L, C, Y).

The probability of drawing a Y, given that the letter drawn is a consonant, is the ratio of the number of favorable outcomes (i.e., the number of ways to draw a Y from the 4 consonants) to the number of possible outcomes (i.e., the total number of consonants):

Probability of drawing a Y, given that the letter drawn is a consonant = Number of favorable outcomes / Number of possible outcomes

= 1 / 4

Therefore, the probability of drawing a Y, given that the letter drawn is a consonant, is 1/4 or 0.25.
