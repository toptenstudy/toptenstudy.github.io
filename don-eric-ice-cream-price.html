Answer:

a) Hot weather - The effect on demand for ice cream cones will increase due to hot weather. This means that the demand curve will shift to the right, resulting in an increase in both equilibrium price and quantity of ice cream cones.

b) Storm destroys sugarcane crops - The effect on supply of sugar used in ice cream cones will decrease due to the storm's destruction of sugarcane crops. This means that the supply curve will shift to the left, resulting in an increase in the equilibrium price and decrease in the equilibrium quantity of ice cream cones.

c) Tax increase for sugar-based products - The effect on supply for ice cream cones will decrease due to the tax increase for sugar-based products. This means that the supply curve will shift to the left, resulting in an increase in the equilibrium price and decrease in the equilibrium quantity of ice cream cones.

d) Mass lay-off of employees - The effect on demand for ice cream cones will decrease due to mass lay-off of employees. This means that the demand curve will shift to the left, resulting in a decrease in both equilibrium price and quantity of ice cream cones.

e) Price of the ice cream cones increase - The effect on demand for ice cream cones will decrease due to an increase in price. This means that the demand curve will shift to the left, resulting in a decrease in equilibrium quantity of ice cream cones, while the equilibrium price increases.

23. An ice cream machine is being sold by a couple since they wanted to venture into another business. They were trying to sell it for 10% off of the marked price but no one wanted to buy it. Then they tried to sell for 20% of the first sale price still no one bought the machine. Finally, they offered it for 25% off the second sale price when someone bought it for P32,400.00. What was the original price of the ice cream machine?​


Answer:

The original price of the ice cream machine was, 60,000 pesos

Step-by-step explanation:

Let's denote the original price of the ice cream machine as x.

According to the problem, the couple tried to sell it for 10% off the marked price, which means they were trying to sell it for 0.9x. However, no one wanted to buy it at that price.

Then they tried to sell it for 20% off the first sale price, which is equivalent to selling it for 0.8(0.9x) = 0.72x. Again, no one bought it.

Finally, they offered it for 25% off the second sale price, which means the machine was sold for 0.75(0.72x) = 0.54x = P32,400.00.

To find x, we can solve for it:

0.54x = P32,400.00

x = P32,400.00 / 0.54

x = P60,000.00

Therefore, the original price of the ice cream machine was P60,000.00.

For every 6 gallons of vanilla ice cream frozen, the ice creamery freezes 2 gallons of chocolate ice cream. C. For every 6 gallons of chocolate ice cream frozen, the ice creamery freezes 2 gallons of vanilla ice cream. D. There are always 6 – 2 = 4 more gallons of vanilla ice cream frozen than chocolate ice cream frozen.


The point (6, 2) represents a specific ratio of chocolate ice cream to vanilla ice cream that the ice creamery freezes daily.

From the graph, we can see that the ratio of chocolate ice cream to vanilla ice cream is constant and equal to 1/3. This means that for every 1 gallon of chocolate ice cream frozen, the ice creamery freezes 3 gallons of vanilla ice cream.

Using this ratio, we can determine the amount of vanilla ice cream that the ice creamery freezes when 6 gallons of chocolate ice cream are frozen:

6 gallons chocolate ice cream x (3 gallons vanilla ice cream/1 gallon chocolate ice cream) = 18 gallons vanilla ice cream

Therefore, the point (6, 2) represents the fact that the ice creamery freezes 6 gallons of chocolate ice cream and 18 gallons of vanilla ice cream daily, maintaining a ratio of 1/3 (or 2/6) of chocolate ice cream to vanilla ice cream.

So the correct answer is B: For every 6 gallons of vanilla ice cream frozen, the ice creamery freezes 2 gallons of chocolate ice cream.
