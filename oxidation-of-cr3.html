16. A Cr3+(aq) solution is electrolyzed using a current of 7.75 A. What mass of Cr(s) is plated out after 1.50 days?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Calculate the amount of charge.

There are 129,600 seconds in 1.50 days.

[tex]\begin{aligned} Q & = It \\ & = \text{(7.75 A)(129,600 s)} \\ & = \text{1,004,400 C} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the mass of Cr plated out by using dimesional analysis.

Three electrons are required to reduce Cr³⁺ to Cr.

There are 96,485 C of charge in 1 mole of electrons.

The molar mass of Cr is 51.996 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of Cr} & = \text{1,004,400 C} \times \frac{\text{1 mol e}}{\text{96,485 C}} \times \frac{\text{1 mol Cr}}{\text{3 mol e}} \times \frac{\text{51.996 g Cr}}{\text{1 mol Cr}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{180. g}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the mass of Cr plated out after 1.50 days is 180. g.

29. How many minutes will it take to plate out 2.19 g of chromium metal from a solution of Cr3+ using a current of 35.2 amps in an electrolyte cell _______ ?


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: Write the balanced redox equation

Cr³⁺ + 3 e⁻ → Cr

Step 2: Calculate the charge flowing through a solution in coulombs using dimensional analysis.

Based on the balanced redox equation, 3 moles of e⁻ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 1 mole of Cr. Note that the molar mass of Cr is 52.00 g/mol and there are 96,485 C in 1 mole of e⁻.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{charge} & = 2.19 \: \cancel{\text{g Cr}} \times \frac{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol Cr}}}{52.00 \: \cancel{\text{g Cr}}} \times \frac{3 \: \cancel{\text{mol e}^-}}{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol Cr}}} \times \frac{\text{96,485 C}}{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol e}^-}} \\ & = \text{12,191 C} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Final Step: Calculate the time spent (in minutes) on producing Cr by using dimensional analysis.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{time} & = 12,191 \: \cancel{\text{C}} \times \frac{1 \: \cancel{\text{A}} \cdot \cancel{\text{s}}}{1 \: \cancel{\text{C}}} \times \frac{1}{35.2 \: \cancel{\text{A}}} \times \frac{\text{1 min}}{60 \: \cancel{\text{s}}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{5.77 min}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the time taken to plate out 2.19 g of Cr metal from a solution of Cr³⁺ using a current of 35.2 amps in an electrolyte cell is 5.77 min.

30. How many coulombs of charge are required to cause reduction of 0.20 mole of cr3 to cr?


SOLUTION:

Three electrons are required to reduce Cr³⁺ to Cr.

There are 96,485 C of charge in 1 mole of electrons.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{charge} & = \text{0.20 mol Cr} \times \frac{\text{3 mol} \: e^-}{\text{1 mol Cr}} \times \frac{\text{96,485 C}}{\text{1 mol} \: e^-} \\ & = \text{57,891 C} \\ & \approx \boxed{\text{57,900 C}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, 57,900 C of charge are required.
