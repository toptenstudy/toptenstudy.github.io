4. 5. It is an idealized motion in which air resistance is neglected and acceleration is constant.​


answer:

Everyone has observed the effect of gravity as it causes objects to fall downward. In the absence of air resistance, it is found that all bodies at the same location above the earth fall vertically with the same acceleration. Furthermore, if the distance of the fall is small compared to the radius of the earth, the acceleration remains essentially constant throughout the descent. This idealized motion, in which air resistance is neglected and the acceleration is nearly constant, is known as free-fall.

Problem:

A bullet is shot vertically into the air with a velocity of 450 m/s. Neglecting air resistance, (a) how  long is the bullet in the air? (b)How high does the bullet go?​

how  long is the bullet in the air?  91.84 second

How high does the bullet go?​ 10,332 m

Given Data:

Vi (initial velocity) = 450 m/s

Vf (final velocity) = 0 m/s (at maximum height)

a (acceleration (g) ) = -9.8 m/s² (decelerating going up)

t (time)  =  ? unknown

Solution:

The time the bullet going up is the same as it falls back to the ground. We have to solve the time the bullet going up with the formula t=(Vf-Vi)/a and multiply it by 2.

t=(Vf-Vi)/a

  = 0 m/s - 450 m/s / -9.8 m/s²

  = - 450 m/s / -9.8 m/s²

  = 45.92 s

45.92 × 2 = 91.84 second

How high does the bullet go?​ 10,332 m

We can use the formula  d = 1/2 (Vf + Vi)t

d = 1/2 (Vf + Vi)t

  = 1/2 (450 m/s + 0 m/s) 45.92 s

  =  225 m/s × 45.92 s

  = 10,332 m
