8. Two musical elements in Indian music.**Two forms of Indian popular music.**Two basic Indian music.**​


Answer:

Two musical:

Indian classical music has two foundational elements, raga and tala. The raga, based on a varied repertoire of swara (notes including microtones), forms the fabric of a deeply intricate melodic structure, while the tala measures the time cycle.

Two forms:

The two main traditions of Indian classical music are Carnatic music, which is practised predominantly in the peninsular (southern) regions, and Hindustani music, which is found in the northern, eastern and central regions.

Two basics:

The two main traditions of Indian classical music are Carnatic music, which is practised predominantly in the peninsular (southern) regions, and Hindustani music, which is found in the northern, eastern and central regions.

25. How did Mohandas Gandhi contribute in the development of Indian nationalism? a. He protested against Rowlatt Act of 1919. b. He brought nationalism to the reach of the common people of India c. He introduced education to Indian nationals d. He led the Amritsar Massacre.


Answer:

a. He protested against Rowlatt Act of 1919.

Explanation:

Mohandas Gandhi played a significant role in the Indian Nationalist Movement. He protested against the Rowlatt Act of 1919, which allowed the British government to arrest and imprison Indian nationalists without trial. This led to widespread protests across the country and ultimately resulted in the Jallianwala Bagh massacre in Amritsar in 1919.
