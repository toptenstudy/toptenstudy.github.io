2. Criminal intent: The accused must have the intent to commit the crime or, at the very least, the knowledge that their actions or omissions would result in the commission of a crime.

3. Concurrence: The act or omission and the criminal intent must occur at the same time.

4. Causation: The act or omission must be the direct cause of the crime.
