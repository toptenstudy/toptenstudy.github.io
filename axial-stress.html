1. What are the daily life examples of axial stress?


Axial stress describes the amount of force per unit of cross-sectional area that acts in the lengthwise direction of a beam or axle. Axial stress can cause a member to compress, buckle, elongate or fail. Some parts that might experience axial force are building joists, studs and various types of shafts.

4. what is axial skeleton?​


Answer:

The adult human body has 206 bones. These bones are divided into two main parts: Your appendicular skeleton and your axial skeleton. Your axial skeleton is made up of the bones along your vertical axis. Axial comes from the word “axis,” which means line. The bones line up along the central core of your body.

Your axial skeleton is made up of the 80 bones within the central core of your body. This includes bones in your skull (cranial and facial bones), ears, neck, back (vertebrae, sacrum and tailbone) and ribcage (sternum and ribs).

Understanding shear stress

When a force is transferred from one object to another, a force parallel to the surface is experienced by the object, such a force is generated Shear stress.

It is important to know about the shear stresses acting on materials when designing products. Shear damage is the most common damage due to improper consideration of shear forces.

Definition of Shear Stress

When the applied force is parallel to/tangible to the application surface area, the resulting stress is known as shear stress.

Here the applied force is in contact with the application surface.

The stress tensor component in a direction parallel to the application area.

Shear stress also occurs under axial loading, bending, etc.

30. A cast iron column supports an axial compressive load of 250KN. Determine the outside diameter of the column if its inside diameter is 185mm and the limiting compressive stress is 50MPa​


Answer:

[tex]D_{o} = 201.48mm[/tex]

Explanation:

We will use  the formula of applied stress in getting the measurement of outside diameter

Given:

[tex]Force = 250KN\\[/tex] or [tex]250000N[/tex]

[tex]Stress = 50MPa[/tex] or [tex]50N/mm^{2}[/tex]

[tex]Outside diameter = 185mm[/tex]

The given are converted to equivalent value with common unit of measurement used for easier computation

Formula:

[tex]stress = \frac{F}{A}[/tex] where F is Force and A is cross sectional area

[tex]A_{column} = \frac{\pi}{4} (D_{o} ^2-D_{i} ^2)[/tex] where [tex]D_{o}[/tex] is outside diameter, [tex]D_{i}[/tex] is inside diameter, and [tex]\pi[/tex] is 3.14

Therefore, to get outside diameter:

[tex]D_{o} = \sqrt{D_{i}^2+\frac{F}{s(\frac{\pi }{4})}}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]D_{o} = \sqrt{185mm^2+\frac{250000N}{50N/mm^2(\frac{3.14}{4})}}[/tex]

[tex]D_{o} =\sqrt{40594mm^2}[/tex]

[tex]D_{o} = 201.48mm[/tex]
