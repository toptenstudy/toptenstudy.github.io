9. is history a interpretative; it invites students to debate multiple perspectives, offer their opinions and educated interpretations, and challenge existing beliefs?​


Answer:

Yes, history is interpretive in nature. History is not simply a collection of facts or events, but rather an interpretation of those events through the lens of the historian's perspective and the available evidence.

Explanation:

Historians examine primary sources, such as documents, letters, and artifacts, to construct a narrative of the past. However, even with access to the same primary sources, historians may interpret events differently based on their own biases, values, and cultural perspectives.

As a result, history invites students to engage in debate and offer their opinions and interpretations based on the available evidence. History is not a static discipline, but rather a dynamic process of inquiry that is constantly being challenged and revised based on new evidence and perspectives.

Challenging existing beliefs and interpretations is an essential part of historical inquiry, as it allows for a more complete understanding of the past and its relevance to the present.

12. the Philippines association of campus student leaders is in the process of publishing a literary folio that include poems, short stories,comics, and other creative output written during the lockdown by students all over the Philippines. You were contracted and invited by the organization to write an essay about the effects of online learning on student's behavior and scholastic performance during the pandemic. You will produce an argumentative essay that will be included as part of the folio to be distributed nationwide. ​


Answer:

The COVID-19 pandemic has had a profound impact on the way students learn and has led to a widespread shift towards online learning. While this has certainly had some benefits, it has also had significant effects on student behavior and scholastic performance.

One of the main effects of online learning on student behavior has been the decrease in social interaction and the increase in isolation. Students who were used to learning in a physical classroom with their peers have had to adjust to learning from home, often without the same level of interaction with classmates and teachers. This lack of social interaction can lead to feelings of loneliness and isolation, which can in turn affect a student's motivation and engagement.

Another effect of online learning on student behavior has been the increased temptation to procrastinate and engage in distracting activities. With fewer structure and supervision, students may find it easier to put off their work or get sidetracked by other activities. This can lead to a decline in productivity and a decrease in overall performance.

In terms of scholastic performance, research has shown that online learning can be less effective than in-person learning, especially for students who struggle academically. This is because online learning often lacks the immediate feedback and support that students receive in a physical classroom setting. Additionally, the lack of structure and supervision in an online environment can make it more difficult for students to stay on track and complete assignments.

Overall, while online learning has certainly had some benefits, it has also had significant effects on student behavior and scholastic performance. It is important that educators and policymakers take these effects into account as they continue to navigate the challenges of the pandemic and consider the best approaches to teaching and learning in the future.

28. 2. A student has 9 friends, consisting of 4 boys and 5 girls. In how many ways can he invite them to his birthday party if he can only invite 3 boys and 3 girls?​


Answer:

To solve this problem, we can use the combination formula, which is:

nCk = n! / k!(n-k)!

where n is the total number of objects, k is the number of objects we want to choose, and ! denotes factorial, which means the product of all positive integers up to that number.

In this case, we want to choose 3 boys out of 4 and 3 girls out of 5, so we have:

4C3 = 4! / 3!(4-3)! = 4

5C3 = 5! / 3!(5-3)! = 10

The total number of ways to invite 3 boys and 3 girls is the product of these two numbers:

4 x 10 = 40

Therefore, the student can invite his friends in 40 ways.
