A Filipino person is designated as a National Artist in acknowledgment of their outstanding contributions to these fields.

6. 1-5 quiz about tsunami with answers ​


Answer:

What is a tsunami?

A tsunami is a large ocean wave caused by a seismic event, such as an earthquake, volcanic eruption, or underwater landslide.

How do tsunamis form?

Tsunamis form when there is a sudden displacement of water, such as from an underwater earthquake or volcanic eruption. This displacement creates a series of large ocean waves that can travel long distances and cause significant damage along coastlines.

What are some warning signs of a tsunami?

Some warning signs of a tsunami include a sudden retreat of water from the shoreline, a loud roaring noise coming from the ocean, and strong shaking or ground rumbling.

What should you do if you are in a tsunami warning area?

If you are in a tsunami warning area, you should move to higher ground immediately and stay away from the coast. It is also important to stay informed about the latest weather and safety information from local authorities.

How can communities prepare for a tsunami?

Communities can prepare for a tsunami by creating evacuation plans, building early warning systems, and educating residents about the dangers of tsunamis and how to stay safe. It's also important for communities to develop strategies for protecting key infrastructure and assets, such as hospitals and emergency services.

21. QUIZ 6-2 When the nucleus of a cell is removed, the cell dies. What could be inferred about the role of the nucleus?​


Answer/Explanation:


(Based on what I researched and stock notes)

According to (Meisel, 2019), "the nucleus plays a central role in the life of a cell." The control center of the cell, containing the genetic material (DNA), provides the instructions for all of the cell's functions. The nucleus also plays a crucial role in cell division, the process by which cells grow and reproduce.

Therefore, removing the nucleus from a cell would effectively disable its ability to carry out its functions and reproduce, leading to its death. This suggests that the core plays a vital role in maintaining and continuing a cell's life.

29. Elsa joined as Science and Mathematics quiz bee on television there are 30 questions.18 questions are about Math. what is the percentage of science questions?​


Answer:

My answer is "40%"

Explanation:

Because 18 is 60% of 30 so the remaining percent is 40%.

Answer:

40 %

Step-by-step explanation:

1. Subtract the number of Math questions to the total questions.

30 - 18 = 12

2. Divide the difference by the number of questions and multiply the quotient by 100.

12 / 30 = .4

.4 x 100 = 40

Lastly, move two decimal places to the left to make it a percent number.

.40 — 40%

If 40% is the percentage of Science questions, then 18 questions is equal to 60% and is also the percentage for Math questions.
