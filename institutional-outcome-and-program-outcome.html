22. provides for the inclusion of environmental education programs in various national institution


Answer:

The “National Environmental Awareness and Education Act of 2008” or Republic Act No. 9512 is the act to promote environmental awareness through environmental education and for other purposes.

This began during the second regular session of the 14th Congress on July 28, 2008 and was passed by the Senate and the House of Representatives on October 8, 2008.

It was signed by Manny Villar during his term as the President of the Senate and  Prospero C. Nograles, Secretary General of the House of Representative on December 12, 2008. The Act was approved by the former President of the Philippines, Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo.

Explanation:

What is RA 9512?

1. The Act shall be known as the “National Environmental Awareness and Education Act of 2008”.

2. The declaration of this policy is to be aligned with the State’s policy to protect and advance the right of the people to a balanced and healthy ecology according to the harmony of nature. It recognizes the role and involvement of the youth in nation building and the role of education to the following aspects:

● promote patriotism and nationalism

● accelerate social progress

● promote total human liberation and development

● promote national awareness on the importance of the natural resources in economic growth

● raise awareness of the importance of environmental conservation and ecological balance towards a sustainable development

3. The following government agencies are involved in the integration of the environmental education:

● Department of Education (DepEd)

● Commission on Higher Education (CHED)

● Technical Education and Skills Development Authority (TESDA)

● Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD)

● Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR)

● Department of Science and Technology (DOST)

The environmental education includes all environmental concepts and principles, laws, the state of international and local environment, the best practices, threats, and its impact on human well-being, responsibility of the citizens, conservation, protection and rehabilitation of natural resources, and sustainable development both on the theoretical and practical aspects.

Through the programs and activities conducted in relation to the environmental education, the policy aims for the full understanding, cooperation, and involvement of all the citizens in taking care of the environment. This aims to make the citizens realize that they are accountable and responsible for the effects of what they do to the environment and how these can also affect human well-being in return. It aims for a lifestyle with a raised awareness to create a relevant change in the environment.

4.  The involvement of the youth will also be evident through the environmental activities and awareness programs in the National Service Training Program (NSTP) under RA 9163, as a requirement of the Civic Welfare Training Service (CWTS) for all baccalaureate degree courses and vocational courses with a curriculum of at least two (2) years.  

5. In pursuance to the policy set forth in this act, the “Environmental Awareness Month” is celebrated during the month of November every year.
