3. What are the qualities and characteristics of an officiating officials?


What are the qualities and characteristics of an officiating officials

This is the QUALITIES OF AN OFFICIATING OFFICIALS must have

PHYSICAL QUALITIES EMOTIONAL QUALITIES  MENTAL QUALITIES SOCIAL QUALITIES

PHYSICAL QUALITIES

As described in previous modules of physical education, physical fitness is an individual's ability to perform his or her daily activities without excessive exhaustion and still has extra energy for leisure and emergency situations.  

EMOTIONAL QUALITIES EMOTIONAL READINESS OF THE OFFICIAL OFFICIAL EMOTIONAL ATTRIBUTES OFFICIAL Must POSSESS INCLUDES CONFIDENCE.  IS HAVING BELIEF IN ONESELF

SOCIAL QUALITIES

Social consistency refers to the willingness to communicate with others in any given situation. Complaints in a game are inevitable. It is the skill of the officer to resolve disagreements  without compromising the dignity of the game and the officiating staff.  

MENTAL Attributes

JUST AS PHYSICAL AND EMOTIONAL Attributes ARE Important, MENTAL  CHARACTERISTICS ARE ALSO EQUALLY Required. MENTAL TOUGHNESS IS THE TERM USED TO Apply TO ALL of the QUALITIES Relating To MENTAL PREPARATIONS IN OFFICIATION AND GAME.

The officer must have the following characteristics.

Good Communication skills Integrity Has common sense   The ability to Hustle Has good Judgement   Is very consistent Has the courage

ROLES OF OFFICIALS

Sports officials must be able to keep confusion under control, recognize justice, encourage health and  foster good sportsmanship. A sports officer must have the positive qualities of a police officer, lawyer,  judge, accountant, author, athlete and diplomat. You are also someone who can be put in a  position of authority and who can take responsibility without being overbearing.  

In addition to the rules of the game, you must fully understand your position before, during and after the  game has ended.

IMPROVING YOUR PERFORMANCE

Part of enhancing your performance as a sports official is focusing on your last performance – so you  know what to improve on or avoid in the future. It takes a structured approach to focus on your results.  

There are three different methods:  

1. Using the logbook or the diary.  

2. Recording the key calls or decisions you made during a sporting event that you managed.  

3. Reflecting on what you have learned and areas for change. Create a schedule to work and follow it up on a daily basis.

MENTORS

Speak to other administrators, coaches and/or players to see what they think about your results and what your areas of growth are. Using a mentor in a structured way – ask them if they're willing to work with you over time to enhance your results.

VIDEO REVIEW

Record and review your results so that you can see what the others see. Look for areas of progress and change, and then prepare to deal with any problems posed.
