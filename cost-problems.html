28. How much would It cost to run the microwave in Problem 1 if the cost of energy is $0.10 per kWh?​


Answer:

if it costs $.10 for 1.0 kWh, then clearly 0.55kWh (a little more than half a kWh) should cost $.10 * 0.55 = $0.055, ie 5.5 cents. Additional comment: “operates using” sounds like a description of power. kWh is a measure of energy, which is the product of power (in kilo Watts) and time (in hours). So if I had to use the phrase “operates using,” I’d probably say it like this: “operates using 5.5kW for 0.1 hours, consuming 0.55 kWh of energy.”

What Is the High-Low Method? In cost accounting, the high-low method is a way of attempting to separate out fixed and variable costs given a limited amount of data. The high-low method involves taking the highest level of activity and the lowest level of activity and comparing the total costs at each level.
