Come in various lengths of 6, 8, 10, and 12 inches. The smaller sized knives are typically referred to as mini chef's knives while the longer lengths are known as traditional chef's knives.

Designed for thick sandwiches, this knife is made to cut easily and quickly through a variety of sandwich ingredients. The deli knife has offset blade allowing ease of slicing and handling.

Plastic serrated edge knife that is designed to slice lettuce without causing the edges to turn brown. It is efficient in slicing lettuce.

A small knife with a straight, sharp blade that is generally three to five inches long. Its thin, narrow blade is tapers to a point at the tip. It is easy to handle and works well for peeling and coring foods or mincing and cutting small items.

11. SANDWICH KNIFE

A sharp-bladed kitchen utensil used to slice through a medium amount of food ingredients "sandwiched" between two slices of bread. Similar in use to a deli knife, the sandwich knife is shorter in length with a shorter blade depth in order to easily cut through smaller to medium-sized sandwiches.

12. SERRATED KNIFE

A knife with a sharp edge that has saw-like notches or teeth. The blade of a serrated knife is 5 to 10 inches long and is used to slice through food that is hard on the outside and soft on the inside, such as slicing through the hard crusts of bread. Utensils and Equipment used in preparing sadwiches:

1. Butter Knife – A butter knife is a small knife with a rounded blade that is used to spread spreads like butter, peanut butter, and cream cheese on bread during a meal.

2. Cookie Cutters - A sharp metal or plastic device that is used to cut out the smaller breads. medium and large for constructing larger sandwiches, and small for tea sandwiches.

3. Deli knife - This knife has an offset blade that makes slicing and handling easier. It's meant to slicing through a big sandwich without compressing the bread or tearing the meat apart.

4. Lettuce Knife - This knife has a serrated edge and is designed to cut delicate salad greens without damaging them.

5. Paring Knife - This knife has a plastic serrated edge and is used to cut delicate salad greens without damaging them.

6. Sandwich Knife - A sandwich knife is similar to a deli knife in that it is shorter in length and has a shallower blade depth, making it easier to cut through smaller to medium-sized sandwiches.

7. Serrated Knife - A knife with a scalloped, toothlike edge is ideal for cutting through foods with a hard exterior and a softer interior, such as a crusty loaf of bread.

8. Sandwich Spatula - a small flat, round bladed utensil with a serrated one side and a smooth one. It's used to spread deviled ham, peanut butter, jelly, egg salad, butter, condiments, and a variety of other foods on bread pieces.

9. Grater - A grater is a kitchen utensil for grating foods into small pieces.

10. Spatula - A long, flexible blade with a rounded end that is used to spread fillings on sandwiches.

11. Scissors - These are used to cut customized edges on bread for tea sandwiches or children's sandwiches, as well as cut sandwiches into shapes such as rectangles, triangles, and circles.

12. Cutting Board - It is used to protect the table when slicing bread.

13. Griddles - It is a flat cooking surface with a heat source underneath and can be in one of three formats. It can be a piece of cookware, such as a non-stick or cast iron griddle that is heated over a burner, or it can be built into a range.

14. Bread toaster - A bread toaster is an electrical appliance that toasts bread.

15. Toaster oven - This oven has a front door, a removable wire rack, and a removable baking pan.

16. Salamanders - are kitchen appliances that are used for broiling, browning, caramelizing, and glazing. The tops of sandwiches are grilled and toasty.

17. Bread Slicer - It is used to slice bread more evenly and uniformly.

18. Eggbeater -A hand tool for manually mixing and beating eggs or other similar ingredients such as sauces, batter, egg whites, and dressings.

19. Measuring cup and spoon – individual Measuring Cup and Spoon for dry ingredients, glass measuring cup for liquids, and measuring spoon for small quantities of ingredients

20. Mixing bowl - This is where you mix the ingredients together. It is available in a variety of sizes. There are three sizes: small, medium, and giant.

21. Opener for cans, bottles, and cartons opener - It's used to open a food tin with a smooth operation and a pleasant grip and rotating motion.

More Information about sandwiches:

There are 4 types of sandwiches

1. Open Sandwich - Any sandwich made with only one slice of bread is known as an open sandwich. It is then topped with the filler or food item. It employs a variety of spread styles. Sweden, Austria, and Germany are among the countries where these are popular. A hot brown is a type of open sandwich that combines turkey and bacon.

2. Closed Sandwich - In this type of sandwich, the filling is sandwiched between two or more slices of bread. When making this style of sandwich, trimming and cutting are critical. The way it is cut and served determines the type and amount of fillings.

3. Cooked Sandwich - Either grilled or toasted, this type of sandwich is prepared.

4. Toasted Sandwich - This is a sandwich with two thin slices of bread and a thin slice of heavily buttered toast as the filling. Butter should be placed on top or between the slices of bread and warmed in a salamander or on a hot plate before filling them.
