19. A toy car, 40 g, is propelled by a constant force of 2.0 N. What is the acceleration of the toy car?


Acceleration of the toy car

The acceleration of the toy car is 50 m/s^2 because the constant force of 2.0 N acting on the toy car, divided by its mass of 0.04 kg, results in an acceleration of 50 m/s^2 according to the formula acceleration = force / mass.

Steps to Solve the problemTo find the acceleration of the toy car, we can use the formula:

acceleration = force / mass

where force is the constant force of 2.0 N and mass is the mass of the toy car, which is 40 g (or 0.04 kg).

So, plugging in the values:

acceleration = 2.0 N / 0.04 kg

acceleration = 50 m/s^2

Therefore, the acceleration of the toy car is 50 m/s^2.

AccelerationAcceleration is the rate at which an object's velocity changes over time. In this case, the toy car is being propelled by a constant force, which causes its velocity to increase. The acceleration of the toy car is calculated using the formula acceleration = force / mass, where force is the constant force acting on the car, and mass is the mass of the car. By plugging in the values for force and mass, we get an acceleration of 50 m/s^2, which means that the car's velocity increases by 50 meters per second every second.
