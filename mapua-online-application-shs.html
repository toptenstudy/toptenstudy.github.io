9. Why the nsto mapua office created 3 components of nstp?


Answer:

The Republic Act No. 9163, which is the act that establishes the National Service Training Program for tertiary level students, stipulated that the program will have three components tertiary level students can sign up for with the purpose of enhancing civic consciousness and defense preparedness in the youth by developing the ethics of service and patriotism. These programs are the following:

Reserve-Officers’ Training Corps or ROTC which is a program institutionalized under Sections 38 and 39 of Republic Act No. 7077 intended to provide military training to tertiary level students in order to motivate, train, organize and mobilize them as part of national defense preparednessLiteracy Training Service or LTS which is a program developed to train students to become teachers of reading, writing, and number skills to school children, out of school youth, and other segments of society who are in need of their serviceCivic Welfare Training Service or CWTS refers to programs or activities contributory to the general welfare and the enhancement of life for the members of the community or the betterment of its facilities, especially those devoted to improving health, education, environment, entrepreneurship, safety, recreation and morals of the Filipino citizens.

Mapua, just like other universities, is following the rules and regulations set by the Republic Act No. 9163.

14. conduct an interview either online or offline with five SHS students about their experiences on communication breakdowns Decide the most appropriate strategy to avoid each communication breakdown.​


Answer:

1. The class next door is making noise so the teacher and us, her students, are not able to concentrate well on the discussion.

Strategy: Stay in a more quiet area. Or else, remind the class next door to act accordingly.

2. Groupmates are not able to do assigned tasks because of a disagreement.

Strategy: Do away with emotional barriers. Talk out disagreements. If not possible, learn to draw a dividing line between personal feelings and school work.

3. Teacher's soft voice is hardly audible.

Strategy: The teacher can make use of a microphone. Also, he can get nearer to the students or adjust the volume of his voice.

4. Students are bored in class so they are not able to draw attention to the topic being discussed.

Strategy: The teacher needs to make the class lively and interesting. The instructor should use verbal and non-verbal language. Also, he needs to vary the tone of the voice or crack jokes at times to energize the listeners.

5. Lary has hearing defects. Most of the time, he finds it hard to hear lectures.

Strategy: Larry needs to find a better spot where he is nearer the teacher. The teacher may also choose to use visual aids to aid him in understanding the lectures.

Explanation:

Communication breakdown can be addressed with the right kind of strategy.

25. Exploring the Lived Experiences of SHS Students in Online Distance Learning​


Answer:

Online distance learning has become the norm for many students due to the COVID-19 pandemic. While it has provided a way for students to continue their education, it has also brought about significant changes in the way they learn and engage with their peers and teachers.

To explore the lived experiences of SHS students in online distance learning, several research methods can be employed, including:

Surveys - A survey can be used to gather information about students' experiences in online distance learning. Questions could include how they feel about online classes, how much time they spend studying, and their overall satisfaction with the learning experience.

Interviews - Interviews can be conducted to gain a deeper understanding of students' experiences. Open-ended questions could be used to allow students to share their thoughts and feelings in more detail.

Focus groups - Focus groups can be organized to bring together a small group of students to discuss their experiences. This can provide a platform for students to share their experiences with others and hear the experiences of their peers.

Observations - Observations can be conducted to see how students engage with online classes and how they interact with their peers and teachers. This can provide insight into the challenges they face and the strategies they use to overcome them.

Through these research methods, several themes may emerge regarding the lived experiences of SHS students in online distance learning. For example, students may feel isolated and disconnected from their peers and teachers, struggle with motivation and time management, and face technological challenges such as internet connectivity and device issues.

Understanding these themes can inform the development of interventions and support systems to enhance the online learning experience for SHS students
