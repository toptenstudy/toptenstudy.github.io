9. Bertha deposited 1000 into a retirement account when she was 18. How much will Bertha have in this account after 50 years at a yearly simple interest rate of 7.5%​


Answer:

To solve this problem, we can use the formula for simple interest:

I = P * r * t

where I is the interest earned, P is the principal or initial deposit, r is the interest rate, and t is the time in years.

We know that Bertha deposited $1000 when she was 18, and that the interest rate is 7.5% per year. We need to find out how much she will have in the account after 50 years, so we can set t = 50.

Using the formula for simple interest, we get:

I = P * r * t

I = 1000 * 0.075 * 50

I = 3750

This means that Bertha will earn $3750 in interest over 50 years. To find out how much she will have in total, we need to add the interest to the initial deposit:

Total = P + I

Total = 1000 + 3750

Total = 4750

Therefore, after 50 years, Bertha will have $4750 in her retirement account.

Answer:

Collars are both functional and decorative features sewn to the neckline of a garment. As decoration, the collar is often the focal point of the garment drawing the eye upward to frame the face. As a functional feature, collars often provide warmth and protection from the elements

Collar determines the overall style for a dress shirt, dictating its appearance and formality. It also serves as an extension of the face so there needs to be a balance between the collar and the wearer. And finally, the collar determines the type of tie and knot that should be worn.
