8. give three examples of unitary form and give three examples of strophic formpls answer ​


Unitary Form:

Sonata Form: A multi-sectional form often used in classical music, typically consisting of an exposition, development, and recapitulation.

Rondo Form: A multi-sectional form that alternates between a recurring main theme (the "rondo") and contrasting sections.

Concerto Form: A multi-sectional form used in concertos, typically consisting of an orchestral introduction, a solo section, and a tutti section.

Strophic Form:

Ballad: A song in which each verse is sung to the same melody and usually tells a story.

Blues: A musical form characterized by a simple harmonic structure and repeated verses with changing lyrics.

Hymn: A song of praise or worship, often sung in religious services.

It is worth noting that there are many forms of music and it's possible to find some overlaps between them.

Some examples may not be strictly classified as Unitary or Strophic, and there are many variations and subcategories of each of these forms.

22. give atleast three example of inductive and three example of deductive ​


Answer:

Deductive:

1. All women are mortal. Maria is a woman. Therefore, Maria is mortal.

2. All birds have feathers. All robins are birds. Therefore, robins have feathers.

3. It's dangerous to drive on icy streets. The streets are icy now, so it would be dangerous to drive on the streets.

Inductive:

1. The coin I pulled from the bag is a penny. That coin is a penny. A third coin from the bag is a penny. Therefore, all the coins in the bag are pennies.

2. The first lipstick I pulled from my bag is red. The second lipstick I pulled from my bag is red. Therefore, all the lipsticks in my bag are red.

3. My mother is Irish. She has blond hair. Therefore, everyone from Ireland has blond hair.

Examples of Current and Non-current AssetsASSETS

In accounting, assets are any resources with value owned by the business, company, entity or person. Assets have two categories, these are:

Current AssetsNon-current Assets1. Current Assets

-these are assets that can be readily converted to cash in a normal operating cycle of a business. The normal operating cycle of a business is within 1 year.

Some Examples of Current Assets

Cash and cash equivalentsAccounts receivableInventoryPrepaid ExpensesMarketable Securities2. Non-current Assets

-these are business resources that are considered long-term assets or long-term investments that have a longer useful life that is usually more than 1 year. Not easily convertible to cash.

Some Examples of Non-Current Assets

LandProperty, Plant and EquipmentTrademarksLong-term InvestmentsGoodwill

All fixed assets and intangible assets fall under the category of non-current assets.
