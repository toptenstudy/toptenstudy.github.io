Answer:

The one that contains the most lipids is letter C. Olive oil.

Explanation:

Let us define each choice so we can choose the best answer for the question.

Choice A: Banana

Banana is a tropical fruit that is famous to be a rich source of potassium. In biochemistry, fruits are known to have fructose.

Fructose or fruit sugar is a type of sugar found in many fruits and vegetables. Fruits are source of sugar in a human diet, and sugar is a type of carbohydrate.

Choice B: Champorado

Champorado is a Filipino food that is eaten during breakfast or snack (merienda) time.

Champorado is cooked using sticky rice or glutinous rice. Rice is a good source of carbohydrate, hence, like fruit that contains sugar, champorado also contains carbohydrate.

Choice C: Olive Oil

Olive oil is simply fat in liquid form. From its name, it comes from a crop named olive. This oil is commonly used in cooking.

In biochemistry, lipids are defined to be molecules which contain hydrocarbons. Lipid examples include fats, oils, and waxes.

Since Olive oil are mainly produced from fats, hence, olive oil contains the most lipids.

Choice D: Cheese

Cheese is a product of fermentation, to be specific, fermentation of milk. So basically, cheese is also milk, and milk is a good source of plenty of nutrients; calcium, Vitamin B2 and B12, protein, potassium, and phosphorus.

28. lipids composed of which contain ​


Answer:

Lipids are a diverse group of organic compounds that are insoluble in water but soluble in organic solvents such as ether, benzene, and chloroform. The main elements that make up lipids are carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen.

Lipids can be broadly classified into several categories, including fatty acids, triglycerides, phospholipids, steroids, and waxes.

Fatty acids are long-chain hydrocarbons with a carboxyl group (-COOH) at one end. Triglycerides are composed of three fatty acids attached to a glycerol backbone. Phospholipids are similar to triglycerides but have a phosphate group attached to one of the fatty acid chains. Steroids have a four-ring structure and include compounds such as cholesterol, testosterone, and estrogen. Waxes are esters of long-chain fatty acids and long-chain alcohols.

Overall, lipids are important for energy storage, insulation, cell membrane structure, hormone production, and many other biological functions.
