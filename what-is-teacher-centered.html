7. In the learner-centered teacher takes on the role of facilitator: guide, coach, conductor, midwife, gardener. In what way you can explain the above profession to be as facilitator?​


Answer:

As a facilitator, the teacher takes on the role of guiding, coaching, and supporting students in their learning journey. Instead of being the sole source of knowledge, the teacher encourages and facilitates active and collaborative learning among students. The teacher creates a learning environment that fosters exploration, inquiry, and critical thinking. The teacher also serves as a conductor, orchestrating and coordinating learning activities and resources to ensure that students achieve their learning goals. Like a midwife, the teacher nurtures and supports the growth and development of students, helping them to overcome obstacles and challenges. Finally, like a gardener, the teacher cultivates and tends to the learning environment, providing the necessary resources and support for students to thrive and flourish. Overall, the teacher as a facilitator empowers students to take ownership of their learning, and encourages them to become lifelong learners.

9. 3. Teacher Cindy wants to measure the ALS Community Learning Center (CLC). The room floor is n exact square and the one side measures 3 meters. What is the perimeter of the CLC?​


Answer:

12 Meters

Step-by-step explanation:

If one side of the room measures 3 meters, and the room is an exact square, then all sides of the room must also measure 3 meters.

To find the perimeter of the room, we need to add up the lengths of all four sides. Since all four sides have the same length of 3 meters, we can multiply 3 by 4 to get the total perimeter:

Perimeter = 4 x 3 meters

Perimeter = 12 meters

Therefore, the perimeter of the ALS Community Learning Center (CLC) is 12 meters.

16. each wall of your classroom forms a straight angle. From the teachers table to the center of the wall in front a line is dividing the straight angle into two. if the first angle measures 78° what is the measure of the 3rd angle?pls need kona po answer​


Answer:

Since each wall of the classroom forms a straight angle, the sum of the first angle and the third angle is 180 degrees.

Let x be the measure of the third angle.

We know that the line dividing the straight angle in front of the teacher's table forms two angles with measures of 78 degrees and x degrees. Thus, the second angle formed by the line dividing the straight angle is:

180 - 78 - x = 102 - x

Since the line dividing the straight angle forms two adjacent angles, the sum of the second angle and the third angle must also be 180 degrees:

(102 - x) + x = 180

Simplifying the equation, we get:

102 = x + 180 - x

102 = 180

Therefore, the measure of the third angle is:

x = 78 degrees

Hence, the measure of the third angle is 78 degrees.

21. what are the Advantages and disadvantages of Teacher- centered instruction?​


Answer:

Advantages of Teacher Centered Approach

1. As the curriculum is designed by the teacher, it become easy to achieve the desired goals.

2. Subject matter become psychologically sound due to its relevance with the interests, needs and level of the children.

3. Content/Subject matter is logically arranged. – Irrelevant material/ Subject matter is avoided.

4. Teachers feel comfortable and confident in the classroom activities.

5. Democracy is encouraged.

6. Co-operation is developed.

7. Society/Community is also involved (directly or indirectly) in the development of curriculum.

8. No objection is raised by the teacher in connection with the availability of sources and resources.

Disadvantages of Teacher Centered Approach

1. A change in the attitude on the part of learners, teachers and community is difficult to develop.

2. Lack of sources and resources.

3. Hinderance due to rigid administration, planning and management.

4. It will become difficult to maintain a common standared in various institutions.

5. The existing curriculum for the teaching training institutions is not suitable for the teacher centered approach.

6. A drastic change in the examination system/evaluation will be required.
