8. which of the following substances when dissolve in water will conduct electricity? a)glucose b)oil c)gasoline d)monosodium glutamate ...


ELECTROLYTE (IONIC) SOLUTION

- In Electrochemistry, an electrolyte solution is a medium (Ionic compound dissolved in solvent - usually water) containing ions that is electrically conducting through the movement of ions, but not conducting electrons. This includes most soluble salts, acids, and bases dissolved in a polar solvent like water (universal solvent). An electrolyte solution is a solution that generally contains ions, atoms or molecules that have lost or gained electrons, and is electrically conductive. Therefore, they are often referred to as ionic solutions, but there are some cases where the electrolytes are not ions.

Question: Which of the following substances when dissolve in water will conduct electricity? a)glucose b)oil c)gasoline d)monosodium glutamate

- The answer is D. MONOSODIUM GLUTAMATE, in common language it is known as "VETSIN"

how? When MSG or monosodium glutamate 'VETSIN" is dissolved in water or formed an aqueous solution, the ions of the compound will separate, thus forming an ionic or electrolyte solution that can conduct electricity. In addition, MSG are soluble in water, thus it will dissolve. Take note that monosodium glutamate cannot conduct electricity on its solid form or solid state/phase.

Another common ionic solution is the table salt "ASIN" or NaCl. Unfortunately, glucose or any sugar form cannot conduct electricity even if it is dissolved in a solvent, because it is not an ionic compound.
