15. 7 product or solution found at home


Characteristics of the product are also called "features".  These descriptions differentiate the product from others at the market.  Characteristics of a product might also help determine the value or price of it.
Thus, premium and special features might also able to fetch a premium price. These features and characteristics are combinations of demand for the products and their market values help determine the marketing mix.

The following are products commonly found at home:

1. Detergent - it is a mixture of surfactants with cleaning properties in dilute solutions. Alkylbenzenesulfonates are commonly found in detergents.  It is a family of compounds that are similar to soap but are more soluble in hard water. That is because the polar sulfonate of detergents is less likely than the polar carboxylate of soap to bind to calcium and other ions found in hard water.

2. Dishwashing Soap - these are manufactured variously as cartridges, gel, liquids, pacs, powder, and tablets. Any dishwashing product may contain bleach, enzymes, or rinsing aids. However, they are may be homemade: using ingredients such as borax, essential oil, eucalyptus oil and grated bar soap, etc.

3. Shampoo - Shampoo is generally made up by combining surfactant, sodium lauryl sulfate and a co-surfactant, and most often cocamidopropyl betaine in water.

4. Chlorine-based Bleach -  Chlorine is the basis for the most common bleaches: the solution of sodium hypochlorite and calcium hypochlorite. Oxidizing bleaching agents that do not contain chlorine are usually based on peroxides (hydrogen peroxide, sodium percarbonate and sodium perborate). Most bleaches are oxidizing agents, some are reducing agents such as sodium dithionite and sodium borohydride.

5. Baking Soda - Also called "sodium bicarbonate" and it is usually used in cooking, for cleaning, or in toothpaste. It's a salt composed of sodium ions and bicarbonate ions. It is a white solid that is crystalline but often appears as a fine powder. It has a slightly salty, alkaline taste resembling that of washing soda (sodium carbonate). 

6. Coffee -  it is a brewed drink prepared from roasted and ground coffee beans. More than 1,000 chemical compounds are found in coffee: Hdrocarbons, Alkaloids, Alcohols, acids and anhydrides, Nitrogenous compounds and amines, Sulfurs, Carbohydrates, Proteins, Lipids, Phenolic compounds, Oils, etc.

7. Milk - it is a pale liquid produced by the mammary glands of mammals. It is the primary source of nutrition for infant mammals, including humans. A single glass of milk can contain painkillers, antibiotics and growth hormones. 

8. Honey - Its natural sweetness from the monosaccharides fructose and glucose has about the same relative sweetness as granulated sugar. It is a sugary food substance produced and stored by certain social hymenopteran insects (bees). It is from floral nectar or aphid honeydew, through regurgitation, enzymatic activity, and water evaporation.The variety of honey produced by honey bees (the genus Apis) is the most well-known.

9. Cooking Oil - This oil is a Saturated fat, Monounsaturated fat Polyunsaturated fat, or Trans fat. It is plant, animal, or synthetic fat used in frying, baking, and other types of cooking. It is usually used in food preparation and flavouring. Some oils that contain saturated fat, such as coconut oil, palm oil and palm kernel oil are solid. Sources are olive oil, palm oil, soybean oil, canola oil (rapeseed oil), corn oil, peanut oil and other vegetable oils, as well as animal-based oils like butter and lard.
10. Toothpaste - Derived from a variety of components, the three main ones being abrasives, fluoride, and detergents.
