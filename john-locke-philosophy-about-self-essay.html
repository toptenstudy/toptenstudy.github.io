2. John Lock philosopy about self


John Locke's Philosophy About Self

John Locke was an English philosopher who is famous for his theory of personal identity. This theory is considered as John Locke's Philosophy About Self. In his theory, personal identity or the "self" is found on a person's consciousness or memory. In an essay that he himself wrote, Locke proposed that the "self" is a "thinking intelligent being that has reasons and reflection." Furthermore, he said that the "self" considers itself to be itself with the same thinking, in different times and places." Thus, for Locke, personal identity is defined as the "sameness of a rational being." Simultaneously, John Locke's philosophy about self is also called as "Lockean Memory Theory of Personal Identity".

Aside from John Locke, more philosophers have their own definition of self. Some of them are the following:
