Explanation:

Phospholipids are amphiphilic lipids consisting of a glycerol backbone or an amino-alcohol sphingosine backbone, which is esterified to one or two fatty acids, a phosphate group and a hydrophilic residue.

The structure of phospholipids in building off of a glycerol backbone​

structure of phospholipids in building off of a glycerol backbone

Like fats, they are usually composed of fatty acid chains bound to the glycerol skeleton. However, instead of the three fatty acid tails, there are generally only two phospholipids, and the third carbon in the glycerol skeleton is occupied by modified phosphate groups. Different phospholipids have different modifiers on the phosphate group, with choline (nitrogen-containing compounds) and serine (amino acids) being common examples. Different modifiers give  different properties and roles to intracellular phospholipids. What prevents the intracellular watery goo (cytosolic sol) from leaking? 24. What is the molality of 38% by mass of glycerol (C3H8O3) solution?


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: Assume that the mass of solution is 100 g.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \text{mass of solute} = \text{38 g} \\ & \text{mass of solvent} = \text{62 g = 0.062 kg} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute (C₃H₈O₃).

Note that the molar mass of C₃H₈O₃ is 92.09 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of solute} & = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}} \\ & = \frac{\text{38 g}}{\text{92.09 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{0.41264 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Final Step: Calculate the molality of solution.

Note that 1 molal (m) is equal to 1 mole per kilogram (mol/kg).

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{molality} & = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{mass of solvent}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.41264 mol}}{\text{0.062 kg}} \\ & = \text{6.66 mol/kg} \\ & = \boxed{6.66 \: m} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molality of the solution is 6.66 m.

28. 5. What are the mole fractions of the components of the solution formed when 92 g glycerol is mixed with 90 g water? (molar mass of water = 18; molar mass of glycerol = 92).


The answer is 0.167

Solution:

Moles of water = 90g/18g = 5 mol water

Moles of glycerol = 92g/92g = 1 mol glycerol

Total moles in solution = 5+1 = 6mol

Mole fraction of water = 5mol/6mol = 0.833

Mole fraction of glycerol = 1mol/6mol = 0.167

Glycerol (1,2,3-trihydroxypropane) has three -OH groups per molecule while ethylene glycol (1,2-dihydroxyethane) has two -OH groups.
