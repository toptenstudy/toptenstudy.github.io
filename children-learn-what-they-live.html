3. Essay on children learn what they live with


Answer:

Children do not learn through what parents preach but children learn through the action of their parents. Therefore, parents must be very careful about what they do to their children and how they behave in front of them.

If a Child Lives with Criticism He/She Learns to Condemn

Criticism can have a very bad effect on the growth, development, and thinking of children. Parents cannot even imagine how bad its effects could be. It affects their overall personality. As children absorb everything that they are told during the early childhood years very easily, the criticism may seep deep inside them. What they hear in form of criticism may become their actual personality and surely will come out in form of condemnation from them.

If a Child Lives with Hostility He/She Learns to Fight

A loving and caring atmosphere is what children need to grow up into well-balanced individuals. If they are surrounded with hostility, they may not be able to develop the qualities like empathy, friendliness, and a caring attitude. Hostility will make children aggressive and they will learn to fight without thinking about what is right and what is wrong.

If a Child Lives with Ridicule He/She Learns to be Shy

If children are constantly subjected to ridicule their self-esteem and confidence are crushed. Lack of self-esteem and confidence will give rise to shyness in them. If children become shy and introverted, they may face a lot of difficulties while walking the path of success in life.

If a Child Lives with Shame He/She Learns to Feel Guilty

Children are innocent; they do not know the difference between right and wrong. Due to constant shaming, they will develop guilt which will never let them live peacefully. Guilt may be responsible for stress and tension in them which may also affect their health.

If a Child Lives with Tolerance He/She Learns to be Patient

Patience is a very important trait that every parent must develop in their children. It is possible to foster patience in children only if parents show tolerance. It does not mean that giving in to their unreasonable demands. It means that parents should not get hyper when children make any mistake or behave badly. They must provide the right guidance by being calm and showing tolerance.

If a Child Lives with Encouragement He/She Learns Confidence

Encouragement from parents is very important for children to succeed in life. Parents can play an important role in encouraging children and boosting their confidence.

If a Child Lives with Praise He/She Learns to Appreciate

Praising children for the right things and at right time can help them learn to appreciate the good deeds and hard work of others. If they are praised for good behavior or their hard work they will repeat the same behavior for earning your praise.

If a Child Lives with Fairness He/She Learns Justice

If you practice fairness with your child and if you teach your child to be fair, he will never be unjust to others throughout his life.

If a Child Lives with Security He/She Learns to Have Faith

When you always stand by your child, he will develop a sense of security. In the future when he has to take any important decision or if he is in distress he will surely have faith in you. He will have faith that you have solutions to all his problems.

If a Child Lives with Approval He/She Learns to Like Himself/Herself

Let your child follow his dreams. Do not burden him with your expectations. Never force him to do something in life to satisfy your ego. Only then your child can live his life to the fullest and learn to like himself.

If a Child Lives with Acceptance and Friendship He/She Learns to Find Love in the World

Shower your unconditional love on your child. Be there for him always. Celebrate his every little achievement. Teach good values to him and accept him for, however, he is. Do not compare him with other children. Let him grow in your love and care.
