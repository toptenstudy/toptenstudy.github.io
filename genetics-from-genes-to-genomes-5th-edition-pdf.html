29. genetically modified organism is simply the product of gene editing. Agree or disagree?​


Genome editing, also known as gene editing, is a form of genetic engineering that produces genetically modified organisms (GMOs). Genome editing is a group of procedures that modifies the genetic make-up of bacteria, animals, and plants.

Gene editing is distinct from genetic modification because it does not result in the introduction of DNA from other species and produces new varieties similar to those that could be produced more slowly by natural breeding processes. However, they are currently regulated in the same way as genetically modified organisms.

What is genetically modified organism (GMO)?

An organism that has had its DNA altered through the use of genetic engineering methods is referred to as a genetically modified organism (GMO). Breeding techniques have been used by humans to alter creatures for thousands of years. Dogs, livestock, and even corn have all undergone years of selective breeding to develop specific desired features.

Foods that have been genetically modified (GM) are those that come from species whose genetic material (DNA) has been altered in a way that does not occur naturally, such as by introducing a gene from another organism.

DNA insertion into an organism's genome is a technique used in genetic engineering (GE). New DNA is inserted into plant cells to create a genetically modified plant. The cells are often cultured in tissue culture where they eventually become plants.
