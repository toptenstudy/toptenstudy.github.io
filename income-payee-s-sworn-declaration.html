19. Which involves more action from the payee? What do you think are the advantages and disadvantages of each?


Answer:

A. Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT)

B. Check

In electronic funds transfer (EFT), the payee typically only needs to provide their account information to the payer and the funds are transferred electronically. This requires less action from the payee compared to writing and mailing a check.

The advantage of EFT is that it is generally faster and more convenient for both the payer and payee. It also reduces the risk of lost or stolen checks. However, there is a risk of fraud if the payee's account information is compromised.

On the other hand, writing and mailing a check involves more action from the payee as they need to physically write the check and send it to the payer. The advantage of this method is that it provides a paper trail for both parties and can serve as a record of payment. However, it is slower and there is a risk of lost or stolen checks.
