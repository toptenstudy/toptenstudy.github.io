"A Father Goes to Court" is a short story written by Carl Sandburg.

In the story, the father is summoned to court for abandoning his family. The father argues that he left because he was unable to provide for his family and wanted to give them a better life. The judge listens to both sides and ultimately decides to dismiss the case, but not before delivering a powerful speech about the importance of family and responsibility.

Explanation:
The central event that affects the course of the story is the father's decision to leave his family. This leads to his appearance in court and the subsequent legal proceedings. The story explores the complex issues of poverty, responsibility, and family relationships. The father's testimony and the judge's decision ultimately lead to a resolution of sorts, but the story leaves open the question of whether the father's actions were justified or not.
