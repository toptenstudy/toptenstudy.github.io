6. Recall the purposes of military training and enumerate them​


The purposes of military training:

Forming patterns of thought, attitude, and patterns of action as a marine soldier.Forming the mental attitude of a responsive soldierForming a mental attitude and high morals and assigned to various fields both at sea and on land.Forming physical and spiritual excellence so that they are able to carry out their duties and responsibilities agilely.Strengthen the understanding of defending the country.Instill a sense of love for the country.Introducing and preparing discipline and character traits that must be possessed by members of the army.Introduce and train the basics of military skills that can be applied.

FURTHER EXPLANATION:

Military training is the basis of education to form mindsets, attitudes, and patterns of action as a marine soldier. Military education is full of hard physical forging and high discipline, but physical development is not synonymous with violence. Education in the military is a measured, gradual, and continuous physical training, he explained again.

The military world is synonymous with strong physical strength. Military-style physical exercises include planks, push ups, sit ups, cooper running, and pull ups. If these exercises can be done regularly, you will get maximum results.

The purpose of physical training in military education, he continued, aims to create a soldier's mental attitude that is responsive. Responsive means that he must be able to appear and be proficient in his duties which is balanced with adequate ability and intelligence. Second, it means having high mentality and morals and being assigned to various fields, both at sea and on land. Third, it means having physical and spiritual excellence so that they are able to carry out their duties and responsibilities agilely.
