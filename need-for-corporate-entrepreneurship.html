12. Give at least five (5) advantages and (5) disadvantages of group entrepreneurshipB. Define the legal form of ownership of business.Partnership1 .Cooperative2.Corporations3.partnership​


1. COOPERATIVE

is an organization which is owned and run jointly by its members, who share the profits or benefits.

️ADVANTAGES:

Funding OpportunitiesCooperatives help people come together as a community.Less TaxationReduces liabilities for each member.Perpetual Life

️DISADVANTAGES:

Fewer investment incentives to offer.Struggle to establish unique branding.Slow decisions come out of cooperatives.Aren’t as profitable to the owners or founders.Lack of staff

2. CORPORATION

has rights, powers, and attributes where owners are called stockholders.

ADVANTAGES:

The corporation has perpetual lifeThe stockholders have limited liability.The shares of stock can be easily transferredCan attract and induce the capital and savings of many individuals who may want to invest in a business enterprise.Management Expertise

DISAVANTAGES

Closely watched by the government.Hired men may be clumsy in their methods of running the affairs of the corporationSubjected to "double taxation"Control over the majority of the stocks would mean the control of the affairs of the corporation.The relation between the stockholders and the corporation is often not very close.

3. PARTNERSHIP

Involves two or more persons in agreement. These persons are called partners.

ADVANTAGES:

More capitalGreater borrowing capacityEasy to establishBetter decision-makingAccess to knowledge, skills, experience and contacts.

28. Describe the principles of entrepreneurship? need help


Answer:

1. Be a Solution Provider

You must note that entrepreneurship is not just about making money

2. Have a Vision

You are not an entrepreneur merely to make a living

3. Choose the Right Team

When assembling your team, it is imperative to gather a team of individuals with the same mindset and attitude towards achieving a common goal.

4. Viable Product/Service

Let your product/service fulfill a need, be innovative and the approach, a little different from other regular businesses.

5. Capital

Good business plan always draw investors. Capital should be your least worry when you have a solution.

6. Accountability

As an entrepreneur, you are accountable to the success or failure of your business, not your employees, investors or advisors

7. Growth and Marketing

Every successful business grew over the years. Most big companies started small.

8. Know Your Customer

Your customer base determines the life of your business.

9. Priorities

For success in business, you must categorize things in order of importance.

10. Never Give Up

The never give up attitude is one quality an entrepreneur must possess.

When you are running a business, you must be aware of the risks and weaknesses of your chosen product and business type. You must develop a contingency plan to overcome these risks and challenges.
