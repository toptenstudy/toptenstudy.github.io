8. It is a behaviour of light that cause the spreading of light. ​


BEHAVIOUR OF LIGHT THAT CAUSES THE SPREADING OF LIGHT

The small bending of light as it travels around an object's edge is known as diffraction. It is so bright that what has been reflected might result in light, dark, or colorful bands. The silver line that appears around the margins of clouds or coronas encircling the sun or moon is an optical illusion caused by light diffraction.

Diffraction of light happens when a light wave travels through a corner or via a hole or slit that is physically the same size as, or even smaller than, the wavelength of that light.

The degree of bending is determined by the distance between the aperture and the wavelength of light. The bending will be nearly undetectable if the opening is substantially greater than the wavelength of the light. The amount of bending is large and plainly visible with the naked eye if the two are closer in size or equal in size.

When waves bend around small barriers or spread out after passing through small apertures, this is known as diffraction. All waves, including sound waves, water waves, and electromagnetic waves such as visible light, are subjected to diffraction.
