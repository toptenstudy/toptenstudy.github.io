17. what are the different parts of plant cell have you observe under the compound microscope using an onion cell​


Answer:

When using a compound microscope to observe an onion cell, you should be able to see several different parts of the plant cell, including the cell wall, cell membrane, cytoplasm, and nucleus. You may also be able to see organelles such as the endoplasmic reticulum, Golgi apparatus, and mitochondria. Other structures that may be visible depending on the stage of the cell's life cycle include the vacuole, nucleolus, and cell wall. These parts of the plant cell can be seen when a thin slice of onion tissue is placed on a microscope slide and viewed under high magnification.
