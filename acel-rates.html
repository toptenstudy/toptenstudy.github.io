1. A bicycle in ally at rest uniformly acel rates at 2.0 m/² For 5s Determine the final velocity?​


Answer:

The final velocity of the bicycle is 10 m/s.

Explanation:

Given :

A bicycle is initially at rest uniformly accelerates for 5 s at a rate of 2 m/s².

To Find :

Final velocity of the bicycle.

Solution :

Here, we have : Acceleration (a) = 2 m/s², Initial velocity (u) = 0 m/s (Since, the bicycle is initially at rest), Time taken (t) = 5 s. So, by using the first equation of motion, we will find the final velocity of the bicycle.

According to the question :

[tex] \sf \longrightarrow {v\ =\ u\ +\ at} [/tex]

[tex] \sf \longrightarrow {v\ =\ 0\ +\ 2 \times 5} [/tex]

[tex] \sf \longrightarrow {v\ =\ 0\ +\ 10} [/tex]

[tex] \sf \longrightarrow {\underline{v\ =\ 10\ m/s}} \bigstar [/tex]

∴ Hence, the final velocity of the bicycle is 10 m/s.
