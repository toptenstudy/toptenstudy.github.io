16. What is the format for sources of data in research chapter 2?


Answer:

In chapter 2 of a research paper, which typically covers the literature review, it is important to properly cite and reference all sources of data used in the research. The format for sources of data may vary depending on the citation style required by your institution or journal. Some common citation styles include APA, MLA, Chicago, and Harvard.

In general, the format for citing sources of data in research chapter 2 includes the following information:

Author(s): The name(s) of the author(s) who produced the data. If the data is produced by an organization, use the name of the organization as the author.

Year of publication: The year the data was published or released.

Title: The title of the dataset, report, or document that contains the data.

Publisher: The organization or company that published the dataset, report, or document.

URL or DOI: The URL or digital object identifier (DOI) for the dataset, report, or document.

Here are some examples of how to format sources of data in different citation styles:

APA Style:

Author, A. A. (Year). Title of dataset [Data set]. Publisher. URL or DOI

MLA Style:

Author. Title of dataset. Publisher, Year. URL or DOI.

Chicago Style:

Author. Year. Title of dataset. Publisher. URL or DOI.

Harvard Style:

Author. Year. Title of dataset. Publisher, URL or DOI.

Note that some citation styles may require additional information, such as the version number of the dataset or the page numbers of the report. Always consult the guidelines for the specific citation style you are using to ensure that you include all required information.

Chapter 2 of a research paper typically discusses the various sources of data that were used to conduct the research project. The format for these sources of data typically include details such as the name of the source, the author or organization associated with the source, and the date the data was collected. These details are important for readers to understand where the information came from and how it might influence the research findings.

In addition to providing details about the source, the research chapter 2 should also explain how the data was collected. This can vary depending on the type of data and research methodology used, but generally should include information such as the sample size or number of participants, the method of collecting data (e.g. surveys, interviews, observation), and any procedures or probtocols that were followed during data collection. This helps readers understand the potential limitations of the data and the research results overall.

Finally, chapter 2 of a research paper should also provide a critical discussion of the data sources used. This can include an evaluation of the reliability and validity of the data, as well as any potential biases or limitations that might influence the research findings. This helps readers interpret the results of the research in a more nuanced way and understand the strengths and weaknesses of the overall study.

24. types of qualitative research pdf


Qualitative Research

The following are various types of qualitative research:

In-depth interview Focus groups Ethnographic research Content analysis Case study research

In-depth interview

This type of qualitative research is conducted by doing an interview regarding the topic. The researchers try to get the information they needed as much as possible. This can be done by one-on-one or group interview.

Focus groups

This type of qualitative research is similar to a group discussion. The people involved have similar knowledge and backgrounds relevant to the research topic they have.

Ethnographic research

Focuses on the behavior and environment of people.

Content analysis

Careful analysis of data provided that is done by experts on their respective fields.

Case study research

This type of research focuses more on a specific case and how that case could affect or influence other future researches.
