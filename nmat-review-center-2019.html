13. Based on the data gathered from World population review (Accessed April 3, 2019) on the population of the Philippines in 2019, the top three cities in terms of population in the Philippines are Quezon City with 2, 761, 720; Manila with 1, 600, 000; and Caloocan City with 1, 500, 000. Write the scientific notation of the total population of these three cities.​


Expressing Numbers in Scientific Notation

Quezon City - 2, 761, 720

    Scientific Notation: 2.761720 x [tex]10^{6}[/tex]

Manila - 1, 600, 000

    Scientific Notation: 1.60 x [tex]10^{6}[/tex]

Caloocan City -1,  500, 000

    Scientific Notation: 1.50 x [tex]10^{6}[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

Scientific notation is a shorthand method to write very large or very small numbers. Numbers are expressed in terms of exponents of 10 in a scientific notation. To write a number in scientific notation, we follow the steps below.

1. Move the decimal point to the right of the first digit in the number. Take note that if the number is a whole number, the decimal point is after the last digit or number.

  For example, the number is 2, 761, 720, we move the decimal point to the right of the first digit making it 2. 761720.

2. Count the number of places n that you moved the decimal point.

  In the previous example, we moved the decimal point six times.

3. Multiply the decimal number by 10 raised to a power of n. The n here is the number of times we moved the decimal point.

  The number now based on the example is 2.7610 x  [tex]10^{6}[/tex]

4. Indicate the sign of n or the power. If the decimal point was moved to the left as in a very large number, the n is positive. If the decimal point is moved to the right as in a very small number, the n is negative.

   In the previous example, since we move the decimal to the right then n is positive, giving us 2.7610 x  [tex]10^{6}[/tex]

24. REVIEWDo you still remember the earthquake that happen in Pampanga last April 22, 2019around 5 o'clock in the afternoon?What is an earthquake?Why some scientist says that earthquake had a good effect​


About the earthquake that happened in pampanga

On 22 April, at 5:15 p.m., a magnitude-6.1 earthquake occurred in northern Philippines, with the epicenter located near Castillejos, Zambales, according to the Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology (PHIVOLCS). The earthquake was tectonic in origin with a depth of 10 km.

What is an earthquake?

An earthquake is the shaking of the surface of the Earth resulting from a sudden release of energy in the Earth's lithosphere that creates seismic waves.

Why do some scientists says that earthquake had a good effect

Earthquakes are very useful to humans because they provide a picture of what’s going on underground. This can make oil and gas extraction more efficient, and allows scientists to monitor the progress of water during geothermal energy extraction.

They also tell you what is happening in the structure of the earth, for example, magma chambers, and allow scientists to monitor volcanoes and the threat of eruption.

Earthquakes can also tell us about the internal structure of the Earth. By measuring the time it takes the seismic waves to travel through the Earth we can map out the structure of the Earth down to the core.

On a global scale, earthquakes are just a symptom of the movements of the plates going on all the time, and they occur because the material at the surface at the earth is changing due to convection within the Earth’s mantle – mountains are being created, minerals from below are being brought up and new sea floors are being made.

Tectonic activity is essential to sustaining life on Earth. Matter is constantly being recycled between the atmosphere and the crust. We have continents because of tectonic activity. Mid ocean ridges support a huge amount of life, and may have been important in the origin of life, and the atmosphere is reliant on volcanic eruptions for it’s composition.
