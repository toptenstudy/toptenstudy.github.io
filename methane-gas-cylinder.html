16. A student collects 545 ml of methane (CHA) gas at a temperature of 25 °C at a pressure of 13.2264 psi. How manymoles of methane gas did the student collect?​


Given:

[tex]V = \text{545 mL = 0.545 L}[/tex]

[tex]P = \text{13.2264 psi} × \frac{\text{1 atm}}{\text{14.696 psi}} = \text{0.900 atm}[/tex]

[tex]T = \text{25°C + 273 = 298 K}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]n[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]PV = nRT[/tex]

[tex]n = \frac{PV}{RT}[/tex]

[tex]n = \frac{\text{(0.900 atm)(0.545 L)}}{(0.0821 \: \frac{\text{L • atm}}{\text{mol • K}})(\text{298 K})}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{n = \text{0.0200 mol}}[/tex]

18. What branch of science is methane gas?


Answer:ethane (US: /ˈmɛθeɪn/, UK: /ˈmiːθeɪn/) is a chemical compound with the chemical formula CH4 (one atom of carbon and four atoms of hydrogen). It is a group-14 hydride and the simplest alkane and is the main constituent of natural gas. The relative abundance of methane on Earth makes it an economically attractive fuel, although capturing and storing it poses technical challenges due to its gaseous state under normal conditions for temperature and pressure.

Naturally occurring methane is found both below ground and under the seafloor and is formed by both geological and biological processes. The largest reservoir of methane is under the seafloor in the form of methane clathrates. When methane reaches the surface and the atmosphere, it is known as atmospheric methane.[9] The Earth's atmospheric methane concentration has increased by about 150% since 1750, and it accounts for 20% of the total radiative forcing from all of the long-lived and globally mixed greenhouse gases.[10] Methane has also been detected on other planets, including Mars, which has implications for astrobiology research.[11]

21. Calculate the rate of effusion of methane gas, CH4, to nitrogen gas, N2.


Answer:

r CH₄ / r N₂ = 1.32

Explanation:

Quantities:

MM N₂ → molar mass of N₂

MM CH₄ → molar mass of CH₄

r N₂ → rate of effusion of N₂

r CH₄ → rate of effusion of CH₄

Calculating the molar mass of N2 and CH₄

MM N₂ = (14.01 g × 2) = 28.02 g

MM CH₄ = (12.01 g × 1) + (1.008 g × 4) = 16.04 g

Calculating the rate of effusion

r CH₄ / r N₂ = √(MM N₂ / MM CH₄)

r CH₄ / r N₂ = √(28.02 g / 16.04 g)

r CH₄ / r N₂ = 1.32
