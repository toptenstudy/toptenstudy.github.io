5. Matthew 17: 1-9 Relfection based on ur learnings about this gospel


Matthew 17:1-9 is a powerful gospel that tells the story of the Transfiguration of Jesus. In this story, Jesus takes Peter, James, and John up to a high mountain, where he is transfigured before them. His face shines like the sun, and his clothes become as white as light. Moses and Elijah also appear with Jesus, and they talk with him.

For me, this gospel is a powerful reminder of the divinity of Jesus and the importance of faith. The Transfiguration shows how Jesus is not just a human being, but also the Son of God. It also shows how faith can transform us, just as Jesus was transformed on the mountain.

The appearance of Moses and Elijah also reminds us of the importance of the past and the legacy of those who came before us. It shows how Jesus is connected to the history of God's people and how he fulfills the prophecies of the Old Testament.

Furthermore, the voice from the cloud that says, "This is my Son, the Beloved; with him, I am well pleased; listen to him!" is a powerful reminder of the importance of listening to Jesus and following his teachings. It shows how Jesus is the way, the truth, and the life, and that by following him, we can find salvation and eternal life.

In conclusion, Matthew 17:1-9 is a powerful gospel that reminds us of the divinity of Jesus, the importance of faith, the legacy of those who came before us, and the importance of listening to Jesus. It is a reminder that by following Jesus, we can find salvation and eternal life.

My reflection about Matthew 20:28 is centered on Jesus' teachings about "servant leadership" and what it means to be a leader.

"For Jesus came into this world not to be served, but to serve, and to give his life as a ransom for many."

This came when some of his disciples asked who is the greatest among them. They normally think that to be the greatest is tantamount to being the leader of the twelve. But to their surprise, Jesus' concept of leadership, and of being great is not in congruence with theirs.

For Jesus, to be great is to be the least of all, to be a leader is to be the servant of all. This is so becasue for Jesus, the true essence of leadership is "servant leadership".

Thus, he told his disciples, "if anyone who wish to be the leader of the twelve, he must be the servant of all, if anyone wish to be great, must make himself the least of all".

Jesus, showed them the essence of servant leadership in his ministry. He, being the son of God, exhibited the value of humility by serving his disciples.

In a single instance, Jesus was seen cleansing and washing the feet of all his disciples. He chose to be the least of them, he chose to be a leader by serving them.

Jesus' ministry is centered on serving the the people of his time. He served the possessed by driving away the demons inside them, he served the sick by healing them, he served the sinners by teaching them the value of repentance. and lastly, he served the world by offering His life for our sins. This is the reason why Jesus came into this world, to serve, and not to be served.

9. reflection about Matthew 5:44-45​


Answer: “But I say to you, love your enemies and pray for those who persecute you, that you may be children of your heavenly Father…” - Matthew 5:44-45

Today’s Gospel is probably one of the toughest to live out. When I was younger, I understood Jesus’ command to love my enemies as a strange one, but I went along with it because it didn’t seem that hard. Although I knew people that I didn’t particularly like, I was pretty generous in wishing everyone well, being kind, etc. As I’ve gotten older, however, I have found this Gospel harder to live by. With age has come experience and perspective, much of which is good but much of which is also bad. When another has offended me or a loved one, or even when I reflect on the evil that some people do to others whom I might not know, I find that it is increasingly difficult to love that person because of what they have done.

And yet, love is not something given to someone because of anything they have done or not done but because of who s/he is: a child of God. Jesus tells us that God loves all his children, the good and the bad, by giving them both the rain and the sunshine, and, as St. Paul says in today’s first reading, Jesus showed us this love by becoming poor for us so that we could become rich. His message shows us that love for all is essential to being God’s children and that he does not expect any less from us than to show that love to others.

Of course, this is challenging because it seems to go against our natural desire. This is the genius of Jesus’ call, though: we are called to move beyond ourselves to become like him. I don’t wish here to offer recommendations on how to implement this practically: our different experiences, dispositions, and outlooks are so varied that we probably already know areas in our life that we need to work on in order to be more loving, and each of us probably knows best the people and counsel that can help us achieve this goal. In any case, the theme among them is the call to be children of our Heavenly Father through loving all, even our enemies, just as he did. Let’s pray today that the Lord may help us identify who or what in our lives are our enemies so that we may be more demonstrative in our love toward them.

is the parable of the wedding banquet. The king represents God who sends out his messengers (the disciples) to invite VIP guests. 27. write reflection about st. matthew 22:14"For many are called but few are chosen"(make your own reflection about st. Matthew 22:14)​


If you will read the context before matthew 22:14, it talks about the king who prepared a wedding banquet for his son and he invited many people but they refused to attend. Jesus then ends the parable with this statement: “For many are invited, but few are chosen”. In this context, Jesus' statement touches on the great working together of man's choices and God's choices. Why didn't they attend the wedding reception? Because they turned down the invitation. Why didn't they attend the wedding reception? Because they were called but not chosen.
