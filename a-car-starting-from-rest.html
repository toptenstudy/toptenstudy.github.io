4. A car starts from rest and attains a speed of 60m/s in 20 s​


Answer:

Did you mean: "A car stars from rest and attains a speed of 60 m/s in 20s. What is its acceleration?"? If yes, then that would be our objective for this problem.

To find the acceleration of the car, recall first the formula of the acceleration.

[tex]a = \frac{v*v_{o}}{t}[/tex]

Where [tex]a[/tex] is the acceleration of the car (m/s²), [tex]v[/tex] as the final velocity of the car (m/s), [tex]v_{0}[/tex] as the starting velocity (m/s), and [tex]t[/tex] as the time measured in seconds.

Next, substitute the following given.

[tex]a = \frac{60-0}{20}[/tex]

When we say that the car is at rest. It has no speed. So, the speed of the car at rest is at 0 m/s.

Then, solve for [tex]a[/tex].

[tex]a = \frac{60}{20}[/tex]

a = 3 m/s²

Therefore, we can conclude that the acceleration of the car when it starts at rest and attains a speed of 60 m/s in 20 seconds is 3 m/s².

27. a car starts from rest and attains and maintains a of 50m/s in is secondhas the car traveled in 15second​pleasee pasagottt


Answer:

The car has traveled 750 meters in 15 seconds. This can be calculated using the formula: distance = speed x time. In this case, the speed is 50m/s and the time is 15 seconds, so 50 x 15 = 750 meters.

Keep in mind that this is assuming that the car maintains a constant velocity of 50m/s for the entire 15 seconds. If the car's velocity changes during that time, then the distance traveled would be different.

The car's displacement (distance traveled) after 15 seconds can be calculated using the formula:

displacement = initial velocity * time + (1/2) * acceleration * time^2

Since the initial velocity is 0 m/s, the formula becomes:

displacement = (1/2) * acceleration * time^2

Plugging in the values:

acceleration = 50/2 m/s^2 (since the car attains a velocity of 50 m/s in 2 seconds)

time = 15 seconds

displacement = (1/2) * (50/2) * (15)^2

displacement = (1/2) * 25 * 225

displacement = (1/2) * 5625

displacement = 2812.5 m

Therefore, the car has traveled 2812.5 meters in 15 seconds.
