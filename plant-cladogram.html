15. What are the parts of a cladogram and give definition each.​


Answer:

A cladogram is a diagram consisting of the following parts:

RootNodesCladesTaxon/OutgroupBranchesDefinition:

Root

A root is the starting point for any given cladogram. However, the root might also indicate that it comes from some other larger clades.

Nodes

Nodes indicate the bifurcating branch point of divergence in all cladograms.

Clades

Clades are groups of organisms or genes that include the most recent common ancestor of all of its members and all of the descendants of that most recent common ancestor.

Taxon / Outgroup

A taxon or an outgroup is the most distantly related group of animals that isn’t necessarily a clade.

Branches

A branch in a cladogram is a line that connects all the other parts of the cladogram.

21. What is similarity of cladogram and phylogenetic tree?​


Cladograms give a hypothetical picture of the actual evolutionary history

Cladograms give a hypothetical picture of the actual evolutionary historyof the organisms. Phylogenetic trees give an actual representation of the evolutionary history of the organisms. All the branches in a cladogram are of equal length as they do not represent any evolutionary distance between different groups.

Cladograms are basically based on the differences in the morphological characteristics of the group to be depicted. Hence, a cladogram is a hypothetical diagram. In contrast, phylogenetic trees are based on the genetic relationships between the organisms.
