8. 2. What are the [Na], [C] and [Ca] in a solution containing 0.015 M each of NaCl and CaCl2?​


Answer:

In a solution containing 0.015 M each of NaCl and CaCl2, we need to determine the concentration of Na+ (sodium ions), Cl- (chloride ions), and Ca2+ (calcium ions).

NaCl dissociates into Na+ and Cl- ions in solution, while CaCl2 dissociates into Ca2+ and 2Cl- ions. Therefore, the total concentration of Cl- ions in solution will be:

2 x [Cl-] = 2 x 0.015 M = 0.03 M

To calculate the concentration of Na+ ions, we can assume that all the NaCl dissociates completely in solution, so the concentration of Na+ ions will be equal to the concentration of NaCl:

[Na+] = 0.015 M

To calculate the concentration of Ca2+ ions, we need to consider that CaCl2 dissociates into two moles of Cl- ions and one mole of Ca2+ ions. Therefore, the concentration of Ca2+ ions will be:

[Ca2+] = 0.015 M / 2 = 0.0075 M

Therefore, the concentrations of Na+, Cl-, and Ca2+ ions in the solution are:

[Na+] = 0.015 M

[Cl-] = 0.03 M

[Ca2+] = 0.0075 M

11. CaCO3(s) + 2HCl -> CaCl2(aq) + H2O(l) (a) How much CO2(in L) will be produced by 250g of CaCO3 if it is completely reacted with HCl at 25oC and 1.0 atm?


Answer:

V = 61 L

Explanation:

Compute for the number of moles of CO2 produced.

n CO2 = 250 g CaCO3 × (1 mol CaCO3 / 100.09 g CaCO3) × (1 mol CO2 / 1 mol CaCO3)

n CO2 = 2.5 mol

Convert 25 °C to K

T = 25 °C + 273.15 = 298.15 K

Use Ideal Gas Law to solve for the volume of CO2.

PV = nRT

V = nRT / P

V = (2.5 mol)(0.082057 L•atm/mol•K)(298.15 K) / 1.0 atm

V = 61 L

Further explanation  

There are several chemical reactions  

1. Combination / Synthesis  

The combination from two-element

A + B ⇒ AB

2. Decomposition  

One compound breaks down into 2 components

AB ⇒ A + B

3. Single displacement

One element replaces another element in a compound  

A+BC  ⇒ AC + B

4. Double-Replacement reactions  

Two elements in two compounds replace each other to form new products  

AB + CD ⇒ AD + CB

5. Combustion  

Reaction with oxygen which produces CO₂ and water  

CxHy + O₂ ⇒ CO₂ + H₂O

(a) N₂(g) + 3H₂(g) → 2NH₃(g)

2 reactants N₂ and H₂ combine to form NH₃ : Syntesis

(b) Mg(OH)₂(s) + 2HCl(l) → MgCl₂(s) + 2H₂O(l)

Cation Mg⁺ and anion Cl⁻ switch places : Double-Replacement reactions  

(c) CH₄(g) + 2O₂(g) → CO₂(g) + 2H₂O(l)

Oxygen combines with hydrocarbon to form water and carbondioxide : Combustion

(d) H₂(g) + 1/2O₂(g) → H₂O(l)

2 reactants H₂ and O₂ combine to form H₂O : Syntesis

(e) HCl(aq) + NaOH(aq) → NaCl(aq) + H₂O(l)

Cation Na⁺ and anion Cl⁻ switch places : Double-Replacement reactions  

(f) CaCO₃(s) → CaO(s) + CO₂(g)

A single reactant CaCO₃ breaks down into CaO + CO₂ : Decomposition

16. balance the following equations ? CH4 + O2 CO2 + H2O CaCl2 + AgNO3- Ca(NO3)2 + AgCl C2H6O + O2 CO2 + H2O


Further explanation  

Equalization of chemical reactions (balanced chemical equations) can be done using variables. Steps in equalizing the reaction equation:  

1. gives a coefficient on substances involved in the equation of reaction such as a, b, or c, etc.  2. make an equation based on the similarity of the number of atoms where the number of atoms = coefficient × index (subscript) between reactant and product  3. Select the coefficient of the substance with the most complex chemical formula equal to 1  

1. CH₄ + O₂ ⇒ CO₂ + H₂O

CH₄ + aO₂ -----> bCO₂ + cH₂O  

C: left = 1, right = b -> b = 1  

H: left = 4, right = 2c ---> 4 = 2c ---> c = 2  

O: left = 2a, right = 2b + c ---> 2a = 2b + c ---> 2a = 2.1 + 2 -> 2a = 4 -> a = 2  

The equation becomes:  

CH₄ + 2 O₂ -----> CO₂ + 2 H₂O  

2. CaCl₂ + AgNO₃ ⇒Ca(NO₃)₂ + AgCl

aCaCl₂ + bAgNO₃ ⇒Ca(NO₃)₂ + cAgCl

Ca : left = a , right = 1 ⇒ a=1

Cl : left = 2, right = c ⇒ c=2

Ag : left = b, right = c  ⇒ b=c = 2

NO₃ : left = b, right = 2 ⇒ b=2

The equation becomes:  

CaCl₂ + 2AgNO₃ ⇒Ca(NO₃)₂ + 2AgCl

3. C₂H₆O + O₂ ⇒ CO₂ + H₂O

C₂H₆O + aO₂ ⇒ bCO₂ + cH₂O

C : left=2, right =b ⇒ b=2

H : left =6, right = 2c ⇒ 2c=6 ⇒c=3

O : left =2a+1, right=2b+c ⇒2a+1=2b+c⇒2a+1=2.2+3⇒2a=6⇒a=3

The equation becomes:  

C₂H₆O + 3O₂ ⇒ 2CO₂ + 3H₂O

25. How many millimeters of 0.55 M HCl are needed to react with 3.7 g CaCO3? 2HCl (aq) + CaCO3 (s) → CaCl2 (aq) + CO2 (g) + H2O (l)


Answer:

The balanced chemical equation for the reaction between HCl and CaCO3 is:

2HCl(aq) + CaCO3(s) → CaCl2(aq) + CO2(g) + H2O(l)

From the balanced equation, we can see that 2 moles of HCl react with 1 mole of CaCO3.

Calculate the moles of CaCO3:

moles of CaCO3 = 3.7 g / 100.09 g/mol = 0.037 mol

Use the mole ratio from the balanced equation to determine the moles of HCl needed:

moles of HCl = 2 x moles of CaCO3 = 2 x 0.037 mol = 0.074 mol

Calculate the volume of 0.55 M HCl required:

0.55 M HCl means 0.55 moles of HCl are present in 1 liter of solution.

moles of HCl = Molarity x Volume (in liters)

Volume (in liters) = moles of HCl / Molarity

Volume (in liters) = 0.074 mol / 0.55 mol/L = 0.1345 L

Convert the volume from liters to milliliters:

Volume (in milliliters) = Volume (in liters) x 1000

Volume (in milliliters) = 0.1345 L x 1000 = 134.5 mL

Therefore, 134.5 milliliters of 0.55 M HCl are needed to react with 3.7 g of CaCO3.
