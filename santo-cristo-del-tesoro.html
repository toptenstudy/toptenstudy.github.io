24. History of mother francisca del espiritu santo


Francisca de Fuentes, was born to Don Simon de Fuentes and Ana Maria Tamayo del Castillo in the City of Manila in 1647. Widowed and childless herself after a brief marriage, she dedicated herself to helping the sick, the needy and the young. In 1682, she received the habit of a Dominican tertiary and took the name Mother Francisca del Espiritu Santo. She was joined by a small group, and a family of religious founded in the Philippines had its beginning. Final approval came later, but the early years brought many difficulties. Francisca persevered. Her pioneering ideal of a religious community for Filipinas, in the spirit of St. Dominic, was based on a life of prayer, of penance and service for others. She herself set an example of these; and continued to guide and inspire her companions.

On August 24,1711, Mother Francisca del Espiritu Santo died at the age of 63, bringing to a happy end a life of deep fidelity, and leaving a legacy of service to the sick, the poor, and the young.

30. Mr. Santos and Mr del Rosario are business partners How much does each receive if a profit of P130,000 is divided in the ratio of 7 to 3 in favor of the Mr Santos ​


Answer:

Mr. Santos will receive ₱91,000, while Mr. del Rosario will get ₱39,000.

[tex]\\[/tex]

Explanation:

Based from the given problem, the profit of ₱130,000 will be divided by the business partners, Mr. Santos and Mr. del Rosario, in the ratio of 7:3 favoring Mr. Santos.

[tex]\\[/tex]

Given:

Profit = ₱130,000

Mr. Santos: 7x

Mr. del Rosario: 3x

[tex]\\[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Find the value of x

[tex] \large \sf 7x+3x = ₱130,000[/tex]

Add the ratio

[tex] \large \sf 7x+3x = ₱130,000 \implies 10x=₱130,000[/tex]

Divide both sides by 10

[tex]\large \sf 10x=₱130,000 \implies \LARGE \frac{10x}{10} = \frac{₱130,000}{10}[/tex]

Cancel

[tex]\LARGE \sf \frac{\cancel{10}x}{\cancel{10}} = \frac{₱130,00\cancel{0}}{1\cancel{0}} \implies \large x= ₱13,000[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the amount they'll receive by multiplying the value of x by the ratio

Mr. Santos: [tex]\large \sf 7x = 7(₱13,000) = \bold{₱91,000}[/tex]

Mr. del Rosario: [tex]\large \sf 3x = 3(₱13,000) = \bold{₱39,000}[/tex]

Step 3: Check if the sum of the products matches the profit

[tex]\large \sf ₱91,000+₱39,000=130,000[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

The sum of the products matches the profit, therefore, Mr. Santos will indeed receive ₱91,000, while Mr. del Rosario will get ₱39,000.
