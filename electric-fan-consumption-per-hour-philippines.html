Answer:

Thank you for providing more information. Here's how you can identify which of your electrical appliances uses up the most energy:

Calculate the energy consumption of each appliance: To calculate the energy consumption of each appliance, you need to multiply its power rating (in watts) by the number of hours it is used per month. For example, if your electric fan has a power rating of 50 watts and you use it for 8 hours per day, its energy consumption per month would be:

50 watts x 8 hours/day x 30 days/month = 12,000 watt-hours or 12 kilowatt-hours (kWh)

Compare the energy consumption of each appliance: Once you have calculated the energy consumption of each appliance, you can compare them to see which one uses up the most energy. The appliance with the highest energy consumption is the one that uses up the most energy.
