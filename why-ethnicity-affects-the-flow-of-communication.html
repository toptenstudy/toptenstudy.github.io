25. Barriers in communication include ethnicity and gender.


Barriers in communication include ethnicity and gender, it's refer to barrier intercultural communication.

Below are other barriers that exist in intercultural communication.

Barriers to cross-cultural communication

In all communication that occurs, both between individuals and between communities (groups), there are always obstacles that occur. According to Chaney and Martin, the following obstacles may arise:

1. Physical

Physically, cultural differences must have geographic barriers. In addition, other barriers such as communication media, time, and the environment are also.

2. Culture

The second difference is a matter of cultural differences that include religion, ethnicity, race, and other social differences. If it is not done well, then there could be misunderstandings in the future.

3. Perception

Everyone has their own views and different views and ways of judging. Sometimes even communication can occur, but on the other hand, it can also be hampered because each has considerations that are not always the same.

4. Motivation

This motivation is the willingness of individuals or groups to communicate. It could be that the listener doesn't want to hear, so there is a clear communication barrier.

5. Experience

Each individual has their own ability to understand something or think of an idea. Usually, a comprehensive and broad understanding is obtained by individuals if they have good experience as well.

In addition, the listener's experience is the same. If the levels are different, there will be obstacles to understanding the communication that occurs.

6. Emotions

Emotions are basic to human nature and how we can control them. The obstacle is that when there are bad feelings and the heart is not okay, communication will be hampered and it will even be difficult to reach the same point of view.

7. Language

Differences in language must be an obstacle when establishing communication. Ensure communication is in a mutually understandable language so as not to hinder the information transfer process.

8. Competition

Competition is meant here as a multitasking activity. If listeners listen to information while listening to other information or doing other activities, the potential for hearing errors is great.
