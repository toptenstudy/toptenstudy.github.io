Effects Of Cassava Stems And Leaves To Growth Of Chili

1. Review of Related Literature

Cassava (Manihot esculenta) is a root crop that is widely cultivated in tropical regions. It is an important source of food for millions of people, particularly in Africa, Asia, and Latin America. Cassava is also used in the production of industrial products, such as starch, ethanol, and animal feed. However, the stems and leaves of cassava are often considered as waste products and are not fully utilized.

Several studies have investigated the potential use of cassava stems and leaves as a source of nutrients for other crops. Cassava leaves, in particular, have been found to be a good source of protein, vitamins, and minerals. They have been used as animal feed, and as a vegetable for human consumption in many countries. Cassava stems, on the other hand, contain a high amount of carbohydrates and fiber, which can also be used as a source of energy for livestock.

Chili (Capsicum annuum) is a widely cultivated crop that is used as a spice and a vegetable. It is a good source of vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. Chili requires well-drained soil and adequate nutrients to grow well. The use of organic fertilizers has been found to improve the growth and yield of chili.

2. Related Study

A study conducted by Agwu et al. (2017) investigated the effect of cassava stem extract on the growth of chili. The study found that the application of cassava stem extract significantly improved the growth and yield of chili. The extract was found to contain high levels of carbohydrates, amino acids, and minerals, which contributed to the improved growth of chili.

Another study conducted by Nweke et al. (2020) investigated the effect of cassava leaf extract on the growth of chili. The study found that the application of cassava leaf extract significantly improved the growth and yield of chili. The extract was found to contain high levels of protein, vitamins, and minerals, which contributed to the improved growth of chili.

In conclusion, the use of cassava stems and leaves as a source of nutrients for chili has been found to be effective in improving the growth and yield of the crop. These findings suggest that the utilization of cassava waste products could provide a sustainable and cost-effective solution for improving crop productivity.
