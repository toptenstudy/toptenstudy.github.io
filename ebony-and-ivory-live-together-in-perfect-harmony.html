20. what is the Ebony and an ivory​


Answer:

Ebony and Ivory" is a song that was released in 1982 as a single by Paul McCartney and Stevie Wonder. It was issued on 29 March that year as the lead single from McCartney's album Tug of War. Written by McCartney, the song aligns the black and white keys of a piano keyboard with the theme of racial harmony. The single reached number one on both the UK and the US charts and was among the top-selling singles of 1982 in the US. During the apartheid era, the South African Broadcasting Corporation banned the song after Wonder dedicated his 1984 Academy Award for Best Original Song to Nelson Mandela.

Explanation:

McCartney and Wonder began recording "Ebony and Ivory" in Montserrat in early 1981. The single marked the first time that McCartney had released a duet with another major artist, and anticipated his 1980s collaborations with Michael Jackson. While a major commercial hit, the song has received derision from music critics who view its message as overly simplistic and sentimental. The track also appears on McCartney's All the Best! compilation (1987) and on the two-disc version of Wonder's The Definitive Collection (2002). In 2013, Billboard ranked it as the 69th biggest hit of all-time on the Billboard Hot 100 charts.

21. Ebony and ivory / Live together in perfect harmony"" (McCartney & Wonder) what figure of speech is this?


The lines in the song exhibit metaphor as a kind of speech.

Ebony stands for the black race and ivory refers to the white race.  In pair, ebony and ivory are the black and white keys in a piano.  The imagery shows that playing these keys together make a harmonious and pleasant music to the ear.

Hence, the song presents that when different races learn to deal with each other peacefully (not taking each other’s differences against each other), then they could live together perfectly (without the threats of terrorism, war, discrimination, racial and culture appropriation, etc.).  The writer must have been advocating for peace and unity among different races among nations.

These lines in a song advocates for the termination of racism among all the nations in order to empower a better environment and that we all should live together without terrorism, war, discrimination, racial separation, culture appropriation, and societal norms and divisions.

25. what is an ebony and an ivory​


Answer:

Ebony is a dense black/brown hardwood, most commonly yielded by several species in the genus Diospyros, which also contains the persimmons. Ebony is dense enough to sink in water. It is finely-textured and has a mirror finish when polished, making it valuable as an ornamental wood.

Ivory is a hard, white material from the tusks and teeth of animals, that consists mainly of dentine, one of the physical structures of teeth and tusks. The chemical structure of the teeth and tusks of mammals is the same, regardless of the species of origin.
