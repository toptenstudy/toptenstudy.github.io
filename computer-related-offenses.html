2.The Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA) defines computer fraud as gaining access to a protected computer without authority or beyond authorization.

3.The practice of registering and using Internet domain names that are identical or similar to trademarks, service marks, company names, or personal names without permission is known as cybersquatting.

4.Illegal interception: When an interception is made without permission, it is considered illegal. non-public communication of computer data to, from, or within a using technical means computer system; aims to keep non-public computer data flows private. from surveillance and recording.
