17. How do teachers equip the pupils with learning opportunities guided by the four pillars of education?


The four (4) pillars of education are: learning to know, leaning to do, learning together, and learning to be. Each one of them has a different perspectives and point of view that helps students see things and understand different things.

To equip the students with learning, the teacher may:

1. (Leaning to know) Ask the students to read for reading comprehension, to research for a topic to let them seek for new knowledge, and to analyze a story that a teacher read to the whole class.

2. (Learning to do) Quizzes and exams are perfect example for this. This is to test and challenge the students' ability to apply knowledge. Projects may suffice too.

3. (Learn to be) Assigning reports to present in class or any public speaking project about certain matter might boost the self-esteem, critical thinking and emotional intelligence of the students.

4. (Learn together) Cultural sensitivity is always important to hone the values of the students. Thus, asking and encouraging students to connect their knowledge to their everyday life and to their habits should be implemented in schools.
