1. Perpendicular Construct a line perpendicular to line I that passes through A​


Answer:

To construct a line perpendicular to line I that passes through point A, you can follow these steps:

Draw line I and point A on a piece of paper.

From point A, draw a line segment that intersects line I at a 90-degree angle. This can be done by using a protractor or by using a compass to draw an arc that intersects line I at a 90-degree angle.

Label the point of intersection between the line segment and line I as point B.

Draw a line that passes through points A and B. This line will be perpendicular to line I and will pass through point A.

Label the point where the perpendicular line intersects line I as point C.

Double-check that the angle between the perpendicular line and line I at point C is 90 degrees.
