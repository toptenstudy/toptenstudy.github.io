1. how to decompose the fraction into partial fraction?​


How to decompose the fraction into partial fraction?

The method is called "Partial Fraction Decomposition", and goes like this:

Step 1: Factor the bottom

Step 2: Write one partial fraction for each of those factors.Step 3: Multiply through by the bottom so we no longer have fractions.Step 4: And we have our answer.

8. A cylinder contains 20g He, 84 g N 2 , and 20 g Ar. What is the mole fraction of each gas in the mixture? If the total pressure of the mixture is 10 atm, what is the partial pressure of He?


Answer:

X He = 0.588

X N₂ = 0.353

X Ar = 0.059

P He = 5.88 atm

Explanation:

Calculating the number of moles of each gas

n He = 20 g × (1 mol / 4 g) = 5 mol

n N₂ = 84 g × (1 mol / 28 g) = 3 mol

n Ar = 20 g × (1 mol / 40 g) = 0.5 mol

Calculating the total number of moles

n_T = n He + n N₂ + n Ar

n_T = 5 mol + 3 mol + 0.5 mol

n_T = 8.5 mol

Calculating the mole fraction of each gas

For He

X He = n He / n_T

X He = 5 mol / 8.5 mol

X He = 0.588

For N₂

X N₂ = n N₂ / n_T

X N₂ = 3 mol / 8.5 mol

X N₂ = 0.353

For Ar

X Ar = n Ar / n_T

X Ar = 0.5 mol / 8.5 mol

X Ar = 0.059

Calculating the partial pressure of He

P He = (X He)(P_T)

P He = (0.588)(10 atm)

P He = 5.88 atm

9. The mole fraction of nitrogen gas (Ny) in air is 0.78. This means that 78% of the surrounding air is nitrogen gas. What is the partial pressure exerted by nitrogen gas if the reading from a nearby mercury barometer is 745 mm of Hg?


Answer:

—The mole fraction of nitrogen gas (N2) in air is 0.78. To determine the partial pressure of nitrogen gas, we can use the formula:

Partial Pressure = Mole Fraction x Total Pressure

Given that the reading from a nearby mercury barometer is 745 mm of Hg, we can substitute this value for the total pressure:

Partial Pressure = 0.78 x 745 mm Hg

To calculate the partial pressure of Nitrogen gas, we can multiply 0.78 with 745mm Hg.

Partial Pressure = 581.1 mm Hg

So, the partial pressure exerted by nitrogen gas is 581.1 mm Hg.

12. A mixture of H2 and O2 exerts a total pressure of 2.8 atm on the walls of its container. If the partial pressure of H2 is 1.5 atm, what is the mole fraction of O2 in the mixture.


Given:

[tex]P_{\text{H}_{2}} = \text{1.5 atm}[/tex]

[tex]P_{\text{T}} = \text{2.8 atm}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]X_{\text{O}_{2}}[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the partial pressure of O₂.

[tex]P_{\text{T}} = P_{\text{O}_{2}} + P_{\text{H}_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{\text{O}_{2}} = P_{\text{T}} - P_{\text{H}_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{\text{O}_{2}} = \text{2.8 atm} - \text{1.5 atm}[/tex]

[tex]P_{\text{O}_{2}} = \text{1.3 atm}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the mole fraction of O₂.

[tex]X_{\text{O}_{2}} = \frac{P_{\text{O}_{2}}}{P_{\text{T}}}[/tex]

[tex]X_{\text{O}_{2}} = \frac{\text{1.3 atm}}{\text{2.8 atm}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{X_{\text{O}_{2}} = 0.46}[/tex]

The total pressure of a mixture of gases = the sum of the partial pressures of the component

The partial pressure of He = 0.8 atm

Required

The mole fraction of each gas.
