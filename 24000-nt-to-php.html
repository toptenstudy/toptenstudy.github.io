13. Php 15,000 is invested in an account that yields 5% interest per year. After how many years will the account be worth Php 91,221.04 if the interest is compounded semi-annually? Using the Formula: = (1 + /n ) nt​


The correct answer is 37 years. Rate of interest (r) per year is 5% compounded yearly. Amount (A) of the principal is php91221.04.24

:

A = p[1 + r/n]nt -------------- eqn(1)

where,

P is the principal or the amount invested

A is the new balance

t is the time

r is the rate, (in decimal form)

n is the number of times it is compounded.

p = 15000

r = 5% = 5/100 = 0.05

A = 91221.04

n = 1 [because it is compounded yearly]

Substituting the above parameters into eqn(1),

91221.04 = 15000[1 + 0.05/1]t

91221.04/15000 = [1 + 0.05]t

6.0814 = [1.05]t

take the log of both sides

log(6.0814) = log(1.05)t

log(6.0814) = t log(1.05)

t = log(6.0814) / log(1.05)

t = 37 years
