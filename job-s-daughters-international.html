Answer:

Sandra's daughter can complete the manuscript in 60 hours.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let's assume that Sandra's daughter can complete the manuscript in x hours.

If Sandra can complete the manuscript in 6 hours, her rate of work is 1/6 manuscripts per hour.

If Sandra and her daughter can complete the manuscript in 5.5 hours, their combined rate of work is 1/5.5 manuscripts per hour.

If Sandra and her daughter work together, they can complete the manuscript in 5.5 hours:

1/6 + 1/x = 1/5.5

Multiplying both sides by the LCM of 6, x, and 5.5:

5.5x + 5.5(6) = 6x

30 = 0.5x

x = 60

Therefore, Sandra's daughter can complete the manuscript in 60 hours.

29. A daughter going at 15m/s increases its velocity at 25m/s in 4seconds. What is it's acceleration?


Acceleration

[tex]____________________________________[/tex]

A daughter going at 15m/s increases its velocity at 25m/s in 4seconds. What is it's acceleration?

Solution

List down the given data:

Initial Velocity (Vi) - 15 m/sFinal Velocity (Vf) - 25 m/sTime (t) - 4 seconds

Fornula to be used:

[tex]\tt A = \frac{vf - vi}{t} [/tex]Solving for:

Acceleration

[tex]\tt A = \frac{vf - vi}{t} [/tex]

[tex]\tt \: A = \frac{25 \: m/s - 15 \: m/s}{4 \: s} [/tex]

[tex]\tt \: A = \frac{10 \: m/s}{4 \: s} [/tex]

[tex]\tt A = \tt \: \boxed{2.5 \: m/s {}^{2} }[/tex]

Answer

- Therefore, the acceleration is 2.5 m/s².
