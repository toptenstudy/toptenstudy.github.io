3. 2. Discuss the difference between return on equity and return in assets.​


Answer:

Return on Equity (ROE) is generally net income divided by equityReturn on Assets (ROA) is net income divided by average assets.

Step-by-step explanation:

ROE tends to tell us how effectively an organization is taking advantage of its base of equity, or capital. This has gained in popularity for several reasons and has become the preferred measure at larger banks. One huge reason for the growing popularity of ROE is, simply, that it is not asset-dependent. ROE can be applied to any line of business or any product. You must have “assets” for ROA, since one cannot divide by zero.

If you are interested in how well a bank is managing its assets, or perhaps its overall size, ROA may be of assistance. Lately, what constitutes a good and valid portrayal of assets has come into question at several of the largest banks. Any measure is only as good as its components. Should profit and return on assets be the only concerns of retailers?

Yes,An ROA of 5% or better is typically considered a good ratio while 20% or better is considered great. In general, the higher the ROA, the more efficient the company is at generating profits. However, any one company's ROA must be considered in the context of its competitors in the same industry and sector.

A low or even negative ROA suggests that the company can't use its assets effectively to generate income, thus it's not a favorable investment opportunity at the moment. Although ROA is often used for company analysis, it can also come handy for analyzing personal finance.

A high ROA shows that the company has a solid performance as far as finance and operation of the company is concerned. A low ROA is not a good sign for the growth of the company. A low ROA indicates that the company is not able to make maximum use of its assets for getting more profits.
