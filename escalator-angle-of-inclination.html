12. An escalator has an angle of elevation of 10 degrees and a vertical rise of 6 m. find the length of the escalator.


Answer:

The length of escalator is 35 meters.

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

Vertical rise of elevator: 6 meters  (opposite of the angle)Angle of elevation: 10°Length of escalator: x  (hypotenuse)The length and vertical rise of elevator and the distance between its foot and base of vertical rise along the ground form a right triangle.

Use trigonometric functions to solve for x, the length of elevator:

Sin θ = opposite/hypotenuse

Find the length of elevator, x:

Sin 10° = 6 meters/x

x = (6 meters) / (Sin 10°)

x = (6 meters) / (0.1736)

x ≈ 34.56 meters

x ≈ 35 meters

The length of escalator is 35 meters.

14. An object of mass m=1kg is sliding from top to bottom in the frictionless incline plane of inclination angle 0=30o and the length of inclined plane is 10m as shown in the figure.Calculate the work done.​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The work done by an object of mass m on a frictionless inclined plane with an angle θ and at a length of an inclined plane d is given by

[tex]\boxed{W = mgd \: \sin \theta}[/tex]

where:

W = work

m = mass

g = acceleration due to gravity = 9.8 m/s²

d = length of the inclined plane

θ = angle of repose of the inclined plane

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the work

W = mgd sin θ

W = (1 kg)(9.8 m/s²)(10 m)(sin 30°)

[tex]\boxed{W = \text{49 J}}[/tex]

Therefore, the work done is 49 J.

28. Describe how varying the incline angle affects the force


Explanation:

As the angle increases, the component of force parallel to the incline increases and the component of force perpendicular to the incline decreases. It is the parallel component of the weight vector that causes the acceleration. Thus, accelerations are greater at greater angles of incline.

The force increases as the angle increases.

If we assume theincline plane is frictionless, the only force

acting on an object on the incline plane is the force of

gravity:

F = mg

where m is the mass and g the acceleration due to gravity.

However, the actual force that makes the object sliding

down along the incline is not the gravity itself, but the

component of the force of gravity parallel to the surface of

the incline, which is given by

Fp = mgsinØ

where Ø is the angle of the incline.

Therefore, we see that as the angle of the incline increases,the force on the object increases.As the angle increases, the component of force parallel to the incline increases and the component of force perpendicular to the incline decreases. It is the parallel component of the weight vector that causes the acceleration. Thus, accelerations are greater at greater angles of incline.

29. Does acceleration depend on angle of inclination


I'm assuming your scenario involves you dropping a item on a ramp. Acceleration in this situation would be solely by gravity. Acceleration is going to increase as your angle of inclination increases to a maximum of 90 degrees or effectively dropping your item. Since acceleration is a vector this relationship between inclination and acceleration would result in a simple trigonometric relationship that would depend on how your angle of inclination is defined. For mine it's g*sin(Theta) where g is acceleration due to gravity (9.81 m/s^2) and theta is the angle of inclination with 0 degrees equivalent to horizontal.

The mass of your item is irrelevant in a ideal world assuming no friction or air resistance. If there is air resistance (really only relevant at higher speeds) a heavier (higher mass) object will accelerate faster by overcoming the airs mass more readily than a object with less mass. 

Another point to note is if your talking about rotating bodies, higher mass could slow down the objects acceleration depending on where the mass is located in the body. This has to deal with the idea of mass moment of inertia which you didn't mention, so I wont explain any further.
