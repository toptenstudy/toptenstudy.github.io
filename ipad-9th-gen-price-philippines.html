Compute for the MARK UP % (nearest hundredths). MIL%​


Answer:

Markup is the difference between the cost price of a product and its selling price. It is usually expressed in monetary terms. For example, if a product costs $10 to make and is sold for $15, the markup on the product is $5.

Markup percentage, on the other hand, is a measure of the profit made on a product, expressed as a percentage of the cost price. To calculate the markup percentage, divide the markup by the cost price and multiply by 100. In the example above, the markup percentage would be (5 / 10) x 100 = 50%.

It's important to note that markup and markup percentage are not the same thing. Markup is a specific amount of money, while markup percentage is a ratio that shows the profit as a percentage of the cost price. I hope this helps! Let me know if you have any other questions.

24. What significant role did gen. Gregorio del pilar played during the philippine revolution and the filipino american war?


Answer:

General Gregorio del Pilar was the hero of Tirad Pass. Known as Agila in the Katipunan, he disguised (alongside with his brother Julian and friend Vicente) as a woman to steal weapons in Paombong. Later on in the Philippine-American War, del Pilar was promoted as General at the age of 22 by Emilio Aguinaldo. Gen. del Pilar also led the troops of 60 soldiers in Tirad Pass, for the escape of Aguinaldo. Sadly, del Pilar was shot on his nape and the bullet passed to his mouth. Someone said that a fellow Filipino soldier betrayed him, while others said that an American shot at him. At the age of 24, Gregorio del Pilar was dubbed as Ang Batang Heneral or The Young General*

*But in this case, del Pilar is not the youngest general. In fact, Manuel Tinio was. Tinio was promoted as general at the age of 20 while del Pilar was 22.

Araw ng Kagitingan commemorates the fall of the Bataan peninsula during the second world war. Called 'Araw ng Kagitingan' in Filipino, this official regular nationwide holiday is celebrated annually on April 9th.

If the date clashes with an Easter holiday, then the day may be moved as was the case in 2009. The holiday may also be known as 'Bataan Day' or 'Bataan and Corregidor Day'. It commemorates the fall of the Bataan peninsula during the second world war.

on 1987, it was renamed officially as 'Araw Ng Kagitingan' (Day of Valor) to pay tribute to those who defended Bataan, Corregidor and Bessang. From 2007, the holiday was designated to be observed on the Monday nearest April 9th, but since 2009 a yearly presidential decree has meant it is observed on April 9th. As it is decreed as an official regular nationwide holiday, employers should abide by the Holiday pay rules.
