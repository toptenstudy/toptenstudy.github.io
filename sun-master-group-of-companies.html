14. A dog named brutus has accompanied his masters sun to school.on his return home,he walked 100meters east before turning north to continue walking within 70meters.after that,he ran 80 meters east to greet his beloved master who was waiting for him at the front door of his home​


Answer:

Assuming that Brutus walked in a straight line and did not backtrack, we can draw a diagram to help visualize his journey:

         (school)

              |

              |

              | 70m

              |

              |

              |

              |

    100m |

        -----|----- (home)

              |  80m

              |

              |

              |

        (master)

From the diagram, we can see that Brutus walked 100 meters east from the school, then turned north and walked 70 meters. After that, he ran 80 meters east to greet his master at the front door of his home.

To find the total distance that Brutus traveled, we can use the Pythagorean theorem to calculate the diagonal distance between the school and his home:

distance = sqrt(100^2 + 70^2) + 80

              = sqrt(10000 + 4900) + 80

              = sqrt(14900) + 80

              = 122.15 + 80

              = 202.15 meters

Therefore, Brutus traveled a total distance of approximately 202.15 meters.

22. Direction: Differentiate each:1.) Slide Master - ________________________________________________2.) Note Master - ________________________________________________3.) Handout Master - ________________________________________________​


Answer:

1.) Slide Master - the Slide Master is the top slide that controls all information about the theme, layout, background, color, fonts, and positioning of all slides. Using the Slide Master is an easy way to adjust the look of an existing theme or make changes to all slides in your presentation.

2.) Note Master - The notes master allows you to customize how your presentation looks when printed out with notes.

3.) Handout Master - The Handout Master controls what the slides on the audience handouts will look like. The slide image placeholders show where slides are placed on the master.
