17. riddles for kindergarten


What is Riddle and its examples?It is a tricky question that has telling the characteristic of an object by using metaphoric words and you are trying to guess it, of what it is. It is also presented as a brain active game.

Here's are the Examples of Riddles:

Riddle: It has many holes, but still holds water.

Answer: A  sponge.

Explanations: Because a sponge has many holes in it, and it can holds water by absorbing it.

Riddle: How many letters are there in the English alphabet?

Answer: "18" because 3 in ‘the’ and 7 in ‘English,’ and also 8 in ‘alphabet.’

Explanations: It literally the letters of the word "English Alphabet"

Riddle: What answer can you never answer yes to?

Answer: Are you asleep yet?

Explanations:Because you are sleeping, how can you answer if you were asleep.

Riddle: Where can you find cities, towns, shops, and streets but no people?

Answer: A map

Explanations: Because a map has the locations of everything, but there is no specific living things in it.

Riddle: What has a face and two hands but no arms or legs?

Answer: A clock!

Explanation: Because a clock has a two "clock arrow."
