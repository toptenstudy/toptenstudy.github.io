15. your insights about the Philippine Educational System in the historical and legal context


HISTORICAL PHILIPPINE EDUCATIONAL SYSTEM

Education in the Philippines is offered through formal and non-formal systems. Formal education typically spans 14 years and is structured in a 6+4+4 system: 6 years of primary school education, 4 years of secondary school education, and 4 years of higher education, leading to a bachelor's degree.

LEGAL CONTEXT

Section 1, Article XIV of the 1987 Constitution provides that the state shall protect and promote of all citizens to quality education at all levels and shall take appropriate steps to make such education accessible to all.

25. how do you define globalization in the context of education and literacy?​


Answer:

Globalization can be defined as "the process of interaction and integration among people, companies, and governments worldwide" (Globalization 101, n.d.). In the context of education and literacy, globalization has both positive and negative impacts on different aspects of human development, such as culture, economy, politics, environment, and health.

On the positive side, globalization can promote education and literacy by:

Expanding access to information and knowledge through digital technologies and media (Brooks & Normore, 2010⁴)Enhancing cross-cultural communication and understanding through language learning and exchange programs (Oxford Research Encyclopedia of Education³)Fostering global citizenship education and education for sustainable development that aim to cultivate critical thinking, ethical awareness, and social responsibility among learners (Oxford Research Encyclopedia of Education³)Encouraging innovation and creativity through collaboration and competition among educational institutions and researchers across borders (Impact of Globalization in Education⁵)

On the negative side, globalization can hinder education and literacy by:

Widening the gap between the rich and the poor in terms of educational opportunities and outcomes (Impact of Globalization in Education⁵).Eroding local cultures and languages by imposing dominant values and norms through standardized curricula and assessments (Brooks & Normore, 2010⁴).Threatening the quality and relevance of education by prioritizing market-driven skills over holistic development (Oxford Research Encyclopedia of Education³).Exacerbating environmental degradation and health risks by increasing consumption, pollution, and mobility (Oxford Research Encyclopedia of Education³).

Therefore, globalization is a complex phenomenon that has both benefits and challenges for education and literacy. It requires educators to adopt a global perspective that balances global demands with local needs and contexts. It also requires learners to develop multiple literacies that enable them to navigate and participate in an interconnected world.
