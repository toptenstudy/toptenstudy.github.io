Poetry is a literary work in the form of writing in which there is a rhythm, rhyme, rhythm, and lyrics in each stanza. Poetry is a written work of art that combines the beauty of language, and focuses on sound, rhythm, and use of diction.

Physical Structure of Poetry

Typography

Typography is a form of poetry that is filled with words, left and right edges, and does not use line rules, so lines of poetry do not always start with a capital letter or end with a period punctuation mark.

Diction

Diction is an appropriate and harmonious choice of words, and in its use, it can help the author express his ideas so that a certain effect is obtained. Poetry has the nature of using dense language, therefore the choice of words that are appropriate and contain meaning is mandatory. The words chosen have also taken into account the rhythm, tone, and beauty of the language.

Imagination

Image is an element that aims so that the reader can see and imagine or imagine and even feel what the poet feels.

Concrete Words

Concrete words are words that support the occurrence of images. An example of a concrete word is "like a sunset jewel" which can be interpreted as a beach or other beautiful and suitable place to see the coming of the twilight.

Language Style

Style of language is the use of language that gives rise to connotative meanings with the use of figurative language. Usually used in making poetry in the form of a figure of speech, metaphor, anaphora, paradox, simile, and others.

Rhythm

Rhythm or rhyme is the similarity of sound at the beginning, middle, and end of the poem.
