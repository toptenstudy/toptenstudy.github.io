13. The rate of a certain reaction is given by the following rate law: Rate = k (N2)2 (H2)2 Use this information to answer the questions below. What is the reaction order in N2? What is the reaction order in H2? What is the overall reaction?


Answer:

What is the reaction order in N2? 2

What is the reaction order in H2? 2

What is the overall reaction? 4

Explanation:

You get the reaction orders from the exponents of the given elements or compounds. In this case, the exponent of N2 is 2 and the exponent of H2 is 2. To determine the overall order of reaction, you just add the exponents together: 2 + 2 = 4.


14. What are scientific laws? a. They are unverifiable information. b. They are untested scientific principles. c. They do not relate to natural phenomena. d. They are explanations valid for all conditions. e. They are usually mathematical explanations ​


Answer:

Scientific laws or laws of science are statements, based on repeated experiments or observations, that describe or predict a range of natural phenomena. The term law has diverse usage in many cases across all fields of natural science.

Answer:

In general, a scientific law is the description of an observed phenomenon. It doesn't explain why the phenomenon exists or what causes it. The explanation of a phenomenon is called a scientific theory. It is a misconception that theories turn into laws with enough research.
