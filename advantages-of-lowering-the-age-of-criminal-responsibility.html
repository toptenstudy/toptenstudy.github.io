20. Explain The main principle of the Bill on Lowering the Age of Criminal Responsibility.​


Answer:

It has been proposed that lowering the minimum age of criminal responsibility will discourage adults from using children as vehicles for illegal activity, but this logic is flawed: there is a risk that lowering the minimum age of criminal responsibility will encourage them to target and exploit even younger children.

Explanation:

The Coalition for Street Children is deeply concerned about legislative efforts in the Philippines to lower the age of criminal responsibility. The current legal minimum age for a child to be held criminally responsible is 15. House Bill 8858 proposed lowering the minimum age to nine years old at first. It was later amended to lower the proposed minimum age to 12 years old, and the House of Representatives approved it on second reading. The bill only needs one final reading before it can be approved and signed into law.

POSITION PAPER OF THE PHILIPPINE ACTION FOR YOUTH OFFENDERS (PAYO) AND THE CHILD RIGHTS NETWORK (CRN) ON LOWERING OF THE MINIMUM AGE OF CRIMINAL RESPONSIBILITY

JULY 15, 2016CRC ASIA ACTIVITIES AND STATEMENTS, LATESTNO COMMENTS

Lawmakers in the Philippines are working to be able to jail 9-year old children.

Presumptive House Speaker and Davao del Norte 1st District Representative Pantaleon Alvarez and Capiz 2nd District Representative Fredenil Castro recently filed House Bill No.2 which seeks to amend Republic Act No. 9344 or the “Juvenile Justice Delinquency Act of 2006” by lowering the MACR from 15 to 9 years old.

Child rights advocates and organizations vehemently oppose this move. Below is the position paper of the Philippine Action for Youth Offenders (PAYO) and The Child Rights Network (CRN) on the issue.

PAYO is a coalition of 16 civil society organizations and several individuals working together for the realization of a just and humane society for children in conflict with the law (CICL) while The Child Rights Network (CRN) is an alliance of government and non-government organizations advocating for the passage of national laws that will protect and fulfill the rights of Filipino children
