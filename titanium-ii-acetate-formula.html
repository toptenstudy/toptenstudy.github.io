9. Isopentyl acetate is the substance responsible for the scent of banana. Analysis shows that it contains 64.61% of carbon, 10.77% of hydrogen, and 24.62% of oxygen. Determine the molecular formula of isopentyl acetate.


Answer:

First of all,we can express the percentage by mass of these elements into grams by simply converting them into decimals and multiplying by 100.

[tex]64.61\% \: carbon = (0.6461) \times (100) = 64.61 \: grams[/tex]

[tex]10.77\% \: hydrogen = (0.1077) \times (100) = 10.77 \: grams[/tex]

[tex]24.62\% \: oxygen = (0.2462) \times (100) = 24.62 \: grams[/tex]

To make it simple,the whole compound is 100%,so we can use 100 g as the base mass.Since all of their percentage add up to 100%,we can convert them into whole number masses.

After that,let us divide each resulting elemental mass by its molar mass to find the number of moles of each element.

[tex] \frac{64.61 \: g}{1} \times \frac{1 \: mol}{12.011 \: g} = 5.4 \: moles = 5 \: moles[/tex]

[tex] \frac{10.77 \: grams}{1} \times \frac{1 \: mol}{1.00784 \: g} = 10.69 \: moles = 11 \: moles[/tex]

[tex] \frac{24.62 \: grams}{1} \times \frac{1 \: mol}{15.999 \: g} = 1.54 \: moles = 2 \: moles[/tex]

Now,we need to divide all number of moles of each element by the smallest number of moles,which is 2 moles.

[tex]5 \div 2 = 2.5 = 3[/tex]

[tex]11 \div 2 = 5.5 = 6[/tex]

[tex]2 \div 2 = 1[/tex]

Thus,our empirical formula is C3(H6)O

Now,we need to determine the molar mass of isopentyl acetate using its chemical formula,C7(H14)O2.

[tex]7(12.011) = 84.077 \: grams[/tex]

[tex]14(1.00784) = 14.10976 \: grams[/tex]

[tex]2(15.999) = 31.998 \: grams[/tex]

[tex](84.077) + (14.10976) + (31.998) = 130.84176 \: grams \: per \: mole[/tex]

Now,let us determine the molar mass of our empirical formula.

[tex]3(12.011) + 6(1.00784) + 1(15.999) = 36.033 + 6.04704 + 15.999 = 58.07904 \: g \: per \: mol[/tex]

Let us divide the molar mass of isopentyl acetate and our empirical formula.

[tex]130.84176 \div 58.07904 = 2.42 = 2[/tex]

Finally,simply multiply all moles of each element in our empirical formula by this result,which is 2.

[tex] 3 \times 2 = 6[/tex]

[tex]6 \times 2 = 12[/tex]

[tex]1 \times 2 = 2[/tex]

Our Molecular Formula is C6(H12)O2.

14. the density of titanium is 4.54g/ml. what is the volume in ml of 163g of titanium


Given: d = 4.54 g/ml

m = 163 g

Unknown: V = ?

Formula: V = md

where in, V = Volume

m = mass

d = density

Note: Original formula of Density is d = m/V since Volume(V) is missing, we derived the original formula by simply transposing d to V,

d = m/V ––> V = m/d

therefore we got the formula for Volume

Solution: V = m/d

= 163 g / 4.54 g/ml

= 35.9 ml

Final Answer: V = 35.9 ml

Note: ml is equal to cm³ , so it's right to use this unit for Volume.

Answer:

For acetic acid, the molar mass is 60.05 g/mol, and the molar mass of the empirical formula CH2O is 30.02 g/mol. The value of the integer n for acetic acid is therefore,

n=60.05g/mol30.02g/mol=2(4.5.2)

And the molecular formula is C2H4O2.

Note that n must be an integer and that your calculation should always yield a whole number (or very close to one).
