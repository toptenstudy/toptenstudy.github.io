1. principle of unity of command


Answer:

Unity of command provides that an employee is responsible to only one supervisor, who in turn is responsible to only one supervisor, and so on up the organizational hierarchy. This is true even if the top of the organization is led by a group of people. For example, imagine you are the CEO of a technology firm in Silicon Valley. While the board of directors of your company governs the policy making and strategic planning, under the concept of unity of command, you do not answer to all members of the board, but only the chairman of the board.

Let's take a minute and climb a corporate ladder to demonstrate how unity of command works from the bottom up. Imagine that you are a new systems analyst for the technology company in Silicon Valley. For better or worse, you are at the bottom rung and you supervise no one. However, you do answer to your unit supervisor, who answers to her department manager. The department manager answers to the vice president of operations, who answers to the CEO. The CEO answers to the chairman of the board of directors.

Principles of the Fine Arts

Here are some principles that you can consider in studying fine arts :

1.The Principle of Alignment

The first principle is harmony or harmonization, namely the relationship between the proximity of different elements in terms of shape, lighting, and color in creating beauty.

2. The Principle of Emphasis (Contrast)

Emphasis is the impression obtained due to the presence of two opposing elements. Striking differences in color, shape, and size will give the impression that it is not monotonous.

3. The Principle of Rhythm

Rhythm is the principle that underlies the regular repetition of one or more elements. The repetition of arranged art elements can be in the form of lines, shapes, or color variations. Repetition that is done in various ways will produce a harmonious rhythm that can increase the aesthetic value of the work of art.

4. Principle of Gradation

Gradation is an arrangement of colors based on a certain level in a work of art. Gradation is most often applied in the manufacture of mosaics, caricatures, paintings, and other works of art. Gradation can make a piece more lively and interesting.

5. The Principle of Proportion

Proportion or comparability is the process of comparing one part to another as a whole. For example, comparing the size of the body with the head, the size of the object with the size of the background, and the suitability of the size of one object with other objects that are near or far away

6. Composition Principle

Composition is the organization of elements of art that are arranged in an orderly, harmonious, and attractive manner. Composition is the most important principle in underpinning the beauty of a work of art.

7. The Principle of Balance

Balance is the principle that is responsible for the impression of an arrangement of elements of art. The elements of art that are arranged in such a way through the principle of balance will be an attraction for art connoisseurs.

8. The Principle of Unity

Unity is a principle that supports how the elements in art combine with each other so that they support each other in building an attractive and beautiful composition. Unity can make a work of art aesthetically valuable.

9. The Accentuation Principle

Accentuation is an element that is very prominent or different from the elements around it.

10. The Principle of Harmony

Harmony is the principle used to unite visual elements even though they come in different forms. The purpose of harmony is to create harmony and balance among the different elements.
