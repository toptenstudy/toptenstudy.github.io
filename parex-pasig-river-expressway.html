6. description of pasig river​


Answer:

Pasig River, river draining Laguna de Bay, the largest lake in the Philippines, into Manila Bay at Manila. It flows north-northwest through the market town of Pasig and bisects Manila, then enters the bay between the North and South harbours. Its length is 14 mi (23 km).

Explanation:

Answer:

The Pasig River is a river in the Philippines that connects Laguna de Bay to Manila Bay. Stretching for 25.2 kilometers, it bisects the Philippine capital of Manila and its surrounding urban area into northern and southern halves. Its major tributaries are the Marikina River and San Juan River. Pasig River is an important river in the Metro Manila, Philippines, since it provides food, livelihood and transport to its residents, and connects two major water bodies; Laguna de Bay and Manila Bay.


7. The pasig river rehabilitation council in october 2018 won the first asia riverprize in recognition of its efforts to rehabilitate the pasig river


Answer:

Pasig River has been an environmental problem for decades. It was so problematic that it was pronounced dead in the ‘90s. However, Asia River Prize Awards has recognized the efforts of the Pasig River Rehabilitation Commission to revitalize its waterways. It is noted that the 27-kilometer river is given recognition during the 21st International River Symposium held in Sydney, Australia on Tuesday. The PRRC hopes to attract international support after gaining recognition. This will open doors to continue their projects such as relocating families along the riverbanks, diverting solid wastes, and develop a system for a significant water quality improvement.
