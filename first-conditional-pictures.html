Background:

if + present simple, will + infinitive

The conditional is one of the most difficult grammatical topics for English learners. Conditional sentences in English are used to express the outcome of something that may have occurred in the past or may occur in the future. We'll go over the structure and applications of the first conditional in this article.

The First Condition's Structure

The first conditional in English is one of the first things to master. It is used to discuss events that may occur in the future. While we cannot anticipate the future, we can speculate on how events may unfold.

In English, the first conditional is constructed with the present simple after "if," whereas the second clause is constructed with the future simple.

The affirmative structure is:

If + present simple, will + verb (in infinitive).  

The negative structure is:

If + do/does not + present simple, will + verb (in infinitive).  

The interrogative structure is: Interrogative + will + verb (in infinitive) + if + present simple?)

What Are the Differences Between the First, Second, and Third Conditional

The first conditional isn't the only one used in English, as you may know. It's a good idea to learn the distinction between first, second, and third conditional in English to make sure you grasp them. The first conditional, as previously stated, specifies events that may occur in the future. In English, the second conditional expresses events that we are unsure will occur. It is, in fact, highly subjective since it is dependent on how we perceive things.
