9. A 100g of water are mixed with 150g of alcohol


The specific gravity of the mixture is 0.862

Further explanation

Specific Gravity (SG) is a ratio of fluid density to standard fluid (reference). Normally the reference fluid used is water with a density of 1 g / cm³ or 1000 kg / m³

SG has no units

A 100g of water are mixed with 150g of alcohol (ρ = 790 kg / m³). we assume the question wants specific gravity from the mixture

then we determine the density of the mixture first

mixture density = total mass: total volume

volume of water = 100 g = 100 ml ( density water = 1 gr / cm³)

alcohol volume = 150 g: 0.79 g / cm³ = 189,873 ml

total volume = volume water + volume alkohol

Total volume = 100 + 189.73 = 289,873

mass total = mass of water + mass of alcohol \

total mass = 100 g + 150 g

total mass of 250 g

So that the density of the mixture:

= total mass: total volume

= 250: 289,873

= 0.862 g / cm³

Then the specific gravity:

SG = mixed density / reference density (water)

SG = 0.862 g / cm³ / 1 g / cm³

SG = 0.862
