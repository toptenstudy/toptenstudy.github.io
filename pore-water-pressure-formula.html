10. The sweat glands release water through the pores when the temperature of a person rises


SWEAT GLANDS

Body temperature is regulated by the sympathetic nervous system, which is in charge of the eccrine sweat gland. Eccrine glands produce water on the skin's surface when the internal temperature rises, where it evaporates to reduce heat.

This is because sweating relies on the physics concept known as "heat of vaporization," which states that heat is required to cause perspiration to evaporate off your skin. You begin to cool down as beads of sweat are transformed into vapor by your body's excess heat.

Heat is removed from the body when liquid perspiration transforms into a vapor and evaporates off the skin's surface. The sweating mechanism is impeded when the body is chilly. The hypothalamus, a region of the brain that responds to the blood's core temperature, regulates body temperature.

Although sweat glands are found throughout the body, they are most prevalent on the forehead, armpits, palms, and soles of the feet. While salts do appear in sweat, water makes up the majority of it. Its primary job is to regulate body temperature. The skin's surface cools when the water in the perspiration evaporates.

Some people are born without sweat glands, however, this is uncommon. As an example, men with 5 to 6 percent ectodermal dysplasia have this issue and are consequently at a higher risk of hyperthermia-related death, especially in hot situations.

20. Ella filled a bucket 7/10 of the way with water.she then pored 2/10 of the water. How much water remains in the bucket


Answer:

12

Step-by-step explanation:

To do this problem, you'll have to subtract fractions. To subtract fractions, find a common denominator on both fractions (if needed, which you don't need to do for this problem!), subtract the numerators, and put the difference over the common denominator. Simplify if needed. 

Ella started out with 7/10 a bucket of water and then lost 2/10 by pouring it out. That means you're finding the difference of 7/10 - 2/10!

1) Find a common denominator

This is already done because both fractions have "10" in the denominator.

2) Subtract the numerators

7 and 2 are the numerators and you're subtracting 2 from 7:

7 - 2 = 5

3) Put the difference of the numerators over the common denominator

Difference /common denominator = 5/10

4) Simplify if needed

5/10 = 1/2

This is because you can divide both the numerator and denominator by 5/5 (and remember 5/5 is the same as 1, and dividing any number by 1 equals itself!)

23. Most of the pores in the vadose zone are filled with air, rather than water.


Answer:

In hydraulics  

The vadose zone is the undersaturated subsurface portion that lies above the groundwater table. In the vadose zone the soil and rock are not completely saturated with liquid; such that, the pores inside them comprise both air and water.

Explanation:

Nsaturated field  

Pore spaces may not be filled with water completely in the top layers of soil, unconsolidated sediments, or bedrock. One might contain water, some air and may even be filled with water only in part. It's also called the unsaturated zone (also known as the aeration zone, or the vadose zone).

Watershed  

The saturated zone, a zone filled with water by all of the pores and rock fractures, underlies the unsaturated area. The Water table is called the peak of a saturated region (Diagram 1). The water table can be just under the land surface, or hundreds of feet below.

Uppermost layer of subsurface water where air and water are usually trapped in porous spaces. ... Water in aeration zone, the uppermost flooring layer of water. Saturation region. The subsurface water zone in which all voids in rock and soil are always filled with water; the water table has been at the top of such a zone.

The porosity, or percentage of all its total volume taken up by pores, is the amount of pore space in rock , soil, or sediments. ... The rock and soil are unsaturated in shallower depths; that is, the pores contain some air, and therefore are not filled with water. This is recognized as unsaturated zone.

Groundwater, which is well below the Earth's surface in aquifers, is among the most important natural resources. Groundwater is the source of about 33 per cent of the water supplied to households and businesses through state and city irrigation services (public supply).

The one closest to the surface is the "aeration zone," where both air and water fill the gaps between soil. Below layer is the "saturation zone," where water needs to fill the gaps. ... The amount of water able to hold in the soil is called "porosity"
