3. What is a paraphrase, in the form of a summary question, of each stanza of "The Man with the Hoe" by Edwin Markham?


Edwin Markham, who has been called “the dean of American poets,” received national fame, and later worldwide fame, when he published “The Man with the Hoe.” It changed his career immediately. The poem consists of forty-nine lines divided into five stanzas of social commentary that focus on America’s working class and their sufferings. It is a striking poem of protest against exploited labor.

After viewing French artist Jean-François Millet’s world-famous painting of a peasant leaning on his hoe, The Man with the Hoe (1862), Markham was inspired to write his poem in 1898. He is reported to have seen the original painting, which had a profound effect on him, in San Francisco. Markham was at a New Year’s Eve celebration when he read the poem to an editor of the San Francisco Examiner. Shortly thereafter, the poem was published in that paper.

Because of its popularity, the poem was translated into many languages and reprinted in magazines, newspapers, and books numerous times. The poem’s success allowed Markham to spend more time writing and lecturing. In regard to the reform movements concerning labor struggles of the time, the poem generated much controversy. The newspapers received many letters regarding “The Man with the Hoe.” The poem was open to different interpretations. Some readers said that the poem was advocating socialism: Some were in support of the concept; others were against it. Others said the poem contained a prophetic message that could incite unessential reforms. Still others considered the poem a medium for expressing farmers’ and workers’ grievances.

For Markham, Millet’s peasant symbolized the exploited classes worldwide. Markham said that he viewed it as “a poem of hope. a cry for justice.” In the fourth stanza, Markham addresses the “masters, lords, and rulers in all lands.” He interrogates them with an implied sense of optimism:

Is this the handiwork you give to God,This monstrous thing distorted and soul-quenched?How will you ever straighten up this shape,Touch it again with immortality;Give back the upward looking and the light;Rebuild in it the music and the dream;Make right the immemorial infamies,Perfidious wrongs, immedicable woes?

12. Paraphrasing must be done properly to avoid plagiarism. What is paraphrasing?


Paraphrasing is an art of copying a phrase or a sentence without exactly copying the phrase or the sentence itself. This can be done by rearranging the sentence structure and also substituting words with its synonyms, thus altering the appearance of the sentence without altering the meaning of it.

For example:
"Playing video games for a long time may affect your attitude and character"
You can paraphrase it to:
"Gaming for extended periods of time might change one's values and personality"

21. Topic:The biggest challenge in controlling COVID-19. Question: what are the challenge we face in combating COVID-19? Paraphrase question:


Answer:

What is the most significant obstacle in managing COVID-19?

The COVID-19 pandemic has presented a multitude of challenges to healthcare systems, governments, and individuals worldwide. However, the most significant challenge in controlling COVID-19 is the virus's highly contagious nature. COVID-19 spreads through respiratory droplets when an infected person talks, coughs or sneezes. Asymptomatic individuals can also spread the virus unknowingly, making it challenging to identify and isolate all cases. Additionally, the virus can survive on surfaces for extended periods, making it easy to transmit through contact with contaminated objects.

Controlling COVID-19 requires a multi-pronged approach, including widespread testing, contact tracing, isolation of infected individuals, and social distancing measures. However, implementing these measures has been difficult due to various factors such as inadequate testing capacity, limited resources for contact tracing, and non-compliance with social distancing guidelines by some individuals.

Furthermore, the emergence of new variants of the virus has added another layer of complexity to controlling COVID-19. These variants have shown increased transmissibility and potential resistance to existing vaccines, making it even more challenging to control the spread.

In conclusion, the most significant challenge in managing COVID-19 is its highly contagious nature and the difficulty in identifying and isolating all cases. Overcoming this challenge requires a coordinated effort from individuals, healthcare systems, and governments worldwide.

When we say paraphrasing, this is a method of reconstructing, restating, or making another meaning of composition of a text, statement and passage.

Original
