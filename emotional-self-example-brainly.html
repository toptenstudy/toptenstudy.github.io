7. give explaination and example of this?to exercise self-discipline and control the emotions​


Answer:

Exercising self-discipline and controlling emotions means being able to regulate one's own behavior and emotional responses. It involves having the ability to resist impulses or urges that might be harmful or counterproductive in the long run. It also means being able to manage one's emotional responses, such as anger, fear, or sadness, in a way that is appropriate and healthy.

Example: Imagine you're in a situation where someone is insulting or belittling you. Exercising self-discipline and controlling your emotions means being able to resist the urge to lash out in anger or retaliate with insults. Instead, you may choose to respond calmly and assertively, addressing the situation in a way that is respectful but firm.

Another example could be exercising self-discipline by sticking to a diet or exercise routine even when you don't feel like it. It involves resisting the temptation to indulge in unhealthy foods or skip workouts, and instead, committing to your goals and staying on track.
