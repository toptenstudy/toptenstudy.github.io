MOLAR MASS

Calculate the molar mass of water.

The chemical formula of water is H₂O

Molar Mass of Oxygen = 16 g/mol

16 • 1 = 16 g

Molar Mass of Hydrogen = 1.01 g/mol

1.01 • 2 = 2.0 g

Molar Mass of H₂O = molar mass O + molar mass H₂

Molar Mass of H₂O = 16 g + 2.0 g

Molar Mass of H₂O = 18 g/mol

11. VI.A 26-27 (2 points) DIRECTION: Solve for the MOLE, If you have sample of 12 grams of Sodium Chloride (NaCl) by determining first the molar mass of the compound. FORMULA: MOLE: Mass (gram) Molar Mass (gram/mole)​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of NaCl.

Note that the molar mass of Na and Cl are 22.99 g/mol and 35.45 g/mol respectively.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{molar mass of NaCl} & = \text{22.99 g/mol + 35.45 g/mol} \\ & = \text{58.44 g/mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Final Step: Calculate the number of moles of NaCl by using the formula.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles} & = \frac{\text{mass}}{\text{molar mass}} \\ & = \frac{\text{12 g}}{\text{58.44 g/mol}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{0.21 mol}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, there is 0.21 mol of NaCl in 12 g of NaCl.

25. SHOW THE COMPLETE SOLUTION1.) 10.0g of NaCI is dissolved in 5.0L of water. What is the molarity of the solution? (first convert grams to moles!)pa help naman po!​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of solute (NaCl).

Note that the molar mass of solute (NaCl) is 58.44 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of solute} & = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}} \\ & = \frac{\text{10.0 g}}{\text{58.44 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{0.171 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Final Step: Solve for the molarity of solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{molarity} & = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.171 mol}}{\text{5.00 L}} \\ & = \text{0.0342 mol/L} \\ & = \boxed{0.0342 \: M} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molarity of NaCl solution is 0.0342 M.
