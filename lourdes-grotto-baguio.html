4. what is ascent to a grotto all about? Please help meNo joke pls​


"Ascent to a Grotto" is a painting by Jean-Baptiste-Camille Corot, an artist who was known for his landscape paintings. The painting depicts a winding path that leads up to a rocky grotto surrounded by lush greenery and trees. The painting is focused on capturing the beauty and spiritual essence of nature, which was a common theme in Corot's work. The painting invites the viewer to take a journey through the natural world, to explore the winding path that leads up to the grotto, and to experience the tranquility and serenity of the landscape. Overall, the painting conveys a sense of peace, harmony, and contemplation, which is characteristic of Corot's style.

11. 1. Rhea is climbing up a large number of steps going to Lourdes Grotto in Baguio City. Her friend counted the number of steps she completes every minutes. From then on, she kept a regular pace. After 2 minutes , she has climbed 56 in total and has climbed 224 steps after 8 minutes.a. find the number of steps she climbs every minute.b. if she had not maintained a regular pace, would you still consider this as an application of arithmetic sequence? why?​


Answer:

a. Find the number of steps she climbs every minute.

[tex] \frac{56}{2} = 28[/tex]

or

[tex] \frac{224}{8} = 28[/tex]

28 step per minute

b. If she had not maintained a regular pace, would you still consider this as an application of arithmetic sequence? Why?

No, because an Arithmetic Sequence has a constant term called "common difference" that is being added to the current term to get the next term. If she had not maintained a regular pace, then it can't be an example of an Arithmetic Sequence since an Arithmetic Sequence requires a common difference for it to be called an Arithmetic Sequence.

17. Ano ang grotto? Ipaliwanag.


Answer:

A grotto is a natural or artificial cave used by humans in both modern times and antiquity, and historically or prehistorically. Naturally occurring grottoes are often small caves near water that are usually flooded or liable to flood at high tide. Sometimes, artificial grottoes are used as garden features
