11. what are the similarities of survivor and victim?​


Answer:

They are both harmed by any means. Can be physical, psychological, or emotional.

Answer:

A victim is defined by the harm that has come to them; a survivor is defined by their life afterwards. A victim has been destroyed and mistreated; a survivor has continued to live and prosper despite having been victimized. A victim is powerless, at the mercy of others; a survivor has reclaimed their power.


12. How effective was the collaboration of the Philippines government and non state institutions in responding to the needs of the survivors of typhoon yolanda?


Yolanda was a very powerful and destructive typhoon that hits the Philippines. There are lots of infrastructure ruined, like roads, buildings, and power supplies. The damage to the infrastructure left many areas without running water, electricity, and caused devastation to others. There was also lack of medical assistance due to the devastated hospitals. Due to severe damage, help came not only from the Government, but also from the other non-state institutions, relief organizations and other countries. The collaborations of these sectors helped a lot by means of providing the needs of the survivors. They gave funds for the reconstruction of infrastructures, new houses for the people, donation of clothes and foods, and rehabilitation for those who traumatized on what happened during the typhoon Yolanda.

ALTITUDE

Without access to an altimeter, you may calculate pressure altitude by multiplying (standard pressure minus your current pressure setting) by 1,000 and adding field elevation.Because the air is less thick at a high altitude, planes can fly faster while using less fuel. In addition to avoiding weather systems, flying over 30,000 feet makes for significantly more comfortable travel for passengers.

PILOT'S KNOWLEDGE OF ALITITUDE

A pressure value selected by the pilot is compared to the atmospheric pressure as measured by an altimeter at the aircraft's flying altitude. The accuracy of the altimeter measurement will be impacted if pressure fluctuations during flight are not corrected for. A plane's true altitude is its height above sea level.

COFFIN CORNER

A passenger jet can reach "Coffin Corner" if it flies too high.At this moment, the aircraft experiences both a low-speed stall and a high-speed buffet, and it is no longer able to maintain its height, forcing it to fall.
