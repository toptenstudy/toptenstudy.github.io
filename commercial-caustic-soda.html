7. 1. What's the percent mass of 15g of caustic soda dissolved in 120g of water?​


Answer:

The percentage concentration of any solution is most commonly expressed as mass percent:

Mass % of any component of the solution =

(Mass of the component in the solution / Total mass of the solution) x 100

Other methods are:

Volume percentage:

Volume % of a component =

(Volume of the component/Total volume of the solution) x 100

Mass by volume percentage:

It is the mass of solute dissolved in 100 mL of the solution.

i.e. Mass by Volume percentage =

(Mass of solute in grams/Volume of solution in mL) x 100

Here's a point to be kept in mind :

Whenever we say mass or volume of the solution, you need to add the respective masses and volumes of ALL the components of the solution. Do NOT commit the error of taking the mass or volume of only the solute or solvent in the denominators of the above expressions.

The concentration of a solution is most of the time expressed as the number of moles of solute present in 1 Liter of the solution (also called molarity )

9. sodium bicarbonate (NaHCO3) is commercially known as baking soda. How many grams and astoms of oxygen are there in 15 grams of sodium bicarbonate? The molar mass of NaHCO3 is 84.01g/mol. PLS ANSWER ASAP


SOLUTION: (mass of oxygen)

There are 3 moles of O in 1 mole of NaHCO₃.

The molar mass of O is 16.00 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of O} & = \text{15 g} \: \text{NaHCO}_3 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{NaHCO}_3}{\text{84.01 g} \: \text{NaHCO}_3} \times \frac{\text{3 mol O}}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{NaHCO}_3} \times \frac{\text{16.00 g O}}{\text{1 mol O}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{8.6 g}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, there are 8.6 g of O in 15 g of NaHCO₃.

------------------------------------------------------------

SOLUTION: (atoms of oxygen)

There are 6.022 × 10²³ atoms in 1 mole of O.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{number of O atoms} & = \text{15 g} \: \text{NaHCO}_3 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{NaHCO}_3}{\text{84.01 g} \: \text{NaHCO}_3} \times \frac{\text{3 mol O}}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{NaHCO}_3} \times \frac{6.022 \times 10^{23} \: \text{O atoms}}{\text{1 mol O}} \\ & = \boxed{3.2 \times 10^{23} \: \text{atoms}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, there are 3.2 × 10²³ atoms of O in 15 g of NaHCO₃.

21. Calculate molarity of 4 g caustic soda dissolved in 200 ml of solution.


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of solute (NaOH).

The molar mass of NaOH (caustic soda) is 40 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n_{\text{solute}} & = \frac{mass_{\text{solute}}}{MM_{\text{solute}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{4 g}}{\text{40 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{0.1 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the molarity of solution.

The volume of solution must be expressed in liters. Thus, 200 mL = 0.2 L

[tex]\begin{aligned} M & = \frac{n_{\text{solute}}}{V_{\text{solution}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.1 mol}}{\text{0.2 L}} \\ & = \text{0.5 mol/L} \\ & = \boxed{0.5 \: M} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molarity of 4 g caustic soda dissolved in 200 mL of solution is 0.5 M.
