Answer:

There is limited research on the specific percentage of senior high school students in the Philippines that struggle with academic writing. However, some studies have found that many students do find academic writing challenging. Some key reasons for this include:

1. English as a second language. For many Filipino students, English is their second language. Developing proficiency and fluency in English, especially academic English, takes time and practice. This can pose challenges for academic writing.

2. Lack of experience. Students are still developing their writing skills and experience in senior high school. They are still learning the conventions of academic writing and various writing styles. This inexperience can make academic writing more difficult.

3. Difficulty with critical thinking. Academic writing often requires critical thinking skills such as analysis, synthesis, and evaluation. Students who struggle with these higher-order skills will likely find academic writing more challenging.

4. Lack of writing instruction. Some research has found that students in the Philippines would benefit from more explicit instruction in writing. With limited feedback and guidance, students may feel unprepared for the demands of academic writing.

5. Resource challenges. Some schools may lack resources to fully support the development of students' academic writing skills. Factors like large class sizes, limited access to technology, and lack of professional development for teachers can make it more difficult for students to excel in academic writing.

So while there is no definitive statistic on the percentage of students struggling with academic writing, research points to several barriers that likely lead to at least some difficulties for many senior high school students in the Philippines. More support, resources, experience, and targeted writing instruction could help address these challenges. But developing proficiency in academic writing, especially in English, takes significant time and practice.


3. 1. What is Stress 2 Give 10 examples of Stress 3. Explain each number on how you can manage istress your 4. Is stress all bad? Why? 5. What Strasses you out?​


1. Stress is a physical, mental, or emotional response to a demand, threat, or challenge that exceeds a person's ability to cope with it. It is a natural reaction that prepares the body to respond to danger or pressure, also known as the "fight or flight" response.

2. Examples of stress include:

Financial problems

Work deadlines

Family conflicts

Relationship issues

Health problems

Traffic jams

Academic pressure

Social anxiety

Performance anxiety

Life transitions (moving, divorce, etc.)

3. Here are some ways to manage stress:

Practice relaxation techniques such as deep breathing, meditation, or yoga

Exercise regularly to release tension and boost endorphins

Get enough sleep and establish a regular sleep routine

Eat a healthy diet and avoid excessive caffeine, alcohol, or drugs

Set realistic goals and prioritize tasks to avoid feeling overwhelmed

Seek social support from friends, family, or a therapist

Practice time management and avoid procrastination

Engage in hobbies or activities that bring joy and relaxation

Learn to say "no" to commitments that cause unnecessary stress

Maintain a positive attitude and practice gratitude and self-compassion

4. Stress is not all bad. It can motivate us to take action, achieve goals, and adapt to new situations. However, chronic or excessive stress can have negative effects on our physical and mental health, including increased risk of heart disease, anxiety, depression, and other illnesses.
