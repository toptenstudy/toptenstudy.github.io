16. what can you say about the parody of marcelo h del pilar about " aba ginoong maria, and ama namin"? ​


ABA GINOONG MARIA, AND AMA NAMIN AS PARODIES OF MARCE H DEL PILAR

Marcelo H. del Pilar's parodies of the "Aba Ginoong Maria" and "Ama Namin" prayers were a clever and effective way to criticize the abuses of the Spanish colonial regime in the Philippines. By using humor and irreverence, del Pilar was able to expose the hypocrisy of the Catholic friars and inspire Filipinos to resist colonial rule. His parodies are a testament to the power of satire and humor in challenging oppressive systems of power.

ABA GINOONG MARIA (HAIL MARY)

One of his famous parodies is "Aba Ginoong Maria," a satirical take on the Catholic prayer "Ave Maria" or "Hail Mary." In his version, he replaces the traditional phrases with humorous and irreverent ones that criticize the friars and their corrupt practices. For example, he replaces "Hail Mary, full of grace" with "Hail Mary, full of grease" and "Blessed art thou among women" with "Blessed art thou among frying pans."

AMA NAMIN (OUR FATHER)

Del Pilar's parody of the "Ama Namin" or "Our Father" prayer is also notable. In his version, he criticizes the friars and their hypocrisy, using humorous and biting language to expose their abuses of power.

For example, he replaces "forgive us our sins" with "forgive us our loans"and "deliver us from evil" with "deliver us from the friars."

CONCLUSION

Overall, del Pilar's parodies were meant to expose the hypocrisy and corruption of the Spanish colonial regime in the Philippines, particularly the abuses of the Catholic friars. They were also meant to inspire Filipinos to resist colonial rule and fight for their independence.
