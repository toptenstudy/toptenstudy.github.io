9. A building was builed for P10,000,000.00 has an estimated Life of 20 years and a salvage value of one million, how much is the depreciation charge per year? Answer?​


Step-by-step explanation:

To calculate the annual depreciation charge, you can use the straight-line method, which allocates an equal amount of the depreciable base (the difference between the cost of the building and its estimated salvage value) over the asset's useful life.

In this case, the depreciable base is P10,000,000.00 - P1,000,000.00 = P9,000,000.00. The annual depreciation charge would be P9,000,000.00 / 20 years = P450,000.00 per year.

Answer:

depreciation charge for a building with a cost of P10,000,000.00, an estimated life of 20 years, and a salvage value of P1,000,000.00 would be P9,000,000.00 / 20 years = P450,000.00 per year.
