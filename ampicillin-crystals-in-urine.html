2. The most valuable initial aid in identifying crystals in a urine sample is:


Answer:

Urinary crystals:

• Crystals in urine appear as amorphous material or geometrical form.

Identification of crystals:

• Normal crystals sometimes resemble abnormal crystals.

• The primary tool to identify crystal is pH.

• Crystals are categorized as abnormal and normal and also based on their appearance in alkaline or acidic urine.

• Acidic urine contains all types of abnormal crystals.

Therefore, primary aid for identifying crystals is “pH” of urine.

6. Ampicillin sulbactam nursing responsibilities


Answer:

Nursing Responsibilities for Ampicillin Sulbactam

*Assess patient history for allergies such as cephalosporin or penicillin.

* During intervention, hold medication and promptly report rash (although common with ampicillin, may indicate hypersensitivity) or diarrhea (fever, abdominal pain, mucus and blood in stool may indicate antibiotic-associated colitis).

*Evaluate IV site for phlebitis.

*Check IM injection site for

pain, induration. Monitor I&O, urinalysis, renal function tests.

*Be alert for superinfection: fever, vomiting, diarrhea, anal/genital pruritus, oral mucosal

changes (ulceration, pain, erythema).

*Advise patient to take antibiotic for full length of treatment.   

* Inform patient that discomfort may occur with IM injection.

Explanation: Supersaturated Solution- A supersaturated solution is a solution that contains more than the maximum amount of solute that is capable of being dissolved at a given temperature. The recrystallization of the excess dissolved solute in a supersaturated solution can be initiated by the addition of a tiny crystal of solute, called a seed crystal.

Supersaturation-   occurs with a chemical solution when the concentration of a solute exceeds the concentration specified by the value equilibrium solubility. Most commonly the term is applied to a solution of a solid in a liquid. A supersaturated solution is in a metastable state; it may be brought to equilibrium by forcing the excess of solute to separate from the solution. The term can also be applied to a mixture of gases.

14. Smooth muscle in the walls of the urine move urine along to the bladder by:


Answer:

Smooth muscle stretch initiates the micturition reflex by activating stretch receptors in the bladder wall. This autonomic reflex causes the detrusor muscle to contract and the internal urethral sphincter muscle to relax, allowing urine to flow into the urethra.

Muscles in the walls of the ureters send the urine in small spurts into the bladder, in a process called peristalsis.

Answer:

peristalsis

Explanation:

Peristalsis is a distinct pattern of smooth muscle contractions that facilitates the passage of urine from the kidneys to the bladder.
