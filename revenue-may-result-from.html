7. The revenue of T- eleven Bookstore increased from Php 2,000,000 ( in 2014) to Php 2,250,000 ( in 2015 ). What was the percent of increase of the yearly revenue from 2014-2015?


Answer:

The percent of increase in revenue from 2014 to 2015 is 12.5%.

Step-by-step explanation:

2015 Revenue: 2,250,000

2014 Revenue: 2,000,000

To solve for the increase in revenue:

2,250,000 - 2,000,000 = 250,000 ⇒ Revenue increase

To solve for the rate of increase (percent):

[250,000 ÷ 2,000,000] × 100

= 0.125 × 100

= 12.5%

The percent of increase in revenue is 12.5%.

11. Which of these would not result in unearned revenue?


Answer:The correct answer among the given listed choices is letter d. Deferred revenue or unearned revenue is not considered revenue. It is a liability account. Money is already received from the customer or client for a service or product that has yet to perform or deliver. In accounting initial entry, cash is normally debited and unearned revenue is credited since it is a liability that the business need to provide yet a service or product. Unearned Revenue will be reversed once the service or product is already done or delivered to the customer.

Definition of Revenues

Revenues are fees earned from providing services and amounts from selling merchandise/goods/products. According to the accrual basis of accounting, revenues should be recorded at the time of delivering the service or the merchandise, even if cash is not received at the time of delivery.

What is Scientific Method and Why is it Important?

Scientific method is a step-by-step method of acquiring information. It is a logical and organized way to answer a question. The main purpose of it is to discover the unknown or challenge the existing hypothesis and theories, and to interpret the answer in the clearest way possible.

Steps of Scientific Method

The following steps will be explained through examples.

1. Observation.

You observed or noticed that your oven toaster was not working.

2. Ask a question.

You now started to wonder why is it not working.

3. Formulation of hypothesis.

Maybe the oven toaster is broken that is why it won't work anymore.

4. Conduct an experiment.

You plugged the oven toaster to test if it is really broken.

5. Analyze the results.

You noticed that the oven toaster was heating after you plugged it in.

6. Drawing a conclusion.

After knowing that the oven toaster was working, you concluded that it was not broken but unplugged.

7. Writing your result.

Now, you know that your oven toaster was working but you just forgot to plugged it. With this experimentation, you will remember that you should plug your oven toaster first before operating it.
