If AC=15, then AB=10

If BD=x+1 and CE=x+10, then x=9

Step-by-step explanation:

If BD=2, then CE=12.

Explanation: In a trapezoid, the sum of the lengths of the two parallel sides is equal to the sum of the lengths of the other two sides. Therefore, BD + CE = AD + BC. Since BD is given as 2, and AD = BC (opposite sides of an isosceles trapezoid), we can write BD + CE = 2AD. Substituting CE = 2AD - BD, and BD = 2, we get CE = 12.

If AC=15, then AB=10.

Explanation: In a right triangle, the Pythagorean Theorem states that the square of the length of the hypotenuse (AC) is equal to the sum of the squares of the lengths of the other two sides (AB and BC). Therefore, AC^2 = AB^2 + BC^2. Since BC is given as 8, and AC = 15, we can write 15^2 = AB^2 + 8^2. Solving for AB, we get AB = 10

If CE=6x+8 and ED=18, then x=2.

Explanation: In a triangle, the sum of the lengths of any two sides is always greater than the length of the third side. Therefore, CE + ED > CD, which gives us (6x+8) + 18 > 24. Simplifying this inequality, we get 6x > -2, which implies x > -1/3. We are also given that CE = ED + CD, which gives us 6x+8 = 18 + CD. Simplifying this equation, we get CD = 6x-10. Substituting x = 2, we get CD = 2, which satisfies the inequality we derived earlier. Therefore, x=2

If BD=x+1 and CE=x+10, then x=9.

Explanation: In a trapezoid, the sum of the lengths of the two parallel sides is equal to the sum of the lengths of the other two sides. Therefore, BD + CE = AD + BC. Since BD is given as x+1, and CE is given as x+10, we can write (x+1) + (x+10) = AD + BC. Simplifying this equation, we get 2x + 11 = AD + BC. Since AD = BC (opposite sides of an isosceles trapezoid), we can write 2x + 11 = 2AD. Substituting AD = (BD+CE)/2 and BD = x+1 and CE = x+10, we get 2x + 11 = x + 6 + x + 11, which simplifies to x = 9
