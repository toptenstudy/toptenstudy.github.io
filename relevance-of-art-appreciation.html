2. What is the relevance or art appreciation?


Art appreciation is known for being a subject that is taught in school. Some students like it, some students also dislike it. But what is really the relevance and importance of art appreciation for us?

From the name itself, art appreciation teaches us to “appreciate art”. In this subject, we discuss who invented the art, when is it discovered or done, where does it originate, and what is its meaning and background. If we know everything about famous artworks or if we know how to define and identify an artwork, then we can appreciate a simple art. Artworks often reflects its artist’s point of view. Some artists use art as a medium to express themselves or to give message to their audience.

Relevance of Art Appreciation It's a good method to learn about the piece's history and the time period in which it was created. Art appreciation facilitates thoughtful dialogue and the understanding that there are multiple approaches to anything by allowing people to listen to diverse perspectives and ideas which will help as a future professional. It establishes effective communication. It helps us to open our minds, and eyes to view others'p erspectives.

Relevance of Literature It helps us improve concentration and focus which are needed soft skills as a professional. The development of cognitive skills includes both stretching the imagination and boosting attentiveness. With literature, it helped to enhance our cognitive skills. When a reader improves their writing abilities by reading, they are also improving their communication skills in general. This applies to more than just writing; it also applies to everyday conversations and relationships. Communication is essential in many aspects of life, including work and relationships. Life needs critical thinking. It enables people to solve difficulties and figure out what is true. Reading literature is an excellent way to improve critical thinking abilities which will help in our careers.

 

Art Appreciation Art Appreciation is a broad overview of the visual arts, including various media, techniques, and history. The goal of the course is to provide students a better understanding of the creative processes involved in the visual arts. This course analyzes two- and three-dimensional art forms, processes, and media; examines the visual aspects and principles of design, and briefly surveys art styles from prehistoric to the 20th Century.

Literature Literature is a broad term that refers to any written material. Literature can be divided into two categories: fiction and non-fiction, as well as poetry and prose. It is further divided into key forms such as the book, short story, or drama, and works are frequently classified according to historical periods or adherence to particular aesthetic traits or expectations.
