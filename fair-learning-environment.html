Explanation:

Frist, what its PPST?

The PPST or Philippine Professional Standards for Teachers is a public statement of what teachers need to know, value, and be able to do in their practice. It has four career stages: Beginning, Proficient, Highly Proficient, and Distinguished. It is built on the National Competency Based Teacher Standards.

In the DepEd Order No. 42, 2017 series or National Adoption and Implementation of Philippine Professional Standards for Teachers, Secretary for Education Leonor Magtolis Briones stated that the new standards for teachers were driven by the introduction of national and international frameworks, such as the K to 12 program and ASEAN integration.

PPST generates the necessary skills and competencies of quality teachers, which can enable teachers to cope with an ever-evolving global framework.

There are a several purposes from PPST, namely:

providing a clear set of expectations for teachers throughout well-defined career development stages from inception to prominent practiceinvolve teachers to be actively involved in continuous efforts to achieve skills and proficiencyapply linear standards to assess teacher performance, identify needs, and provide support for professional development.

In domain 2 of PPST highlights the role of teachers to provide learning environments that are safe, secure, fair and supportive in order to promote learner responsibility and achievement. This Domain centers on creating an environment that is learning-focused and in which teachers efficiently manage learner behavior in a physical and virtual space. It highlights the need for teachers to utilize a range of resources and provide intellectually challenging and stimulating activities to encourage constructive classroom interactions geared towards the attainment of high standards of learning.
