1. 2. **A standard aspirin tablet contains 324 mg of aspirin. How many grams of aspirin are in a standard aspirin tablet?


There is 0.324 g of aspirin in a standard aspirin tablet

Further explanation

Mass is one of the principal quantities, which is related to the matter in the object

The main mass unit consists of 7 units

Kilogram, kg Hectogram, hg Decagram, dag gram, g Decigram, dg Centigram, cg Milligrams, mg

one unit upward divided by 10, one unit downward multiplied by 10

Conversion of other mass units:

10⁻³ g = mg milligrams mg

10³ g = kg kilograms, kg

10⁻⁶ g = µg micrograms (mcg)

10⁶ g = Mg megagrams (tons)

10⁻⁹ = ng nanograms

10⁹ g = Gg gigagrams

10⁻¹² g = pg picogram

Because 1 mg = 10⁻³ g, then for 324 mg :

[tex]\tt =324~mg\times \dfrac{10^{-3}~g}{1~mg}\\\\=324\times 10^{-3}~g\\\\=0.324~g[/tex]

3. aspirin tablet generally contains 325 mg of aspirin. how many such tablets may be prepared for 5kg of aspirin. Give a detailed explanation​


Answer:

There are 15,384 aspirins tablets that can be made from 5 kilograms of aspirin.

Step-by-step explanation:

The firs step is to make all the unit of measurements of our given be the same with each other. We know that 325 mg is needed to make 1 tablet of aspirin. If there is a total of 5 kg of aspirin, we just need to get how any 325 mg are there in 5 kg. We cannot directly divide the two numbers because they differ in their unit of measurement. So we need to convert 5 kg to mg. We know that 1 kg is equal to 1,000 g. So we can multiply 1,000 by 5.

[tex](\frac{5 kg}{1})( \frac{1,000g}{1kg} ) = 5,000 g[/tex]

In doing so we get, 5,000 g.

We now then convert 5,000 g to mg. We know that 1 g is equal to 1,000 mg. So we can multiply 5,000 g by 1,000.

[tex](\frac{5,000g}{1})(\frac{1,000mg}{1g} ) = 5,000,000 mg[/tex]

In doing so, we get 5,000,000 mg.

We can now divide 5,000,000 by 325 mg.

5,000,000 mg ÷ 325 mg = 15,384.6154

Since we need 325 mg to make a tablet, we can disregard the decimal places and just get the whole number which is 15,384. Therefore, we can make 15,384 aspirin tablets from 5 kg of aspirin.

24. A student was synthesizing aspirin in the laboratory. Using the amount of limiting reactant, she calculated the mass of aspirin that should form as 9.95 g. When she weighed her aspirin product on the balance, its mass was 7.54 g. a. What is the actual yield of aspirin? b. What is the theoretical yield of aspirin? c. Calculate the percent yield for this synthesis.​


a. ANSWER:

Note that the actual yield is the measured mass.

Actual yield is always less than the theoretical yield.

[tex]\boxed{\text{The actual yield of aspirin is 7.54 g.}}[/tex]

-----------------------------------------------------

b. ANSWER:

Note that the theoretical yield is the calculated mass.

Theoretical yield is always greater than the actual yield.

[tex]\boxed{\text{The theoretical yield of aspirin is 9.95 g.}}[/tex]

-----------------------------------------------------

c. SOLUTION:

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{percent yield} & = \frac{\text{actual yield}}{\text{theoretical yield}} \times 100\% \\ & = \frac{\text{7.54 g}}{\text{9.95 g}} \times 100\% \\ & = 75.779\% \\ & \approx \boxed{75.8\%} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the percent yield for the synthesis of aspirin is 75.8%.
