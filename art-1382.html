Answer:

ARTICLE 1382

Payments made in the state of insolvency for obligation to whose fulfillment  the debtor could not be compelled at the times they were effected, are also rescissible (1292)

Explanation:

In these circumstances, the creditor prejudiced may set aside the transfer. However even if the consideration for a sale is a preexisting debt, for as long as it is due, owing and enforceable at the time the deed was made, the transfer is valid.
