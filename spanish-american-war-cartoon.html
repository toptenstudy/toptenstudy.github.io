Answer:

The Intellectual Property Code of the Philippines was signed into law 22 years ago today and became effective on January 1,1998. But did you know the intellectual property system in the Philippines existed before the country even declared itself an independent state?

The Spanish Law on Intellectual Property, approved on January 10,1879 and came into force in 1880, was the first known copyright law in the Philippines. Under Spanish laws, copyright is deemed as a property right and governed by civil law but with special legislative provisions.

Chronology of Puerto Rico in the Spanish-American War

1868

23 September

Manuel Rojas organized the Separatist Party and pledged to create the independent Republic of Puerto Rico as part of an uprising known as the Grito de Lares ("The Cry of Lares"). His plantation in the town of Lares became the headquarters for like-minded revolutionaries who would push for a split from Spain.

1870

The Spanish provincial government in Puerto Rico established the Liberal Reform Party and the Liberal Conservative Party as the first true political organizations. The Liberal Conservatives opposed any movement for reform while debate raged among the Liberal Reformers between those who sought to be as much like Spain as possible and those who sought autonomy from the mother country.

1873

22 March

The Spanish Crown abolished slavery in Puerto Rico.

28. who was the president during the spanish american war, and won election of 1896?


Answer

The American President was William McKinley who won the Republican nomination for the presidency on November 3, 1896. Emilio Aguinaldo was declared as President of the First Republic of the Philippines by Malolos Congress on the 21st of January 1899.

Additional Information

Before the Malolos Congress, there was growing political tension between Andres Bonifacio and Emilio Aguinaldo in the Philippines. It was a moment in Philippine history that our country had two declared presidents by different political factions. At first, Aguinaldo and Bonifacio were both leaders of the Political parties called Magdalo and Magdiwang. These two factions were under the movement of the Katipunan.

The division between the two leaders started when Andres Bonifacio kept on losing skirmishes. Meanwhile, Aguinaldo stacked up his victories against the Spaniards. Emilio Aguinaldo then decides to take complete control of the leadership of the Katipunan but Andres Bonifacio was against it. So the Katipunan was divided between the Magdalo (Aguinaldo) and the Magdiwang (Bonifacio). It was after the death of Bonifacio that Aguinaldo merged the two factions into one.
