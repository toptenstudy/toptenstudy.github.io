An entertainment speech is normally more lighthearted and has jokes-- which normally aren't custom.

It's better to start off with a joke because it keeps the reader/receiver engaged with the speech; of course, with any sort of writing, it's good to have an outline or general idea of what you want the writing to come out to.

You can do this by brainstorming-- writing down your ideas quickly-- and then organizing them, or by taking more time to think about the ideas and place they go- however, there is very little difference between them as far as I know.

After you have an outline you can start writing sentences; make sure they connect and flow well:

I couldn't believe they offered me the spot! And then I hurried to quit my now old job.

I couldn't believe they offered me the spot, I was so excited I hurried to quit my now old job.

Both sentences are okay but by adding more detail it makes it easier to read and a bit more evenly paced-- however try not to add too many commas.

( ' -- ' and ';' can sometimes work in place of a comma once you get the hang of how to use them)
