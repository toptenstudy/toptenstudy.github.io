3. main ideas in "The Parable of a Lost Sheep."


Answer:

Main Ideas in "The Parable of a Lost Sheep"

Parables are stories narrated by Jesus Christ that tell spiritual values and lessons that bring people closer to God. This is his way of teaching spiritual lessons during his time as stated in the Bible. There are a lot of parables that Jesus told his people and the Parable of the Lost Sheep is one of the most commonly used stories to bring people into a closer relationship with God nowadays.

Ideas of the ParableThe Shepherd will always look for those who are lost. Will you look for something that is present, visible to your eyes? It is certain that when you lose something, especially those that are important and valuable to you, you will do everything to find it. From the beginning, the Bible is clear that our souls are the most important to God and we became "lost" when our ancestors committed sin that passed through generations. God, as the shepherd, sent Jesus so that those "lost" people who will put their faith in Him will be "found."Left the ninety-nine (99) and looked for the one (1). This is the main concept of the parable in which defies human reasoning: Who, in his right thinking, will leave the rest of the flock and look for just one which is lost? Practically speaking, any shepherd could have lied to the owner and say that it was attacked by wolves or get strayed somewhere. Or maybe the flock might be attacked, stolen, or lost too if he left them just to find the lost one. But God's ways and reasoning is far different from ours. He is the kind of shepherd that cares for everyone in his flock, treasures every sheep in his flock, and will do everything for his flock because we are important to Him.Being found means repentance. The very purpose of God to Jesus is for the sinners to be reconciled to God by having a personal relationship with Him through putting their faith in Jesus Christ. That is why He sent His Son Jesus to be with us on earth so that as He lived a life of intimacy with God, people will have faith in God through Him. Leaving heaven and everything, God made a way for the "lost" people who are living in a sinful life be found and redeemed through Jesus Christ. Through Christ we are found, we are forgiven, and we are restored and when we repent for our sins and accepted Jesus as our savior, Heaven rejoices as stated in the parable, "there will be more rejoicing in heaven over one sinner who repents than over ninety-nine righteous persons who do not need to repent."

17. plss give me a moral lesson from The Parable of the Lost Sheep


The parable of the lost sheep was the story taught by Jesus about the story of the shepherd who had 100 sheep. When one of those sheep was gone, he left the 99 to rescue the one who wandered around. When the sheep is out of the shepherd's sight, anything can happen to the sheep. It can starve himself, it can get sick, or it can be eaten by predators.  

In reality, some shepherd would leave the one lost sheep behind because would it really be worth it to leave the 99 for the one? But the parable wants us to learn the love of God to humankind. He is the Good Shepherd.  

We are not perfect, therefore we will always sin. But there are also some of us who go astray and completely go away from Him. As the Good Shepherd, He will devise a plan to save these people.  That's the reason why He sent His only son, Jesus Christ, to save us. Not only did Jesus die on the cross, He also ministered during His time and had been with the people and His disciples.  He became a personal savior.

God knows who is lost and who is on the right track. Therefore, Jesus wanted to tell us one spiritual truth-- God will never leave anyone behind, and He sent Jesus so that everyone will be saved.

The protagonist in the Parable of the Lost Son is the younger son who asks his father for his inheritance and then squanders it all in a distant country, before finally realizing the error of his ways and returning home to seek forgiveness and reconciliation with his father. The story is told in Luke 15:11-32 in the New Testament of the Bible, and it is often interpreted as a representation of God's forgiveness and unconditional love for his children.

29. Summary of THE PARABLE OF THE LOST SON​


Answer:

The parable begins with a man who had two sons, and the younger of them asks his father to give him his share of the estate. The implication is the son could not wait for his father's death for his inheritance, he wanted it immediately. The father agrees and divides his estate between both sons.
