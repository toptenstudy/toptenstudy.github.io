7. C. Short term funds

Further explanation:

The money market is a place where short-term funding instruments are traded by owners of capital to borrowers of capital.

The money market exists to answer the need for a number of costs or short-term funds that must be met as quickly as possible. This market is also referred to as the short-term credit market because the instruments traded have a short period of time, which is up to one year.

8. B. Money market

Further explanation:

The money market is a market where the owners of funds or funds meet with those who need funds or consumers. The money market arises because there is a demand or supply transaction for a number of funds or short-term securities.

9. B. Financial Market

Further explanation:

The first source of external funding can be obtained by the company from the equity it sells, for example selling shares in the financial market. Through equity, companies can get large amounts of capital from investors and private groups.

The financial market is a meeting place for parties who have excess funds and those who lack funds. So that in the Financial Market there will be transactions or mobilization of funds, whether trading Money Market instruments, Capital Market instruments, or Futures Market instruments.
