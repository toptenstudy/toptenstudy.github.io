Step-by-step explanation:

Basic Elements of GeometryPoint (.)

A point is the most significant object in geometry. It is just a little tiny dot in space, but it does a crucial job in Geometry. They help us name distinct locations in space, like the end of the line, the edge of a square, or the center of a circle. We define points by appointing a capital letter. A point is assumed to be a dot with no size (no length or width).

Line (↔)

A line is the next element of Geometry. We label it by the points that it goes within. For instance, we would call a line 'line AB' because its end-points (where it starts and stops) are points A and B. A line has one dimension; length. It has an indefinite length.

Plane

It's a flat surface like a window, or sheet of paper, or a tabletop. A plane (or flat surface) is what we call a two-dimensional object because there are two dimensions that you can move in. You can go up and down, or you can go left and right. It has an indefinite length and width.
