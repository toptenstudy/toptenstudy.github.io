7. create a parabolic word problem about a bowl and solve it


Answer:

Here is a possible word problem and solution:

A bowl has the shape of a parabola with the equation y = -0.1x^2 + 8, where x and y are measured in centimeters. The vertex of the parabola is at the bottom of the bowl and the x-axis is the diameter of the bowl.

a) What is the height and diameter of the bowl?

b) How much water can the bowl hold if it is filled to the brim?

Solution:

a) To find the height of the bowl, we need to find the y-coordinate of the vertex. Since the vertex is on the axis of symmetry, we can use the formula x = -b/2a to find its x-coordinate. Plugging in the values of a and b from the equation, we get:

x = -b/2a

x = -0/2(-0.1)

x = 0

So, the x-coordinate of the vertex is 0. To find the y-coordinate, we plug in x = 0 into the equation:

y = -0.1x^2 + 8

y = -0.1(0)^2 + 8

y = 8

So, the y-coordinate of the vertex is 8. This means that the height of the bowl is 8 cm.

To find the diameter of the bowl, we need to find the x-intercepts of the parabola. These are the points where y = 0. Setting y to 0 in the equation and solving for x, we get:

y = -0.1x^2 + 8

0 = -0.1x^2 + 8

-0.1x^2 = -8

x^2 = 80

x = ±√80

So, the x-intercepts are √80 and -√80. These are also the endpoints of the diameter of the bowl. To find the length of the diameter, we subtract them:

diameter = √80 - (-√80)

diameter = 2√80

diameter ≈ 17.89

So, the diameter of the bowl is about 17.89 cm.

b) To find how much water can the bowl hold, we need to find the volume of revolution of the parabola around the x-axis. This is given by the formula:

V = π ∫[a,b] y^2 dx

where [a,b] is the interval of x-values that define the parabola. In this case, a = -√80 and b = √80. Plugging in these values and using a calculator, we get:

V = π ∫[-√80,√80] (-0.1x^2 + 8)^2 dx

V ≈ 1205.76

So, the volume of revolution is about 1205.76 cm^3. This means that the bowl can hold about 1205.76 mL or 1.21 L of water if it is filled to the brim.

12. What are the specialized testing devices used to diagnose computer and cable problems?


Answer:

Here are specialized testing devices used to diagnose computer and cable problems:

*Multimeter: A device that measures AC/DC voltage, electric current, and other cable and electrical characteristics.

*Power supply tester: A device that checks whether the computer power supply is working properly. A simple power supply tester might just have indicator lights, while more advanced versions show the amount of voltage and amperage.

*Cable tester: A device that checks for wiring shorts or faults, such as wires connected to the wrong pin.

*Loopback plug: A device that connects to a computer, hub, switch, or router port to perform a diagnostic procedure called a loopback test. In a loopback test, a signal is transmitted through a circuit and then returned to the sending device to test the integrity of the data transmission.
