The cardiorespiratory system shows how the circulatory and respiratory systems work. Specifically, they work together to carry oxygen to the different parts of the body and collect and eliminate carbon dioxide.

Without the circulatory system, materials such as nutrients, gas cannot be transported to the different parts of the body, also the wastes produced will not be eliminated. The main parts of the circulatory system are the heart, blood vessels, and blood. These parts are further made of specific parts that help in the functioning of the whole system.

The heart is divided into four chambers to ensure the efficient flow of blood. The atria or atrium pump/s blood away from the heart while the blood coming from the different parts of the body is received by the ventricles. The atria and ventricle are divided into left and right by a septum. Oxygen-depleted blood can be found on the right chambers of the heart while the left chambers facilitate oxygen-rich blood.

The blood vessels come in different types. The arteries are the blood vessels the carry blood from the heart to different parts of the body. The veins on the other hand carry blood back to the heart. Arterioles and venules have the same function as arteries and veins but they are smaller in size than arteries and veins but both are bigger than capillaries. Capillaries are the thinnest blood vessel. It is just one cell thick that allows diffusion of some materials.

Blood basically carries the materials that need to be circulated and transported all throughout the body.

The respiratory system, on the other hand, is responsible for the exchange of gases. Its main part is the lungs. The lungs are made up of alveoli where gas exchange takes place. It is surrounded by capillaries to facilitate the exchange of gases.

The types of circulation are systemic and pulmonary circulation. Systemic circulation is responsible for circulating blood from the heart to the different parts of the body and vise versa. the other type is pulmonary circulation wherein the blood flows from the heart to the lungs and vice versa.

An internal auditor reviews and adapts a systems flowchart to understand the flow of information in the processing of cash receipts. Which of the following statements is true regarding the use of such flowcharts? The flowcharts

A. Show specific control procedures used, such as edit tests that are implemented and batch control reconciliations.

B. Are a good guide to potential segregation of duties.

C. Show only computer processing, not manual processing.

D. Are generally kept up to date for systems changes.

B. Are a good guide to potential segregation of duties.

Answer B is correct.

Systems flowcharts are overall graphic analyses of the flow of data and the processing steps in an information system. Accordingly, they can be used to show segregation of duties and the transfer of data between different segments in the organization.
