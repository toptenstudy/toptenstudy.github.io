4. 5 important facts of the rizal law​


Answer:

1. mandates all educational institutions in the Philippines to offer courses about José Rizal.

2. Immediate and wide dissemination of this Memorandum to all concerned is desired.

3. It is hoped that teaching Rizal’s life, shall strengthen nationalism, patriotism, national identity, love of humanity, respect for human rights and appreciation of the role of national heroes in the historical development of the country.

4. At the elementary level, Rizal’s virtues/values extolling untarnished love of country must be taught and followed specifically in the learning areas of Sibika at Kultura in Grades I-III and in Heograpiya/Kasaysayan/Sibika in Grades IV-VI. Likewise “Noli Me Tangere” and “El Filibusterismo” are required reading in the third and fourth year high school respectively.

5. The Department of Education, Culture and Sports reiterates the teaching of the life, works and writings of Jose P. Rizal, specifically Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo, as mandated by Republic Act 1425 entitled “An Act to Include in the Curricula of All Public and Private Schools, Colleges and Universities, Courses on the Life, Works and Writings of Jose Rizal, Particularly His Novels, Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo, Authorizing the Printing and Distribution Thereof, and for Other Purposes”

25. Do you believe that the Rizal law is sensible and important? Why?


Well… Rizal did not make laws. He had a lot of professions but I don’t recall him ever making any laws.

However, the Rizal Law you might be referring to is RA 1425 that was enacted in 1956. It mandates educational institutions to study about Rizal’s life and works. Legislators wanted to rekindle a sense of nationalism to the youth of that time. Also, they probably would have wanted to reintroduce some Filipino values that were lost during World War 2.

Personally, this has affected me when I read El Filibusterismo and Noli Mi Tangere when I was in junior high school. It was a refreshing experience to read Filipino texts from a different time period. Along with this, it showed the differences in cultural and social interactions from Rizal’s time and today. The Rizal Law enabled me to learn more about the history, values, and heritage of the Philippines. The difficulties people faced at the time were always mentioned in history books but the way Rizal depicted it in his novel’s made it more tangible in the sense that you could feel the atrocities experiences by the characters.
