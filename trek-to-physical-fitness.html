1) Muscular strength

Muscular strength is the power of a muscle or muscle group produced to be able to fight resistance with maximum effort. Muscular strength is important for everyone, because muscle strength is a carrying capacity for movement in completing tasks.

2) Agility

Agility literally means agility, but the real meaning of agility is the ability of the body and or body parts to change the direction of movement suddenly at a relatively high speed. For example, the ability to turn while running, the ability to run back and forth within a certain distance and period of time, and also the ability to turn quickly but still stand in a stable position.

3) Muscular Endurance

Muscular endurance is the capacity of a group of muscles to perform continuous contractions while holding a submaximal load for a certain period of time. Physical activity performed in order to increase muscular strength and endurance is done to prevent non-communicable diseases (NCDs). Muscular endurance training is also important to lower the risk of various health problems. Muscular endurance plays a big role in almost every athletic sport for example long distance running or marathon running.

4) Speed

What is meant by speed is the ability to perform certain movements, or consecutive / similar movements, and movements to move from one place to another (covering distance) in the shortest possible time. Thus, this ability is always bound and measured by time. The shorter the time, the better the person's speed ability. Examples of sports that rely on speed include running, cycling, and swimming.

5) Flexibility

Flexibility is a person's ability to perform movements in the maximum space of motion without significant obstacles. There are two flexibility training movement techniques, namely dynamic flexibility and static flexibility.

6) Coordination

Coordination is a person's ability to effectively integrate a variety of different movements into a movement pattern so as to combine unconscious movements that have endurance factors with speed, agility, strength and flexibility.

7) Cardiovascular Endurance

Cardiovascular endurance is the functional ability of the heart, lungs, and blood vessels during work and rest. Cardiovascular endurance can be trained by running long distances, cycling long distances, swimming for a long time. Do endurance training continuously and rhythmically. Before starting the exercise, warm up first so you don't get injured.

8) Balance

Balance is the body's ability to maintain a pressure or load from the body in a still or motionless state. By practicing good balance, you have a lower risk of falling and getting injured during sports or activities.

9) Body Composition

A component of physical fitness that should not be overlooked is body composition. Body composition measurements can describe the various elements that make up your body, such as muscle, bone, water content and fat. By knowing this, you can determine your diet and physical activity as well as monitor the changes that occur afterward.

10) Reaction time

Reaction time is the closing dash factor for skill-related aspects of bodily fitness, and it is measured in seconds. When an athlete responds shortly to an exterior stimulus, this is referred to as response time. Although response time is strongly associated to agility, it is a lesser thing of standard bodily fitness.

28. why do you think trekking belongs to moderate to vigorous physical activities ?​


Answer:

Trekking is classified as a moderate to vigorous physical activity because it involves a sustained level of physical exertion. During trekking, you walk for extended periods, often over uneven terrain, carrying a backpack with supplies, which adds extra weight and increases the physical demand of the activity. This combination of physical demands leads to an increase in heart rate, respiration, and energy expenditure, which are hallmarks of moderate to vigorous physical activity.

The level of exertion required for trekking can vary depending on the terrain, weather conditions, altitude, and the individual's physical fitness level. However, in general, it is considered a moderate to vigorous activity, as it requires a significant amount of energy expenditure, especially compared to sedentary activities such as sitting or reading.

It is also important to note that trekking can provide numerous health benefits, including improving cardiovascular fitness, strengthening bones and muscles, and reducing stress levels.
