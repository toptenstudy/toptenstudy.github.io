What is Extensive Reading?

Extensive Reading (ER) is a method of reading in a second language. Learners who read a lot read simple, entertaining books to improve their reading speed and fluency. Another way to put it is that children learn to read by actually reading rather than studying vocabulary, grammar, and phrases in texts. The contrast between Intensive Reading (IR) and Extensive Reading (ER) is instructive.

What are the benefits of extensive reading?Extensive reading expands a person's vocabulary. When students read extensively, they come across thousands of words and lexical (word) patterns that are not covered in textbooks. The student can gain an awareness of collocations (common word pairs) and thousands of vocabulary phrases through extensive reading.Extensive reading contributes in the comprehension of grammar. Learners encounter hundreds of grammar patterns in textbooks. However, textbooks do not provide enough opportunities for students to interact with grammar in order for true learning to take place. Extensive Reading allows students to perceive language in context, allowing them to have a better understanding of how grammar is actually utilized.
