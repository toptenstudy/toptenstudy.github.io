14. what is the role of deontology in determining moral actions? ​


Answer:

Owing to some attribute of the action itself, an action is considered morally good in deontological ethics, not because the product of the action is good. Deontological ethics holds that, irrespective of their implications for human wellbeing, at least certain actions are morally compulsory.

Answer:

In deontological ethics an action is considered morally good because of some characteristic of the action itself, not because the product of the action is good. Deontological ethics holds that at least some acts are morally obligatory regardless of their consequences for human welfare. Deontology states that an act that is not good morally can lead to something good, such as shooting the intruder (killing is wrong) to protect your family (protecting them is right). According to Kant, morality is affected by rational thought moreso than by emotion.

Morality is a complex and highly subjective concept that has been debated and discussed by philosophers, theologians, and ordinary people for centuries. At its most basic level, morality refers to a set of principles or values that guide individuals in determining what is right and wrong behavior. Good and bad actions are those that align with or violate these moral principles and values.

Becoming a moral person is a lifelong journey that requires constant self-reflection and effort. It requires an individual to reflect on their own values, beliefs, and principles and to make conscious decisions based on these internalized moral standards. This is not a one-time event, but rather a continuous process of personal growth and improvement.

The qualities of a moral person include honesty and integrity, empathy and compassion, responsibility and accountability, fairness and justice, and respect for others and their beliefs and values. To become a moral person, one must engage in actions that demonstrate these qualities. This includes things such as practicing honesty in all interactions, making an effort to understand the perspectives of others, taking responsibility for one's actions, treating others fairly and justly, and respecting the beliefs and values of others, even if they differ from one's own.

Examples of moral actions include standing up for what is right, even if it is unpopular, helping those in need, even if it requires personal sacrifice, apologizing and making amends for one's mistakes, and demonstrating fairness and justice in all dealings. These actions serve as a guide for what it means to be a moral person and demonstrate the qualities that define morality.

It is impossible to determine whether someone is a moral person or not, as morality is a highly subjective concept. However, consistently exhibiting qualities such as honesty, empathy, responsibility, fairness, and respect can serve as a guide in determining one's level of morality.

In conclusion, being a moral person is a lifelong journey that requires self-reflection, effort, and a commitment to personal growth and improvement. The essence of being a moral person lies in living by a set of values and principles that align with one's own beliefs and the beliefs of others, and consistently exhibiting qualities such as honesty, empathy, responsibility, fairness, and respect in all actions and dealings.
