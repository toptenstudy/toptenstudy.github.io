21. Which of the following molecules can act as hydrogen bond donors with water? ch4 nah hf ch3coch3


Answer:

The molecules that can act as hydrogen bond donors with water are NAH and CH3COCH3. These molecules contain hydrogen atoms that are bonded to nitrogen and oxygen atoms, respectively, which are highly electronegative and can form a temporary dipole with the hydrogen atom, allowing it to act as a hydrogen bond donor. CH4, HF, and CH3COCH3 do not contain hydrogen atoms bonded to nitrogen or oxygen atoms, and therefore cannot act as hydrogen bond donors with water.
