Applied science is anything in science that has a practical application in industry and our life. social science is the study of a human behavior, relationship with other human in terms of emotions, psychologic, the impact of belief to environment based on scientific research.How social science and applied social science alike Social science is concerned with empirical research (in concepts and theories) of societal activities. The Applied of social science principles and theories to social events is the aim of applied social science. There are several points of intersection between these two broad divisions.Applied social science is the application of social science theory, principles, processes, and observations to societal issues (D. Using this understanding to separate social science into distinct fields gives rise to the principles of applied social sciences such as psychology, social work, and communication. Social sciences are disciplines occupied with the systemic investigation of social phenomena Essentially, the word "social sciences" refers to the study of human culture; the specific field of study that is concerned with human activity and culture. It is a division of science that deals with societies, the functioning of human society, and the interpersonal interactions of people as members of society. There are distinct disciplines or categories of human culture that deal with a certain process or aspect of human nature, such as socio-cultural facets of human behavior.

An Overview: Disciplines of Social Sciences

The disciplines of social sciences Anthropology, economics, sociology, political science, history, criminology, psychology, geography, and communication studies are some examples. The aim of these disciplines is to understand human activity in all of its cultural expressions, including the past and present, individually or in communities, in national and international geopolitical contexts, and to encourage them as democratic participants in a rapidly changing environment. Collectively, the fields of social science offer a substantive perspective into the understanding of culture and the relationships between human members and classes within society. Applied social science is an applied science that cuts through and transcends numerous social science fields in order to solve a broad variety of problems in a modern, imaginative, and diverse manner. Its aim is to provide a thorough understanding of social institutions, social philosophy, and social policy. It combines philosophy, psychological science, expertise and practical practice, as well as critical thinking abilities, all of which are needed to fully discuss social phenomena. As a consequence, applied social science is a large field that builds on numerous social theories and insights, as well as integrating philosophy and research from various social sciences to illustrate the complexities of social issues. The focus of applied social science is less rigid and allows for specializations in other fields that have insights It is important to acknowledge that applied social science is not a set of different technical social science disciplines. It moves beyond individual specialized social science to find its true meaning in constructive involvement with the broader public in reality. It is practice. Three applied social sciences are highlighted in this worktext to illustrate how ideas and principles from other fields can be brought together to bear on one field in an applied sense: counseling, social work, and communication.
