9. write an essay about values and attributes of a senior high school student in work immersion ​


Answer:

Senior high school students who undergo work immersion programs have a unique opportunity to acquire practical knowledge, skills, and values necessary for their future careers. Work immersion programs expose students to a professional work environment and allow them to experience the demands and challenges of real-world work. As such, it is essential for senior high school students to develop specific values and attributes that will help them excel in their work immersion experiences.

One of the most important values that a senior high school student should possess during work immersion is professionalism. Professionalism refers to the ability to behave appropriately, responsibly, and ethically in a work environment. This includes dressing appropriately, arriving on time, being reliable and dependable, and communicating effectively with colleagues and supervisors. Professionalism also involves having a positive attitude, being willing to learn, and taking responsibility for one's actions.

Another critical value for senior high school students in work immersion is adaptability. The ability to adapt to new situations and environments is crucial, particularly in work immersion programs that expose students to different types of work and industries. Adaptability includes being open-minded, flexible, and willing to learn new skills and concepts. Students who are adaptable can adjust to changes in the work environment quickly, work well with others, and handle unexpected situations with ease.

Communication is also an essential attribute for senior high school students in work immersion. Effective communication involves the ability to express oneself clearly and listen actively to others. Senior high school students who communicate well can ask questions, give and receive feedback, and work collaboratively with others. Strong communication skills are crucial in any work environment, and students who can communicate effectively will have an advantage in their future careers.

Another attribute that senior high school students should develop during work immersion is initiative. Initiative refers to the ability to take action without being told what to do. Senior high school students who take initiative can identify problems, propose solutions, and take steps to implement those solutions. This attribute is particularly valuable in work environments that require creative problem-solving, innovation, and self-motivation.

Finally, a senior high school student in work immersion should develop a strong work ethic. Work ethic refers to the values and principles that guide one's work habits and performance. Students with a strong work ethic are diligent, disciplined, and committed to their work. They take pride in their work, are accountable for their actions, and are willing to go above and beyond what is expected of them. Developing a strong work ethic during work immersion will prepare senior high school students for the demands of the professional world and help them achieve success in their future careers.

In conclusion, work immersion programs provide senior high school students with an invaluable opportunity to gain practical knowledge, skills, and values necessary for their future careers. By developing values and attributes such as professionalism, adaptability, communication, initiative, and work ethic, senior high school students can make the most of their work immersion experiences and prepare themselves for success in their future careers.

The K to 12 Basic Education Curriculum for Senior High School in the Philippines requires a Grade 12 student to undergo Work Immersion in an industry that directly relates to the student's post-secondary goal in order to graduate.

Through Work Immersion, the students are exposed to and become familiar with work-related environment related to their field of specialization to enhance their competence. Specifically, the students are able to: (1) gain relevant and practical industrial skills under the guidance of industry experts and workers; (ii) appreciate the importance and application of the principles and theories taught in school, enhance their technical knowledge and skills; (iv) enrich their skills in communications and human relation; and (v) develop good work habits, attitudes, appreciation, and respect for work. These prepare them to meet the needs and challenges of employment or higher education after employment. Work Immersion provides the students with a venue to test themselves and apply what they have learned in a non-school scenario. The learners are not only able to apply their previous training but are also able to experience the social interactions in a work environment. Their experiences during work immersion will develop many skills and values that would help them as they transition from high school to real life.

To assure the achievement of the objectives of Work Immersion in the Philippines, the Department of Education issued DepEd Order No. 30, s. 2017 which serves as the basis in the implementation of work immersion in all Senior High Schools (SHSS). The policy serves to guide SHSS in: (i) creating flexible work immersion arrangement for their learners; (ii) providing options for work immersion that are relevant to learners' purposes and needs; (iii) organizing work immersion opportunities for learners that are consistent with the diverse human resource requirements for partner institutions for work immersion; and (iv) articulating the scope and limits of work immersion in the context of basic education when building relationships with work immersion partners. To assess the learners' performance during the Work Immersion, the Work Immersion Teacher and the Work Immersion Partner Institution Supervisor will jointly assess the learners' performance following DepEd Order No. 8, 2015.

8 WAYS TO BECOME PROFESSIONAL IN THE WORKPLACE

Acting like a professional really means doing what it takes to make others think of you as reliable, respectful, and competent. However it depends on where you work and the type of job you have, it may take various forms. But here are the common traits of being a Professional.

1. Develop Competency: A workplace competency is a description of desired skill, attribute or behavior for a job which is used to measure an individual’s effectiveness. Usually workplace competency is judged from 2 aspects: Behavioral and Technical. Behavioral competencies are judged based on work confidentially, inspire others and submit monthly reports on time, while technical competencies are judged based on written communication skills, knowledge of procedures and ability to resolve conflict.

2. Reliability: Reliability is essential to employee performance. A reliable employee is the one who always complete his/her work in time. 

3. Honesty: Being honest means “refrain from lying”. You tell the truth and are upfront about where things stand. You should always address mistakes as soon as possible. 

4. Be systematic: A professional is the one who follows time and keeps his/her employers happy. Always create a schedule of your task and try to manage your work with proper breaks and time slots.

5. Keep good relation: Treat all people as important as if they mattered a lot. Assure them you are approachable and they can discuss their problem during tough times, but maintain the required distance. A good professional always share his/her knowledge, opinion or simply an extra pair of hands. 

6. Being Positive and friendly: Have you ever noticed that the employee with friendly and positive attitude in the work place have more friends? Employees with positive attitude are more productive and result oriented as they make it easier for everyone to get along in the workplace. 

7. Communicate and listen carefully: Communicate in a professional manner, listen attentively to others ideas, problems, maintain a eye contact and practice a solid handshake. 

8. Dress code and appearance: Choose modest and conservative clothing that reflect the more formal attire accepted at the workplace. However there are certain workplace where dress code was often used, this is done in the office premises to communicate a corporate image and ensure that customer can easily identify them.
