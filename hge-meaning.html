7. Answer and solve the following:EFGH is a rhombusFind the indicated measure.1.m<HGE=25.Find m<FGE.2.m<HEI=10.Find m<FEI.3.m<IHE=32.Find m<IEH.4.m<IEF=28.Find m<IEF+EIF.5.m<IGH=20.Find m<IGH+m<HIGplease help me!​


1. Since EFGH is a rhombus, its opposite angles are congruent. Therefore, m<HGE = m<EFH. Since m<HGE = 25, we have m<EFH = 25. In triangle FGE, the sum of the measures of the angles must be 180 degrees. We already know that m<FGE + m<EFH = 180, so we can substitute m<EFH = 25 to get:

m<FGE + 25 = 180

Subtracting 25 from both sides, we get:

m<FGE = 155

Therefore, m<FGE = 155 degrees.

2. Since EFGH is a rhombus, its diagonals bisect each other. Therefore, in triangle FEI, the angles at F and I are congruent. Since m<HEI = 10, we have m<HFE = 180 - 2(10) = 160 (since HFE is a straight angle). Therefore, m<FEI = (180 - m<HFE)/2 = (180 - 160)/2 = 10.

Therefore, m<FEI = 10 degrees.

3. Since EFGH is a rhombus, its diagonals bisect each other. Therefore, in triangle IHE, the angles at H and E are congruent. Therefore, m<HIE = m<IEH = 32. Since the sum of the angles in triangle IHE is 180 degrees, we have:

m<IHE + m<HIE + m<IEH = 180

Substituting m<IEH = 32 and m<HIE = 32, we get:

32 + 32 + m<IHE = 180

Simplifying, we get:

m<IHE = 116

Therefore, m<IHE = 116 degrees.

4. We cannot solve this problem as stated, since m<IEF and m<EIF are parts of the same angle (m<IEF + m<EIF = m<HEI = 10). Therefore, the sum of their measures is simply m<HEI - 28 = 10 - 28 = -18. However, since angles cannot have negative measures, the problem as stated is impossible to solve. If you meant to ask for the measure of angle IEF or angle EIF individually, please let me know.

5. Since EFGH is a rhombus, its diagonals bisect each other. Therefore, m<IGH = m<HIG. Let's call this measure x. Then we have:

m<IGH + m<HIG = 2x = 20

Simplifying, we get:

x = 10

Therefore, m<IGH = m<HIG = 10 degrees.
