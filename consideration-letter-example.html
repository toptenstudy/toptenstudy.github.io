22. Suppose we want to choose 2 letters, without replacement, from the 4 letters A, B, C, and D. (a) How many ways can this be done, if the order of the choices is taken into consideration (b) How many ways can this be done, if the order of the choices is not taken into consideration


(a) If the order of the choices is taken into consideration, then we are choosing two letters from four. We can choose the first letter in 4 ways, and the second letter in 3 ways (since one letter has already been chosen), giving us a total of 4 x 3 = 12 possible ways to choose 2 letters.

(b) If the order of the choices is not taken into consideration, then we are simply choosing a pair of letters. The number of ways to do this is given by the combination formula:
C(4, 2) = 4! / (2! (4 - 2)!) = 6

So there are 6 ways to choose 2 letters from A, B, C, and D, without regard to order.
