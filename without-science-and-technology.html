22. what would be a society look like without science and technology?​


Answer:

A society without science and technology would be very different from the one we live in today. Here are some possible scenarios:

Agriculture and food production: Without science and technology, agriculture would be limited to traditional, labor-intensive methods of farming. There would be no genetic modification of crops to improve yields or resistance to pests and diseases. Irrigation systems would be limited, which would restrict the types of crops that could be grown in different regions. Food preservation techniques would be limited, making it difficult to store food for long periods of time.

Healthcare: Healthcare would be much more limited without science and technology. Many common diseases would still be incurable, and there would be no effective treatments for conditions like cancer or HIV. There would be no medical devices like X-ray machines, ultrasound scanners, or MRI scanners, which means that doctors would have to rely solely on physical examination to diagnose illnesses. Surgery would be much more dangerous, and there would be no anesthesia to help patients manage pain.

Transportation: Without science and technology, transportation would be limited to walking, horse-drawn carriages, and boats. There would be no cars, airplanes, or trains. This would severely limit the ability of people to travel long distances, which would impact trade and communication between different regions.

Communication: Communication would be much more limited without science and technology. There would be no telephones, radios, or televisions. This would make it much more difficult for people to communicate with each other over long distances. The only way to communicate would be through written letters, which would take a long time to deliver.
