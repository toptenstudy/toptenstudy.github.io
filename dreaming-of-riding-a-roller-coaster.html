2. what makes a roller coaster safe to ride?​


Answer:

There are several factors that make a roller coaster safe to ride:

Design: Roller coasters are designed with safety in mind. Engineers use advanced computer technology and physics calculations to ensure that the coaster can withstand the forces and stresses that it will encounter during operation.

Construction: Once the design is complete, roller coasters are constructed using high-quality materials that are specifically selected for their strength and durability.

Inspections: Before a roller coaster can open to the public, it must go through a rigorous inspection process to ensure that it meets all safety standards and regulations. This includes regular inspections throughout its lifespan.

Safety systems: Roller coasters are equipped with a variety of safety systems, such as lap bars, seat belts, and other restraints to keep riders securely in their seats.

Training and maintenance: Ride operators and maintenance staff receive extensive training to ensure that they can operate and maintain the roller coaster safely.

Overall, a combination of good design, quality construction, regular inspections, safety systems, and well-trained staff all contribute to making a roller coaster safe to ride

Derivatives in Roller Coasters.

Derivatives play a crucial role in the design and construction of roller coasters. Here are five important ways that derivatives are applied in building roller coasters:

Velocity: Roller coasters rely on the principles of calculus to calculate the velocity of the ride. By taking the derivative of the position of the coaster over time, engineers can determine how fast the coaster is traveling at any given moment.Acceleration: Derivatives are also used to calculate the acceleration of the roller coaster. This information is important for determining how much force the coaster will exert on riders as it moves through its various twists and turns.Slope: Calculus is used to calculate the slope of the track at different points in the ride. This information is used to design the roller coaster's twists, turns, and drops to ensure that riders experience the desired level of excitement while still maintaining safety.Optimization: Calculus is used to optimize the design of the roller coaster track. Engineers use derivatives to find the minimum and maximum values of different variables, such as speed and acceleration, to create the most thrilling ride possible while still ensuring rider safety.Safety: Derivatives are also important for ensuring the safety of riders on the roller coaster. Calculus is used to calculate the maximum and minimum forces that riders will experience during the ride, and engineers can adjust the track design accordingly to ensure that the ride remains safe for riders.

Overall, derivatives play a critical role in the design and construction of roller coasters. By using calculus to calculate velocity, acceleration, slope, optimization, and safety, engineers can create thrilling rides that are both safe and enjoyable for riders.

240[tex]km/h^{2}[/tex]

Explanation:

What is asked: Accelartion

Formula: [tex]a=\frac{v_f-v_i}{t}[/tex] or a=(vf-vi)/t

Whereas: a = acceleration

vf= final velocity

vi= initial velocity

t=time

Given:

vf = 36km/h

vi = from rest hence 0km/h

t = 0.15h

Solution:

a=(36-0)/0.15

a=36/0.15

The Law of Conservation of Energy states that within a closed system, energy can change form, but it cannot be created or destroyed. In other words, the total amount of energy remains constant.
