1. How important mass is?​


Answer:

Mass is important because of two major factors affecting how things move in space: inertia and gravity. The more mass something has, the more of both it experiences. ... Gravity is also proportional to how much mass each thing has. The bigger an object is, the larger the gravitational pull it exerts.

9. Why is the conservation of mass important?


Answer:

According to the law of conservation of mass, the mass of the products in a chemical reaction must equal the mass of the reactants. The law of conservation of mass is useful for a number of calculations and can be used to solve for unknown masses, such the amount of gas consumed or produced during a reaction.
