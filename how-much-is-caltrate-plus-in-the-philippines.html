2. How much is 1/9 plus 1/3?​


Answer:

[tex]\frac{4}{9}[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

Since we can not add dissimilar fractions or fractions with different denominators, we must change them first to similar fractions.

First, we will find the LCD (Listing Method):

9: 9, 18, 27, 36, 45...

3: 3, 6, 9, 12, 15...

Next, we will find the new numerators.

[tex]\frac{1}{9} = \frac{1}{9}[/tex]

[tex]\frac{1}{3}= \frac{3}{9}[/tex]

(9 ÷ 3 × 1)

Now, we can add them.

[tex]\frac{1}{9} +\frac{3}{9} =\frac{4}{9}[/tex]

19. A salesman earns 2,500 a week plus 6% commission on weekly sales exceeding 7,500. If his salary plus commission during one week amounted to 5,500 , how much was his sales?


ANSWER: His sales for the weeks was 57,500 pesos.

Solution:
1)  Subtract the salary from 5,500 to find the commission.
      5,500 - 2,500 = 3,000

2)  Find the source/basis of the 3,000 commission at 6%.  This is the sales 
     exceeding 7,500.

    Basis of commission = x
     
    Equation:
     
         0.06x = 3,000
         0.06x/0.06 = 3,000/0.06
         x = 50,000    ⇒  amount exceeding 7,500

3)  Add 50,000 to 7,500 to solve for sales for the weeK
     50,000 + 7,500 = 57,500

In one equation:
Let S be the sales for the week:
      S = [(5,500 - 2,500) /0.06] + 7,500
      S = (3,000)/0.06) + 7,500
      S = 50,000 + 7,500
      S = 57,500
