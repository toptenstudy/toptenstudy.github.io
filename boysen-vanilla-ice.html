11. 12 The proportional relationship between the number of gallons of chocolate ice cream and the number of gallons of vanilla ice cream that an ice creamery freezes daily is graphed below. What does the point (6, 2) represent on the graph? A. There are 6 times the number of gallons of vanilla ice cream frozen than chocolate ice cream. B. For every 6 gallons of vanilla ice cream frozen, the ice creamery freezes 2 gallons of chocolate ice cream. C. For every 6 gallons of chocolate ice cream frozen, the ice creamery freezes 2 gallons of vanilla ice cream. D. There are always 6 – 2 = 4 more gallons of vanilla ice cream frozen than chocolate ice cream frozen.


The point (6, 2) represents a specific ratio of chocolate ice cream to vanilla ice cream that the ice creamery freezes daily.

From the graph, we can see that the ratio of chocolate ice cream to vanilla ice cream is constant and equal to 1/3. This means that for every 1 gallon of chocolate ice cream frozen, the ice creamery freezes 3 gallons of vanilla ice cream.

Using this ratio, we can determine the amount of vanilla ice cream that the ice creamery freezes when 6 gallons of chocolate ice cream are frozen:

6 gallons chocolate ice cream x (3 gallons vanilla ice cream/1 gallon chocolate ice cream) = 18 gallons vanilla ice cream

Therefore, the point (6, 2) represents the fact that the ice creamery freezes 6 gallons of chocolate ice cream and 18 gallons of vanilla ice cream daily, maintaining a ratio of 1/3 (or 2/6) of chocolate ice cream to vanilla ice cream.

So the correct answer is B: For every 6 gallons of vanilla ice cream frozen, the ice creamery freezes 2 gallons of chocolate ice cream.

19. 1/2 of the children at a party like chocolate ice cream, 2/3 like vanilla ice cream, and 1/3 like chocolate and vanilla. What fraction of those who like vanilla also like chocolate?


Answer:

[tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

First we need to take the total number of children who like vanilla which is 2/3  then we take the total number of children who like chocolate which is 1/2. Then we have the total of 1/3 who like vanilla and chocolate together.

Now that we have both values, we can then proceed to divide both of the children who like chocolate and who like chocolate and vanilla by each other.

This then gives us:

[tex]\frac{ \frac{1}{3}}{\frac{2}{3} }[/tex]

In fraction, we know that dividing a fraction with another fraction can also be the same as multiplying them together by getting the reciprocal of the second fraction.

We then have:

[tex]\frac{1}{3}[/tex] × [tex]\frac{3}{2}[/tex]

After cancelling out 3 from numerator and denominator of our fraction, we will get:

[tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex]
