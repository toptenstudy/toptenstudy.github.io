5. Advantage of Quantitative research in stem strand​


Answer:

The purpose of quantitative research is to attain greater knowledge and understanding of the social world. Researchers use quantitative methods to observe situations or events that affect people.

1 Quantitative research produces objective data that can be clearly communicated through statistics and numbers. We do this in a systematic scientific way so the studies can be replicated by someone else.

Quantitative research is structured around the scientific method. After observing the situation to be investigated, the researcher forms a hypothesis and then uses deductive reasoning by predicting how the data should look if the hypothesis is true, after collecting the data and analyzing it to confirm or reject the hypothesis.

-Exploratory research is defined as a research used to investigate a problem which is not clearly defined. It is conducted to have a better understanding of the existing problem, but will not provide conclusive results.

-Exploratory studies usually create scope for future research and the future research may have a conclusive design. For example, 'a study into the implications of COVID-19 pandemic into the global economy' is an exploratory research.

-It explores questions without coming up with a definite conclusion. An example of explanatory research is if an animal shelter tries to find out why cities tend to experience an increase in animals being abandoned. They may find some influencing factors, such as higher populations, but won't reach a conclusion.
