16. explain what is threat, hazard, vulnerability and risk in security?​


THREAT
Any circumstance or event with the potential to harm an information system through unauthorized access, destruction, disclosure, modification of data, and/or denial of service.

HAZARD
The probability of occurrence of a potentially damaging phenomenon, vulnerability

VULNERABILITY
the degree of loss resulting from the occurrence of the phenomenon.

RISK
something that could cause harm to people or that exposes information or assets to compromise, loss, unavailability or damage.

A threat is a statement indicating that you will cause harm to or create some other kind of negative consequences for someone, especially to pressure them to do something or not to do something. Many threats involve a promise to physically harm.

A hazard is any source of potential damage, harm or adverse health effects on something or someone.

Vulnerability describes the characteristics and circumstances of a community, system or asset that make it susceptible to the damaging effects of a hazard.

Risk is defined as the potential for loss or damage when a threat exploits a vulnerability


17. what is the hazard of Inadequate securing of anchor points​


t Hazar Here are some potential hazards:

Falling: When anchor points are not adequately secured, there is a risk of the anchor point failing or dislodging, which can cause the worker to fall and sustain injuries or even fatalities.

Equipment damage: If anchor points are not secured properly, the equipment being used to secure the worker, such as ropes, harnesses, or lanyards, can become damaged or even break, which can also cause injury.

Delayed rescue: If a worker does fall, inadequate securing of anchor points can make it more difficult and time-consuming for rescue personnel to reach the worker, increasing the risk of serious injury or death.

Legal liability: Employers have a duty to ensure that their workers are provided with a safe working environment, including securing anchor points. Failure to do so can result in legal liability for the employer.
