Molecular and crystal structure

The three oxygen atoms form a trigonal planar geometry around the boron. The B-O bond length is 136 pm and the O-H is 97 pm. The molecular point group is C3h. Crystalline boric acid consists of layers of B(OH)3 molecules held together by hydrogen bonds of length 272 pm.

29. A mixture containing 6% boric acid is to be mixed with 2 quarts of a mixture which is 15% boric acid in order to obtain a solution which is 12% boric acid. How much of the 6% solution must be used?​


MIXTURE PROBLEMS:

A mixture containing 6% boric acid is to be mixed with 2 quarts of a mixture which is 15% boric acid in order to obtain a solution which is 12% boric acid. How much of the 6% solution must be used?

[tex] \tt \: 1st \: mixture \: = 6 \tt\% \: \\ [/tex][tex] \tt \: 2nd \: mixture \: = \: 15 \tt \% \\ [/tex]

[tex] \boxed{\tt \: TOTAL \: = \: 12 \% } \\ [/tex]

The question asks, "How much of the 6% solution must be used? Therefore, let x represent the amount of 6% solution used. The total solution will have x quarts plus 2 quarts (or the total of the other two solutions).

[tex] \tt \: Let \: x \: = \: quarts \: of \: 6 \% \: solution \\ [/tex][tex] \tt \: x \: qt \: + \: 2 \: qt \: = (x + 2) \: qt \\ [/tex][tex] \tt \: 6 \% \: + 15 \% \: = 12 \% \\ [/tex]If you multiply the amount of solution by the percent of acid in the solution, you will find the amount of puric boric acid in each solution.

The amount of puric acid in the final solution is equal to the sum of the amounts of puric acid in the two orginal solutions.

[tex] \small\tt \: 0.06x \: + \: 2(0.15) \: = \: 0.12(x + 2) \\ [/tex][tex] \small \tt \: 0.06x \: + \: 0.030 \: = \: 0.12x \: + \: 0.24 \\ [/tex]

To clear decimals, multiply by 100 which moves all decimal points two places to the right:

[tex] \tt \: 6x \: + \: 30 \: = \: 12x \: + \: 24 \\ [/tex][tex] \tt \: - 6x \: = - 6 \\ [/tex][tex] \tt \: x = 1 \\ [/tex]
