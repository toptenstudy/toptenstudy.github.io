Easy enough~

Explanation:

The statement is totally biased for several reasons:

- Personal Appearance: The writer begins by criticizing the Mayor's appearance, describing him as a "hobo" with unshaven and dirty clothes. This statement is irrelevant to the Mayor's ability to govern the city, and it is a personal attack that detracts from any legitimate criticism of his policies.

- Horrible Plan: The writer uses subjective language to describe the Mayor's plan, calling it "horrible" without providing any specific details or evidence to support this claim. This language is meant to invoke an emotional response rather than a logical evaluation of the plan's merits.

- Benefiting Friends: The writer alleges that the plan mostly benefits the Mayor's friends in the construction business, but again, no evidence is provided to support this claim. This is a classic example of an ad hominem attack, which attempts to discredit the Mayor's plan by attacking his personal relationships rather than addressing the plan's merits.

- Italian Descent: The writer's reference to the Mayor's Italian descent and the negative reputation of Italian Americans in this part of the country is a clear example of prejudice and bias. It is an attempt to associate the Mayor with negative stereotypes without any evidence to support such a claim.

In summary, the statement is biased because it relies on personal attacks, subjective language, and unfounded allegations to discredit the Mayor and his policies. It is important to evaluate political leaders based on their actions and policies, not on their appearance, ethnicity, or personal relationships.
