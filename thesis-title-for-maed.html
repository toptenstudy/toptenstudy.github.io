5. how to explain the title of thesis​


Answer:

A thesis title is a short header of the paper, which usually consists of two parts. The first part contains information about the topic of the work and the second section is about the methods of research. The main objective of the title is to attract the reader's attention and give a brief description of the paper.

answer:

thesis title, "Online games Social media.to student" thesis justification

A potential thesis title on the topic of online games and social media could be "Exploring the Impact of Online Gaming and Social Media on Students' Social Skills and Academic Performance." The justification for this thesis would be to investigate the potential effects of online gaming and social media on students, both positive and negative, and to understand how these tools can be used effectively in education. Online gaming and social media can provide opportunities for social interaction and collaboration, but they can also be distractions that interfere with learning. By studying the impact of these technologies on students, the thesis could provide insight into how they can be integrated into education in a way that maximizes their benefits and minimizes their drawbacks.

c). ‘How and why it is important to reconcile the differences between Customers' and Managements' perceived service quality (PSQ) of an F&B establishment’.

PSQ is defined as the difference between an entity' Expectation and their Perception of service. (use PZB Service Quality Model).

e). ‘The importance of a high quality ‘service recovey’ approach in F&B businesses to garner and ensure customers' trust and loyalty’.

(Service recovery is a service management system used specifically to redress service failures). Service fails because of the nature, characteristics of service and other factors, which may be cultural and personal preferences.

d). The challenges encountered by F&B businesses in delivering PSQ to customers in a cross-cultural context (see e) below).

e). The importance of effective service recovery approach in F&B establishments within Five-Starred hotel establishment.

f). ‘Customers are found to engage in ‘counterfactual thinking’ in their assessment of service provisions of a service recovery approach’.

‘It is important for service providers in F&B establishments to understand how customers engage in Counterfactual thinking (CT) when devising their service recovery provisions, to ensure it is efficacious’. (CT is a Social psychological concept).
