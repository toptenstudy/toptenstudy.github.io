10. aqueous solution of potassium sulfate (K2SO4) has a freezing point of –2.24 ºC. What is its molality? (Kf = 1.86​


Answer:

m = 1.204 molal

Explanation:

Calculate the freezing point depression.

FP solution = FP solvent - ∆Tf

∆Tf = FP solvent - FP solution

∆Tf = 0.00 °C - (-2.24 °C)

∆Tf = 2.24 °C

Solve for the molality.

∆Tf = m • kf

m = ∆Tf / kf

m = 2.24 °C / 1.86 °C/molal

m = 1.204 molal

19. 1. A reaction with calculated yield of 10.9 g produced 9.8g. What is the percent yield of this reaction?2. Potassium sulfate or Sulfate of Potass is generally used in fertilizer to provide Potassium and sulfur. The chemical reaction is given below:2KCl+H2SO4 yields to 2HCl+K2SO4If 60.0g og KCl react with H2SO4, how many grams of K2SO4 will be produced? If the percent yield was 78%, what was the actual yield? ​


Answer:

1. 90%

2. 70.1 g, 55 g

Explanation:

1. Given: actual yield = 9.8 g

theoretical yield = 10.9 g

Required: percent yield

Equation: percent yield = (actual yield / theoretical yield) × 100

Solution: percent yield = (9.8 g / 10.9 g) × 100

Answer: percent yield = 90%

2. Step 1: Compute for the mass of K2SO4 produced.

mass K2SO4 = 60.0 g KCl × (1 mol KCl / 74.55 g KCl) × (1 mol K2SO4 / 2 mol KCl) × (174.27 g K2SO4 / 1 mol K2SO4)

mass K2SO4 = 70.1 g

Step 2: Compute for the actual yield. Note that the mass we obtained in Step 1 is the theoretical yield.

actual yield = theoretical yield × percent yield / 100

actual yield = (70.1 g)(78) / 100

actual yield = 55 g
