4. 1. A human body at birth consists of 78 percent water. This gradually decreases to 60 percent in an adult. Assume a baby weighed 3.2 kg at birth and grew into an adult weighing 95 kg. Calculate the approximate number of kilograms of water the human gained. 2. Assume an adult woman weighs 65 kg and an adult man weighs 90 kg. Calculate how much more water, in kilograms, the man has compared to the woman.


CALCULATING THE WATER WEIGHT

In order to calculate the water weight, we multiply the weight of the person with the estimated percentage of water in the human body. For newborns, it is 78%, and then at adult 60%.

1. Calculate the approximate number of kilograms of water the human gained.

Weight of Child: 3.2kg

Water weight of Child: 3.2 x 78% = 2.496kg

Weight of Adult: 95kg

Water weight of adult: 95 x 60% = 57kg

Water weight gained: 57kg-2.496kg = 54.504kg

2. Calculate how much more water, in kilograms, the man has compared to the woman.

Water weight of woman: 65kg x 60% = 39kg

Water weight of man: 90kg x 60% = 54kg

Water weight difference= 54kg - 39kg = 15kg

The man has 15kg more water than the woman.
