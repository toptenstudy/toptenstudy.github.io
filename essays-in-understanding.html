7. essay: What are the benefits of understanding the self? ​


Answer:

This ability to empathise facilitates better personal and professional relationships. When we are more self-aware we are better at understanding ourselves. We are then able to identify changes we want to make and recognise our strengths so we can build on them. Self-awareness is often a first step to goal setting

Explanation:

When we have high internal self-awareness, we understand how we affect the environment and those around us. High internal self-awareness makes us happier and more satisfied. It also helps to combat anxiety, stress, and depression.

External self-awareness has more to do with being aware of how other people see us. This includes how others see our strengths, weaknesses, values, passions, and aspirations. When a person has high external self-awareness, they are better at seeing other people’s perspectives and showing empathy. This greatly increases their effectiveness in leadership positions because this practice strengthens relationships.

It’s easy to see how high self-awareness in both areas can benefit you in the workplace. When you are aware of your own values and passions you better align your skills with them. Additionally, the ability to see yourself accurately allows you to detect and fix mistakes more quickly. You can also maintain better working relationships with colleges through empathy.

The history of the Philippines is a rich and complex tapestry spanning thousands of years. It covers indigenous cultures that existed long before the arrival of the Spanish, the colonial era of Spanish rule, the American occupation, and the struggles for independence that followed. Understanding the history of the Philippines is essential to understanding the country's identity, culture, and current level of development.

Before the arrival of the Spanish in the 16th century, the Philippines was home to a variety of indigenous cultures, each with their own traditions and customs. These cultures were characterized by a strong sense of community, with people living in closely knit villages and practicing subsistence farming and fishing. The arrival of the Spanish in 1521 marked the beginning of a colonization that would last more than 300 years.

During the colonial era, the Philippines became an important trading post for the Spanish Empire, with Manila serving as a center of trade and commerce. The Spanish imposed their culture and religion on the Filipino people, converting many to Catholicism and adopting Spanish as the official language. The colonial era was also marked by a series of uprisings and rebellions as Filipinos struggled to regain their independence and resist Spanish rule.

In 1898, after the Spanish-American War, the Philippines became a colony of the United States. The American occupation of the Philippines brought a wave of modernization and development as Americans introduced new technology, improved infrastructure, and established a public education system. However, the American period was also one of conflict and opposition as Filipinos continued to fight for their independence.

In 1946, after years of struggle and resistance, the Philippines finally gained independence. The post-independence period was one of political instability and economic turmoil as the country struggled to form a stable government and build a strong economy. Today, the Philippines is a rapidly developing country with a growing economy and a diverse and vibrant culture.

In summary, Philippine history is a complex and fascinating subject spanning thousands of years. From the indigenous cultures that existed long before the arrival of the Spanish to the struggles for independence that followed, the history of the Philippines has played a crucial role in shaping the country's identity and culture. Understanding the history of the Philippines is essential to understanding the country's current level of development and its place in the global community.
