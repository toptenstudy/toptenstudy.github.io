9. give one example of each1. cosequentialism2. hedonism3. maximalism4. universalism​


Answer:

Consequentialism: A classic example of consequentialism is the "greatest happiness principle" put forward by the philosopher Jeremy Bentham, which holds that actions should be evaluated based on their ability to promote the greatest overall happiness for the greatest number of people.

Hedonism: An example of hedonism is the philosophy of Epicurus, who believed that the ultimate goal of life is to achieve pleasure and avoid pain.

Maximalism: An example of maximalism is the idea that one should always strive to achieve the greatest possible outcome or result, regardless of the cost or effort required.

Universalism: An example of universalism is the belief that all people will ultimately be saved, regardless of their actions or beliefs during their lifetime. This is often associated with religious views such as Unitarian Universalism

20. example of profit maximization and competitive based theory​


Answer:

Lower cost of producing the product

Explanation:

Profit maximization is basically about price setting. The company would determine or set the price for a product or service that will give them maximum profits. This strategy is to increase revenue. The company, at the same time, will also check on how to lower expenses to get maximum profits. Example, the company can lower cost of producing the product or lower fixed costs or lower taxes.

Competitive-based theory, on the other hand, is defined as company's effort to pursue policies that will create high quality products at high prices in the market. This is sometimes referred to as competitive advantage. The company develops an element or attribute that will make its products outperform the products of its competitors. Example: Jollibee, its competitive advantage is it being 'masa' and 'pinoy taste'.
