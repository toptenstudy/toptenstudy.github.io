4. 6.80 g of Sodium Chloride is added to 2750 mL of water. Find the mole fraction of the sodium chloride and of the water in the solution.​


Solution:

Step 1: Determine the mass of water.

[tex]mass_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = density_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} × volume_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}}[/tex]

[tex]mass_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = \text{1.00 g/mL × 2750 mL}[/tex]

[tex]mass_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = \text{2750 g}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the molar mass of each component.

For NaCl

molar mass = (22.99 g/mol × 1) + (35.45 g/mol × 1)

molar mass = 58.44 g/mol

For [tex]H_{2}O[/tex]

molar mass = (1.008 g/mol × 2) + (16.00 g/mol × 1)

molar mass = 18.016 g/mol

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of each component.

For NaCl

[tex]n_{\text{NaCl}} = \text{6.80 g} × \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{58.44 g}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{\text{NaCl}} = \text{0.116359 mol}[/tex]

For [tex]H_{2}O[/tex]

[tex]n_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = \text{2750 g} × \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{18.016 g}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = \text{152.642096 mol}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the mole fraction of NaCl and water.

For NaCl

[tex]X_{\text{NaCl}} = \frac{n_{\text{NaCl}}}{n_{\text{NaCl}} \: + \: n_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}}}[/tex]

[tex]X_{\text{NaCl}} = \frac{\text{0.116359 mol}}{\text{0.116359 mol + 152.642096 mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{X_{\text{NaCl}} = 0.000762}[/tex]

For [tex]H_{2}O[/tex]

[tex]X_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = \frac{n_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}}}{n_{\text{NaCl}} \: + \: n_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}}}[/tex]

[tex]X_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = \frac{\text{152.642096 mol}}{\text{0.116359 mol + 152.642096 mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{X_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = 0.999238}[/tex]

16. 12.Lance prepared a solution that contains 6% of sodium chloride. How will you describe the concentration of solution prepared by Lance? A. The solution contains 6% of sodium chloride and 94% of water.B. The solution contains 0.6 % of sodium chloride and 0.94 % of water. C. The solution contains 60 grams of sodium chloride and 40 grams of water. D. The solution contains 100 grams of sodium chloride and 100 grams of water.​


Concentration of Solution

Answer:

12. A. The solution contains 6% of sodium chloride and 94% of water.

Explanation:

A solution is a kind of homogeneous mixture. It is made up of a solute and solvent. The solute is the substance being dissolved and the substance that does the dissolving is the solvent. The concentration of solution varies on the amount of solute and solvent. Specifically, it is the ratio of solute in a given solution. This can be described as percent by mass or percent by volume. The combination of solute and solvent is equal to 100%

In a salt solution, the salt or sodium chloride is the solute and water is the solvent. Thus, if the sodium chloride is 6%, then the solvent is 94% to form 100% solution.

Among the given choices above the correct answer is letter D. Dissolving sodium chloride (table salt) with water produces a new substance .
