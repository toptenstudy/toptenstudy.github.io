13. Why Noli Me Tangere book by Jose Rizal is considered as art? Explain your answer


Answer:

Noli Me Tangere by Jose Rizal is considered art because it is an incredibly powerful work of literature that challenges the reader to think critically about the social and political issues of 19th century Philippines. The novel is a vivid depiction of the oppressive colonial rule of Spanish authorities in the country as well as the challenges faced by its people. Rizal’s use of symbolism and allegory to portray his message makes the novel an art form, as it requires the reader to dig deeper into the text to fully appreciate the work. In addition, the book has been praised for its strong characterization and its captivating narrative. All these elements make Noli Me Tangere a masterpiece of literature and an important art form.

29. what kind of book was Noli Me Tangere during those times?​


Answer:

oli Me Tángere (Latin for Touch me not) is a novel written by José Rizal, one of the national heroes of the Philippines, during the colonization of the country by Spain to describe perceived inequities of the Spanish Catholic priests and the ruling government.

Originally written in Spanish, the book is more commonly published and read in the Philippines in either Tagalog or English. Together with its sequel, El Filibusterismo, the reading of Noli is obligatory for high school students throughout the country. The two novels are widely considered as the national epic of the Philippines and are adapted in many forms such as operas, musicals, plays, and other forms of art.
