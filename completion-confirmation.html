30. 1. what is the connection of confirmation with the gifts of the holy spirit?2. what are the effects of confirmation?3. Confirmation facts:pa tolong ​


Explanation:

Confirmation is a sacrament in the Catholic Church in which a person receives the gifts of the Holy Spirit, including wisdom, understanding, counsel, fortitude, knowledge, piety, and fear of the Lord.

The effects of confirmation can include a deeper understanding of and commitment to one's faith, an increase in the ability to resist temptation and sin, and a strengthening of one's relationship with God.

Confirmation is typically administered by a bishop and is one of the three sacraments of initiation in the Catholic Church, along with baptism and Eucharist. It is usually received by Catholics in their teenage years. Confirmation marks a person as a fully initiated member of the Church and it strengthens the grace of baptism. It is often seen as the sacrament that marks a person as an adult in the church. The sacrament of Confirmation is typically celebrated with the anointing of the forehead with Chrism oil, which is blessed by the bishop, and the laying on of hands. Confirmands are also given a Confirmation name, which is often a saint’s name, as a way of asking that person’s intercession in their life.
