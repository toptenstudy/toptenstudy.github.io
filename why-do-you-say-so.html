State of Matter

1. Which box represents the particles in solid? Why do you say so?

The first jar, because the particles of solid are closely packed in an orderly manner. These particles are held together by very strong attractive forces.

2. Which box represents the particles in liquid? Why do you say so?

The second jar, because it's particles are loosely packed together. There are spaces between them because the forces of attraction that hold the particles together are weaker than those in a solid.

3. Which box represents the particles in gas? Why do you say so?

The third jar, the particles of a gas are far apart. This is due to the very weak force attraction between the particles.
