9. 2. What type of instruments are used to accompany their music? Are they Asiatic inst ruments (from South East Asia) or Western instruments? How do you say so?​


The type of instruments used to accompany music can vary greatly depending on the specific genre, cultural context, and geographic region. In Southeast Asia, for example, traditional music may be accompanied by a range of instruments, such as the gamelan in Indonesia, the khaen in Laos, or the piphat ensemble in Thailand.

In other parts of Asia, such as India, traditional music may be accompanied by instruments such as the sitar, tabla, or sarod. Meanwhile, in Western music, commonly used instruments include the piano, guitar, drums, and various orchestral instruments such as the violin, cello, and trumpet.

However, it's worth noting that there is also significant overlap between these categories, with many musical traditions borrowing or incorporating instruments and techniques from other cultures over time. Ultimately, the specific instruments used to accompany music depend on a variety of factors, and may vary widely even within a single culture or musical style.
