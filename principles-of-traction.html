2. Using traction in proportion


Answer:

Traction, or tractive force, is the force used to generate motion between a body and a tangential surface, through the use of dry friction, though the use of shear force of the surface is also commonly used.[1][2][3][4]

Traction can also refer to the maximum tractive force between a body and a surface, as limited by available friction; when this is the case, traction is often expressed as the ratio of the maximum tractive force to the normal force and is termed the coefficient of traction (similar to coefficient of friction). It is the force which makes an object move over the surface by overcoming all the resisting forces like friction, normal loads(load acting on the tiers in negative 'Z' axis), air resistance, rolling resistance, etc.

We can change improper fraction into mixed number by simply dividing the numerator into its denominator.

[tex] \frac{23}{4} = 23 \div 4 = 5 \: r. \: 3[/tex]

After we divided it the quotient will be the whole number and the remainder will be the new numerator and still copy the denominator.

From the fraction above we have 5 r. 3 as the answer therefore the mixed fraction is,

[tex]5 \frac{3}{4} [/tex]

Also we can change mixed fractions into improper fraction by simply multiplying the whole number by its denominator and add the product to the numerator.

From the fraction above we have;

[tex]5 \frac{3}{4} = 5 \times 4 = 20 + 3 = 23[/tex]The answer will be the numerator and still copy the denominator.

30. What is Traction in Science?​


Answer:

Traction forces act in directions parallel to contacting surfaces in rolling/sliding contacts, analogous to friction forces in pure sliding contacts. Traction is non-zero when contacting surface velocities are not equal, such as when small amounts of sliding are present in a rolling/sliding contact
