7. What would costumes be like in the time of Greek Dramas? Elizabeth Theater? Modern Times?


Answer:

GREEK DRAMAS - Costumes, along with masks and props, helped indicate the social status, gender, and age of a character. Athenian characters wore more elaborate, decorated versions of everyday clothing, such as a tunic or undergarment (chitôn or peplos), a cloak or over-garment (himation).

ELIZABETH THEATER - The dresses were full skirted and sleeved. The sleeves were worn apart and the ensemble accessorized with shoes, capes, hats and reticules. The men's costume at the Elizabethan theatre was a shirt, stockings, britches, doublet which was a well fitted coat, sleeves separate, same stiff high collar and an overcoat.

MODERN TIMES - Street style is a modern phenomenon. In essence the term refers to what regular, everyday people are wearing "on the streets," and thus reflects general trends that many people are wearing. ... Modern street style is about dressing yourself in your own unique style based loosely on common trends.
