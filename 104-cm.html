9. 500 students participated in a field demonstration Their height is normally distributed with an average of 110cm and a standard deviation of 6cm a What is the probability that a stupent has a height greater than 116 cm? b. b What is the probability that the height of a student is less than 104 cm? c What is the probability that a student will have a height between 100 cm and 115 cm?​


ANSWER

a. To find the probability that a student has a height greater than 116cm, we need to calculate the z-score and look up the corresponding probability in the standard normal table.

z-score = (x - μ) / σ

where x is the height, μ is the mean, and σ is the standard deviation.

z-score = (116 - 110) / 6 = 1.0

Looking up the probability corresponding to a z-score of 1.0 in the standard normal table, we get 0.1587.

Therefore, the probability that a student has a height greater than 116 cm is 0.1587.

b. To find the probability that the height of a student is less than 104 cm, we again need to calculate the z-score and look up the corresponding probability in the standard normal table.

z-score = (x - μ) / σ

z-score = (104 - 110) / 6 = -1.0

Looking up the probability corresponding to a z-score of -1.0 in the standard normal table, we get 0.1587.

However, since we want the probability that the height is less than 104 cm, we need to find the area to the left of -1.0 on the standard normal distribution. This is the same as finding the area to the right of 1.0, which is 1 - 0.1587 = 0.8413.

Therefore, the probability that the height of a student is less than 104 cm is 0.8413.

c. To find the probability that a student will have a height between 100 cm and 115 cm, we need to calculate the z-scores for both heights and find the area between those z-scores on the standard normal distribution.

z-score for 100 cm: (100 - 110) / 6 = -1.67

z-score for 115 cm: (115 - 110) / 6 = 0.83

Looking up the probabilities corresponding to these z-scores in the standard normal table, we get 0.0475 for -1.67 and 0.7967 for 0.83.

Therefore, the probability that a student will have a height between 100 cm and 115 cm is the difference between these two probabilities, which is 0.7967 - 0.0475 = 0.7492.

Therefore, the probability that a student will have a height between 100 cm and 115 cm is 0.7492.
