1. Characteristics sa ugoy ng duyan by lucio san pedro​


Answer:

"Sa Ugoy ng Duyan" (literally in Tagalog: "In the Rocking of the Cradle"; official English title: "The Sway of the Baby Hammock"[1]) is a Filipino lullaby. The music was composed by Lucio San Pedro while the lyrics was written by Levi Celerio.[1] Both of them were National Artists of the Philippines and this song was their most popular collaboration. Due to its popularity in the Philippines, it has been regarded by an entertainment writer in Philippine Entertainment Portal as familiar as the Philippine national anthem.[2] It has been interpreted and recorded by various Filipino artists.

"Sa Ugoy ng Duyan" is a lullaby that expresses a mother's love for her child. The song speaks of a child being cradled in a hammock or "duyan" as the mother sings to comfort and lull the child to sleep. The song's message is about the love and care that a mother gives to her child, ensuring their safety and comfort, even in the midst of the world's troubles and difficulties.

Tempo: The tempo of "Sa Ugoy ng Duyan" is slow and soothing, fitting for a lullaby.

Rhythm: The rhythm of the song is gentle and flowing, like the swaying of a hammock. It has a 3/4 time signature and is in a waltz-like rhythm.

Pitch: The pitch of the song is generally low and gentle, reflecting the calming mood of the lullaby.

Timbre: The timbre of the song is soft and gentle, emphasizing the soothing and comforting nature of the lyrics.

Dynamics: The dynamics of the song are mostly soft and quiet, with occasional crescendos in certain parts of the melody to add emphasis and emotion.

Melody: The melody of "Sa Ugoy ng Duyan" is simple and repetitive, with a gentle and soothing flow that complements the lyrics. The melody has a range of just a few notes, making it easy to sing and remember.
