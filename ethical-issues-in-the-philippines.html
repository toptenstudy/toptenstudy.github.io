15. alzheimer's ethical issues​


Answer:

Ethical and Care Issues

Individuals and families living with Alzheimer's will face many decisions throughout the course of the disease including decisions about care, treatment, participation in research, end-of-life issues, autonomy and safety. These statements were created to help persons with dementia and their families as they face these issues.

The following statements represent the Alzheimer's Association's positions around ethical, medical and care topics related to Alzheimer's disease and dementia. These statements are based on relevant research, best practice and expert opinion.

Dementia Related Behaviors (PDF)

Individuals living with dementia may experience behavioral and psychotic symptoms during the course of the disease due to the alteration in processing, integrating and retrieving new information. This statement addresses various interventions for dementia-related behaviors.

Diagnosis Disclosure (PDF)

This statement discusses telling the person with Alzheimer's about the diagnosis.

Driving and Dementia (PDF)

While a diagnosis of Alzheimer's does not mean a person needs to stop driving immediately, there will come a time when it is no longer safe to drive. This statement addresses the issues around driving including recommendations on planning ahead and steps to take when driving is no longer safe.

Electronic Tracking (PDF)

This statement provides information on the use of electronic tracking for individuals with Alzheimer's disease who are at risk for wandering.

End-of-Life Care (PDF)

The end or late stages of Alzheimer's disease can raise questions about the type of care offered to individuals in the final stages of a fatal disease. This statement provides information on the types of care available and stresses the importance of advance directives.

Feeding Issues in Advanced Dementia (PDF)

Individuals with advanced Alzheimer’s and other dementias commonly develop difficulty with eating and drinking, even with assistance, in the final stages of the disease process. This statement provides information on options for addressing nutrition at the end of life.

Genetic Testing (PDF)

This statement addresses the Association's position on genetic testing for Alzheimer's disease and provides information on the risks and benefits.

LGBT and Dementia (PDF)

This report developed by the Alzheimer’s Association and SAGE (Advocacy and Services for LGBT Elders) aims at increasing awareness around key intersections of Alzheimer’s disease, sexual orientation, and gender identification and expression so advocates and care providers can better meet the needs of LGBT elders and their caregivers facing dementia.

Placebo Control (PDF)

This statement reviews the use of placebos in clinical trials.

Protection of Participants in Research (PDF)

This statement reviews the risks and benefits to a surrogate enrolling an individual with Alzheimer's in a clinical trial.

Respect for Autonomy (PDF)

As cognitive abilities decline, respect for autonomy of the person with dementia can come in conflict with competence to make sound decisions and safety concerns. This statement addresses the ethical considerations of taking away a person's right to autonomous decision making.

Right to Treatment (PDF)

This statement addresses the rights of a person with dementia to not be denied medical treatment due to having dementia. It also addresses the right of individuals or their surrogates to refuse treatment.


16. Relate a story to the current trend of business in the Philippines regarding Ethical issues.​


Answer:

Once upon a time, there was a businessman in the Philippines named Juan. Juan owned a small manufacturing company that produced plastic toys for children. He was known for his cheap prices and quick turnaround times, which made him very popular among local retailers.

However, one day, a news report surfaced, exposing the unethical practices of Juan's company. The report showed that his company was dumping toxic waste into nearby rivers, which was causing harm to the environment and nearby communities.

Juan was shocked when he saw the report. He had always believed in the importance of being a responsible corporate citizen, but he had never paid much attention to the impact of his business on the environment.

After reading the report, Juan decided to take action. He immediately halted all production at his company and called for a meeting with his employees. At the meeting, Juan explained the situation and asked his employees for their thoughts on how to rectify the issue.

Together, Juan and his employees came up with a plan to clean up the waste and reduce the company's environmental impact. They installed waste management systems, hired an environmental consultant, and began using biodegradable materials in their products.

Juan's efforts did not go unnoticed. Customers and retailers alike praised his company for taking responsibility and making a positive change. As a result, Juan's business grew even more successful, as more and more people recognized his commitment to ethical business practices.

In the end, Juan learned that being a responsible corporate citizen wasn't just the right thing to do - it was also good for business. By taking action to address ethical issues, he was able to earn the trust and loyalty of his customers and contribute to the betterment of his community.

25. Cite some ethical issues in education in the Philippines? Explain​


Answer:

1. Conduct

Conduct is an important aspect of ethical practices. Conduct can include boundary violations, abuse or improper relationships. In the education context, teachers should have vision, hope and creativity. However, be cautious as sarcasm and cynicism tend to inhibit these qualities. Know also that cynicism and sarcasm are signs of anger or pain and they should be respectfully addressed so that possible deeper needs and concerns of instructors can be nurtured.

2. Assessment

Assessment of students is another “hot spot” of ethical practices. Teachers must be honest and promise this ethical habit. Falsification of data or information related to assessment violates every model of ethical conduct and law. While on the surface, in the moment, or initially such conduct may seem to benefit students or the school, it in fact injures not only both the student and school, but also the teacher and society as well.

3. Planning

Good planning is a part of the commitment that every teacher needs to make as a professional. Remember, teachers are committed to prudence. Prudence, for centuries meant the establishment of right goals and selecting the right means to achieve this goal. A prudent teacher creates the right goals and selects the best means to accomplish the goals.

Learning activities and lesson plans are developed in advance and the learning environment is created and assessed in a dynamic way so that it addresses the real needs of each student maximally, across all modes of learning.
