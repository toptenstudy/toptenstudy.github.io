30. Example of perfect competition in the philippines


The concept of "perfect competition" in economics is a hypothetical market system in which all suppliers are on an even footing and total supply and demand are in balance. Real markets typically fall somewhere between monopolies, which are the theoretical antithesis of perfect competition and where one manufacturer has entire control over a market, and perfect competition. Examples from everyday life that resemble the concept of the ideal competition include:

1. Farmers’ markets

The closest example of ideal competition in real life may be a typical farmers' market. Small producers charge relatively similar pricing for products that are almost equivalent. The market as a whole is unaffected by the entry and leave of some vendors, and prices and product details are transparent and largely consistent.

2. Emergent tech

Many times, much like the early online shops, there are no obvious competitive advantages, and other tech firms provide essentially the same services at comparable costs. Early social media companies are one example; other new businesses provided similar services for essentially the same cost.

3. Discount brands

In supermarkets, there could be less expensive variants of the same product, such as cereal produced by other brands. These alternatives will not differ greatly in either quality or cost, thus they mimic a market with ideal competition.
