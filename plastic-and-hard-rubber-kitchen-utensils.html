Further explanation:Materials of Kitchen Utensils and Equipment

Aluminum

Great for all applications. Most popular, lightest, most attractive, and cheapest. Maintenance is necessary to keep shining beautifully forever. Furthermore, it ensures uniform heat distribution regardless of the temperature of the heat. Available in sheet or cast aluminum. Being a soft metal, light gauges can easily dent or scratch, rendering your cookware unusable. When aluminum becomes a concentrated acid, vegetables brighten it up.

Stainless Steel

Most popular tool and equipment material, but more expensive. It's easy to clean and doesn't wear out or wear out as quickly as aluminum. Choose these copper, aluminum, or laminated steel bases to help distribute heat and prevent blackening from forming on your pan.

Glass

Good for baking, but not practical for top and surface cooking. Great care must be taken to ensure long shelf life.

Cast Iron

It's sturdy but needs seasoning to keep it from rusting. Salad oils without salt or shortening can be dried by rubbing them inside and out. Wash with soap before use.

Ceramic and Heat-Proof Glass

Especially used for casserole dishes, casseroles, and measuring cups. Glass and ceramics transfer heat slowly and evenly. this bakewareMany of this bakeware are decorated and can go from the stovetop or oven to the dinner table.

Teflon

It is a special coating applied to the inside of some aluminum or steel pots and pans. It prevents food from sticking to the pan. Easy to clean and maintain, but be careful not to scratch the coating with sharp instruments.

Plastic and Hard Rubber

Can be used for cutting and chopping boards, tabletops, bowls, trays, trash cans, and canisters. As a knife, it is less dull than metal and more hygienic than wood. →Plastic is very strong and cheap, but it may not last long.←

6. plastic and hard rubber explanation​


Answer:

Rubber is tough, holds shape once formed, even at high temperatures. It stretches, snaps back, has good tensile strength and is medically safe, soft, pliable, and pure. It is used to make automobile tires, rubber bands and baby bottle nipples. Plastic has no stretch, it is not pliable, is brittle, and cracks easily.
