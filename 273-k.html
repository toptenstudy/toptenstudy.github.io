12. A 20-milliliter sample of a gas is at 546 K and has a pressure of 6.0 atmospheres. If the temperature is changed to 273 K and the pressure to 2.0 atmospheres, the new volume of the gas (in Liters) will be


Given:

[tex]P_{1} = \text{6.0 atm}[/tex]

[tex]T_{1} = \text{546 K}[/tex]

[tex]V_{1} = \text{20 mL}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = \text{2.0 atm}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = \text{273 K}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]V_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\frac{P_{1}V_{1}}{T_{1}} = \frac{P_{2}V_{2}}{T_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = V_{1} × \frac{P_{1}}{P_{2}} × \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{20 mL} × \frac{\text{6.0 atm}}{\text{2.0 atm}} × \frac{\text{273 K}}{\text{546 K}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{V_{2} = \text{30 mL}}[/tex]

17. what is the total pressure exerted by a mixture of 2.00 g of H2(g) and 8.00 g of N2(g) at 273 K in a 10.0-L vessel?


Answer:

Use gas equation:

PV = nRT

calculate number of moles of gas

H2 : Molar mass H2 = 2 g/ mol Mol gas = 1 mol

N2 Molar mass = 28 g/mol : 8 g = 8 g/28 g/mol = 0.286 mol

You have total 1.286 mol gas

P * 10 L = 1.286 mol * 0.082057 * 273 K

P = 2.88 atm

20. The volume of a gas sample at 273 K is 2 L. At what temperature would the gas be to increase it's volume to 4 L? a. 712 Kb. 145 Kc. 215 Kd. 546 K​


Answer:

D. 546 K

Explanation:

At a constant temperature, the volume of a given amount of any gas is directly proportional to its absolutely temperature ( Charles' law)

Assuming that the pressure is constant, the volume of the gas is doubled ( increased from 2L to 4L), when the temperature is doubled, that is, increased from 273 K( or 0° C) to 546 K ( or 273°C)
