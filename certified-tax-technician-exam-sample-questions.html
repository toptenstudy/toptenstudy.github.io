10. B. Read the situation carefully and solve the problem. 8. The new COVID-19 testing facility in Tacloban City is operating with two laboratory technicians. Technician A takes 2 hours to finish 50 samples of specimens from COVID-19 patients. Technician B takes 3 hours to finish 45 samples of specimens from COVID-19 patients. Working together, how long should it take them to finish 150 samples of specimens from COVID-19 patients? Hint: Think about how many samples of specimens each technician can finish in one hour. This is their testing rate.​


3.75 hours

Step-by-step explanation:

First Step: What is the problem?

How long will it take the two technicians to finish 150 samples of specimen.

Second Step: What are the given?

Technician A can do 50 samples of specimen in 2 hours.

Technician B can do 45 samples of specimen in 3 hours.

The number of samples of specimen needed to be done is 150.

Third Step: What is the formula/formulas we can use to solve the problem?

First, we need to find the number of samples of specimen they can finish in 1 hour. To do that, we divide the number of samples of specimens by the time it takes them to finish. Once we do this, we will come up with 25 sample specimens for Technician A, and 15 sample specimens for Technician B.

\begin{gathered}Technician A = 50/2=25 \\Technician B = 45/3=15\end{gathered}

TechnicianA=50/2=25

TechnicianB=45/3=15

Next, we find out how long it will take them to work through 150 samples of specimen. Since they will be working together, we have to factor it in too. Let's say n is the number of hours it takes them to finish.

\begin{gathered}25n+15n=150\\40n=150\\n=150/40\\n=3.75\end{gathered}

25n+15n=150

40n=150

n=150/40

n=3.75

Last Step: The final answer.

According to our computations, if both technicians work together, they can finish 150 sample specimens in 3.75 hours.


11. a laboratory technician a drops a 0.0850 kg of sample unknown solid material at a temperature of 100.0 C


Answer:

1013.32 J/kg.K

Explanation:

The heat transferred by a changing in temperature without phase change can be calculated by:

Q = m*c*ΔT

Where m is the mass, c is the specific heat, and ΔT is the change in temperature (final - initial).

The values of c for water and copper can e found in thermodynamics tables:

cwater = 4.19x10³ J/kg.K

ccopper = 0.39x10³ J/kg.k

By the conservation of energy:

Qwater + Qcopper + Qmaterial = 0

0.200*4.19x10³*(26.1 - 19.0) + 0.150*0.39x10³*(26.1 - 19.0) + 0.085*c*(26.1 - 100) = 0

5949.8 + 415.35 - 6.2815c = 0

6.2815c = 6365.15

c = 1013.32 J/kg.K
