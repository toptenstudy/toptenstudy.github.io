6. In the given reaction below determine the following: BaSO4 + Al(OH)3 Al2(SO4)3 + Ba(OH)2 If 25 grams of BaSO4 reacts with 30 grams of Al(OH)3 a) Limiting reagent b) Excess Reagent c) Number of grams Al2(SO4)3 formed d) Number of grams excess reagent unused in the reaction e) % Yield if the actual yield is 21.50 grams Al2(SO4)3


Answer:

a) BaSO4 is the limiting reagent.

b) Al(OH)3 is the excess reagent.

c) mass Al2(SO4)3 = 12.22 g

d) mass Al(OH)3 unused = 24.43 g

e) percent yield = 94.11%

Explanation:

Writing the balanced chemical equation

3BaSO4 + 2Al(OH)3 → Al2(SO4)3 + 3Ba(OH)2

Calculating the number of moles of Al2(SO4)3 produced.

Starting with 25 g BaSO4

n Al2(SO4)3 = 25 g BaSO4 × (1 mol BaSO4 / 233.39 g BaSO4) × (1 mol Al2(SO4)3 / 3 mol BaSO4)

n Al2(SO4)3 = 0.035706 mol

Starting with 30 g Al(OH)3

n Al2(SO4)3 = 30 g Al(OH)3 × (1 mol Al(OH)3 / 78.003 g Al(OH)3) × (1 mol Al2(SO4)3 / 2 mol Al(OH)3)

n Al2(SO4)3 = 0.19230 mol

a) Limiting reagent

Since BaSO4 produces less number of moles of Al2(SO4)3,

BaSO4 is the limiting reagent.

b) Excess reagent

Since Al(OH)3 produces greater number of moles of Al2(SO4)3,

Al(OH)3 is the excess reagent.

c) Number of grams Al2(SO4)3 formed

Since BaSO4 is the limiting reagent,

mass Al2(SO4)3 = 0.035706 mol Al2(SO4)3 × (342.15 g Al2(SO4)3 / 1 mol Al2(SO4)3)

mass Al2(SO4)3 = 12.22 g

d) Number of grams excess reagent unused in the reaction

Calculating the mass of Al(OH)3 reacted

mass Al(OH)3 reacted = 0.035706 mol Al2(SO4)3 × (2 mol Al(OH)3 / 1 mol Al2(SO4)3) × (78.003 g Al(OH)3 / 1 mol Al(OH)3)

mass Al(OH)3 reacted = 5.57 g

Calculating the mass of excess reactant unused

mass Al(OH)3 unused = initial mass Al(OH)3 - mass Al(OH)3 reacted

mass Al(OH)3 unused = 30 g - 5.57 g

mass Al(OH)3 unused = 24.43 g

e) % Yield if the actual yield is 21.50 grams Al2(SO4)3

Note: The theoretical yield of the reaction is equal to the mass of Al2(SO4)3 formed, which is equal to 12.22 g

percent yield = (actual yield / theoretical yield) × 100

percent yield = (11.50 g / 12.22 g) × 100

percent yield = 94.11%

15. They are the reactants that are not used up when the reaction is finished? A. Excess Reagents B. Solute C. Limiting Reagents D. Solution


Answer:

A. Excess Reagents

Explanation:

The reactants that are not used up when the reaction is finished are called excess reactants or excess reagents. Excess reactants are the reactants that are present in a quantity greater than what is needed to completely react with the limiting reactant, and therefore remain unreacted when the reaction is completed.

The limiting reactant, on the other hand, is the reactant that is completely consumed in the reaction, limiting the amount of product that can be formed. Once the limiting reactant is consumed, any excess reactants that are present will remain unreacted and will not contribute to the formation of additional product.

It is important to identify the limiting reactant in a chemical reaction to accurately predict the amount of product that can be formed, and to minimize the waste of excess reactants.
