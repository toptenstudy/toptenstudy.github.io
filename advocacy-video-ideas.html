6. from the video of women empowerment advocacy, what are the different wrong connotation on being a women?pa help...​


Answer:

There are many negative connotations associated with being a woman, some of which include:

Weakness: Historically, women have been portrayed as physically and emotionally weak. This stereotype is often reinforced in media and popular culture, with women being shown as delicate and in need of protection.

Emotional: Women are often characterized as being overly emotional or hysterical. This stereotype is used to discount women's experiences and opinions and is often used to dismiss women's concerns or complaints.

Sexual objectification: Women are often portrayed as sexual objects in media and popular culture. This can lead to a culture where women are judged based on their physical appearance rather than their abilities or accomplishments.

Domesticity: Women are often expected to be the primary caregivers and homemakers in their families. This can lead to a belief that women are only suited for domestic work and not capable of taking on more challenging roles.

Inferiority: Women have historically been denied equal rights and opportunities, leading to a belief that women are inferior to men. This belief is often reinforced through sexist attitudes and discrimination.

25. what is your idea about advocacy and developmental communication.please answer ​


Answer:

Advocacy communication offers a critical approach within the field of development, working toward social justice.  Advocacy communication engages strategic intervention with clear political positions, having no pretense toward neutrality, and resisting hegemonic dominance in valuing social justice. What defines advocacy communications is that they focus tightly on influencing specific audiences and using specific messages in order to deliver changes in policy or practice.

Advocacy works primarily to change the behavior of public leaders or decision-makers. Communication generally targets individuals and small groups.

A communication plan for an advocacy campaign is different from any other information campaigns because it aims to go beyond raising awareness and provide information to trying to encourage people to take action and sometimes change their minds.

Development communication techniques include information dissemination and education, behavior change, social marketing, social mobilization, media advocacy, communication for social change, and community participation.
