2.) Who are the usual characters of myths?

- Usually gods, demigods or supernatural humans.

3.) What is the typical setting of myths?

- Setting is typically ancient, or prior to the time when actual records were kept. Myths are typically set in a world very similar to our own, but with supernatural monsters or areas.

4.) What are the uses of myths?

- They serve a more profound purpose in ancient and modern cultures.

5.) What is the usual plot of myths?

- The plot of a myth usually includes incredible or miraculous events, where characters behave in superhuman ways using unusual powers or with the help of superhuman beings.

8. facts of aetiological myths ang historical myths?​


Etiological Myths - Etiological myths are those myths that explain origins and causes. Creation myths are etiological, explaining how the universe or the world or life in the world came into being. Etiological myth does not have to situate itself at the beginning of all things, it can account for the creation of a new entity or activity within the established order of creation, just as much as for the creation of an ordered world out of primal chaos.

Historical Myths - Throughout history there have been famous and mysterious figures that have captivated their societies. Their appeal comes from the unknown, uncertain, or exaggerated information surrounding their lives, actions, and influences. These types of stories or information that change over time are called myths. Equality is a concept that has been touted for centuries, yet it remains a myth. Despite the progress that has been made in terms of equal rights and opportunities, there is still a long way to go.

Let's start by looking at the wage gap. Even in today's society, women are paid less than men for doing the same job. This is unacceptable and it's a clear indication that equality is still a myth.

Racism and discrimination are also major issues that continue to plague our society. People of color and those from marginalized communities are still facing discrimination and prejudice on a daily basis. This is not only morally wrong, but it also hinders the progress of our society as a whole.

It's also important to remember that equality is not just a problem for certain groups of people, it's a problem for all of us. We are all affected by inequality in some way, whether it's through discrimination, prejudice, or lack of representation.

So, what can we do to move closer to true equality? It starts with each one of us. We must challenge our own biases and prejudices, and work to create a more inclusive and equitable society. We must also hold our leaders and institutions accountable for promoting and protecting equality.

In conclusion, equality is a myth that we must strive to make a reality. Only by acknowledging and addressing the inequalities that exist, can we truly achieve a society where everyone is treated with dignity and respect.
