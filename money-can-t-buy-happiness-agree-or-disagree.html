20. agree or disagree those the love of money is against the first commandment of god? why?​


Answer:

Does the love for money violates the First Commandment of God?

To begin with, let us put on the table God's first commandment.

"I am the lord your God, you shall have no other God's beside me."

Explicitly, it says that God doesn't want us to worship other god's; He wants us to be loyal to Him.

Implicitly speaking however, God is telling us that He is God and all glory and power belongs to Him, thus we should love Him above all else.

Now, going back to our question, is the love money a violation of God's first commandment?

Well, logically speaking, the love of money may NOT go against the first commandment if your love for money doesn't exceed your love for people and your love for God.

But if your love for money has made you turn your back from God, compelled you to destroyed your relationship with your family, and greed has vanquished your heart, then there is no doubt that your love for money violates the first commandment of God.

Remember that it is God that we should worship and love. Money has no intrinsic value except if it is used for God's purpose.

26. Qualitative and quantitative can be mixed in a research design. Agree ordisagree? If agree, how? If disagree, explain why.​


I agree. Qualitative and quantitative data can be combined and integrated within one research design. The emergent methodology of research that is conducted to achieve this data combination is called mixed methods.

3 WAYS TO COMBINE QUANTITATIVE AND QUALITATIVE RESEARCH:

Explanatory Sequential Design. An explanatory sequential design emphasizes quantitative analysis, which we follow with interviews or observation (qualitative measures) to help explain the quant findings.Exploratory Sequential Design. An exploratory sequential design starts with the qualitative research and then uses insights gained to frame the design and analysis of the subsequent quantitative component.Convergent Parallel Design. If you collect qualitative data and quantitative data simultaneously and independently, and if you then analyze the results, you’re executing a convergent parallel design. You look to compare and contrast the results to look for patterns or contradictions.
