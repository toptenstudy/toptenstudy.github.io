1. Landbank of the Philippines:

- Pagpapahiram ng pondo at pagbibigay ng mga serbisyong pampinansyal sa mga magsasaka, mangingisda, at iba pang sektor ng agrikultura.

- Pagtitiyak ng pagsasakatuparan ng mga programa ng pamahalaan para sa pagpapaunlad ng agrikultura at kabuhayan ng mga magsasaka.

- Pagsasagawa ng mga proyekto at programa para sa pagpapalago ng imprastruktura at ekonomiya sa mga maliliit na bayan at komunidad.

- Pagpapahiram ng pondo at pagbibigay ng tulong pampinansyal sa mga sektor ng kalakalan at industriya upang mapalago ang mga negosyo at industriya sa bansa.

- Pagpapahiram ng mga pondo sa mga negosyante at mga indibidwal na nagpapatakbo ng mga negosyo na sumusunod sa mga alituntunin ng Shariah law.

- Pagbibigay ng mga serbisyong pangkabuhayan sa mga Muslim na komunidad sa bansa.

- Pagsasagawa ng mga proyekto at programa para sa pagpapaunlad ng mga sektor ng ekonomiya ng mga Muslim na komunidad sa bansa.

15. purpose of philippine amanah bank?​


Explanation:

The Philippine Amanah Bank (PAB) is a government-owned and controlled Islamic bank in the Philippines. The primary purpose of PAB is to provide financial services that comply with Islamic banking principles. The bank aims to provide accessible and affordable banking services to the Muslim community in the Philippines and promote financial inclusion in the country. Some of the services provided by PAB include:

1. Shariah-compliant financing: PAB offers various financing products that comply with Islamic banking principles, such as Murabahah, Ijarah, and Musharakah.

2. Deposits: PAB accepts various deposit products, including current accounts, savings accounts, and term deposits.

3. Remittance services: PAB offers remittance services to its customers, allowing them to transfer money to other parts of the Philippines or abroad.

4. Investment services: PAB provides investment services to its customers, including investment in Shariah-compliant stocks and sukuk.

Overall, the purpose of PAB is to provide Islamic banking services to the Muslim community in the Philippines while promoting financial inclusion and supporting economic development in the country.

22. Define and differentiate commercial banks from investment banks (mention their roles as "banks").​


Answer:

Explanation:

The term commercial bank refers to a financial institution that accepts deposits, offers checking account services, makes various loans, and offers basic financial products like certificates of deposit (CDs) and savings accounts to individuals and small businesses. while investment bank is a financial services company or corporate division that engages in advisory-based financial transactions on behalf of individuals, corporations, and governments. Traditionally associated with corporate finance, such a bank might assist in raising financial capital by underwriting or acting as the client's agent in the issuance of securities. An investment bank may also assist companies involved in mergers and acquisitions (M&A) and provide ancillary services such as market making, trading of derivatives and equity securities, and FICC services (fixed income instruments, currencies, and commodities).
