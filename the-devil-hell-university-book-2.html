19. his book heaven and hell gives a detailed description of the afterlife, how people live after the death of the physical body.


Answer:

Divine Comedy by Dante Alighieri

Explanation:

The Divine Comedy is an epic poem written by Dante Alighieri during his exile from Florence. He himself was the main character and he wrote the epic in the first person.

The epic poem is divided into three parts or cantiche: Inferno, Purgatorio, and Paradiso. You can probably already guess what these three parts are about.

In the first two parts, Dante narrated how he was guided by the dead Roman poet Virgil. He goes through Inferno (hell) and then Purgatorio (purgatory), where he sees the misery and suffering of many different people. These are not ordinary suffering but the torturous punishment that they deserved for their sins in life. Many of these people included prominent figures such as popes and political figures.

In the last part, Dante was no longer guided by Virgil. Instead, he was being guided by his true love Beatrice (who was already deceased in real life). Beatrice guides him to heaven until they reach Paradiso (paradise).
