3. what is Spe, Ape, Mpe, Dpe​


Answer:

Used in civil engineering.

Step-by-step explanation:

Spe, Ape, Mpe, and Dpe are acronyms used in the field of surveying and civil engineering to refer to different types of errors that can occur in measurements.

Spe stands for Systematic Error, which refers to an error that consistently occurs in the same direction and magnitude, often caused by faulty equipment or incorrect calibration.

Ape stands for Accidental or Random Error, which is an unpredictable error that occurs due to various factors such as human error, environmental conditions, or instrument limitations.

Mpe stands for Mistake or Blunder Error, which is a human error that results from a mistake or oversight in the measurement process, such as misreading a measurement or recording incorrect data.

Dpe stands for Drift Error, which is a gradual change or shift in the accuracy of a measuring instrument over time due to wear and tear, aging, or environmental factors.
