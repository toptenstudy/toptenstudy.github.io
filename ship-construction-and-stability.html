26. There are two ships that are sailing form General Santos City. Ship A is sailing east,and ship B is sailing 43° south of east. after an hour, ship A has travelled 115 km and ship B has travelled 98 km how far are the two ships? ​


Given:

θ = 43° south of east

OA = 115 km

OB = 98 km

time = 1 hour

Formula:

Use cosine law

[tex] {AB}^{2} = {OA}^{2} + {OB}^{2} - 2(OA)(OB) \cos(θ) [/tex]

Solution:

[tex] {AB}^{2} = {(115)}^{2} + {(98)}^{2} - 2(115)(98) \cos(43) [/tex]

[tex] {AB}^{2} = 6344.29[/tex]

[tex]AB = \sqrt{6344.29} [/tex]

Final answer:

[tex]AB = 79.65 \: km[/tex]

28. Two ships travelling from Dumaguete to Cagayan de Oro differ in average speed by 10 kph. The slower ship takes 3 hours longer to travel a 240 kilometre route than for the faster ship to travel a 200 kilometre route.Find the speed of the slower ship. Hint: Construct an equation with time t as the variable.​


[tex] \Large \mathbb{SOLUTION:} [/tex]

[tex] \begin{array}{l} \text{Let }t\text{ be the time (in hours) required for the} \\ \text{faster ship to reach its destination.} \\ \\ \text{We know that }\bold{Speed} = \dfrac{\bold{Distance}}{\bold{Time}}. \end{array} [/tex]

[tex] \quad \quad \begin{array}{| l | c|} \hline \: & \text{Speed in terms of }t \\ \hline \text{Slower Ship} & \underset{\:}{\overset{\:}{\dfrac{240}{t + 3}}}\text{ kph} \\ \hline \text{Faster Ship} & \underset{\:}{\overset{\:}{\dfrac{200}{t}}}\text{ kph} \\ \hline \end{array} [/tex]

[tex] \begin{array}{l} \text{Given that the ships differ in average speed}\\ \text{by } 10\text{ kph, then} \\ \\ \quad \quad \quad \quad\quad \dfrac{200}{t} - \dfrac{240}{t + 3} = 10 \\ \\ \text{Solving for t,} \end{array} [/tex]

[tex] \quad \quad \begin{array}{c} t(t + 3)\left(\dfrac{200}{t} - \dfrac{240}{t + 3}\right) = 10t(t + 3) \\ \\ 200(t + 3) - 240t = 10t^2 + 30t \\ \\ 200t + 600 - 240t = 10t^2 + 30t \\ \\ 600 - 40t = 10t^2 + 30t \\ \\ 0 = 10t^2 + 70t - 600 \\ \\ t^2 + 7t - 60 = 0 \\ \\ (t + 12)(t - 5) = 0 \\ \\ t = \{-12, 5\} \end{array} [/tex]

[tex] \begin{array}{l} \text{We want the positive value of }t, \text{ so }t= 5\text{ hrs.} \\ \\ \text{It follows that } \\ \\ \quad \quad {\begin{array}{| l | c|} \hline \: & \text{Speed} \\ \hline \text{Slower Ship} & \underset{\:}{\overset{\:}{\dfrac{240}{5 + 3} = 30\text{ kph}}} \\ \hline \text{Faster Ship} & \underset{\:}{\overset{\:}{\dfrac{200}{5} = 40\text{ kph}}} \\ \hline \end{array}} \end{array} [/tex]
