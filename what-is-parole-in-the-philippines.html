14. what is termination of parole supervision?​


Termination of parole supervision refers to the process of ending the supervision of an individual who was released from prison on parole. When someone is granted parole, they are required to follow specific conditions, such as regularly reporting to a parole officer, refraining from criminal activity, and attending rehabilitation programs.

Once an individual successfully completes their parole period without any violations, their parole officer may recommend to the parole board that the individual's parole be terminated. The parole board will review the case and decide whether to grant the termination of parole supervision.

If the termination is granted, the individual will no longer be required to report to their parole officer or follow any conditions of parole. However, they may still be subject to other legal obligations, such as paying restitution or attending court-ordered classes.

22. What are the similarities and differences of parol and higantes?​


Answer:

A parol (pronounced [paˈɾol], US: /pɑːˈroʊl/ (About this soundlisten), also written as paról or parul) is a Filipino ornamental lantern displayed during the Christmas season. Parols are traditionally constructed using bamboo and Japanese paper, and are illuminated with candles, oil lamps, or carbide lamps. Modern parols can be made using other materials such as plastic, metal, and capiz shells and are usually illuminated with electric lighting. Its most-common form is a five-pointed star, although it can come in various shapes and sizes.[1][2][3] Large disc-shaped electronic versions of parols produced in Pampanga are known as parul sampernandu.[4]

The parol is a traditional part of the Panunulúyan pageant in the nine-day Christmas Novena procession during the Spanish colonial period of the Philippines. It was initially rectangular or oblong in shape and was made with white paper, but eventually came to be made in various shapes and colors. It became standardized to a five-pointed star (symbolizing the Star of Bethlehem) during the American colonial period.[5] It remains an iconic symbol of Filipino Christmas.

The Higantes Festival is a local festival held annually In Angono, Philippines, where hundreds of giant papier-mâché puppets are paraded, representing the common people's mockery of the bad hacienda (land) owners of the past during Spanish colonial rule. It has evolved into also celebrating the feast of Pope St. Clement I, every 22 and 23 November.

In other words, parol evidence is any evidence that is offered to interpret or supplement a written contract, rather than to contradict it. The admissibility and relevance of parol evidence may be subject to legal rules and restrictions, depending on the jurisdiction and the circumstances of the case.
