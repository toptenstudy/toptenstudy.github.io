The Ways Science Affects Culture

Science is a result of systematic efforts with the scientific method in the development and organization of knowledge. Science is proven by proven explanations and predictions as human understanding of the universe and human life on Earth.

The word science itself comes from the Latin scientica which means knowledge. By definition, science is a way to study certain aspects of nature in a systematic, organized, and through various scientific methods.

While culture is the overall attitude and pattern of behavior of a person or group as well as knowledge which is a habit that is inherited from their ancestors and is owned by a member of a particular society. Culture affects many aspects of life, such as religion, customs, politics, language, clothing, buildings, and others.

Science and culture have a relationship with each other. Science is a science or a scientific fact that comes from repeated experiments or tests and is born with a specific purpose in an aspect of life. The scientific system or knowledge system is never separated from the cultural context or in other words the culture of knowledge.

According to Timothy Lenoir, science is knowledge resulting from the interpretation of an object of knowledge and an observer (interpreter) who does not stand apart from one another.

Interpretation is a cultural practice that involves cognitive factors (which are related to mentality) and social factors that are interconnected with each other. These two factors are always attached to the actors of knowledge production (scientists).

Therefore, it can be concluded from Lenior's argument that the understanding of science as a cultural practice is not local, partial, and based on individual or group interests.

Science influences culture based on the results of experiments conducted by scientists on objects in all aspects of life, so this affects the values ​​that apply to society. These values ​​are basically present in various ways, and creativity is the basic manifestation of these values.
