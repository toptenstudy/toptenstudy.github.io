6. what is a summarywhat is a summary ​


Answer:

A summary is a condensed explanation of an event, story, document, etc.. i.e. "summing up the facts." They are not constrained to any medium or topics. Not to be confused with abstract, which is a summary of a document. There are many different levels of summarization that can be done. The summarizer may choose any length, but the sumarees may not appreciate anything more than absolutely necessary to get the required facts.

Summaries help to spread information fast. They also help people make decisions on if it is something worth looking further into.

EXAMPLES: quick "good/bad" can suffice in some situations. In other situations, the summary may rival the length of the original."This book was really cool. This guy drives a pickup to work everyday, and has a dangerous job."

School is an educational institution where students go to learn and acquire knowledge and skills. Schools provide formal education to students and are usually organized into different levels, such as primary, secondary, and tertiary education. In schools, students are taught by teachers who use different teaching methods and strategies to facilitate learning. The Philippines is a country located in Southeast Asia. It is an archipelago consisting of more than 7,000 islands. The Philippines has a diverse culture influenced by its history of colonization by Spain, the United States, and Japan. The country has a population of over 100 million people and is known for its beautiful beaches, rich biodiversity, and delicious cuisine. The Philippines is also home to many historical and cultural landmarks, such as the UNESCO World Heritage Site of the Rice Terraces of the Philippine Cordilleras.

Summary of Elephants:

Elephants are large mammals that are native to Africa and Asia. They are known for their distinctive long trunks, large ears, and tusks. Elephants are social animals that live in herds led by a matriarch. They are herbivores and can consume up to 300 pounds of vegetation in a single day. Elephants are also intelligent animals and have been observed exhibiting behaviors such as empathy and self-awareness. Unfortunately, elephants are also threatened by habitat loss and poaching for their ivory tusks. Conservation efforts are underway to protect elephant populations and their habitats.
