13. What are the legal basis for the existence of these agencies.​


Answer

The National Economic and Development Authority (NEDA) serves as the independent planning agency of the government as provided under Section 9, Article XII of the Philippine Constitution. NEDA was further reorganized structurally and functionally in accordance with the provisions of Executive Order No. 230, s. 1987 to enhance its ability to coordinate the development planning and policy formulation process in order to achieve the objectives of sustainable economic growth coupled with an equitable distribution of income and wealth.

The Authority consists of two separate and distinct entities, namely: the Board and the Secretariat . The NEDA Board is composed of the President as Chairman, the NEDA Director-General as Vice Chairman, Executive Secretary and Secretaries of Finance, Trade and Industry, Agriculture, Environment and Natural Resources, Public works and Highways, Budget and Management, Labor and Employment, Interior and Local Government, Health, Foreign Affairs, Agrarian Reform Science and Technology Transportation and Communications, Energy and the Governor of the Bangko Sentral Ng Pilipinas as members. The NEDA Secretariat headed by a Director-General who carries the rank and title of Secretary of Socio-Economic Planning and who exercises general supervision and control over the technical and administrative personnel of the Secretariat serves as the research and technical support arm and the secretariat of the NEDA Board. It also provides technical staff support and assistance, including the conduct of studies and development of policy measures and other recommendations on the various aspects of the substantive functions of development planning and policy formulation, coordination, evaluation and monitoring of plan implementation.

19. what are the legal basis of human rights


Answer:

The idea of human rights did not exist in ancient societies based on slavery and feudal societies in the Middle Ages, when people were not treated as human beings.

This idea only started to take shape when the feudal system began to collapse, the existence of "humanity" was discovered during the Renaissance, and capitalism began to develop.

Only after the British philosopher of liberalism John Locke and the French philosopher of naturalism Jean-Jacques Rousseau theorized the rights of freedom and equality in the 17th century were human beings deemed entitled to "human rights" as soon as they are born.
