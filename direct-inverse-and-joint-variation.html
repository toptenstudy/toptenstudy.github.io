1. 1. How are direct, inverse, joint and combined variations differ from eachother?Direct Variation -Inverse Variation -Joint Variation -Combined Variation -​


MATH VARIATIONS==============================DIRECT VARIATION

- A variation where the variable closely related to each other. When the variable increases, the other variable also increases. If one decreases, the other also decreases. This is also called Proportional.

[tex] \large \implies \huge \tt \red{y = kx}[/tex]

[tex] \: [/tex]

INVERSE VARIATION

- A variation opposite to Direct Variation. When the variable increases, the other variable decreases and vise versa.

[tex] \large \implies \huge \tt \red{y = \frac{k}{x} } \\ [/tex]

[tex] \: [/tex]

JOINT VARIATION

- A variation just like Direct Variation but has two or more variables to be variated. Like the variable is jointly variated as two or more quantities.

[tex] \large \implies \huge \tt \red{y = kxz}[/tex]

[tex] \: [/tex]

COMBINED VARIATION

- A type of variation where Direct, Inverse, or Joint Variation are Combined. This refers to a variable is Directly or Jointly variated and inversely to other quantities.

[tex] \large \implies \huge \tt \red{y = \frac{kx}{z}}[/tex]

[tex] \large \implies \huge \tt \red{y = \frac{kwx}{z}}[/tex]

3. define Variation- Direct Variation- Direct Power variation- Inverse variation- Joint variation-​


Answer:

Variation - a change in the form, position, condition, or amount of something. : something that is similar to something else but different in some way. : a repeated version of a short piece of music with changes in its rhythm, tune, or harmony.

Direct Variation - mathematical relationship between two variables that can be expressed by an equation in which one variable is equal to a constant times the other. An equation or function expressing direct variation compare inverse variation.

Direct Power Variation - The statement "y varies directly as x," means that when x increases, y increases by the same factor.

Inverse Variation - mathematical relationship between two variables which can be expressed by an equation in which the product of two variables is equal to a constant. An equation or function expressing inverse variation compare direct variation.

Joint Variation - a situation where one variable depends on two (or more) other variables, and varies directly as each of them when the others are held constant. We say z varies jointly as x and y if. z=kxy.

12. What is the difference between Direct, Inverse, and Joint Variation?


Answer:

Joint variation occurs when a variable varies directly or inversely with multiple variables. For instance, if x varies directly with both y and z , we have x=kyz x = k y z . If x varies directly with y and inversely with z , we have x=kyz x = k y z .
