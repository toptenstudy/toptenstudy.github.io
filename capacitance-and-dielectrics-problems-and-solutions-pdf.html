13. In this item, a sample problem is given for you to examine. After a deep examination on the given sample, you are to describe what happens to the capacitance, charge, potential difference, and electric field of a parallel-plate capacitor when a dielectric is inserted between the two plates.​


Answer:

Sample Problem: A parallel-plate capacitor with a capacitance of 10 μF is connected to a battery and charged to a potential difference of 100 V. If a dielectric material with a dielectric constant of 5 is inserted between the plates, what happens to the capacitance, charge, potential difference, and electric field of the capacitor?

Solution:

The capacitance of a parallel-plate capacitor is given by C = εA/d, where ε is the permittivity of free space, A is the area of the plates, and d is the distance between them.

a) Capacitance: When a dielectric is inserted between the plates, the capacitance of the capacitor increases. The presence of the dielectric material increases the capacitance by a factor of the dielectric constant κ (κ = 5 in this case). Thus, the new capacitance of the capacitor is 10 μF x 5 = 50 μF.

b) Charge: The charge on the capacitor plates remains the same. The charge on the capacitor is given by Q = CV, where V is the potential difference across the plates. Since the potential difference remains constant, the charge on the plates also remains constant.

c) Potential difference: When a dielectric is inserted between the plates, the potential difference across the capacitor decreases. This is because the electric field inside the dielectric material is reduced by a factor of κ. Therefore, the new potential difference across the capacitor is 100 V/5 = 20 V.

d) Electric field: The electric field between the plates is reduced when a dielectric is inserted between them. This is because the electric field inside the dielectric material is reduced by a factor of κ. The electric field is given by E = V/d, where d is the distance between the plates. Thus, the new electric field between the plates is (20 V)/(d), where d is the distance between the plates.

28. 1. What is the relationship between capacitance and the area of the plates? 2. What is the relationship between capacitance and the separation distance between the plates? 3. What is the relationship between capacitance and dielectric material? 4. What is the importance of capacitors in electric devices? 5. Alice is a professor of Physics from Baguio, she was teaching and demonstrating the use of capacitors when suddenly the capacitor exploded. Why do capacitors explode?


Answer:

1. Capacitance is directly proportional to the area of the plates. This means that as the area of the plates increases, the capacitance also increases.

2. Capacitance is inversely proportional to the separation distance between the plates. This means that as the separation distance between the plates decreases, the capacitance increases.

3. Capacitance is directly proportional to the dielectric constant of the material between the plates. This means that as the dielectric constant of the material between the plates increases, the capacitance also increases.

4. Capacitors are important in electric devices because they store and release electrical energy on demand. They can be used in filtering, voltage regulation, timing circuits, and many other applications.

5. Capacitors can explode if they are exposed to excessive voltage, excessive current, or if they are damaged internally or externally. This can cause the release of gases, heat, and sometimes flames. It is important to handle capacitors with care and to follow proper safety guidelines when working with them.
