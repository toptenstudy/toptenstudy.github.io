30. : Discuss what you have learned from the various disciplinary perspectives of the Self. choose only 1 Philosophical Sociological Anthropological Eastern and Western cultures essay essay pls>⁠.⁠<​


Answer:

From a philosophical perspective, the self is seen as a complex and subjective concept that is composed of different parts. Philosophers such as Descartes, Hume and Kant have argued that there is a fundamental essence of the self that is both unique and immutable. This essence is often seen as being composed of the mind and body, and is thought to be the basis of our sense of identity. Philosophers have also discussed the relationship between the self and the external world, arguing that our sense of self is shaped by our experiences and interactions with others. The self is also seen as being composed of our beliefs, values and desires, which can be seen as the source of our autonomy and free will. Finally, philosophical theories of the self address the concept of self-knowledge, arguing that we can only truly understand ourselves through introspection and reflection.
