2. Who invented the flu vaccine


In 1933, researchers discovered that viruses (influenza virus types A, B, and rarely C) cause influenza (flu). Prior to 1933, a bacterium named Haemophilus influenzae was thought to cause the flu. In 1938, Jonas Salk and Thomas Francis developed the first vaccine against flu viruses.

11. What are the side effects of the flu vaccine?Does it contain weakened viruses?​


Answer:

The nasal spray flu vaccine contains weakened live flu viruses. So it may cause mild flu-like symptoms, including runny nose, headache, vomiting, muscle aches, and fever. Very rarely, the flu vaccine can cause a severe allergic reaction.

Who is Jean Watson? What is Watson's Theory in Nursing?​

Jean Watson was born on June 10, 1940. She is an American nurse theorist and nursing professor who is best known for her book "Transpersonal Caring - The Philosophy and Science of Caring," as well as the theory of Human Caring. Caring is what distinguishes nursing. Nursing at Redlands Community Hospital has incorporated Jean Watson's Caring Science approach. Caring Science assists us in embracing the wonderful energy that comes from a well-balanced mind, body, and spirit, and it benefits both the patient and the nurse in the long run. Nurses are uniquely positioned to be the heart of healing, according to Florence Nightingale's idea that the "job of a nurse is to place her patient in the greatest situation to be able to self-heal." The nurse is able to maximize her patient's potential to heal from within by actively participating in caring via true presence and intentionality. In an overstressed and pressured profession, how can we as nurses preserve emotional sensitivity and caring attitudes? Caring, according to Jean Watson, re-energizes our lives and enhances our skills. The advantages are immense, and they foster personal and professional self-actualization. Both the patient and the nurse, and among the other members of the health team, caring is a mutually rewarding experience. It's also worth remembering that Watson emphasizes the need of taking care of ourselves before we can take care of others; self-healing is a critical step in recharging our spiritual bank and renewing our energy reserves. Make a difference by being the one who makes a difference. It all comes down to what you say and how you say and do it. Our humanity is protected and affirmed by caring. You may live more truly in your relationships if you reveal your actual ideas, feelings, and attitudes. Patients and customers are happier when they are treated with compassion. It's contagious, and it makes people feel good about themselves. It evokes a sense of awe and intuition. It's uplifting and inspiring. 22. why is flu vaccine should not be mandatory for healthcare providers​


Answer:

CDC, the Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices (ACIP), and the Healthcare Infection Control Practices Advisory Committee (HICPAC) recommend that all U.S. health care workers get vaccinated annually against influenza.

Health care workers include (but are not limited to) physicians, nurses, nursing assistants, therapists, technicians, emergency medical service personnel, dental personnel, pharmacists, laboratory personnel, autopsy personnel, students and trainees, contractual staff not employed by the health-care facility, and persons (e.g., clerical, dietary, housekeeping, laundry, security, maintenance, administrative, billing, and volunteers) not directly involved in patient care but potentially exposed to infectious agents that can be transmitted to and from health care workers and patients.
