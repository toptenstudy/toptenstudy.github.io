4. Methane (CH) is the principal component of natural gas. How many moles of CH4 are present in 7.51 g of CH4?​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of methane (CH₄).

The molar masses of C and H are 12.01 g and 1.008 g, respectively.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{molar mass} & = \text{12.01 g + 4(1.008 g)} \\ & = \text{12.01 g + 4.032 g} \\ & = \text{16.04 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of methane by using dimensional analysis.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of} \: \text{CH}_4 & = \text{7.51 g} \: \text{CH}_4 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{CH}_4}{\text{16.04 g} \: \text{CH}_4} \\ & = \boxed{\text{0.468 mol}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, 0.468 mol of CH₄ are present in 7.51 g of CH₄.

26. Which is not one of the principal elements in sternberg's triarchic theory of intelligence? a. metacomponents b. performance components c. social components d. knowledge-acquisition components


(C). Social components is not one of the principal elements according to the stenbergs triarchic theory of intelligence.

Explanation :

Sternberg's triarchic theory of intelligence is based on this one conception that traditional and hierarchical models of intelligence are not exhaustive because they do not take into account the uses that make intelligence in itself, limiting themselves to conceptualizing its properties and functioning regardless of how it is related to and applied in real context.

Thus, this theory assumes that the measurement of intellectual capacity focuses only on one aspect of intelligence, ignoring other aspects of great relevance that make up cognitive skills themselves. In conclusion. Sternberg stated that it is not enough to look at what is done, but also how and why, when it is acted on.

For Sternberg, intelligence is all the mental activity that guides conscious adaptation to the environment and to this selection or transformation with the aim of predicting outcomes and being able to actively provoke one-to-medium or intermediate-to-one adaptation. It is a set of thinking skills used in the resolution of more or less everyday or abstract problems.

There are several principal elements in the Stenberg in his theory, such as metacomponents, performance components and knowledge acquisition components. But social components is not one of the principal elements in this theory.
