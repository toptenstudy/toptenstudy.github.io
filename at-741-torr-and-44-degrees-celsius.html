1. At 741 torr and 44°C, 7.10 g of a gas occupy a volume of 5.40 L. What is the molar mass of the gas?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P = \text{741 torr = 0.975 atm} \\ & V = \text{5.40 L} \\ & T = 44^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{317.15 K} \\ & mass = \text{7.10 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of gas using ideal gas equation.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n & = \frac{PV}{\text{RT}} \\ & = \frac{(\text{0.975 atm})(\text{5.40 L})}{\left(0.082057 \: \dfrac{\text{L}\cdot\text{atm}}{\text{mol}\cdot\text{K}}\right)(\text{317.15 K})} \\ & = \text{0.20231 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the molar mass of the gas.

[tex]\begin{aligned} MM & = \frac{mass}{n} \\ & = \frac{\text{7.10 g}}{\text{0.20231 mol}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{35.1 g/mol}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molar mass of the gas is 35.1 g/mol.

3. A gas occupies 250 mL at 680 torr and 30 degree celsius. When the pressure is changed to 460 torr, what temperature (in degree celsius) is needed to maintain the same volume?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P_1 & = \text{680 torr} \\ T_1 & = 30^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{303 K} \\ P_2 & = \text{460 torr} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final temperature (in kelvin) by using Gay-Lussac's law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} T_2 & = \frac{T_1P_2}{P_1} \\ & = \frac{(\text{303 K})(\text{460 torr})}{\text{680 torr}} \\ & = \text{205 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Convert the final temperature from kelvin (K) to degree Celsius (°C).

[tex]\begin{aligned} t_2 & = T_2 - 273 \\ & = 205 - 273 \\ & = \boxed{-68^{\circ}\text{C}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the temperature needed to maintain the same volume is -68°C

5. If a sample of a gas occupies 6.72 L at STP, what volume will occupy at 25 degrees Celsius and 800 Torr?


Answer:

V2 = 6.97 L

Explanation:

Convert °C to K.

T = 25°C + 273.15 = 298.15 K

Use Combined Gas Law to solve for the final volume.

P2V2 / T2 = P1V1 / T1

V2 = V1 • (P1 / P2) • (T2 / T1)

V2 = 6.72 L • (760 to rr / 800 to rr) • (298.15 K / 273.15 K)

V2 = 6.97 L

8. a 2450 mL sample of gas at 285 degrees celsius and 620 torr is compressed to a volume of 850mL at 150 degrees celsius. What is the new pressure of the gas in atm?


✏️COMBINED GAS LAW

[tex]\tt{\huge{\green{Solution:}}}[/tex]

The problem asks to solve for the final pressure of the gas. First, we need to identify the quantities to be used. These are

P₁ = initial pressure

P₂ = final pressure

V₁ = initial volume

V₂ = final volume

T₁ = initial temperature (in kelvin)

T₂ = final temperature (in kelvin)

Second, we need to identify the given values. These are

P₁ = 620 torr × (1 atm / 760 torr) = 0.816 atm

V₁ = 2,450 mL

V₂ = 850 mL

T₁ = 285°C + 273 = 558 K

T₂ = 150°C + 273 = 423 K

Finally, we can now solve for the final pressure of the gas. Therefore, the final pressure (in atm) is

[tex]P_{2} = \dfrac{P_{1}V_{1}T_{2}}{V_{2}T_{1}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = \dfrac{(\text{0.816 atm})(\text{2,450 mL})(\text{423 K})}{(\text{850 mL})(\text{558 K})}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P_{2} = \text{1.78 atm}}[/tex]

For this problem, we are going to use one of the formulas in the gas law: Gay-Lussac's Law. This law states that the temperature is directly proportional to the pressure. The formula is [tex]\frac{P_{1} }{P_{2} } = \frac{T_{1} }{T_{2} }[/tex].

Where:

[tex]P_{1}[/tex] is the initial pressure

[tex]P_{2}[/tex] is the final pressure

[tex]T_{1}[/tex] is the initial temperature

[tex]T_{2}[/tex] is the final temperature

Solving:

Note: Convert Celsius to Rankine.

[tex]T_{2} = \frac{T_{1}P_{2}}{P_{1} }[/tex] =[tex]\frac{491.67 x 1500}{250}[/tex]= 2950.02 degrees Rankine
