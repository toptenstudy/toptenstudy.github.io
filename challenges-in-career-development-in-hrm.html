6. Differentiate Global HRM and Domestic HRM.​


Answer:

IHRM is very complicated as it is affected heavily by external factors such as cultural differences and institutional factors. Domestic HRM is concerned with managing limited number of HRM activities at national level. ... International HRM requires greater involvement in the personal life of employees

Explanation:

In an organization, if the HRM is concerned only with the interests of domestic employees, it can be termed as domestic HRM or simply HRM. On the other hand, if the HRM takes care of interests of global employees along with domestic employees, it is termed as International Human Resources Management (IHRM).

11. What do you think is the effect of taking an HRM subject in your future career a HR Manager?​


HRM subjects can have significant effects on the future career of an individual aspiring to become an HR manager. Such subjects provide essential knowledge and skills that an HR manager needs to perform their duties effectively. Some potential effects of taking HRM subjects include:

1. Improved understanding of human resource management principles and practices: HRM subjects cover essential topics like recruitment, selection, training, compensation, employee relations, legal compliance, and organizational development. By studying these subjects, potential HR managers acquire a solid foundation on which they can build their career.

2. Enhanced problem-solving and decision-making abilities: HR managers must deal with complex workplace issues, including employee conflicts, performance management, and grievances. HRM subjects teach students how to analyze situations, identify potential solutions, and make informed decisions.

3. Better communication and interpersonal skills: HR managers must interact with employees, management, and external stakeholders. HRM subjects teach students how to communicate effectively, resolve conflicts, build relationships, and negotiate.

4. Increased job prospects and career advancement opportunities: Employers prefer candidates who have relevant education and training. HRM subjects provide potential HR managers with the knowledge and skills that can increase their job prospects and career advancement opportunities.

In summary, taking HRM subjects can have numerous positive effects on the future career of an individual aspiring to become an HR manager. They can acquire new knowledge, skills, and competencies that can improve their job performance, increase their employability, and advance their career prospects.

Taking an HRM (Human Resource Management) subject can have a positive impact on your future career as an HR Manager. HRM subjects teach students the knowledge and skills needed to manage an organization's workforce effectively. It covers areas such as recruitment and selection, training and development, performance management, compensation and benefits, and employee relations.

By studying HRM, you can gain a deeper understanding of the importance of human resources to an organization and how to manage them strategically. This knowledge can help you to make informed decisions and develop effective policies and practices that can improve employee morale, increase productivity, and achieve organizational goals.

Moreover, having an HRM degree or certification can make you a more competitive candidate in the job market. Employers prefer candidates with relevant qualifications, and an HRM degree can demonstrate your commitment to the field and your readiness to take on the responsibilities of an HR Manager.

In summary, taking an HRM subject can be beneficial for your future career as an HR Manager, as it can provide you with the necessary knowledge, skills, and qualifications to succeed in this role.

25. WHAT DO YOU THINK IS THE EFFECT OF TAKING AN HRM SUBJECT IN YOUR FUTURE CAREER AS HR MANAGER?​


Taking an HRM (Human Resource Management) subject can provide you with a strong foundation in the key principles and practices of managing people in an organizational setting. HRM covers a broad range of topics such as recruitment and selection, employee training and development, performance management, compensation and benefits, labor relations, and strategic human resource management.

By studying HRM, you can develop a deep understanding of the various functions of HR, which can help you perform effectively in your role as an HR manager. You can learn how to manage employee relations, maintain a positive workplace culture, and improve employee performance, which are critical components of HR management.

In addition, taking an HRM subject can also provide you with practical skills, such as communication, problem-solving, critical thinking, and decision-making, which are essential for success in any HR-related career.

Overall, taking an HRM subject can have a positive impact on your future career as an HR manager by equipping you with the necessary knowledge and skills to manage people effectively and make informed decisions.
