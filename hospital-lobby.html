3. A lobby is 15 meters by 20 meters. There is tiled part in the middle which is 12 m by 9 m. Find the area of the lobby that is not tiled?


✏️AREA==============================

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{Problem:}} [/tex] A lobby is 15 meters by 20 meters. There is tiled part in the middle which is 12 m by 9 m. Find the area of the lobby that is not tiled?

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{Solution:}} [/tex] Identify the area of the whole lobby and the area of the tiled part, then subtract the two areas to find the area of the part that is untiled.

» Area of the lobby:

[tex] \sf Lobby's \: Area = 20m \cdot 15m [/tex]

[tex] \sf Lobby's \: Area = 300m^2 [/tex]

» Area of the tiled part:

[tex] \sf Tiled \: Area = 12m \cdot 9m [/tex]

[tex] \sf Tiled \: Area = 108m^2 [/tex]

» Area of the untiled part:

[tex] \sf Untiled \: Area = 300m^2 - 108m^2 [/tex]

[tex] \sf Untiled \: Area = 192m^2 [/tex]

[tex] \large \therefore \underline{\boxed{\tt \purple{The \: untiled \: area \: is \: 192m^2 }}} [/tex]

==============================

Answer:

192 m

Step-by-step explanation:

We know that

Area = lw

Area of lobby = lw

⇒ Area = (15)(20)

⇒ Area = 300 m

Area of tiled part = lw

⇒ Area = (12)(9)

⇒ Area = 108 m

Area of lobby that is not tile = 300 - 108 = 192 m

= 15 * 12

A(not tiled) = A(lobby) - A(tiled)
