5. why does manobo tribe consider as the most prominent ethnic group in Agusan?​


Answer:

The Manobo tribe is considered one of the most prominent ethnic groups in the province of Agusan, particularly in the upland and rural areas of the region. There are several reasons why the Manobo people have achieved this prominence:

Historical Significance: The Manobo people have a long and rich history in the region, dating back to pre-colonial times. They have played an important role in the region's social, cultural, and political development.

Large Population: The Manobo tribe is one of the largest ethnic groups in the region. According to the 2015 Philippine Statistics Authority census, the Manobo tribe is the fourth largest ethnic group in the country, with a population of over 700,000 people.

Cultural Diversity: The Manobo tribe is known for its rich and diverse culture, which includes unique customs, traditions, beliefs, and practices. They have their own language, music, dances, and other cultural expressions.

Economic Importance: The Manobo people are known for their skills in agriculture, particularly in upland farming. They have a strong connection to the land and are able to cultivate crops in challenging environments. They also have a strong tradition of handicrafts, which has helped to support their livelihoods.

Resilience: The Manobo people have faced numerous challenges over the years, including land dispossession, discrimination, and political marginalization. Despite these challenges, they have remained resilient and have continued to preserve their culture and way of life.

Overall, the Manobo tribe is considered one of the most prominent ethnic groups in the province of Agusan due to their historical significance, large population, cultural diversity, economic importance, and resilience in the face of challenges.

Yakan - The Yakan people are an indigenous group in Mindanao known for their colorful and intricate weaving traditions. BAGOBO

- The Bagobo were the first ethnic group in Mindanao encountered by the Spaniards at the end of the nineteenth century. Its name was derived from the words “bago” meaning “new' and “obo/obbo/uvu” meaning “growth”,or “grow” which refer to a recent formation of people along the Davao Gulf coast.

MANOBO

- The Manobo languages are a group of languages spoken in the Philippines. Their speakers are primarily located around Northern Mindanao, Central Mindanao and Caraga regions where they are natively spoken.

MANDAYA

- Mandayas are said to be polygynous; divorce is also socially acceptable.

T'BOLI

- The T'boli rely on Muslim traders for contacts with the lowlands and maritime trade.

TIRURAY

- The word "Tiruray" comes from "tiru," signifying "place of origin, birth or residence," and "ray," from "daya," meaning" upper part of a stream or river." The Tirurau are a traditional hill people of southwestern Mindanao.
