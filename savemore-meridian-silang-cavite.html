2. tradition of silang cavite?​


The traditional origins of the town are attributed to ten Bornean datus who sailed northward on board balangays and ended in Silang through Taal Lake. Its first settlers were Gat Hingiw, his wife Gat Kaliwanag, and their seven children, who later moved to different parts of the town and established their respective barangays. Gat Pandan stayed in the original community and developed the area.

3. what is the culture of silang Cavite?


Municipality of Silang

Silang is a municipality in Cavite located near Tagaytay City. It is located in the eastern part of the province that it's where the municipality name came from (silangan). The municipality is famous for PNP or Philippine National Police Academy.  

The culture and tradition of Silang have a huge influence from the Spaniards during the colonial era. The land of Silang was originally belonged to the land of Diego Jorge de Villalobos. In the year 1746, Silang was purchased for 2,000 pesetas to prevent it from becoming a friar land in Cavite.

27. draw the location of the epicenter of an earthquake in Carmona, Silang, GMA Cavite if the hypothetical distance of epicenter in Carmona is 300 Km, Silang is 200 lkm, and GMA is 200km​ paturo po^_^.​


This method is used by geologists and seismologists called the triangulation method to determine the location of the epicenter. For the triangulation method to work we need three locations along with their hypothetical distance from the epicenter.

You will need:

A compassA pencilDifferent colored pensA copy of the map

Steps in doing the triangulation method:

From the map find the location labeled Carmona.this is where you will put the needle of your compass. Draw at least three circles of increasing size. A pencil would do but I recommend using a different colored pen for each location.Repeat steps 1 to 3 for the location Silang and GMA.

Note: Since there is no numbered scale provided, we will draw different sizes of circles but regarding the measurement of the circles, Silang and GMA’s circles must be roughly the same size and Carmona must have the biggest circle of them all. This is because of the distance provided from the epicenter.

When you’re done drawing the circles, there should be a point in the circles where all three different circles centered in three different locations would intersect. The point where they intersect is the epicenter of the earthquake.
