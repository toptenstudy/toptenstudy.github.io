5. what force does the brakes of the bicycle produces to stop the bike​


Answer:

Static friction

Explanation:

Static friction is also the force that is most effective in stopping the bicycle. In a normal stop—one that does not involve any skidding of the tires—it is the force of static friction between the ground and the tires that causes the deceleration of the bicycle.

6. What will be produced when you pedal a bike?​


Answer:

Pedaling a bike at a reasonable pace generates about 100 watts of power. That's the same energy-per-time used by a 100-watt lightbulb. So if you pedaled eight hours every day for 30 days (no weekends off), then doing the math, you'd generate 24 kilowatt-hours (kWh) of energy.
