What to do before a typhoon

It is important to monitor updates about the weather especially if there is a possibility of a low pressure area (LPA) to develop into a typhoon and if your area will be affected by it.

Here are things that you should do before a typhoon hits your area:

1.Listen to weather updates and advisories.

2.Keep watch for warnings and plans regarding evacuation in your community.

3.Check your house’s condition and make necessary repairs.

4.Keep your typhoon kit in an area where you can easily get it in case of emergency.

5.Bring your pets and livestock in the designated evacuation area for animals. If not, keep them in a safe place.

6.Evacuate immediately once asked by the authorities to do so.

What to do during a typhoonDuring a typhoon, listen to weather updates if possible, stay alert for any sign of threat in your area and do the following:

1.Remain calm and stay inside your house or the evacuation center.

2.Turn off main switch for utilities like water and electricity.

3.Have a flashlight or lamp in hand in case of emergency and loss of electricity.

4.Keep away from possible dangers like glass windows.

What to do after a typhoonEven after a typhoon has passed, we should still be alert and cautious because its aftermath can still post danger to us. Here are things to remember after a typhoon:

1.Before returning home, wait for authorities to announce that it is safe to go back to your area.

29. What to do before, during, and after the typhoon


Typhoon

Typhoons are classified as tropical cyclones having winds of at least 34 knots. Tropical depression refers to a tropical storm with a maximum wind speed of fewer than 34 knots. Only if the greatest wind is less than or greater than the threshold of 34 knots can two types of tropical cyclones be distinguished from one another. Typhoons cause flooding, significant damage to crops, homes, and structures, and fatal accidents as a result of the powerful winds and heavy rain they bring. Typhoon intensity has increased due to climate change.

What to do before a typhoon:

If a low-pressure area (LPA) is forecast to intensify into a typhoon and if your area is likely to be affected by it, it is crucial to keep track of weather updates. Before a typhoon strikes your region, you should take the following actions:

Take note of weather advisories and updates.To prepare for an evacuation, keep an eye out for local alerts and plans.Observe the state of your home and make any required repairs.A place where you can quickly access your typhoon kit in an emergency is where you should keep it.In the designated animal evacuation area, bring your pets and livestock. If not, store them securely.As soon as the authorities tell you to leave, do so right away.

What to do during the typhoon:

When a typhoon is approaching, try to keep up with weather reports, be vigilant for any local threats, and take the following precautions:

Stay calm and confine yourself to your home or the evacuation center.Cut off the main water and electricity switches.If there is a disaster and the power goes out, keep a flashlight or lamp nearby.Avoid potentially dangerous areas like those with glass windows.

What to do after the typhoon:

We should remain vigilant and careful even after a typhoon has passed since its aftermath may still present a threat to us. Following a typhoon, keep in mind the following:

Await the announcement from the authorities that it is safe to return to your neighborhood before going back home.Steer clear of peril.Buildings with damage.Dead trees.Worn-out power line.Rescue and emergency teams must have the route.When performing repairs at home, exercise particular caution.Make sure there are no damp or submerged outlets or equipment before turning on the electricity.Look for potential mosquito breeding sites and drain any standing water.
