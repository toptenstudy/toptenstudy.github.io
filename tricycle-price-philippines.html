5. how do links between the Philippines and the world affect tricycle or jeepney fares? ​


Answer:

Links between the Philippines and the world can affect tricycle or jeepney fares in several ways.

- For instance, fluctuations in fuel prices, which are influenced by global oil markets, can impact transportation costs.

- Changes in government policies or regulations regarding import/export tariffs and taxes can also have a ripple effect on fares.

- Additionally, tourism plays a crucial role in determining fare rates as it affects demand for transportation services.

- Improved infrastructure and better connectivity with other countries may lead to increased competition among transport providers, which could drive down fares.

- Ultimately, any significant changes in the global economy or political landscape may have implications for tricycle and jeepney fares in the Philippines.

PUBLIC TRANSPORT AND SPECIAL SERVICES FOR FRONTLINE WORKERSIn the Philippines, the national government has suspended all forms of public transportation in Luzon, including trains, buses, jeepneys, taxis, and tricycles, to discourage people from traveling during the Enhanced Community Quarantine.[9] The national government has delegated the local governments with assessing mobility needs and facilitating transport options for essential workers. In most cities, government fleets have been deployed to ferry front liners. Several private businesses, including the utility company Meralco, have also volunteered the services of their own fleets. Pasig City made 100 units of bicycles, originally part of their Bike Share Program, available to their front liners. The city is planning to purchase more bicycles for this purpose. Many other organizations have also teamed up with the local government and donated additional units for deployment.
