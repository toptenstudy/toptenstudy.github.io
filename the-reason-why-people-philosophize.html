19. what doyou think is the reason why early philosophers considered the earth to be a flat disc​


The belief that the Earth was a flat disc was a common assumption among early philosophers, mainly because they lacked the scientific evidence and technology to prove otherwise. Without the benefit of modern scientific instruments and space exploration, early philosophers had to rely on observation and deduction to make sense of the world around them.

Many early philosophers believed that the Earth was flat because that was the most logical conclusion they could draw based on their observations. From their vantage point on the Earth's surface, the ground appeared flat, and the horizon seemed to be a straight line. Additionally, the Sun, Moon, and stars all appeared to rise and set on the horizon, which further reinforced the idea that the Earth was a flat disc.

Furthermore, early philosophers also believed that the Earth was the center of the universe, with the celestial bodies revolving around it. This belief, known as geocentrism, was widely accepted until the scientific revolution of the 16th and 17th centuries.

It's important to note that the ancient Greeks, including Pythagoras and Aristotle, did suggest that the Earth was spherical, based on observations of the Earth's shadow during lunar eclipses. However, this idea was not widely accepted at the time, and it was not until the 3rd century BCE that the Greek mathematician and astronomer Eratosthenes was able to accurately calculate the Earth's circumference using trigonometry.

In summary, the belief that the Earth was a flat disc was a common assumption among early philosophers due to the limitations of their observations and the lack of scientific evidence and technology available to them. It wasn't until much later that the idea of a spherical Earth became widely accepted, based on the work of astronomers and mathematicians who were able to provide evidence and calculations to support this theory.
