7. A pet store keeps track of the purchase of customers over a four-hour period. The store manager classifies purchases as containing a dog product, cat product, or a fish product. She tracked: 83 purchased a dog product, 101 purchased a cat product, 22 purchased a fish product, 31 purchased a dog and cat product, 8 purchased a dog and a fish product, 10 purchased a cat and a fish product, and 6 purchased a dog, a cat, and fish product. a. How many purchases were for a dog product only?b. How purchases were for a cat product only?c. How many purchases were for a fish product onlyd. How many purchases were there in total?​


Step-by-step explanation:

To solve this problem, we can use a Venn diagram to represent the relationships between different groups of purchases.

a. To find the number of purchases for a dog product only, we first subtract the number of purchases that included a cat or fish product from the total number of purchases for a dog product:

Dog product only = Total dog product purchases - (Dog & Cat) - (Dog & Fish) - (Dog, Cat & Fish)

Dog product only = 83 - 31 - 8 - 6

Dog product only = 38

Therefore, 38 purchases were for a dog product only.

b. To find the number of purchases for a cat product only, we use a similar approach:

Cat product only = Total cat product purchases - (Dog & Cat) - (Cat & Fish) - (Dog, Cat & Fish)

Cat product only = 101 - 31 - 10 - 6

Cat product only = 54

Therefore, 54 purchases were for a cat product only.

c. To find the number of purchases for a fish product only, we use a similar approach:

Fish product only = Total fish product purchases - (Dog & Fish) - (Cat & Fish) - (Dog, Cat & Fish)

Fish product only = 22 - 8 - 10 - 6

Fish product only = 4

Therefore, 4 purchases were for a fish product only.

d. To find the total number of purchases, we can add up all the individual purchases:

Total purchases = Dog product only + Cat product only + Fish product only + (Dog & Cat) + (Dog & Fish) + (Cat & Fish) + (Dog, Cat & Fish)

Total purchases = 38 + 54 + 4 + 31 + 8 + 10 + 6

Total purchases = 151

Therefore, there were a total of 151 purchases over the four-hour period.

30. What determines the purchasing power of a consumer? What is your purchasing power?


Answer:

Consumer purchasing power measures the value in money for which consumers may purchase goods or services. ... Consumer purchasing power is determined by the Consumer Price Index, which surveys changes in the prices of goods and services over a period of months or years.

Purchasing power is the value of a currency expressed in terms of the number of goods or services that one unit of money can buy. Purchasing power is important because, all else being equal, inflation decreases the number of goods or services you would be able to purchase.
