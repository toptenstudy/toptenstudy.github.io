Answer:

On March 27, 1912, First Lady Helen Herron Taft and the Viscountess Chinda, wife of the Japanese ambassador, planted two Yoshino cherry trees on the northern bank of the Potomac River Tidal Basin in Washington, D.C. The event celebrated the Japanese government's gift of 3,000 trees to the United States.

Cherry blossoms are a symbolic flower of the spring, a time of renewal, and the fleeting nature of life. Their life is very short. After their beauty peaks around two weeks, the blossoms start to fall.

Cherry blossoms, also known as sakura in Japan, are the small, delicate pink flowers produced by cherry blossom trees. The springtime bloom is a lavish spectacle but remarkably brief; after only two weeks, they drop to the ground and wither, falling like snow with the ebb and flow of the winds.

13. Let's say you have a flower in your garden. Every day, the flower develops two new flowers, and each of these new blossoms also bears two flowers each day. After eight days, how many blooms are already present in the garden?


Flower and Blooms Problem SolvingAnswer

255 blooms

Problem

The problem tells us that the flower you have in your garden blooms two new flowers each day. After eight days, we need to determine how many blooms are already present in the garden.

Solution

METHOD 1

We can illustrate this using a table. On Day 1, there is only your flower so one bloom. On Day 2, there would be additional two flowers. On Day 3, there are four new flowers, and so on.

Day         1   |   2   |   3   |   4   |   5   |   6   |   7   |   8   |

Bloom     1   |   2   |   4   |   8   |  16  |  32  |  64  | 128 |

Total number of blooms = sum of all blooms from Day 1 to 8.

= 1+2+4+8+16+32+64+128

= 255 flowers

METHOD 2

We can illustrate this mathematically using the formula below. Let [tex]d[/tex] be the number of days.

[tex]No.\ of\ blooms = \Sigma^{8}_{d=1} (2^{d-1})[/tex]

[tex]Thus,\\No.\ of\ blooms = 2^0+2^1+2^2+2^3+2^4+2^5+2^6+2^7[/tex]

[tex]No.\ of\ blooms = \bold{255}[/tex]

The reason it is [tex]d-1[/tex] because among the eight days, only the first day has one bloom. Remember, the condition is that a day must have two blooms.

24. how bees and other insects help plants by pollinating flower blossoms?​


Answer:

When a bee stops at a flower to gather nectar and pollen, it contacts the anthers. Pollen from the anthers attaches to hairs on the bee's body. When the bee moves on, it carries that pollen to the stigma of the next flower it visits. This transfer of pollen between flowers results in pollination.
