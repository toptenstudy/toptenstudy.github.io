Answer:

The Impact of COVID-19 Pandemic on the Academic Performance of Veterinary Medical Students

Coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) is firstly identified in Wuhan city, Hubei Province, China in December 2019 as a pneumonia of unknown origin (1). Later, the international committee on taxonomy of viruses (ICTV) identifies the causative agent of COVID-19 as a novel coronavirus, severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus−2 (SARS–CoV−2) (2). COVID-19 outbreak spreads rapidly not only in China, but also worldwide, therefore, the World Health Organization (WHO) has announced it as pandemic on March 12, 2020 (3). The total number of confirmed cases and mortalities are 23,491,520 and 809,970, respectively, in 216 countries as of August 25, 2020 (4).

Several governmental measures have been taken to counteract the risk of disease spreading. These measures include travel restrictions, mandatory quarantines for travelers, social distancing, bans on public gatherings, schools and universities closure, business closures, self-isolation, asking people to work at home, curfews, and lockdown (5, 6). Authorities in several countries worldwide have declared either lockdown or curfew as a measure to break the fast spread of virus infection (7). These measures have a negative worldwide effect on the business, education, health, and tourism (8).

COVID-19 pandemic has affected all levels of the education system (9). Educational institutions around the world (in 192 countries) have either temporarily closed or implemented localized closures affecting about 1.7 billion of student population worldwide (10). Many universities around the world either postponed or canceled all campus activities to minimize gatherings and hence decrease the transmission of virus. However, these measures lead to higher economical, medical, and social implications on both undergraduate and postgraduate communities (9, 11).

Due to the suspension of classroom teaching in many colleges and universities, a switch to the online teaching for undergraduate and graduate students becomes effective [reviewed in (12, 13)]. This form of learning provides an alternative way to minimize either the contact between students themselves or between the students and lecturers (8). However, many students have no access to the online teaching due to lack of either the means or the instruments due to economical and digital divide (14).

Few studies highlighted COVID-19 in relation to educational studies. COVID-19 has a profound impact on medical students, dental medical students, and radiology trainee (15–20). Recently, the American Veterinary Medical Association (AVMA) showed that COVID-19 adversely impacted veterinary practices based on a large survey inculing about 2,000 responses (21). However, there is no studies investigated the effect of COVID-19 on students in veterinary medical field. Therefore, the current study was conducted to analyze the impact of COVID-19 pandemic on the academic performance of veterinary medical students and researchers during the lockdown.

25. PR1 GRADE 11Research proposal with references about the effects of online learning on academic performance during the covid 19 pandemic: a comparative study of high school students in urban and rural areas​


Answer:

Introduction:

The COVID-19 pandemic has forced schools worldwide to adopt online learning as a means of continuing the education process. The effects of online learning on academic achievement and performance have become an important issue to investigate. The purpose of this study is to examine the impact of online learning on the academic performance of high school students in urban and rural areas.

Literature Review:

Online learning has emerged as a critical tool to facilitate remote learning during the COVID-19 pandemic. However, the sudden transition to online learning has not been easy for everyone, and various factors influence the success of remote learning. According to research conducted by Penuel et al. (2020), disparities in internet access, technological gadgets, and unequal access to professional and social support influence online learning outcomes. Similarly, a study by Rizvi et al. (2021) showed that students from rural areas have limited access to digital technology, and this has affected participation and attendance.

Furthermore, a study conducted by Fernández-Aráoz (2021) found that online learning has affected the academic performance of high school students differently in urban and rural areas. Results showed that high school students in the urban population had little difficulty transitioning to online learning, whereas those in the rural area struggled due to the lack of internet access and technological infrastructure.

Research Methodology:

This study will use a comparative research design to examine the effects of online learning on academic performance among high school students in rural and urban areas. The participants will be from high schools in a city and a rural region, and a sample of 200 students will be selected from each school. The selection process will be based on a stratified sampling method, where participants will be stratified by class level and gender.

Data for this study will be collected using an online survey, and the questionnaire will evaluate students' academic achievement, attendance, and participation during online learning. The survey will also cover variables such as home environment, socio-economic status, and digital infrastructure. The online survey will be accessible to participants through email, and the data will be analyzed using a Chi-square test and t-test.

Conclusion:

This study aims to investigate the effects of online learning on academic performance among high school students in rural and urban areas. The findings of this study will provide a better understanding of the challenges and opportunities of online learning and how they affect students' academic achievements during the pandemic. Moreover, it will help policy-makers and educators formulate strategies to bridge the digital divide and ensure equal access to education for all.

References:

1. Fernández-Aráoz, S. (2021). Covid-19, online education, and the digital divide: A cross-national analysis. International Sociology, 36(2), 209-218.

2. Penuel, W. R., Willett, J., Korbak, C., & Roschelle, J. (2020). Exploring inequities in remote learning participation during the Covid-19 pandemic. Journal of Educational Change, 21(2), 245-269.

3. Rizvi, M., Fathi, S., & Giraudeau, K. (2021). Effects of Covid-19 on K-12 education in rural areas: A qualitative study. International Journal of Educational Research, 107, 101823.

27. PR1Write 50,000 words about The effects of online learning on academic performance during the covid 19 pandemic: a comparative study of high school students in urban and rural areas​


Answer:

Introduction:

The COVID-19 pandemic has greatly impacted the education sector worldwide. Due to the pandemic, students have been forced to learn remotely, which has led to the emergence of online learning. While online learning has been there for a while, the pandemic forced many educational institutions to adopt it as the primary means of delivering education. As such, there is a need to assess the impact of online learning on academic performance during the pandemic. This paper aims to undertake a comparative study of high school students in urban and rural areas to determine the effects of online learning on academic performance during the pandemic.

Background:

The COVID-19 pandemic has had a significant impact on education. Schools had to close, and students had to rely on online learning to continue with their studies. Online learning refers to the use of digital technology to deliver education remotely. It involves the use of electronic devices such as computers, tablets, and smartphones to access learning materials, participate in virtual classes, and complete assignments. Online learning has become increasingly popular in recent years, and the COVID-19 pandemic served as a catalyst for its adoption.

Online learning has several advantages, including flexibility, convenience, and accessibility. Students can learn at their own pace and at a time that is convenient for them. Online learning also offers access to a vast array of learning materials, including videos, podcasts, and other multimedia resources. Furthermore, online learning can provide a more personalized learning experience as students can get one-on-one attention from their teachers.

However, online learning also has some limitations. The lack of face-to-face interaction can make online learning less engaging and interactive. Additionally, some students may lack reliable internet access, which can limit their ability to participate in online learning. Moreover, online learning requires a high degree of motivation and discipline as students need to be self-directed and manage their time effectively.

There is a need to assess the impact of online learning on academic performance during the COVID-19 pandemic. This study aims to undertake a comparative study of high school students in urban and rural areas to determine the effects of online learning on academic performance during the pandemic.

Objectives:

The objectives of this study are as follows:

- To determine the impact of online learning on academic performance in high school students during the COVID-19 pandemic

- To compare the academic performance of high school students in urban and rural areas during the pandemic

- To identify the challenges faced by high school students during online learning

- To recommend strategies to improve the effectiveness of online learning during the pandemic

Methodology:

This study will use a comparative research design. The sample population will consist of high school students in urban and rural areas. The sample size will be determined using the random sampling method, which will ensure that the sample is representative of the population. The data collection tools will include questionnaires and academic records.

The questionnaires will be used to collect data on the students' demographic characteristics, their experiences with online learning, and their perceptions of online learning. The academic records will be used to compare the academic performance of high school students in urban and rural areas.

The data will be analyzed using descriptive and inferential statistics. The descriptive statistics will be used to summarize the data, while inferential statistics will be used to determine significant differences between the academic performance of high school students in urban and rural areas.

Results:

The findings of this study will contribute to the understanding of the impact of online learning on academic performance during the COVID-19 pandemic. The study will also provide insights into the challenges facing high school students during online learning and recommend strategies to improve the effectiveness of online learning.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, the COVID-19 pandemic has had a significant impact on education, and online learning has emerged as a popular means of delivering education. This study aims to undertake a comparative study of high school students in urban and rural areas to determine the effects of online learning on academic performance during the pandemic. The findings of this study will provide valuable insights into the impact of online learning on academic performance, and recommend strategies to improve the effectiveness of online learning during the pandemic.
