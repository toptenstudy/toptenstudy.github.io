11. A farmer owns a total of 25 ducks and horses. The ducks and horses have 84 legs altogether. How many horses are there?


Answer:

There are 17 horses and 8 ducks.

Number of animals:

Ducks: x

Horses: y

Total: 25

Equation (1):

x + y = 25

y = 25 - x

Number of legs per animal:

Ducks: 2

Horses: 4

Total legs of 19 animals: 84

Equation (2):

2x + 4y = 84

Find the number of ducks, x.  Substitute 25-x to y in Equation (2):

2x + 4y = 84

2x + 4(25-x) = 84

2x + 100 - 4x = 84

-2x = 84 - 100

-2x/-2 = -16/-2

x = 8  ⇒  number of ducks

Find the number of horses, y:

y = 25-x

y = 25-8

y = 17  ⇒  number of horses

There are 15 horses and 8 ducks.

Check:

x + y = 25

8 + 17 = 25

2x + 4y = 84

2(8) + 4(17) = 84

16 + 68 = 84
