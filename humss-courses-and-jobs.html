25. research title;' Factors that can influence SHS Students decision to pursue HUMSS related course on college 'Introduction: ?Research Problem: ?Objective/Goal: ?Significance: ?Participants: ?​


Answer:

Introduction: Senior High School (SHS) students are faced with a critical decision in their academic journey, particularly in determining which college course to pursue. This decision shapes the direction of their careers, and ultimately their future. This research aims to identify the factors that influence SHS students' decision to pursue Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS)-related courses. Research Problem: What are the factors that can influence SHS students' decision to pursue HUMSS-related courses, especially with the limited knowledge or understanding of their possible career pathways, opportunities, and demand in the job market? Objective/Goal: The main objective of this study is to identify and examine the factors that influence SHS students' decision to pursue HUMSS-related courses. The study aims to determine the significance of the following factors in their decision-making process: personal interests, peer influence, education and awareness of career opportunities, socioeconomic status, family expectations, and personality traits. It also aims to provide recommendations on how to encourage more SHS students to consider HUMSS-related courses for their college degree. Significance: This research has both theoretical and practical significance. Theoretically, it adds to the body of knowledge on college course selection by exploring the factors that influence SHS students' decision specifically in HUMSS-related courses. Practically, the results of this research can help educators and policymakers to develop strategies and interventions to promote HUMSS-related courses and provide SHS students with adequate information and awareness about these courses' career opportunities, benefits, and relevance. Participants: The study will involve SHS students in Grade 11 and 12 who are currently enrolled in HUMSS-related strands in selected public and private schools in Metro Manila. At least 100 respondents from each school will be selected through stratified sampling. They will participate in a survey questionnaire that will explore different factors that influence their decision-making and their understanding and perception of HUMSS-related courses.
