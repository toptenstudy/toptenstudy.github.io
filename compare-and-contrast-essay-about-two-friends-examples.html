5. (Introduce the two things being compared/contrasted) (compare and contrast in terms of cost) (compare and contrast in terms of convenience) (compare and contrast in terms of health) (conclusion about the two things) n ct​


Answer:

Let's compare and contrast two popular modes of transportation: driving a car and riding a bicycle.

In terms of cost, driving a car is generally more expensive than riding a bicycle. Cars require fuel, regular maintenance, insurance, and parking fees, all of which can add up quickly. On the other hand, bicycles require minimal maintenance and no fuel costs, making them a more affordable option.

When it comes to convenience, driving a car is generally more convenient than riding a bicycle. Cars can travel faster and farther distances, and provide more protection from the elements. Additionally, cars can carry more people and items, making them a better choice for running errands or transporting large objects. However, bicycles are often more convenient for short trips, especially in urban areas with heavy traffic and limited parking options.

In terms of health, riding a bicycle has numerous health benefits, such as improving cardiovascular health, increasing endurance and strength, and reducing stress. On the other hand, driving a car is a more sedentary activity that can contribute to a sedentary lifestyle and health problems associated with sitting for long periods of time.

Overall, the choice between driving a car and riding a bicycle ultimately depends on personal preferences and circumstances. While driving a car may be more convenient for some situations, riding a bicycle can offer significant cost savings and health benefits.

17. Write a essay about the new normal with contrasting and comparing 500 words​


Answer:

The COVID-19 pandemic has changed the world in unprecedented ways, and the new normal has become a buzzword that everyone is talking about. The term refers to the changes in lifestyle, work, education, and daily routines brought about by the pandemic. While the new normal has brought many challenges and difficulties, it has also opened up new opportunities and ways of living. In this essay, we will compare and contrast the old normal with the new normal, and see how the pandemic has changed the world.

Before the pandemic, people lived their lives in a certain way. They went to work or school in person, socialized with friends and family, and engaged in various activities and hobbies. The fast pace of life was fueled by technology, and people were always connected to their work, friends, and families. However, the pandemic brought a sudden halt to all these activities, and people had to find new ways to live and work.

The new normal has brought about a shift towards remote work and online education. People are now able to work from home, attend online classes, and communicate with their colleagues and classmates through video conferencing. This has allowed for a more flexible work-life balance, but it has also brought its own set of challenges. The lack of physical interaction with colleagues and classmates has led to feelings of isolation, and the home environment can be distracting and make it difficult to focus on work or studies.

Social interactions have also been affected by the new normal. People are now more cautious about physical contact, and social distancing measures have become the norm. This has led to a decrease in face-to-face interactions and an increase in virtual ones. People are now relying on social media and video calls to stay connected with their friends and family. While this has allowed for people to stay in touch, it has also led to feelings of loneliness and a decrease in the quality of social interactions.

The new normal has also brought about a shift towards a more sustainable way of living. With the decrease in travel and physical interactions, there has been a reduction in carbon emissions, and people are now more conscious of their impact on the environment. This has led to an increase in the use of public transportation, cycling, and walking, and a decrease in the use of personal vehicles.

In conclusion, the new normal has brought about significant changes in the way we live, work, and interact with each other. While the pandemic has brought many challenges and difficulties, it has also opened up new opportunities and ways of living. Remote work and online education have allowed for a more flexible work-life balance, and a shift towards a more sustainable way of living has emerged. The new normal has also changed the way we interact with each other, and the fast pace of life has slowed down, allowing people to re-evaluate their priorities and focus on what is truly important. The changes brought about by the pandemic are here to stay, and it will be up to each individual to make the most of them and create a new, better world.
