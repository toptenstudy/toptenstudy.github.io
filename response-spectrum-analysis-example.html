Radio waves, microwaves, infrared waves, visible light, ultraviolet light, and even X-rays and gamma rays are all part of the electromagnetic spectrum! In a lot of respects, all of these electromagnetic waves are the same. To begin with, all electromagnetic waves are capable of traveling through empty space. Electromagnetic waves, unlike sound waves or water waves, do not require a medium to move through. This is significant because the light and heat from the Sun would not be able to reach us on Earth if electromagnetic waves could not travel through empty space!

13. Differentiate visible spectrum from invisible spectrum.​


The visible spectrum is the portion of the electromagnetic spectrum that is visible to the human eye. Electromagnetic radiation in this range of wavelengths is called visible light or simply light. A typical human eye will respond to wavelengths from about 380 to 740 nanometers.

invisible spectrum
The portion of the spectrum either below the red (infrared) or above the violet (ultraviolet), which is invisible to the eye, the waves being too long or too short to affect the retina. The invisible spectrum includes rays less than 3900 Å in length (ultraviolet, roentgen or x, gamma, and cosmic rays) and those exceeding 7700 Å in length (infrared, high-frequency oscillations used in short- and long-wave diathermy, radio, hertzian, and very long waves). These range in length from 7700 Å to 5,000,000 m.
