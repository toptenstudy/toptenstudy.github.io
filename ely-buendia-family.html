20. The sum of the ages of Mark and Eli is 12 years. Mark is thrice as old as Eli. Equation 1: Equation 2: Solve the system: How old is Mark and how old is Eli? What method did you use to solve the sestym?


Answer:

﻿

To solve this system of equations, we can use substitution or elimination.

Substitution:

Equation 1: x + y = 12

Equation 2: 3y = x

Substitute x from Equation 2 into Equation 1:

3y + y = 12

4y = 12

y = 3

Substitute y from the previous step into Equation 2:

3*3 = x

x = 9

Therefore, Mark is 9 years old and Eli is 3 years old.

Elimination:

Multiply Equation 2 by 3:

33y = 3x

9y = x

Subtract 9y from both sides of Equation 1:

x + y - 9y = 12

x - 8y = 12

Substitute x from Equation 2 into Equation 3:

9y - 8y = 12

y = 12/9

y = 4/3

Substitute y from the previous step into Equation 2:

3*(4/3) = x

x = 4

Therefore, Mark is 4 years old and Eli is 3 years old.


21. what did jose arcadio buendia find so fascinating about the instrument he bought despite his poverty?​


Answer:

The instruments' ability to extract iron from surroundings

Explanation:

In the book One Hundred Years of Solitude written by Gabriel Garcia Marquez, it was revealed that Jose Arcadio Buendia was fascinated by two magnetized ingots he bought from Melquíades the gypsy.

Hence, due to the ability of the magnetized ingots to draw irons, Jose Arcadio Buendia thought he could use the instrument's ability to extract gold from the underneath of the earth. He, therefore, traded his mule and pair of goats to buy the magnetized instruments.

27. ely is three times taller than james and the product of their height is equal to sum of their height increased by 55. find the height of ely and james​


Let

Ely = xJames = y

Equation 1

[tex]x = 3y[/tex]

Equation 2

[tex]xy = x + y + 55[/tex]

Substitute eq. 1 to eq. 2

[tex](3y)y = 3y + y + 55 \\ 3 {y}^{2} = 4y + 55 \\ 3 {y}^{2} - 4y - 55 = 0 \\ (3y + 11)(y - 5) = 0[/tex]

Equate to zero

[tex](3y + 11) \\ 3y + 11 = 0 \\ 3y = - 11 \\ \frac{3y}{3} = \frac{ - 11 }{3} \\ y = - \frac{11}{3} [/tex]

[tex](y - 5) \\ y - 5 = 0 \\ y = 5[/tex]

Substitute y to eq. 1

[tex]y = 5 \\ \\ x = 3y \\ x = 3(5) \\ x =15[/tex]

Therefore, Ely is 15 and James is 5.
