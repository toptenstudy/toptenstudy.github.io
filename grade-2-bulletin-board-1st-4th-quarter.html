6. Cindy got 88, 87 and 89 in Algebra in the 1st, 2nd and 3rd quarters, respectively. What possible grade must she get in the 4th quarter to get an average grade of no less than 90?


Let's denote Cindy's grade in the 4th quarter as x.

To get an average grade of no less than 90, the sum of her grades for all four quarters must be no less than 4 times 90 = 360.

So, we can set up the following equation:

(88 + 87 + 89 + x) / 4 ≥ 90

Multiplying both sides by 4 gives:

88 + 87 + 89 + x ≥ 360

Simplifying the left side gives:

264 + x ≥ 360

Subtracting 264 from both sides gives:

x ≥ 96

Therefore, Cindy must get a grade of no less than 96 in the 4th quarter to get an average grade of no less than 90 for the entire year.

14. Anong pinagkaiba ng 1st sem, 1st quarter, 1st grading, meron ba?​


Answer:

There are two academic terms per year in a semester system. Each term lasts about 15 weeks. Exams are administered twice a year, at the end of each term.

There are three academic terms per year in a quarter system. Each term lasts about ten weeks. Exams are given at the end of each term.

A semester class must cover more ground, and end-of-term exams can seem far away at the start of the term.

A quarter-system class can feel rushed and intense; exams are never far away.
