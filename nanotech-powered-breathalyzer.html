1. 16-20. How does a breathalyzer work?​


Answer:

When you blow into a breathalyzer, the ethanol in your breath reacts with water from the air at the anode and is oxidized to form acetic acid (like in vinegar). ... These two coupled reactions produce an electrical current between the electrodes that's proportional to the amount of ethanol present in your breath.

The Advantages and Disadvantages of 21st Century Technologies according to Bill Joy

a. Genetics – Can help in the improvement of quality and number of production of crops, plants, and meat that can help with the hunger issue the world is facing. It also helps in curing diseases and slowing down the rate of aging. However, this is a major threat to biodiversity as genetics can pick the desirable traits only and thus resulting in a very little gene pool for reproduction which can cause new problems in society.

b. Nanotech –  It is helpful in developing faster and newer laptops, power sources, and medical treatments. It can be a huge threat to the security and safety of people and can disrupt the economy.

c. Robotics – helpful in the sense that it can be programmed to do commands and thus makes it easier for humans to focus on other things. This can be a threat to human safety because it can be controlled by different people and can be programmed to danger.

d. Future technologies – While they may seem promising to hear now, they are actually dangerous. Furture technologies will be far better than we have today and thus has much more effect on society when misused by people who mean to cause harm.
