4. The difference and advantages of the types of cartridges according ot the location of primer


Answer:

A cartridge or a round is a type of pre-assembled firearm ammunition packaging a projectile (bullet, shot, or slug), a propellant substance (usually either smokeless powder or black powder) and an ignition device (primer) within a metallic, paper, or plastic case that is precisely made to fit within the barrel chamber of a breechloading gun, for the practical purpose of convenient transportation and handling during shooting. Although in popular usage the term "bullet" is often informally used to refer to a complete cartridge, it is correctly used only to refer to the projectile.

11. the step in the cycle of the that removes the expended cartridge from the weapon out of the ejection Port​


WEAPONS TRAINING

Eight (8) cycles of operation the weapons are:

Ejecting

The process of removing the spent cartridge from the weapon and out of the ejection port is known as ejecting.

further explanation

Feeding

The process of feeding involves the bolt moving forward while removing the top round from the magazine and advancing it toward the chamber.

Chambering

Fully seating the round in the chamber is referred to as chambering.

Locking

The phase of action when the bolt is rotated counterclockwise and inserted into the barrel locking lugs is called locking.

Firing

Pulling the trigger and allowing the hammer to hit the firing pin, which hits the primer, is referred to as firing. The powder charge within the cartridge care ignites as a result of the primer igniting.

Unlocking

After shooting, the bolt is unlocked by rotating it in a clockwise direction, which releases it from the barrel locking lugs.

Extracting

The steps in the cycle of action that remove the round from the chamber are known as extracting.

Cocking

When a weapon is "cocked," it is reset for a new round of shooting by the bolt riding over the hammer moving backward.

16. what are the function of cartridge case?​


Answer:

The function of the cartridge case is basically same whether it is fired in revolvers, pistols, rifles, shotguns, or machine guns. These include:

1.It holds the bullet, gunpowder and primer assembled into one unit.

2.It serves as a waterproof container for the gunpowder.

3.It prevents the escape of the gases to the rear as the sidewalls of the cartridge case are forced against the walls of the chamber by the pressure. It serves as a “gas seal” at the breech end of the barrel
