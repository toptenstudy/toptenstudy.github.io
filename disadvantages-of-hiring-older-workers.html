3. An engineer hired 150 workers to pave a road in 25 days. How many workers would be hired to do the same work in 30 days?


Inverse Proportion

Answer

125 workers should be hired to do the same work in 30 days.

Step-by-step explanation:

Inversely Proportional Problems

Proportion shows the relationship between two ratios or fractions. In real life, there are situations that involve inverse proportion. It means that as one quantity increases, the other decreases. One example is the number of workers required to finish a task. As the number of days required to finish the task increases, the number of workers needed decreases. In this example, the two quantities, the number of days (a) and the number of workers required (b), are related in such a way that when one quantity increases, the other decreases. Below are the steps to solve:

1. Write down the proportional relationship

2. Write the equation using the proportional constant

3. Find the value of the constant using the given values

4. Substitute the value of the constant in the equation.

Ex.

Number of workers in 25 days:

150 workers x 25 = 3750

Number of workers in 30 days:

150 x 25 = 30n

3750 = 30n

[tex]\frac{3750}{30}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{30n}{30}[/tex]

125 = n

23. 11. A hiring manager rejects a job applicant based on their age, assuming that older workers are less productive and less adaptable to new technology. What type of bias is this? Ageism bias Stereotyping bias Selection bias confirmation bias​


Answer:

The type of bias in this scenario is Ageism bias.

Explanation:

This is because the hiring manager has made an assumption or negative generalization about the applicant's abilities and potential performance based on their age. Ageism bias is a form of discrimination against individuals based solely on their age, and it can manifest in various ways, including employment decisions, such as hiring, promotion, and firing. Stereotyping bias may also be involved in this scenario since the hiring manager is making a sweeping generalization about the abilities of older workers. However, the specific type of bias that applies here is ageism.
