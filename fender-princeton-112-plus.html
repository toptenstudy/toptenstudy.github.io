29. robot a takes 6 min to weld a fender robot b takes only 5 1/2 min to do the same job if they work together for 2 min how long will it take robot b to finish welding the fender by itself?


[tex]\mathbb{FORMULA:}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{r=\dfrac{1}{t}}[/tex]

where:

r = rate

t = time

[tex]\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

For robot A:

[tex]\mathtt{r_{1}=\dfrac{1}{6}}[/tex]

For robot B:

[tex]\mathtt{t_{2}=5\:\dfrac{1}{2}=5.5}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{r_{2}=\dfrac{1}{5.5}}[/tex]

If both robots work together, their rate is the sum of their individual rates.

[tex]\mathtt{r=r_{1} \; + r_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{r=\dfrac{1}{6} \: + \: \dfrac{1}{5.5}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{r=\dfrac{23}{66}}[/tex]

Both robots worked together for 2 mins and then robot A left. Let's say t is the time it will take robot B to finish the job.

From our formula r = 1/t, rt = 1

[tex]\mathtt{\dfrac{23}{66}(2) \: + \: \dfrac{1}{5.5}(t)=1}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{\dfrac{23}{33} \: + \: \dfrac{t}{5.5} \: = \: 1}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{\dfrac{(5.5)(23)+33t}{(33)(5.5)}=1}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{\dfrac{126.5+33t}{181.5}=1}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{126.5+33t=181.5}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{33t=55}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{t=\dfrac{55}{33}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{t=\dfrac{5}{3}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathbb{ANSWER:}[/tex]

Robot B can finish the job in:

[tex]\Large{\boxed{\mathtt{\dfrac{5}{3} \: or \: 1.67 \: \: mins}}}[/tex]
