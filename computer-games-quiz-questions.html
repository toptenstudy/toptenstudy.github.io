4. A quiz consist of four multi questions with five possible responses to each question.In how manydifferent ways can the quiz be answered?​


To find the total number of ways to answer the quiz, we need to multiply the number of possible responses for each question together. Since there are four questions with five possible responses for each question, we have:

5 possible responses for the first question × 5 possible responses for the second question × 5 possible responses for the third question × 5 possible responses for the fourth question

= 5 × 5 × 5 × 5

= 625

Therefore, there are 625 different ways that the quiz can be answered.

8. Assignment No. 2 The following are the scores of students in their quiz: 10, 5, 15, 18, 20, 8, 12, 5 1. Compute the population mean 2. Compute the population variance 3. Compute the population standard variation​


Answer:

To solve these problems, we first need to find the population size. Since the problem doesn't state how many students there are, we'll assume that there are 8 students in the population.

To compute the population mean, we add up all the scores and divide by the population size:

Population mean = (10 + 5 + 15 + 18 + 20 + 8 + 12 + 5) / 8 = 93 / 8 = 11.625

Therefore, the population mean is 11.625.

To compute the population variance, we need to find the difference between each score and the population mean, square each difference, add up all the squared differences, and then divide by the population size:

Population variance = [(10 - 11.625)^2 + (5 - 11.625)^2 + (15 - 11.625)^2 + (18 - 11.625)^2 + (20 - 11.625)^2 + (8 - 11.625)^2 + (12 - 11.625)^2 + (5 - 11.625)^2] / 8

Population variance = 196.375 / 8 = 24.546875

Therefore, the population variance is 24.546875.

To compute the population standard deviation, we take the square root of the population variance:

Population standard deviation = sqrt(24.546875) = 4.954

Therefore, the population standard deviation is 4.954.

The following list gives you quality guidelines for how to study for a chemistry test:

Don’t cram. Studying a little bit at a time over the course of the entire unit is far and away the best plan for mastering the material and building your confidence in it.

Allocate your study time wisely. Sadly, your time is limited. You can’t just pause the day and study chemistry for hours on end.

Don’t worry about the exceptions until you know the basics. In chemistry, it sure seems like every time a rule or a theory is laid out, you run across exceptions.

Do example problems. This is perhaps the most important piece of advice in this list. Just looking at your notes and following along with a problem won’t help you learn.

Be confident. Confidence is a key to being successful in all things in life. School and tests — especially chemistry tests — are no different. When you study, start with the easier material and then work your way up to the more challenging stuff.
