23. how to find the general term in finding nth terms of a sequence?​


Answer:

an arithmetic sequence is a linear function. Instead of y=mx+b, we write an=dn+c where d is the common difference and c is a constant (not the first term of the sequence, however). a recursive definition, since each term is found by adding the common difference to the previous term is ak+1=ak+d.

#CarryOnLearning

Answer:

Simply use an= a1+(n-1)d

Step-by-step explanation:

Use an = a1+(n-1)d

Where:

an= nth term

a1= first term

n= no. of terms in the sequence

d= common difference (to get, simply subtract: 2nd term -1st term or 3rd term -2nd therm and so fort)

Example:

3,6,9,12

Get the values of a1 and d:

a1=3

d=6-3

d=3

Then substitute to an=a1+(n-1)d:

an=3+(n-1)3

Distribute d:

an=3+3n-3

Combine like terms:

an= 3n

Answer: an=3n is the general formula of the sequence given.
