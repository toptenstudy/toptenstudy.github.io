5. Activity 1- Complete the spider web. Direction: Enumerate the techniques in arranging and presenting sandwiches.. Creative Sandwich Preparation​


Answer:

Here are some techniques you can use when arranging and presenting sandwiches to make them more creative:

1. Layering: Layering ingredients with different textures, colors, and flavors creates depth and interest. Start with bread, then add condiments, meats, cheeses, and veggies.

2. Cut the sandwiches into creative shapes: Cut sandwiches into triangles, squares, rectangles, or use cookie cutters to create fun shapes like stars or hearts.

3. Toasting bread: Toasting the bread adds texture and makes the sandwich more flavorful.

4. Garnish: Use garnishes to add color and freshness to sandwiches. Some great options are lettuce, pickles, cucumber slices, radishes, or fresh herbs.

5. Spreads: Use spreads like mayonnaise, mustard, ranch dressing or hummus to add flavor.

6. Stack up: You can stack the sandwich on top of each other to create a tower or use toothpicks or skewer to keep it together.

7. Wrap it Up: Wrap the sandwich in waxed paper or parchment for a neat, portable package. You can use a variety of paper or give it stylish twist with scallions, chives or ribbon.

8. Layered Chopped Salad: Add layers of chopped lettuce, cheese, meat, and vegetables as well as dressings to complete the sandwich.

9. Open-Faced Sandwiches: Use open-faced sandwiches to showcase beautiful ingredients such as smoked salmon and cucumber, turkey and brie, or avocado and tomato.

10. Themed Sandwiches: Use themed sandwiches to make them more interesting. For example, a BBQ sandwich can have a slice of smoked brisket, coleslaw, and BBQ sauce.

These creative sandwich presentation techniques can make your meal much more enjoyable and memorable. Beyond just tasting good, with beautiful layers, creative shapes, and fun garnishes, you can make a simple sandwich into a work of art that you'll want to share with others.

7. Keywords are strategically placed in titles, sentences and URLs to increase the chance that a search engine will pick up your content. This is a _______ technique. Select one: a. Search Engine Optimization b. Web Syndication c. Search Engine Syndication d. Optimized Keyword Syndication


The answer to the question is A. Search Engine Optimization. Keywords are strategically placed in titles, sentences and URLs to increase the chance that a search engine will pick up your content. This is a Search Engine Optimization. Search Engine Optimization gets pages to appear higher in different search engines, such as Google, Yahoo, and Baidu.

What is Search Engine Optimization? Search Engine Optimization is also called SEO for short. It uses both art and science, with the aim of making pages appear higher in different search engines, such as Google, Yahoo, and Baidu. When pages appear higher, it results to having an increase traffic to a website. Basis of Search Engines to Rank a Page Higher

Search engines rank pages so that some appear higher, while others, lower. They use algorithms to do this. Moreover, here are three factors that are used by search engines to evaluate the quality and rank of a page:

Links - A website that has links from other websites (especially those that are established), tend to gain higher rankings. Content - The more relevant the keywords of a page are in relation to what users search for, the higher its ranking tends to be. Page Structure - Page structures which contain relevant keywords in the URL, headers, and title tend to gain higher rankings.

16. Directions: Using the web, enumerate the different propaganda techniquesand provide a short definition or description for each technique.​


Explanation:

propaganda techniques

1. Bandwagon Propaganda

As human beings, we have this innate desire to fit in. And that’s exactly the kind of follow-the-herd mentality this technique follow.

Bandwagon propaganda is all about persuading the target audience to take action. It’s about creating an urge amongst people to become a part of the “in crowd”.

To enforce such a feeling, advertisers typically use phrases like, “join the crowd” or “trending now” for their products and services.

2. Card Stacking Propaganda

Now, this technique is perhaps most popularly used. It involves the deliberate omission of certain facts to fool the target audience. The term card stacking originates from gambling and occurs when players try to stack decks in their favor. A similar ideology is used by companies to make their products appear better than they actually are.

Most brands use this propaganda technique to downplay unsavory details about their products and services. For instance, some companies may cleverly conceal “hidden charges” and only talk about the benefits of their products and services.

3. Plain Folks Propaganda

Have you ever noticed how some brands use ordinary folks to promote their products and services as opposed to celebrities?

The ultimate goal here is to demonstrate how the product is meant for everybody and will provide value to everyone. This method is commonly used in the world of advertising as the general public has started to grow skeptical towards paid celebrity endorsements. Today, buyers are on the lookout for REAL experiences instead of the glossy images portrayed on television.

4. Testimonial Propaganda

Testimonial propaganda is popular advertising technique that uses renowned or celebrity figures to endorse products and services. Now in this case, when a famous person vouches for something, viewers are likely to take account of the credibility and popularity of that person.

This establishes trust and boosts the credibility of that particular brand. Consequently, some brands may also use figures like doctors and engineers (basically experts) to promote their products and services.

5. Glittering Generalities Propaganda

Glittering generalities is a propaganda technique where propagandists use emotional appeal or/and vague statements to influence the audience.

Advertising agencies thus use of phrases like as “inspiring you from within” or “to kick-start your day” to create positive anecdotes. This makes the product look more appealing, resulting in better sales.

6. Name Calling Propaganda

Now, this type of propaganda uses a completely opposite approach to glittering generalities. As the name suggests, this propaganda technique involves using derogatory phrases to create a negative opinion about someone or some other brand.

7. Transfer Propaganda

This type of propaganda uses a technique to project certain qualities (this can either be positive or negative) of a person, ideology or object to other things and people.

This kind of propaganda uses many different symbols to achieve a favorable outcome
