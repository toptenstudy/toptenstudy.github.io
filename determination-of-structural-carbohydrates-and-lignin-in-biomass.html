2. Explain how the structures of biological macromolecules such as carbohydrates, ligids nucleicacid and Proteins determine their properties and Function​


ANSWER:

Biological macromolecules such as carbohydrates, lipids, nucleic acids, and proteins are essential to life and perform a wide range of functions within living organisms. The structures of these macromolecules determine their properties and functions.

Carbohydrates: Carbohydrates are molecules composed of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen. The general formula for carbohydrates is (CH2O)n, where n is the number of carbon atoms in the molecule. Carbohydrates can exist as monosaccharides (simple sugars), disaccharides (two sugar molecules), or polysaccharides (many sugar molecules). The properties of carbohydrates are determined by their structure, such as the type of sugarmolecule, the length of the chain, and the presence of functional groups. For example, glucose is a simple sugar that is the primary source of energy for many organisms, while cellulose is a polysaccharide that provides structural support to plant cells.

Lipids: Lipids are molecules composed of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen. They are hydrophobic (water-fearing) molecules and are not soluble in water. Lipids include fats, oils, phospholipids, and steroids. The properties of lipids are determined by their structure, such as the length of the fatty acid chains, the degree of unsaturation, and the presence of functional groups. For example, phospholipids are a major component of cell membranes and have a hydrophilic (water-loving) head and a hydrophobic tail.

Nucleic acids: Nucleic acids are molecules that store and transmit genetic information. The two types of nucleic acids are DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid) and RNA (ribonucleic acid). The properties of nucleic acids are determined by their structure, such as the sequence of nucleotides (the building blocks of nucleic acids) and the hydrogen bonding between complementary nucleotides. For example, the sequence of nucleotides in DNA determines the genetic code, which is responsible for the inheritance of traits from one generation to the next.

Proteins: Proteins are molecules composed of amino acids linked together by peptide bonds. The properties of proteins are determined by their structure, which includes the sequence of amino acids, the folding of the polypeptide chain, and the presence of functional groups. Proteins have a wide range of functions, including enzymatic catalysis, structural support, and signaling. For example, enzymes are proteins that catalyze chemical reactions, while collagen is a structural protein that provides support to connective tissues.

In summary, the structures of biological macromolecules determine their properties and functions. The specific chemical composition, the length of the molecule, the presence of functional groups, and the way the molecule folds are all important in determining the properties and functions of these essential molecules.

Structural Description/ Class of Carbohydrate

1. Lactose is composed of a molecule of galactose joined to a molecule of glucose.

2. Cellulose is a polysaccharide composed of a linear chain linked d-glucose units with a degree of polymerization ranged from several hundreds to over ten thousands.

3. Fructose can be expressed as a six-carbon linear chain with hydroxyl and carbonyl groups.

4. Maltose is a disaccharide made up of two alpha D glucose in which C1 of first glucose unit is bonded to C4 of second glucose unit

5. Stachyose is a tetrasaccharide consisting of sucrose having an alpha D galactosyl 1-6 alpha D galactosyl moiety attached at the 6 position of the glucose.
