7. how grade 11 students feel about globalization affected their career path local​


globalization can have both positive and negative impacts on career paths.

On the positive side, globalization has opened up new job opportunities and career paths, particularly in industries such as technology, finance, and international trade. Globalization has also made it easier for individuals to work remotely for companies located in different parts of the world, allowing for greater flexibility in career choices.

On the negative side, globalization can result in increased competition for jobs, particularly in industries that are more easily outsourced or offshored. This can lead to job loss or difficulty finding employment in certain fields. Additionally, globalization can also lead to a shift in the types of skills and education that are in demand, which may require individuals to constantly adapt and upgrade their skills to remain competitive in the job market.

Overall, how students feel about the impact of globalization on their career paths will depend on their individual experiences and perspectives, as well as the specific industry or field they are interested in pursuing.

19. What are the factors that will affect your choice in selecting a particular health career pathway?​


•Personality

Your personality consists of relatively stable enduring traits. A match between one’s personality and career are associated with better adjustment at work and job satisfaction.

•Interest

One’s interests, although not as stable as personality can also influence your career decision-making process. Your career interests involve your preferences about work environments and the nature of the work that you want to pursue.

•Values

An understanding of our core values that we consider non-negotiable can help us guide our career decision-making process. A career that clashes with our values can lead to dissonance, influencing our job satisfaction. An avenue that can help you realize and express your core values can also lead to a greater sense of purpose and meaning in your work.

•Perception of careers

Popular media has influenced our perception of careers. There are some careers that are perceived to be more glamorous and desirable than others, solely based on how they are portrayed. However, selecting a career path based on a biased perception may not be an ideal method, leaving us vulnerable to experience shock and regret.

•Skills abilities and talents

The skills, abilities, and talents that you have acquired over the years also play a role in your career choices.

•Culture

The culture that you were exposed to does have a significant role in influencing the values and expectations. One’s selection of a career can be influenced by the cultural norms that an individual is expected to follow. However, these cultural norms can also create expectations and pressure to adhere to them.

•Socio-economic factors

Our career choices occur in the context of the economy and forces that affect the economy. Our own socio-economic status might be a significant determining force that will influence the choice of our career. The remuneration that we receive, our long-term financial aspirations influence our career choices.

•Other career related factors

The prospects for growth within a career, the perquisites it offers, the geographical location of the organization, the opportunity to work from home, job security are other parameters that can influence our career choices

30. what are the possible answers of “exploring the factors affecting humss students career choice ”​


Answer:

There are many possible answers to the question of exploring the factors affecting career choice among HUMSS (Humanities and Social Sciences) students. Some of the factors that may influence their career choice include:

1.)Personal interests and values: HUMSS students may choose careers that align with their personal interests, values, and passions. For example, someone who is interested in helping others may choose a career in social work, while someone who is passionate about history may choose a career in education or research.

2.)Family background and expectations: Family background and expectations can also play a role in career choice. For example, a student whose parents are doctors may be more likely to choose a career in medicine, while a student whose family runs a business may be more likely to pursue entrepreneurship.

3.)Academic performance and aptitude: A student's academic performance and aptitude may influence their career choice. For example, a student who excels in math and science may choose a career in engineering, while a student who performs well in writing and analysis may choose a career in journalism or law.

4.)Career opportunities and job market: Career opportunities and the job market can also play a role in career choice. Students may choose careers that are in high demand or that offer good earning potential.

5.)Personal circumstances: Personal circumstances such as financial situation, geographic location, and access to resources may also impact career choice. For example, a student who needs to support their family financially may choose a career that offers stable income and job security.

These are just a few examples of the many factors that can affect career choice among HUMSS students. It's important to consider each student's individual situation and background when exploring these factors.
