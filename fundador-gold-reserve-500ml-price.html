8. 1. Were spain and Portugal experience scarcity in gold reserves?2. Why were these powerful countries in search of gold in their colonies?3. Were the colonizers able to reap a handful of gold from the Philippines?4. How did this affect the monarch/mother country?​


Answer:

Spain and Portugal did experience scarcity in gold reserves during the 16th and 17th centuries.These countries were in search of gold in their colonies as a way to fund their wars and support their growing empires. Gold was a valuable resource that could be used to trade for other goods and resources, and having a large supply of gold would increase the wealth and power of these countries.The colonizers were able to find some gold in the Philippines, although it was not as abundant as they had hoped. The Philippines was a Spanish colony, and the Spanish did extract some gold from the mines there, but it was not a major source of gold for the Spanish empire.The search for gold in the colonies would have had a significant impact on the monarchs and mother countries of Spain and Portugal. The gold that was obtained would have helped to fund their wars and support their empires, but the cost of colonization and the efforts to extract gold from the colonies would also have been a burden on these countries.
