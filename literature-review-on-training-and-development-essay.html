3. explain the steps in developing literature review ​


Developing a literature review involves the following steps:

Define the research question or topic: Before starting a literature review, it is essential to have a clear research question or topic. This will help to focus on the relevant literature that is related to the research question.

Conduct a search for literature: The next step is to conduct a search for literature. This involves searching various databases, journals, books, and other relevant sources to identify the literature that is related to the research question.

Evaluate the literature: Once the literature is identified, the next step is to evaluate it. This involves reading the literature and determining its relevance to the research question. The literature should be critically evaluated for its quality, credibility, and relevance.

Organize the literature: After evaluating the literature, it is important to organize it. This involves categorizing the literature based on the themes, concepts, or ideas that emerge from the literature.

Synthesize the literature: The next step is to synthesize the literature. This involves integrating the information from the literature to provide a comprehensive understanding of the research question. Synthesizing the literature can involve identifying gaps in the literature, identifying trends or patterns in the literature, or identifying conflicting findings in the literature.

Write the literature review: The final step is to write the literature review. The literature review should be written in a clear, concise, and organized manner. It should provide a summary of the literature that is relevant to the research question, and it should be structured in a logical and coherent way. The literature review should also highlight the key findings from the literature and identify the implications for the research question.

17. short essay about traditional and systematic reviews of literature?​


Answer:

Traditional reviews provide a broad overview of a research topic with no clear methodological approach Systematic reviews are overviews of the literature undertaken by identifying, critically appraising and synthesising results of primary research studies using an explicit, methodological approach.

Answer:

A narrative or traditional literature review is a comprehensive, critical and objective analysis of the current knowledge on a topic. They are an essential part of the research process and help to establish a theoretical framework and focus or context for your research.

Traditional reviews provide a broad overview of a research topic with no clear methodological approach(2). Information is collected and interpreted unsystematically with subjective summaries of findings. Authors aim to describe and discuss the literature from a contextual or theoretical point of view. Although the reviews may be conducted by topic experts, due to preconceived ideas or conclusions, they could be subject to bias.

Systematic reviews are overviews of the literature undertaken by identifying, critically appraising and synthesising results of primary research studies using an explicit, methodological approach(3). They aim to summarise the best available evidence on a particular research topic.

22. PR2ESSAYexplain the important of review literature related in different type of research. PA HELP!​


ESSAY:

The review of literature is an essential part of any research study because it provides a background and context for the research. It helps to establish the significance of the research and its contribution to the field.

In a review of literature, researchers gather and critically evaluate existing research on a particular topic. This allows them to identify gaps and inconsistencies in the existing knowledge, as well as to identify trends and patterns. By doing this, researchers can build a solid foundation for their own research and demonstrate its relevance and importance.

A review of literature can also help researchers to avoid duplication of effort and to learn from the mistakes of others. By critically evaluating the existing research, researchers can identify which research methods and approaches have been successful in the past, and which have not. This can help them to choose the most appropriate methods and techniques for their own research, and to avoid common pitfalls.

Furthermore, a review of literature can help researchers to identify potential collaborators and to develop new research partnerships. By reading the existing research, researchers can learn about the work of other researchers in their field, and can identify potential areas for collaboration. This can lead to the development of new research partnerships, which can help to advance the field and generate new insights.

In short, the review of literature is an essential part of any research study because it provides a background and context for the research, helps to establish its significance and relevance, and can help researchers to avoid duplication of effort and to develop new research partnerships.
