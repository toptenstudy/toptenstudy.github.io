Answer:

Education: Business and industry can have a significant impact on education. One of the ways this can happen is through the creation of jobs that require certain educational qualifications. For example, as a technology company grows and expands, it may require employees with specialized computer science or engineering degrees. This can lead to an increase in enrollment in related fields of study at universities and vocational schools, as well as an increase in investment in education programs in these areas.

Additionally, businesses and industries may also provide funding for education through scholarships, grants, and other forms of financial aid. This can help to make higher education more accessible to individuals from diverse backgrounds, including those from low-income families.

Income: Business and industry can have a significant impact on income levels, both directly and indirectly. Directly, businesses and industries provide employment opportunities, and the wages and benefits earned by workers can have a significant impact on their overall income levels. As businesses and industries grow and expand, they can create more job opportunities and raise wages, leading to an increase in income for workers.

Indirectly, business and industry can also have an impact on income levels through the ripple effect of economic growth. As businesses and industries grow, they can stimulate economic growth and lead to an increase in consumer spending, which in turn can lead to more job opportunities and higher wages.

Occupation: Business and industry can also have an impact on the types of occupations that are in demand. As certain businesses and industries grow and expand, they may require employees with specific skills and qualifications, which can lead to an increase in demand for certain occupations. For example, as the renewable energy industry grows, there may be an increased demand for engineers and technicians with expertise in solar and wind power.

Additionally, businesses and industries can also create new occupational opportunities. For example, the growth of the technology industry has led to the development of new occupational fields such as software development, data science, and IT.

On the other hand, business and industry can also have a negative impact on occupation, as it can lead to job loss or redundancy due to automation, outsourcing or offshoring. Especially in field such as manufacturing.

Overall, business and industry can have a significant impact on the socioeconomic factors of education, income, and occupation. While the effects can be positive, it's important to also consider potential negative impacts and work to mitigate them.

23. discuss socioeconomic study and socioeconomic factors​


Answer:

Socio-economics is the study of the relationship between economic activity and social life. In many cases, however, socio-economists focus on the social impact of some sort of economic change. Such changes might include displacement due to development work, a closing factory, village to city migration, market manipulation and even the signing of trade treaties etc.

Socioeconomic factors are measured by the position of the household (individual indicators of education, occupation, income, marital status) or by area-based indicators of deprivation.

• GENETICS

- People's genetics play a large role in their health, their lifespan and their probability of contracting certain diseases. There are also other personal factors that affect health, such as a person’s coping skills and behavior. Do they keep active? Do they smoke? Do they drink alcohol? How do they deal with stress? All of these things have an impact on the health of an individual.

• HEALTH SERVICES

- The ability of people to access and use quality health facilities has an effect on their health. Of particular importance is their access to preventive medication and procedures that protect them from certain diseases.

• ACADEMIC ACHIEVEMENT

- Children who come from families of a lower socioeconomic status will be behind their peers from higher socioeconomic backgrounds by an average of five years by the time they enter high school. The high school dropout rate in 2014 among 16- to 24-year-olds was highest among low-income families. The percentage of dropouts in low-income families was 11.6, while the percentage in high-income families was 2.8. Students who come from low-income families also tend to do worse in the STEM (science, technology, engineering, mathematics) fields than their counterparts from high-income families. According to the United States Census Bureau, in 2014, individuals from families in the top 25 percent are up to eight times more likely to complete a bachelor’s degree before age 24 than their counterparts from low-income families.

• PSYCHOLOGICAL HEALTH

- Students from families of a low socioeconomic status are more likely to suffer from negative psychological conditions, such as learning disabilities, which will have an adverse effect on their academic achievement. These students are exposed to more adversity and toxic stress, especially in early childhood, which will impact their behavior, health and learning abilities in a lasting manner. These children are twice as likely as those from high-income families to exhibit behavior problems related to learning. The socioeconomic status of the mother will also affect the child’s level of interest, attention, and cooperation in school.

• CAREER ASPIRATIONS

- There is a link between socioeconomic status and the career trajectory and aspirations of an individual, as well as the resulting achievements. Such things as socioeconomic, ethnic and racial barriers affect an individual’s ability to get ahead career-wise. Those from poor backgrounds, women, people of color, the disabled and those of an alternative sexual orientation are the most affected. Individuals who come from a higher socioeconomic status, on the other hand, will have a higher probability of success in their careers and will be better prepared for the work environment because of their access to such things as career guidance counselors, better schools and higher quality education.
