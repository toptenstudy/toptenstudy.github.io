= [tex] \sqrt{900 + 1600} [/tex]

13. what is air traffic and meaning


Answer:

Air traffic is the movement of aircraft through the airspace of a region, including the number and types of aircraft, their flight paths, and the associated communication and navigation activities. Air traffic control is the process that is used to ensure that aircraft stay safely separated while they are in the air

Answer:

Air traffic refers to the movement of aircraft in the airspace over a particular area. This includes all planes, helicopters, and other aircraft that are flying within a specific region. Air traffic control is the system that is responsible for managing and directing this movement to ensure the safe and efficient operation of the airspace. This includes issuing instructions and clearances to pilots, monitoring the position and altitude of aircraft, and coordinating the movements of different aircraft to prevent collisions. Air traffic control is an important safety feature that helps to ensure the safe and efficient operation of the aviation industry.

17. air traffic controller with speech example​


Answer:

Here's an example of an air traffic controller's speech:

"Good morning, this is Manila Control Tower. Delta 456, you are cleared for takeoff on runway 24. Wind direction is northeast at 5 knots. Maintain runway heading until reaching 3000 feet, then turn left heading 270. Contact departure control on frequency 120.1 once airborne. Have a safe flight."

This speech is an example of an air traffic controller issuing takeoff instructions to a pilot of an aircraft with the call sign Delta 456. The controller starts by identifying themselves and the location of the control tower. They then provide the pilot with clearance to take off from runway 24, and inform them of the wind direction and speed.

The controller then instructs the pilot to maintain the runway heading until reaching a specific altitude, and provides a heading for the pilot to follow once they reach that altitude. Finally, the controller provides the pilot with the frequency for the next controller to contact, and wishes them a safe flight.
