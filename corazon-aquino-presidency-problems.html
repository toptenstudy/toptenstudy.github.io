1. How did President Corazon Aquino deal with the problems that plagued the country??


When President Corazon Aquino took office in 1986, the Philippines was facing significant political and economic challenges. She faced a communist insurgency, economic stagnation, and a mounting foreign debt. To address these problems, she implemented a series of reforms and initiatives, including:

Restoring democracy: Aquino restored democracy and the rule of law in the Philippines by calling for a new constitution, freeing political prisoners, and ending martial law.

Promoting human rights: She established the Presidential Committee on Human Rights, which investigated human rights abuses and punished those responsible.

Land reform: Aquino implemented a comprehensive agrarian reform program that distributed land to farmers and promoted rural development.

Addressing corruption: She created the Presidential Commission on Good Government, which was tasked with recovering the ill-gotten wealth of the Marcos regime and prosecuting those responsible for corruption.

Economic reform: Aquino implemented a series of economic reforms, including trade liberalization, deregulation, and privatization of state-owned enterprises, to stimulate economic growth and attract foreign investment.

Overall, Aquino's approach to dealing with the problems in the country was through a combination of political, economic, and social reforms. Despite facing numerous challenges and obstacles during her presidency, she was able to help lay the foundation for a more stable and prosperous Philippines.
