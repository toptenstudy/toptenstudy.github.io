29. - In a Math class, 7 pupils received a grade of 95. If 14% of the class got 95, howmany pupils are there in the class?​


PROBLEM:

In a Math class, 7 pupils received a grade of 95. If 14% of the class got 95, how many pupils are there in the class?

SOLUTION:

In order to find the total number of pupils in the class, we need to apply what we've learned about base, ratio and percentage.

The unknown quantity is the base, thus, we'll use the formula:

[tex]\large \begin{array}{l} \tt Percentage=Base×Rate \end{array}[/tex]

Let's solve by substituting the given to the formula.

[tex]\large \begin{array}{l} \tt 7=Base×0.14 \\ \tt Base = \frac{7}{0.14} \\ \tt Base = 50 \end{array}[/tex]

Notice that the rate of 14% is converted first to decimal before solving. You just need to divide 14 by 100 or simply move the decimal point two units to the left.

ANSWER:

There are a total of fifty pupils in the class.
