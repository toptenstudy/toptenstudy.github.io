Scoring Rules.

There are various types of rules in volleyball that players and officials must follow during a game. Here are some of the most important rules:

Scoring rules: In volleyball, teams score points by hitting the ball over the net and making it land in the opponent's court. Each successful hit is worth one point, and the first team to reach 25 points (with a two-point lead) wins the set.
Serving rules: The server must stand behind the end line and hit the ball over the net with an underhand or overhand motion. The ball must clear the net and land in the opponent's court. If the serve hits the net and goes over, it is still in play.

Passing rules: Players are not allowed to carry, catch, or throw the ball. Instead, they must pass it using their hands or arms. The ball must be hit cleanly, without holding or carrying it.

Blocking rules: Players can jump and reach over the net to block the ball, but they cannot touch the ball before it crosses the net. A block does not count as a hit, and the team that blocks the ball can still hit it three times before sending it over to the other side.

Net violation rules: Players are not allowed to touch the net with any part of their body while the ball is in play. If a player touches the net, it results in a point for the opposing team.

Rotation rules: Players must rotate positions on the court after each side-out. This means that they must move clockwise around the court and switch positions with their teammates.

These are just some of the rules in volleyball, and there are many more specific regulations that vary depending on the level of play and the organization governing the game.
