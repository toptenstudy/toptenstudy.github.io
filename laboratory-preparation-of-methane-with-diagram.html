14. We use water in the laboratory for various uses such as the preparation of culture media in PCR (polymerase chain reaction) assays and in immunoassays, among others. What factors influence the water quality in these laboratory procedures?​


The water used in these laboratory procedures should preferably be distilled water. Thus, factors such as contamination, temperature and the presence of other undesirable compounds in the water can affect these procedures.

WATER IN LABOTATORIES

It is known that water is the fundamental substance for organic reactions to occur. In this sense, it can be abundantly used in various processes involving living beings and living organisms.

In the case of culture preparation, assays or other laboratory procedures, it is important that the water used is as clean as possible, that is, preferably distilled water. This is due to the fact that components present in the water can react with other substances and compromise the quality of the procedures.

28. A solution of sulfur and methane was prepared by adding methane to sulfur until the total volume becomes 135mL. By volume, the solution is 30.0% sulfur. How much sulfur, in mL, is in the solution? A. 41.5mL of sulfur B. 42.5mL of sulfur C. 40.5mL of sulfur


Answer:

C.

Explanation:

Formula for %volume:

%volume = volume of solute / total volume of solution

To find volume of solute or volume of sulfur, rearrange formula. Becomes:

volume of solute = %volume x total volume of solution

Substitute:

volume of solute = 0.3 x 135 ml = 40.5 ml sulfur

Answer:

Intramolecular forces are those within the molecule that keep the molecule together, for example, the bonds between the atoms.

Explanation:

Intermolecular forces are the attractions between molecules, which determine many of the physical properties of a substance. the main difference between intermolecular and intramolecular forces is that intermolecular forces exist between the molecules themselves, whereas intramolecular forces exist between atoms within a molecule.
