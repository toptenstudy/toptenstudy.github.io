1. You have been hired as a new Finance VP, and you oversee a team of almost 30 people. Your HR manager has recently informed you that there have been several employee relations in your group in the recent past, and you are concerned about the level of knowledge that your management team has around dealing with these issues. What could you do to close the gap in knowledge and mitigate the risk of issues in your group? (essay) ​


As a new Finance VP, it is important to address any employee relations issues in your group to maintain a positive and productive work environment. One way to mitigate the risk of these issues is to ensure that your management team has the knowledge and skills to effectively manage any employee relations issues that may arise.

One approach you could take is to provide your management team with training and resources to deal effectively with employee relations issues. This could include training on topics such as communication skills, conflict resolution, and diversity and inclusion. Giving your management team access to these resources can help them feel more secure and equipped to deal with any employee relationship issues that may arise.

Another approach you could take is to establish clear policies and procedures for dealing with employee relations issues. This could include establishing a clear process for reporting and dealing with issues, and outlining managers' expectations and responsibilities in dealing with those issues. By providing your management team with clear guidelines and expectations, you can help ensure that any issues are addressed in a consistent and effective manner.

In addition to training and establishing clear policies and procedures, it can also be helpful to regularly visit and support your management team. This could include regular meetings where managers can discuss any challenges or issues they are facing, as well as access to additional support and resources to help them navigate complex employee relations issues. By providing ongoing support and guidance, you can help your management team feel more confident and empowered to deal with any employee relationship issues that may arise.

Overall, closing the gap in knowledge and mitigating the risk of employee relations issues in your group will require a combination of training, clear policies and procedures, and ongoing support and guidance. By taking these steps, you can help to ensure that your management team is equipped to handle any employee relations issues that may come up, and maintain a positive and productive work environment for your team.
