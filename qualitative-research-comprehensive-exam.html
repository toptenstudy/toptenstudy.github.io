1. comprehensive definition of research and qualitative research. Prepare a brief explanation for each definition.​


Answer:

Research refers to the systematic and scientific investigation of a specific topic or problem in order to discover new knowledge or understand existing knowledge better. Research can be either quantitative or qualitative in nature.

Qualitative research is a research method that aims to understand and interpret social phenomena through the collection and analysis of non-numerical data, such as interviews, observations, and written or visual materials. It is used to explore and understand the meaning and experiences of individuals and groups. The goal of qualitative research is to provide a rich, detailed, and in-depth understanding of the topic or phenomenon being studied.

10. Distinguish Qualitative Research from Qualitative Research


QUALITATIVE

- interpretation-based, descriptive, and relating to language

- focuses more on personal accounts or documents that illustrate in detail how people think or respond within society

- categorical variable

- involve “open-ended” research verbs such as explore, understand, narrate, articulate [meanings], discover, or develop.

- depends on the verbal and written data

- collected using questionnaires, interviews, or observation, and frequently appears in narrative form

QUANTITATIVE

- numbers-based, countable, or measurable

- "what" or "how many" aspects of a research question

- numeric variable

- involve “closed-ended” research verbs such as determine, measure, correlate, explain, compare, validate, identify, or examine

- utilizes statistical and analytical statements to get a conclusion

- gathered using instruments, such as a questionnaire/surveys which includes a ratings scale or a thermometer to collect weather data

19. what is the qualitative research design give at least three essential characteristic of qualitative research?​


Answer:

Naturalistic Inquiry

Qualitative observational research is naturalistic because it studies a group in its natural setting. Patton explains, "Naturalistic inquiry is thus contrasted to experimental research where the investigator attempts to completely control the condition of the study" (p. 42). For example, if you wanted to study college students who were speakers of BEV, you would not conduct your research in a predominantly Caucasian college or university.

Inductive analysis

This characteristic is prevalent in qualitative research because it allows the observer to become immersed in a group. The researcher starts with answers, but forms questions throughout the research process. Hypotheses and theories can continuously change depending on what the observer wants to know. For instance, an observer might realize that the purpose of many of BEV speech acts is to build up the reputation of the speaker. Thus, the observer's job is to find out why. This could lead to further research into the rhetorical strategies and purposes of BEV.

Holistic perspective

Patton states, "[A] holistic approach assumes that the whole is greater than the sum of its parts" (p. 40). In other words, almost every action or communication must be taken as a part of the whole phenomenon of a certain community or culture. However, this characteristic of qualitative observational research can be bothersome because it can lead researchers into taking every little action into consideration when writing a narrative. For instance, a researcher might notice that many speakers of BEV employ a particular rhetorical strategy in their writing. However, this phenomenon might not have anything to do with BEV and its traditions or strategies. It might be linked to something else in their lives.
