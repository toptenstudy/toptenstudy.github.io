3. compare how the soda liquid flows with how lava flows


Soda liquid is a low-viscosity fluid, which means that it has a low resistance to flow. It is also relatively thin and has a low density. As a result, soda liquid flows quickly and smoothly, and it spreads out evenly over a surface. When poured, soda liquid usually forms a thin stream or spray, and it can be easily poured from one container to another.

On the other hand, lava is a high-viscosity fluid, which means that it has a high resistance to flow. It is also relatively thick and has a high density. As a result, lava flows slowly and sluggishly, and it tends to form thick, lumpy masses as it cools. When lava erupts from a volcano, it often forms a molten river or stream that can carve out new channels and bury existing structures.

Both soda liquid and lava are fluids that flow, they have different properties that determine how they flow. Soda liquid is a low-viscosity fluid that flows quickly and smoothly, while lava is a high-viscosity fluid that flows slowly and sluggishly, forming thick masses as it cools.
