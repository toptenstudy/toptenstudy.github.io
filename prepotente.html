Answer:

A prepositional phrase includes a preposition, its object, and any modifiers of the object. Ex. “With a reusable tote in hand, Matthew walked to the farmer's market.” A preposition draws a relationship between a noun or pronoun and another word in a sentence. Some examples of prepositions are words like "in," "at," "on," "of," and "to."
