9. What unit operation originated in trona process?


To produce ash from trona ore, a series of refining steps are required. First, the raw ore is crushed and screened. It is then fed to rotary calciners and heated. In this process, the trona decomposes to form crude soda ash, which is dissolved in water. The insoluble shales are separated from the solution by a combination of settling and filtration steps and the resulting insoluble tailings are taken back into the mine as backfill. The soda ash solution is treated to remove organic materials yielding a high-purity saturated solution of sodium carbonate.

Next, the solution is fed to crystallizers where water is evaporated and sodium carbonate monohydrate crystals are formed. The industry-familiar term "mono-process" originates from this process step.

How to Calculate Cost Per Unit?/

There are four main parts to calculating the cost per unit. The steps involved include:

1. Determine your fixed costs

Fixed costs are costs that remain the same over time. Fixed costs are independent of your unit production, meaning no matter how many units you produce or how much demand you have, your fixed costs will remain.

Fixed costs include items such as office space rent, business insurance, annual employee salaries and benefits, equipment rental and property taxes. This is not a complete list of possible fixed costs. Keep in mind that every company is different and what contributes to fixed costs can also be different.

2. Identify your variable costs

Variable costs are costs that can change periodically. These changes can occur from day to day, month to month, quarterly, yearly or even between production times.

Unlike fixed costs, variable costs depend on the number of units you produce, and can change from one calculation to the next. Variable costs are the sum of both direct labor costs, or how much you spend paying hourly employees or freelancers to help make the product, and direct materials costs, or how much you spend on the materials you need for your product.

3. Know how many units you produce

The last number you need to calculate the cost per unit is the number of units you produce. For example, if you make 100 candles every month, your unit number is 100.

This can be the units you produce each month or quarter or you can calculate costs based on how many units you produce in a given production period. Keep in mind that all units of measurement must remain the same, so if you use the unit number every month, your fixed and variable costs must also be monthly.

4. Enter fixed costs, variable costs, and number of units into the formula

To complete the unit cost calculation, you must add up your fixed and variable costs and divide that number by the number of units you produce. The calculation of the cost per unit is:

Cost Per Unit = (Total Fixed Cost + Total Variable Cost) / Total Unit Produced

Cost per unit means more than how much it costs to produce one unit of your product. It also represents your break-even point, or the minimum you have to sell an item before you can start making a profit.

21. -40. What are the difference of the unit of Operations in Computer System?​


Answer:

The difference between the units of operation in a computer system refers to the distinct tasks they perform and their functions within the system. Each unit has a unique role and is designed to perform specific tasks. For example, the CPU is responsible for executing instructions and performing arithmetic and logical operations, while the memory unit stores data temporarily for processing by the CPU. The input unit receives data from external devices, the output unit displays processed data on external devices, the storage unit stores data and programs permanently, and the control unit coordinates the operations of all other units.

In summary, each unit of operation in a computer system has a distinct function and plays a unique role in the overall operation of the computer. These units work together to ensure that the computer system functions efficiently and performs the intended tasks.
