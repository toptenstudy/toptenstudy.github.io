"To convert a magnetic azimuth to a grid azimuth, subtract G-M angle,"

for example. If you have a magnetic azimuth of 270 degrees and an 8-degree G-M angle, your grid azimuth is 262 degrees.

The conversion (whether to add or subtract) is determined by whether the G-M angle on your map is easterly or westerly. Your map has an easterly G-M angle if your magnetic north is to the right (east) of the grid north. Your map has a westerly G-M angle if your magnetic north is to the left (west) of the grid north.
