1. what is commission as being used in business context?​


Answer:

Commission is a payment made to employees based on the value of sales achieved. It can form all or (more often) part of a pay package. Commission is, therefore, a form of "incentive pay" (see also profit-related pay, bonuses). ... After all, businesses exist to sell goods and services for profit – not just to make things.


2. Would there be some disadvantages when a business is implementing commissions?​


Answer:

Disadvantages of Commission-based Pay

Instead of generating a sale, they can discourage people from buying their offerings. The same goes for overly aggressive sales methods wherein new customers may be turned off by too much hard selling and other high-pressure tactics.

Explanation:

In most jurisdictions, sole proprietorships do not need to secure a Security and Exchange Commission (SEC) registration certificate, as they are typically not considered separate legal entities and do not have shareholders or issue securities to the public.
