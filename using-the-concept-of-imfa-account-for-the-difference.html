4. what is interesting fact about IMFA ​


Answer:

The four key intermolecular forces are as follows: Ionic bonds > Hydrogen bonding > Van der Waals dipole-dipole interactions > Van der Waals dispersion forces.

Intermolecular forces are electrostatic in nature; that is, they arise from the interaction between positively and negatively charged species. Like covalent and ionic bonds, intermolecular interactions are the sum of both attractive and repulsive components.

8. 2. Using the concept of IMFA, account for the difference in boiling point and melting point of the following molecular substances: SUBSTANCES. BOILING MELTING Helium gas, He — - 269 — - 272 Methane, CH,. — - 162 — - 184 H₂O — 100 — 0Ethanol, C,H,OH — 78 — - 117​


Answer:

IMFA (Intermolecular Forces of Attraction) is the attractive forces that exist between the molecules of a substance. These forces affect the physical properties of substances such as boiling and melting points.

Helium gas (He) has the lowest boiling and melting points on the list because it consists of individual helium atoms with weak London dispersion forces as the only intermolecular force. This means that helium atoms have very low IMFA, and hence very low boiling and melting points.

Methane (CH4) has a slightly higher boiling and melting point compared to helium gas because it has a small dipole moment due to the difference in electronegativity between hydrogen and carbon atoms, which gives rise to weak dipole-dipole interactions. The London dispersion forces also contribute to the IMFA in methane, but the dipole-dipole interactions are slightly stronger. Hence, the boiling and melting points of methane are higher than helium gas.

Water (H2O) has higher boiling and melting points compared to helium and methane because it has a stronger type of dipole-dipole interaction called hydrogen bonding. Hydrogen bonding is a special type of dipole-dipole interaction that occurs between hydrogen atoms covalently bonded to oxygen, nitrogen, or fluorine atoms in neighboring molecules. The strong hydrogen bonding interactions between water molecules result in higher boiling and melting points.

Ethanol (C2H5OH) has even higher boiling and melting points compared to methane and water due to its stronger IMFA. Ethanol has both hydrogen bonding and dipole-dipole interactions between the polar hydroxyl (OH) group and the rest of the molecule. These interactions are stronger than the dipole-dipole interactions in methane and water, resulting in higher boiling and melting points.

In summary, the boiling and melting points of the substances listed are determined by the strength of their IMFA, with the weakest IMFA in helium and the strongest in ethanol.
