1. what principles and process/methodologies are used in community information collection?​


What principles and process/methodologies are used in community information collection?​

Community-based participatory research (CBPR) is a type of research that entails a collective, reflective, and systematic inquiry in which researchers and community stakeholders participate as equal partners in all stages of the research process with the goal of educating, improving practice, or bringing about social group and occurrence changes.

FORMING A RESEARCH QUESTION: In a community-based research project, forming a solid research question is a vital initial step. Developing a Research Question is a presentation that outlines crucial considerations to keep in mind during this process.

QUALITATIVE IN-DEPTH INTERVIEWS: A qualitative in-depth interview is a type of study in which researchers ask participants largely open-ended questions and record their answers for subsequent transcription and analysis. Qualitative interviews, in contrast to more structured survey questions, are frequently semi-structured and fluid, allowing researchers to follow up on developing themes with further questions. In-depth interviews are beneficial for studying the complexity of the social environment, including the varied textures woven into everyday life as well as research participants' viewpoints, experiences, meanings, and imaginings.

DIGITAL STORYTELLING: VISUAL AND CREATIVE METHODS The Empower Project prepared this brief guidebook. It explains what digital storytelling is and how it may be put to use. Those interested in learning more about the advantages, problems, ethical issues, and planning stages of digital storytelling will find this material valuable.

Photo Voice is a type of participatory photography research in which participants are requested to capture photos that reflect their community or convey their point of view in order to investigate a topic. This poster presentation at the Annual Canadian Conference on HIV, titled "Take a picture, it will endure longer: Peer-driven community action through photography," is an excellent example of a community-based photovoice research on HIV/AIDS and housing in BC.
