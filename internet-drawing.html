Answer:

Copyright: A copyright is a legal right that gives the creator of an original work exclusive rights to use, reproduce, distribute, and sell that work for a certain period of time.

Plagiarism: Plagiarism is the act of using someone else's work or ideas as if they were your own, without giving proper credit to the original source.

fair use: so fair use law depends solely on the country it is used but it is a copyright law that allows people to use copyrighted material without permission in certain circumstances.

cyberbullying: cyberbullying is the act of harrasing intimidating or any act of harm to a person moral or personal mentality through the use of technology such as social media.

internet addiction: a term use to people who are excessively using their phones to browse social media or any kind of online platform for a long period of time, this is very harmful to one's health as it may can inflict serious damage to the eyes and mental health of a person.

responsible netizenship: Responsible netizenship refers to the ethical and respectful use of the internet and related technologies.
