2. what is sacred place,set apart from the profane,ordinary world?.​


Answer:

Sanctuary, in religion, a sacred place, set apart from the profane, ordinary world.

Explanation:

Originally, sanctuaries were natural locations, such as groves or hills, where the divine or sacred was believed to be especially present. Special taboos and rules prevented the profanation of sanctuaries.

Scripture, also called sacred scripture, the revered texts, or Holy Writ, of the world's religions. Scriptures comprise a large part of the literature of the world.
