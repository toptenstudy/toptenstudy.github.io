13. what are the advantages and disadvantages of using broad inclusive definitions?


Answer:

Globalisation as it currently is serves some of interests well and a lot of our interests very poorly.

As Adam Smith realised, trade without currency regulation (or without any regulation at all) creates market distortions. Smith’s ideal, of everyone winning from trade, is not the case in our current form of globalisation.

There are many areas of the economy that are very definitely not globalised. Many people think their jobs are at risk if we allow free movement of people and remove national restrictions on employment, but in fact this would provide economic advantages that are not currently available by getting the right people in the right jobs - wherever they come from.

Recent trade agreements have actually not been about globalisation, but focus more on restricting government’s powers to stop multinational corporations from ravaging their environment and economy.

Finally, globalisation must consider ‘externalities’. These are the things that a company doesn’t have to worry about when it is manufacturing, because the community will bear the cost. Externalities include things such as pollution, resource depletion, worker safety etc. - where companies do not pay for the cost to the community/country of the damage they cause by their operations. As wealthier countries start to put a cost upon these externalities, companies are moving to those countries that do not - and continuing to pollute the air that all of us will eventually breathe.

We - as a global community - need to take a close look at the benefits and costs of globalisation as it is and as it could be, and decide what kind of world we want our children to live in.

Do you agree with the idea that the contemporary world is characterized by high liquidity? Why or why not? ​


Answer:

1.) Advantages of using broad and inclusive definitions of globalization include the ability to capture a wide range of interconnected processes and phenomena, such as economic, cultural, and political interactions between nations. This can provide a comprehensive understanding of the complexity of globalization and its various impacts. On the other hand, disadvantages of broad and inclusive definitions include the potential for confusion and lack of precision in understanding specific aspects of globalization.

Narrow and exclusive definitions, on the other hand, have the advantage of providing a more focused and specific understanding of globalization, such as the economic dimension. This can be useful in studying specific aspects of globalization and their impacts. However, the disadvantage of using narrow and exclusive definitions is that they may exclude important aspects of globalization and lead to a limited understanding of the phenomenon.

2.)The importance of defining globalization is that it helps to understand the various processes and phenomena that constitute it and their implications. A clear and accurate definition is necessary for effective analysis and policy-making related to globalization.

3.)The idea that the contemporary world is characterized by high liquidity refers to the increased speed and ease of movement of goods, capital, information, and people across borders. Some may agree with this idea, as globalization has led to increased interconnectedness and mobility. However, others may disagree, noting that barriers to movement and trade still exist, and that not everyone has equal access to the benefits of globalization.
