24. Essay about Philippine Election â€‹


Answer:

The Philippine election is a process that is held every three years to elect the president and vice president of the country. It is a democratic process that allows citizens to choose their leaders and representatives in government. The election system in the Philippines has been in place since 1935, when it was established by the Commonwealth Constitution. Since then, it has undergone several changes and reforms to ensure its fairness and transparency.

The Philippine election system consists of two stages: first, a national election for President and Vice President; second, local elections for governors, mayors, councilors, etc. In both stages of the elections, candidates are chosen through an open nomination process where any citizen can nominate themselves or another person as a candidate for office. The Commission on Elections (COMELEC) oversees all aspects of the electoral process from registration to voting day itself.

In order to be eligible to vote in Philippine elections one must be at least 18 years old on Election Day and must have registered with COMELEC at least 30 days prior to Election Day. On Election Day itself there are various forms of voting available including manual voting (ballot boxes), automated voting (electronic machines), absentee ballots (for overseas Filipinos) or even mobile polling stations set up by COMELEC for remote areas without access to traditional polling places.

The results of each election are determined by counting all valid votes cast during the day-long event which usually takes place on May 9th each year â€“ this date being chosen as it marks Araw ng Kalayaan or â€œDay of Freedomâ€ which commemorates Philippine independence from Spain in 1898. Once all votes have been counted COMELEC declares who has won each seat based on who received more than 50% plus one vote â€“ if no candidate receives this majority then a run-off between top two candidates will take place within 45 days after Election Day itself until one candidate receives more than 50% plus one vote thus winning their seat in government office officially declared by COMELEC once again after run-off results have been tallied up correctly according to law regulations set out by them beforehand before any official declaration can be made publically known about who won what seat during said electoral event taking place throughout Philippines nation wide every three years come rain or shine!
