1. Lithium- Last Shell Electron Configuration, Highest Energy Level, and number of Valence Electron


Answer:

Lithium has three electrons. The electron configuration for lithium is 1s² 2s¹.

The highest energy level for lithium is n=2.

Lithium has one valence electron, which is the electron in the outermost shell or highest energy level. The valence electron of lithium is in the 2s orbital.

28. Lithium has two known stable isotopes—lithium-6 and lithium-7. Lithium-6 has an isotopicmass of 6.015 amu and percent abundance of 7.59%; lithium-7 has a mass of 7.016 amu at 92.41%abundance. Calculate the average atomic mass of lithium.​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & A_1 = \text{6.015 amu} \\ & f_1 = 7.59\% = 0.0759 \\ & A_2 = \text{7.016 amu} \\ & f_2 = 92.41\% = 0.9241 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the atomic mass of chlorine.

[tex]\begin{aligned} A & = A_1f_1 + A_2f_2 \\ & = \text{(6.015 amu)(0.0759) + (7.016 amu)(0.9241)} \\ & = \boxed{\text{6.940 amu}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the average atomic mass of lithium is 6.940 amu.

30. Line spectrum of lithium has a res line at 670.8 nm. Calculate the energy of a photon with this wavelength


The energy of a photon : 2.96.10⁻¹⁹ J

Further explanation

The photoelectric effect is an electron coming out of a metal because of electromagnetic radiation

One type of electromagnetic radiation is light

Electrons can come out of metal because they absorb electromagnetic energy radiated on metals. There is also kinetic energy released from metal, which is according to the equation:

[tex]\large {\boxed {\bold {E = hf-hfo}}}[/tex]

fo = the threshold frequency of electromagnetic waves

Radiation energy is absorbed by photons

The energy in one photon can be formulated as

[tex]\rm E = h \times f[/tex]

Where

E = energy of light, J

h = Planck's constant (6,626.10⁻³⁴ Js)

f = Frequency of electromagnetic waves, Hz

f = c / λ

c = speed of light

= 3.10⁸

λ = wavelength

The spectrum of lithium line has a red line at 670.8 nm

So  the energy of a photon

[tex]\rm E=\dfrac{h\times c}{\lambda}\\\\E=\dfrac{6.626.10^{-34}\times 3.10^8}{670.8.10^{-9}}\\\\E=2.96.10^{-19}\:J[/tex]
