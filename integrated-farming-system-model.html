1. Which atomic model is proposed by schrodinger?a. Nuclear modelb. Planetary modelc. Raisin bread modeld. Quantum Mechanical Model


Erwin Schrödinger proposed the quantum mechanical model of the atom, which treats electrons as matter waves.

Theory of Quantum Mechanical Models

Erwin Schrodinger, expressed his opinion regarding the atomic theory of quantum mechanics by saying that electrons can be considered as waves of matter whose motion can be equalized with wave motion. His statement can also be called wave mechanics or also known as quantum mechanics.

Erwin Schrodinger also said that the position of electrons in atoms cannot be determined with certainty, and what can be determined is only the probability or the area of their possible existence. Orbital is the space that has the greatest probability that an electron can be found.

In the quantum mechanical model, atoms are explained that have electrons that move like a wave that revolves around a certain orbit like planets around the sun. The electrons or waves do not have a certain position in space, so it is difficult to determine. The position of the electron can only be found in a room through its probabilities or chances. The greater the opportunity, the more electrons will be found. The position is then described by means of quantum numbers.

So it can be concluded that the atomic model based on the principle of electrons moving like waves is the quantum mechanical model of the atom.

13. Why is integrated farming important​


Answer:

1.Increased productivity: Integrated farming can increase productivity by combining multiple crops and livestock in the same farming system. This can help to optimize land use, reduce pests and diseases, and increase yields.

2.Sustainable farming practices: Integrated farming promotes sustainable farming practices by reducing the use of chemical fertilizers and pesticides, improving soil health, and conserving natural resources.

3.Diversification: By integrating crops and livestock, farmers can diversify their income streams and reduce their dependence on a single crop or product.

4.Reduced environmental impact: Integrated farming can reduce the environmental impact of agriculture by improving soil health, reducing greenhouse gas emissions, and conserving water resources.

5.Increased food security: Integrated farming can help to increase food security by improving crop yields, reducing food waste, and increasing access to diverse sources of food.

17. what is integrated farming system 500 words​


Answer:

Integrated Farming System (IFS) is an approach to agricultural production that seeks to combine different agricultural activities into a single, cohesive system. IFS is a holistic approach that integrates crop production, animal husbandry, fishery, and other activities such as mushroom cultivation, beekeeping, and vermicomposting. The system is designed to create a sustainable and profitable farming operation that maximizes resource efficiency and minimizes waste.

The idea of integrated farming is not new. It has been practiced in various forms for thousands of years. Historically, integrated farming systems have been common in subsistence agriculture, where farmers had to make the most of the limited resources available to them. In recent years, the concept of integrated farming has gained renewed interest as a means to promote sustainable agriculture and rural development.

Integrated Farming System involves the use of various farming components to create a sustainable and profitable system. Some of these components include:

Crop production: Crop production is a key component of IFS. Farmers use a variety of crops such as cereals, pulses, oilseeds, vegetables, fruits, etc. in a crop rotation system to improve soil fertility and reduce the risk of pests and diseases. Different crops are grown on the same plot of land at different times of the year, which helps to maintain the soil's nutrients and reduce the risk of soil erosion.

Livestock: Livestock is another crucial component of IFS. Farmers rear different types of livestock such as cows, goats, sheep, poultry, etc. The animals are kept in a controlled environment and are fed with organic feed and fodder produced on the farm. The animal waste is then used as organic manure to improve soil fertility and crop production.

Aquaculture: Aquaculture is another component of IFS. Farmers breed fish in ponds or tanks and integrate them into the farming system. Fish are fed with organic feed, and their waste is used as organic manure for crops.

Agroforestry: Agroforestry is a farming practice that involves growing trees alongside crops and animals. Trees provide shade, help to maintain soil moisture, and improve soil fertility.

Mushroom cultivation: Mushroom cultivation is a component of IFS that involves growing mushrooms using agricultural waste such as crop residues, sawdust, or straw. Mushroom cultivation helps to reduce the waste produced on the farm and provides an additional source of income for farmers.

Beekeeping: Beekeeping is another component of IFS that involves keeping bees for honey production. Bees help to pollinate crops and improve their yield, and honey production provides an additional source of income for farmers.

IFS has several advantages over traditional farming methods. It promotes resource conservation, reduces the use of chemical fertilizers and pesticides, and increases farm income. It also improves soil fertility, crop yield, and biodiversity. IFS is a sustainable farming system that helps to maintain ecological balance and improves the quality of life for farmers.

In conclusion, Integrated Farming System is a holistic approach to farming that integrates various farming components into a cohesive and sustainable system. The system promotes resource efficiency, waste reduction, and biodiversity conservation. IFS is a sustainable and profitable farming system that can improve the livelihoods of farmers and promote rural development.
