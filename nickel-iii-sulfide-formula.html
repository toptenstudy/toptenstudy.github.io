13. Chromium (III) oxide reacts with hydrogen sulfide gas to form chromium (III) sulfide and water. To produce 421 g of Cr2S3, (a) how many moles of Cr2O3 and (b) how many grams of Cr2O3 are required?


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{(a) \: Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: Write the balanced chemical equation.

Cr₂O₃ + 3 H₂S → Cr₂S₃ + 3 H₂O

Final Step: Calculate the number of moles of Cr₂O₃ required using dimensional analysis.

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 1 mole of Cr₂O₃ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 1 mole of Cr₂S₃. Note that the molar mass of Cr₂S₃ is 200.2 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of} \: \text{Cr}_2\text{O}_3 & = 421 \: \cancel{\text{g} \: \text{Cr}_2\text{S}_3} \times \frac{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Cr}_2\text{S}_3}}{200.2 \: \cancel{\text{g} \: \text{Cr}_2\text{S}_3}} \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Cr}_2\text{O}_3}{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Cr}_2\text{S}_3}} \\ & = \text{2.1029 mol} \\ & = \boxed{\text{2.10 mol} \: \text{Cr}_2\text{O}_3} \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{(b) \: Solution:}}}[/tex]

Note that the molar mass of Cr₂O₃ is 152.0 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of} \: \text{Cr}_2\text{O}_3 & = 2.1029 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Cr}_2\text{O}_3} \times \frac{\text{152.0 g} \: \text{Cr}_2\text{O}_3}{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Cr}_2\text{O}_3}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{320. g} \: \text{Cr}_2\text{O}_3} \end{aligned}[/tex]

15. What is the mass of 7.75 x 10²² formula units of lead (II) sulfide, PbS?Please show your complete solution. ​


Answer:

mass = 30.8 g

Explanation:

Calculating the molar mass of PbS

molar mass = (207.2 × 1) + (32.06 × 1)

molar mass = 239.3 g

Calculating the mass of PbS

mass = 7.75 × 10²² formula units × (1 mol / 6.022 × 10²³ formula units) × (239.3 g / 1 mol)

mass = 30.8 g

29. Which of the following shows a chemical reaction in which magnesium and hydrogen sulfide are the reactants and magnesium sulfide and hydrogen are the products? A. magnesium sulfide + hydrogen → magnesium + hydrogen sulfide B. magnesium + magnesium sulfide → hydrogen sulfide + hydrogen C. magnesium + hydrogen sulfide → magnesium sulfide + hydrogen D. magnesium + hydrogen → magnesium sulfide + hydrogen sulfide


Answer:  C. magnesium + hydrogen sulfide → magnesium sulfide + hydrogen

In chemical reactions, it always involve both reactants and products. Reactants are the substances that start a chemical reaction, and products are substances that are produced in the reaction. Reactants are usually at the left of the tail of the arrow (→ ) and products are at the right or tip of the arrow (→) if it is forward reaction. And if it is backward reaction, it is the reverse of the forward reaction.

Given: Reactants: magnesium, hydrogen sulfide

           Products: magnesium sulfide, hydrogen

Answer: magnesium + hydrogen sulfide → magnesium sulfide + hydrogen
