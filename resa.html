17. A study area inside the living room has a width that is 4 inches less than the length. The area is 12 square inches. Find the dimensions. Use RESA (Represent, Equation, Solution, Answer)


Representation:
x = length
x- 4 = width ( four inches less than the length)
12 square inches = Area

Use the formula Area   = L × W to find the dimensions.

Equation:
L × W = Area
(x) (x-4) = 12

Solution:
x² - 4x = 12
Transform to quadratic equation, ax² + bx + c = 0
x² - 4x - 12 = 0
Solve by factoring:
(x - 6) (x + 2) = 0
x - 6 = 0                     x + 2 = 0
x = 6                          x = -2

Choose the positive root, x = 6

Answer:
Length = x               ⇒ x = 6 inches
Width = x - 4            ⇒ 6 - 4 = 2 inches

The dimensions are 6 inches for length and 2 inches for width.

To check:
(6 inches) (2 inches) = 12 square inches
12 square inches = 12 square inches.

Now put the equivalent expression of (lg) into the first equation. Everything will be in terms of (sm).

sm + 4(sm) + 5 =40

5(sm) + 5 = 40

5(sm) = 35

sm= 7 and thus lg must be 33 for them to add up to 40. i.e.

7+ lg = 40

lg = 33
