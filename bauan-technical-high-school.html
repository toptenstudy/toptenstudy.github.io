The given flowchart represents the process of determining whether a student is admitted to the No. 1 High School based on their total score. Here's a breakdown of the symbols and the process:

Start: The start of the process.

Input: This symbol represents the input of data, in this case, the total score of the student.

Total Score: The data entered in the input is the total score of the student.

If: This symbol is used to check a condition. In this case, the condition being checked is whether the total score is greater than 800.

End: This symbol represents the end of the process.

Yes: If the condition in the "If" symbol is true (total score > 800), the process follows the Yes arrow to the "Admitted to No. 1 High School" symbol.

Admitted to No. 1 High School: This symbol represents the result of the process if the condition is true.

No: If the condition in the "If" symbol is false (total score <= 800), the process follows the No arrow to the "Not Admitted to No. 1 High School" symbol.

Not Admitted to No. 1 High School: This symbol represents the result of the process if the condition is false.

In summary, the flowchart represents a decision-making process that determines whether a student is admitted to the No. 1 High School based on their total score.
