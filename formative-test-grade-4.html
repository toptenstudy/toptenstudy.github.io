Answer:

The answer is C. Converse.

The statement "If it forms a line, then the points extend on both directions" is the converse of the statement "If the points extend on both directions, then it forms a line." Converse statements switch the antecedent and the consequent.

The contrapositive is "If the points do not extend on both directions, then it does not form a line." and the inverse is "If it does not form a line, then the points do not extend on both directions."
