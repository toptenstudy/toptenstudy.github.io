Collections Policy Definition

A collections policy is a set of guidelines that govern the accounts receivable team’s procedures and helps to create a more consistent, systematic treatment strategy. Many companies may have their collections policy as part of their credit policy, but the collections policy is worth considering on its own.

28. Why is the philippines debt rising?


Answer:

Explanation:

The Philippines' debt has been rising due to various factors, including increased government spending, borrowing for infrastructure projects, and external economic factors such as fluctuations in currency exchange rates and global economic conditions.

One of the main reasons for the rising debt is the government's ambitious infrastructure development program, which requires significant funding. To finance these projects, the government has increased borrowing from domestic and international sources. While this investment in infrastructure is essential for economic growth and development, it has also led to an increase in the country's debt.

Another factor contributing to the rising debt is external economic conditions. For instance, the COVID-19 pandemic has led to a global economic slowdown, causing the Philippine economy to contract, which has resulted in decreased revenue collection and increased borrowing to support government spending.

Finally, the depreciation of the Philippine peso against the US dollar has also contributed to the rising debt. This is because a significant portion of the country's debt is denominated in US dollars, and as the peso weakens, it takes more pesos to repay each dollar of debt, increasing the country's debt burden.

In summary, the Philippines' debt is rising due to a combination of factors such as increased government spending, borrowing for infrastructure projects, external economic conditions, and currency exchange rate fluctuations.

The Department of Health (abbreviated as DOH; Filipino: Kagawaran ng Kalusugan) is the executive department of the Government of the Philippines responsible for ensuring access to basic public health services by all Filipinos through the provision of quality health care, the regulation of all health services

Answer:

The Department of Health (abbreviated as DOH; Filipino: Kagawaran ng Kalusugan) is the executive department of the Government of the Philippines responsible for ensuring access to basic public health services by all Filipinos through the provision of quality health care, the regulation of all health services and products. It is the government's over-all technical authority on health.[2] It has its headquarters at the San Lazaro Compound, along Rizal Avenue in Manila.

The head of the department is led by the Secretary of Health, currently Francisco Duque, nominated by the President of the Philippines and confirmed by the Commission on Appointments. The Health Secretary is a member of the Cabinet.
