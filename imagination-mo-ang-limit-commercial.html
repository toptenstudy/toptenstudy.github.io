15. 2. Mr. Cruz is watching television every day after his dinner. One day his son asked him, "Dad, why are advertisements and commercials in the television are the same in different channels or stations"? Which of the following statement could Mr. Cruz use to respond to his son? A. Because commercials in the television are in contract. B. Because commercials and advertisements in the television are paid. C. Because advertisements and commercials have time limit. D. Because costumers have the right to correct information about the products and services.​


Answer:

B. Because commercials and advertisements in the television are paid.

Explanation:

The reason why advertisements and commercials on television are the same across different channels or stations is because they are paid for by advertisers to be broadcasted to a wide audience. Advertisers pay for airtime on specific shows or time slots, and those commercials will be broadcasted on whichever channel or station has been paid for that time slot. This is why viewers will see the same commercial multiple times across different channels or stations, as long as the advertiser has paid for the airtime.
