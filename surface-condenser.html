2. what is the purpose of the condensation on a cold surface experiment​


Answer:

The purpose of the condensation on a cold surface experiment is to demonstrate the process of condensation, which occurs when water vapor in the air comes into contact with a cold surface and changes from a gas to a liquid. This experiment can be used to illustrate how dew forms on grass in the morning or how clouds form in the sky.

Answer:

The purpose of the condensation on a cold surface experiment is to demonstrate the process of condensation, which is the change of state of matter from a gas to a liquid. In this experiment, a cold surface is used to lower the temperature of the water vapor in the air, causing it to condense and form tiny droplets of water on the surface. The experiment can be used to explain the formation of dew, fog, and clouds in the atmosphere, as well as the importance of condensation in various industrial and environmental processes.
