MODALS

What are modals?

Modals are auxiliary verbs. These are also called modal verbs. Verbs such as can, will, may, and have to are examples of modals.

Answers:

Below are the statements that have different modals mentioned in the song:

But I want to believe that love can still surviveYou don't have to say it, I don't have to read your mindI need you how so I can feel aliveHow would you know if you won't give me some timeTo see if everything could work out you'll (you will) be mineI'll (I will) be lost forever or someday I may findAll the nights I sit and wonder there must be more to life

What expression does each modal portray?

Modals are used as permission, prohibition, obligation, and no obligation. Verbs that are expressed in the song are the following:

Permission — can, could, and mayProhibition — won'tObligation — mustNo obligation — do not have to

The verb "will" can be used to show probability or condition and what could it be in the future.

Other examples of modal verbs that were excluded in the song are shall, would, should, and might.

18. Learning Task 7:In your notebook, copy, study and underline the different modals mentioned in this song. The analyze if the said modals express permission, obligation and prohibition.​


The following underlined words are modals found in the song “Honestly” by Harem Scarem and how were they used.

That love can still survive: abilityI don't have to read your mind: obligationSo I can feel alive: abilityCould work out you'll be mine?: permissionHow would you know if you won't give me some time: permissionOh, will I be lost forever, or someday I may find: ability There must be more to life I'm sure that: deduction But I want to believe that love can still survive: ability

Modals are words that express advice, capability, requests, and many more. They are paired with a main verb. They can’t be used on their own since they are auxiliary verbs. There are many types of modals and they can be categorized in the following:

Modals of obligation, are used to show if something is needed or not needed, this can also make suggestions. (have to, must, should) Modals of permission, are used to check whether someone is permitted to do something, request if someone can do something, or give advice and help. (could, may, shall, can)Modals of ability, are used to indicate if someone or something has or doesn’t have a certain capability. (can, cannot, could)Modals of deduction, are used to indicate how sure we are of the probability of an event. (must, might, may, could)
