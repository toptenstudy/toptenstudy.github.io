APA

A writing style and format for academic materials like books and scholarly journal articles is called APA style (also known as APA format). In the subject of behavioral and social sciences, it is frequently used to cite sources. The Publication Manual of the American Psychological Association (APA), the style manual of the American Psychological Association, describes it. The rules were created to promote communication clarity, word choice that "best decreases prejudice in language," and reader comprehension in the social and behavioral sciences.

A typical research paper written in APA format satisfies three levels of specification.

Level 1 specifies the format for a research report, including a title page, abstract, introduction, methods, findings, discussion, and references. Additionally, title pages and abstracts must be formatted in accordance with the APA handbook of style. Level 2 defines the writing style. It must be formal, clear, and devoid of slang, references to popular culture, bias, and comedy. It must be concise, and direct, and use the fewest literary tricks possible. The formatting requirements for Level 3 include double spacing, using title case for headings, using numerals for numbers 10 and higher, hyphenating compound adjectives, utilizing in-text citations for sources, aligning all tables and figures to the left, and using footnotes.
