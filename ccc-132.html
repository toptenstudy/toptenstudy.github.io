15. Ano ang meaning ng CCC​


Answer:

Civilian Conservation Corps

Civilian Conservation Corps

    The Civilian Conservation Corps or should we say CCC was an intentional public work alleviation program that worked from 1933 to 1942 in the United States for jobless and such.

    Roosevelt set up the Civilian Conservation Corps in 1933. The CCC or is  now and then known permitted single men between the ages of 18 and 25 to enroll in work projects to improve the America's public grounds, woodlands, and parks.

    The CCC program was rarely authoritatively ended. Congress gave subsidizing to shutting the excess camps in 1942 with the hardware being redistributed. It turned into a model for preservation programs that were actualized in the period after World War II.
