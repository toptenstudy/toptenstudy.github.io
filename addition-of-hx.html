27. solve for the mean of X using this formuy hx or EV [x. P(X)]?​


Answer:

To solve for the mean of X using the formula hx or EV [x. P(X)], we need to multiply each possible value of X by its corresponding probability, then sum up all the products. Symbolically, it can be represented as:

hx or EV [x. P(X)] = Σ[x * P(X)]

where Σ is the summation symbol and x represents the possible values of X.

For example, suppose we have a discrete random variable X with three possible values: 1, 2, and 3, and their corresponding probabilities are 0.2, 0.3, and 0.5, respectively. We can calculate the mean of X as follows:

hx or EV [x. P(X)] = (1 * 0.2) + (2 * 0.3) + (3 * 0.5)

= 0.2 + 0.6 + 1.5

= 2.3

Therefore, the mean of X is 2.3.
