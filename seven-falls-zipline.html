17. Situation: A zipline is a very strong cable between two parallel points attached to it. Draw the zipline connected from a 20 foot tall tower to a 15 foot tower in a slightly inclined ground. The angles created by the line to the top of the poles are (2x+15) and (3x)". Find the measure of these angles.​


Answer:

Therefore, the measure of the two angles is 81° and 99°

Step-by-step explanation:

In this problem, we are tasked to find the measure of the angles and draw the problem.

(picture is attached)

As the figure shown, we can see two angles are adjacent angles which means they add up to 180°. Therefore,

[tex] \rm{(2x+15)+(3x)=180}[/tex]

Group like terms

[tex] \rm{2x+3x+15=180}\\\\\rm{5x = 165}\\\\\rm{x = \frac{165}{5}}\\\\\rm{x = 33} [/tex]

Now, that we find the value of x. We will solve for now the measure of the angles. To find the measure of the angles, simply substitute the value of x to the angles and simplify to get the measure of the angles.

[tex] \cdot \ \ \ 2x+15 = 2(33)+15=81 [/tex]

[tex] \cdot \ \ \ 3x = 3(33) = 99 [/tex]

Therefore, the measure of the two angles is 81° and 99°

✏️TRIGONOMETRY

[tex]\red{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex] \underline{\mathbb{PROBLEM:}} [/tex]

Mr. Gilbert zipline down from a 50 feet tree. If the angle the rope makes with the ground is 25 degree, how long is the rope?

[tex]\red{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex] \underline{\mathbb{ANSWER:}} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad\Large\rm» \:\: \green{\approx 118.32 \: feet} [/tex]

[tex]\red{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex] \underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION:}} [/tex]

» We can represent the angles and the sides of a right triangle.

25° is the referred angle50 ft. is the opposite The length of the rope is the hypotenuse.

» Using trigonometry, we can solve for the length by using the sine ratio. Let x be the length of the rope.

[tex] \rm \sin \theta = \frac{opposite}{hypotenuse} \\ [/tex]

[tex]\sin 25 \degree = \frac{ \: 50 \: }{x} \\ [/tex]

» Exchange x and sin 25°.

[tex]x = \frac{50}{\sin 25 \degree} \\ [/tex]

» Let 0.4226 be the approximate value sin 25°.

[tex]x \approx \frac{ \: 50 \: }{0.4226} \\ [/tex]

[tex]x \approx 118.32[/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] The length of the rope is approximate 118.32 feet.
