What is the probability that a person selected is nurse or physician? What is the probability that a person selected is a nurse or male?


Answer:

If we represent the events "Nurse" and "Female" as N and F respectively, then we can fill in the table as follows:

Staff Females Males Total

Nurses (N) 3 7 10

Physicians (P) 2 3 5

Total 5 10 15

The probability that a person selected from the staff is a nurse or female is:

P(N or F) = P(N) + P(F) - P(N and F) = 10/15 + 5/15 - 3/15 = 12/15 = 4/5

The probability that a person selected is a male or physician is:

P(M or P) = P(M) + P(P) - P(M and P) = 8/15 + 5/15 - 2/15 = 11/15

The probability that a person selected is male or female is equal to 1 because every person is either male or female.

The probability that a person selected is a nurse or physician is:

P(N or P) = P(N) + P(P) - P(N and P) = 10/15 + 5/15 - 2/15 = 13/15

The probability that a person selected is a nurse or male is:

P(N or M) = P(N) + P(M) - P(N and M) = 10/15 + 8/15 - 7/15 = 11/15

26. In a hospital, there are 10 nurses and 5 physicians. Seven nurses and two physicians are males. The sample space can be written in a table form. Staff Females Males Total Nurses: (3 , 7, 10) Physicians: (3, 2, 5) total : If a person is selected from the staff, what is the probability that a person is nurse of female? What is the probability that a person selected is a male or physician? What is the probability that a person selected is male or female? What is the probability that a person selected is nurse or physician? What is the probability that a person selected is a nurse or male?


The sample space can be written as follows:

Staff Females Males Total
Nurses: 3 7 10
Physicians: 2 3 5
Total: 5 10 15

The probability that a person selected is a nurse of female is:
P(nurse or female) = P(nurse and female)/P(total)
P(nurse and female) = 3/15
P(total) = 15/15
Therefore, P(nurse or female) = (3/15)/(15/15) = 3/15 = 1/5 = 0.2

The probability that a person selected is a male or physician is:
P(male or physician) = P(male) + P(physician) - P(male and physician)
P(male) = 10/15
P(physician) = 5/15
P(male and physician) = 2/15
Therefore, P(male or physician) = (10/15) + (5/15) - (2/15) = 13/15 = 0.87

The probability that a person selected is male or female is:
P(male or female) = P(male) + P(female) - P(male and female)
P(female) = 5/15
P(male and female) = 7/15
Therefore, P(male or female) = (10/15) + (5/15) - (7/15) = 8/15 = 0.53

The probability that a person selected is a nurse or physician is:
P(nurse or physician) = P(nurse) + P(physician) - P(nurse and physician)
P(nurse) = 10/15
P(nurse and physician) = 2/15
Therefore, P(nurse or physician) = (10/15) + (5/15) - (2/15) = 13/15 = 0.87

30. A unit educator is conducting an inservice about client advocacy to the nursing staff. how should the educator describe the nurse's role in client advocacy?


The Nurse's Advocate for the Patient Role

The nursing crisis in our country has forced the health care sector to acknowledge the crucial part nurses play in providing effective, high-quality patient care. After all, nurses are in the ideal position to serve as a bridge between patients and families or patients and doctors because they have the most direct interaction with patients.

The educator define the role of the nurse in client advocacy as

To be an advocate in the nursing field is to uphold human dignity, advance patient equality, and end suffering. Additionally, the right of patients to make decisions regarding their own health must be upheld.

We'll instruct you in more detail on how to:

Encourage patients to have frank discussions about their symptoms and self-care routines.Give patients' and their families' queries plenty of leeway.Show compassion while providing all patients with high-quality care.Attend to the patients' specific religious and/or cultural needs.Apply ethical principles and legal expertise to nursing practice.Incorporate patient/family choices and scientific evidence into nursing care.Recognize how institutional policies and the government affect public health.

When you stop to think about it, nurses are the healthcare industry's super heroes. These exceptional caregivers, who don't sprint speeding cars or leap huge buildings in a single bound, fight for justice for their patients, especially the most vulnerable members of society.

The position of the nurse as patient advocate is a strong one that is elevating medical treatment to a new level. It calls for qualities such as courage, compassion, and expertise.
