2. ten (10) sauces and the food where it is commonly used in the Philippines,


Answer:

1. Adobo Sauce - commonly used in adobo dishes like chicken adobo and pork adobo.

9. Sweet Chili Sauce - a sweet and spicy sauce used in dishes like sweet and sour pork and chicken.

10. Mang Tomas Sauce - a popular sauce used as a condiment for lechon, fried pork, and fish.

1. Breton sauce

Breton sauce (fr. sauce bretonne) is a French compound sauce consisting of a velouté base with julienned onion, leeks, celery heart and mushrooms, mounted with butter and cream. It has been referred to as a brown version of sauce soubise, which has as its base a béchamel sauce

2. Julienne cut or matchstick cut

Julienne cut or matchstick is a common cut that is stick-shaped and very thin. It's most commonly associated with stir-fries or garnishes. Vegetables cut in this style cook quickly. It's also great for vegetables that are going to be served raw.

3. Spaghetti with Garlic, Anchovies and Parsley's

If you’ve never tried anchovy pasta before, you’re in for a treat. If it sounds intimidating, don’t worry. Not only is this dish incredibly easy, but the flavors aren’t overpowering. Anchovies and sliced garlic are gently warmed in extra virgin olive oil until the fillets essentially dissolve, creating a rich, savory sauce. This pasta recipe comes together in about 20 minutes, and is adapted from Anthony Bourdain’s final cookbook, Appetites.

Ten Popular Sauces in the Philippines:

1. Adobo Sauce - This sauce is commonly used in the Philippines for cooking the famous Filipino dish, Chicken Adobo. The sauce is made of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and black pepper.

2. Lechon Sauce - A sauce used as a dip for Lechon, a famous Filipino dish, made of roasted pork. The sauce is made of liver, vinegar, brown sugar, onions, garlic, and bread crumbs.

3. Sinigang Tamarind Soup Base - A sour soup base used for cooking Sinigang, a Filipino soup dish. The soup base is made of tamarind, onions, tomatoes, garlic, and fish sauce.

4. Banana Ketchup - A sweet and tangy ketchup made of mashed bananas, vinegar, sugar, and spices. This sauce is commonly used as a dip for fried foods in the Philippines.

5. Spicy Vinegar - A sauce made of vinegar, garlic, chili peppers, and salt. This sauce is commonly used as a dip for grilled meats and fish.

6. Bagoong - A fermented fish paste commonly used as a condiment in the Philippines. This sauce is used to add flavor to various Filipino dishes like Kare-kare, Bagoong Rice, and Binagoongan.

7. Mang Tomas Sauce - A sauce made of liver, breadcrumbs, vinegar, and sugar. This sauce is commonly used as a dip for roasted meats, particularly Lechon.

8. Patis - A fish sauce made of fermented fish and salt. This sauce is a staple in Filipino cuisine and is used to add umami flavor to various Filipino dishes.

9. Suka - A vinegar-based sauce made of vinegar, garlic, and salt. This sauce is commonly used as a dip for grilled meats and seafood.

10. Soy Sauce - A sauce made of fermented soybeans, salt, and water. This sauce is a staple in Filipino cuisine and is used to add saltiness and umami flavor to various dishes.
