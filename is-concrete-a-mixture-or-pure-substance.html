16. In what way are pure substances and mixtures important? Give a concrete example.


Answer:

In chemistry, a pure substance is a material with a constant composition. In other words, it is homogeneous no matter when you sample it. A pure substance consists entirely of one type of atom or compound. It participates predictably in a chemical reaction.

Examples of Pure Substances

The best examples of pure substances are pure elements, molecules, and compounds:

Hydrogen gas

Gold metal

Sugar (sucrose)

Baking soda (sodium bicarbonate)

Ammonia

Diamond

Copper wire

Silicon chip

Copper sulfate

Ethanol (pure grain alcohol)

Explanation:

Elements and Compounds are both examples of pure substances.

Elements: fundamental substances cannot be separated into simpler substances by chemical methods.

Example: Iron metal and Oxygen gas

Compound: substance composed of two or more elements in fixed proportions can be separated into simpler substance and elements only by chemical methods.

Example: Table salt and Aluminium Oxide

Homogeneous and Heterogeneous are both example of mixture.

Homogeneous: mixture having a uniform composition and properties throughout (also called a solution)

Example: Coffee and Apple juice

Heterogeneous: Mixture not uniform in composition and properties throughout.

Example: Salad dressing and Chocolate chip ice cream
