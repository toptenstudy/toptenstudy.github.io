5. Enumerate and Discuss the labels of INTERPOL.​


An intergovernmental organization called INTERPOL, often known as the International Criminal Police Organization, fosters cooperation among the criminal police forces of more than 180 countries. The goal of Interpol is to build and develop systems that will probably contribute to the prevention and elimination of global crime as well as to the promotion of the broadest mutual assistance between criminal police forces. It is the only international police organization with headquarters in Lyon, France.

Interpol focuses on three major areas of transnational crime: terrorism, crimes against people and property, including crimes against children, human trafficking, illegal immigration, car theft, and theft of works of art; economic, financial, and computer crimes, such as bank fraud, money laundering, corruption, and counterfeiting; and illegal drugs and criminal organizations, including organized crime. The General Secretariat, which is led by a secretary general chosen by the General Assembly for a five-year term, is responsible for overseeing the day-to-day operations of Interpol.

The General Assembly is Interpol's highest decision-making body, with one representative from each member nation. The General Assembly appoints the 13 members of the Executive Committee at its annual meeting, with each member representing a different part of the globe. The work of the secretary general is supervised by the Executive Committee, which also monitors how the General Assembly's decisions are carried out.

Each of our member countries hosts an INTERPOL National Central Bureau (NCB). This connects their national law enforcement with other countries and with the General Secretariat via our secure global police communications network called I-24/7.

Law enforcement agencies in their own country
