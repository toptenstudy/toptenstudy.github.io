Answer:

A time deposit is an interest-bearing bank account that has a pre-set date of maturity. A certificate of deposit (CD) is the best-known example. The money must remain in the account for the fixed term in order to earn the stated interest rate.

Time deposits generally pay a slightly higher rate of interest than a regular savings account. The longer the time to maturity, the higher the interest payment will be.

Another name for this type of investment is term deposit.

16. A deposit was made into a 9.8% annual simple interest account. Another deposit, P20,000 less than the first, was placed in a certificate of deposit earning 12% annual simple interest. The total interest earned on both investments for one year was Php 7,410. How much money was deposited in the certificate of deposit? Representation: Working Equation: Solution: Answer:


Answer:

Let's say the amount deposited in the 9.8% annual simple interest account is `x` and the amount deposited in the certificate of deposit is `y`. Since the deposit in the certificate of deposit is P20,000 less than the first deposit, we can write the equation `y = x - 20000`.

The total interest earned on both investments for one year was Php 7,410. The interest earned from the 9.8% annual simple interest account is `0.098x` and the interest earned from the certificate of deposit is `0.12y`. So we can write the equation `0.098x + 0.12y = 7410`.

Now we have a system of two equations with two unknowns:

```

y = x - 20000

0.098x + 0.12y = 7410

```

We can solve this system of equations to find the values of `x` and `y`. The value of `y` will be the amount deposited in the certificate of deposit.

26. A deposit was made into a 7% annual simple interest account. Another deposit, ₱15,000 less than the first, was placed in a certificate of deposit earning 9% annual simple interest. The total interest earned on both investments for one year was ₱5,050. How much money was deposited in the certificate of deposit?


CALCULATING THE AMOUNT OF MONEY DEPOSITED USING SIMPLE INTEREST

There were two kinds of deposit made in the problem. The first deposit is  with a 7% annual simple interest rate while the other one have  a 9% annual simple interest rate. The first deposit  was P15,000 less than the first, and the total interest earned was P5,050. The certificate of deposit was for P25,000.00.

Step by step solution

Given:

The amount of the first deposit serves as the X

The second deposit is then "15,000 less than the first," hence it is "x - 15,000.

To compute the interest earned in the first deposit we will use the formula :

I = Prt,

I-    is the interest earned

P - is the principal (the amount invested),

r - is the annual interest rate

t-  is the time period in years.

So the interest earned on the initial deposit is:

I1 = x0.071 = 0.07x

Similarly, the interest earned on the second deposit is:

I2 = (x-₱15,000)0.091 = 0.09(x-₱15,000)

We are told that the total interest earned on both assets over the course of a year was $5,050:

I1 + I2 = ₱5,050

When the equations for I1 and I2 are substituted, we get:

0.07x + 0.09(x-₱15,000) = ₱5,050

When we simplify and solve for x, we get:

0.07x + 0.09x - ₱1,350 = ₱5,050

0.16x = ₱6,400

x = ₱40,000

So the initial deposit was 40,000, and the subsequent deposit was 15,000 less, or 25,000.

29. Mineral deposits in the philippines and their locations


The Philippines is considered one of the countries in the world with rich mineral deposits despite its small area. One of the major reasons for this was the geographical location and features of the Philippines such as being situated in the region where there is an active process of volcanism and plate convergence - in the Pacific Ring of Fire, which is the major reason for the formation of metallic mineral deposits such as platinum, cobalt, nickel, chromite, iron, copper, and gold. In fact, the Philippines was ranked in the top 6 countries with rich deposits of gold, nickel, copper, and chromite. Triple I Consulting Inc. provided information about the ideal location for each mineral in the Philippines.

There is an abundance of gold deposits in the Luzon Central Cordillera, Northern Sierra Madre, Zambales, Vizcaya-Aurora, Bicol, Southern Tagalog, Central Visayas, Samar-Eastern Mindanao, North Central Mindanao, Zamboanga, Southern Mindanao, and Palawan.

On the other hand, copper can also be found in Luzon Central Cordillera, Zambales, Vizcaya-Aurora, Bicol, Southern Tagalog, Central Visayas, Samar-Eastern Mindanao, North Central Mindanao, Zamboanga, and Southern Mindanao. While Iron can be found in Luzon Central Cordillera, Bicol, Samar-Eastern Mindanao, and Zamboanga.

Manganese can also be found in Luzon Central Cordillera, Central Visayas, and Eastern Visayas. Here are the other minerals such as chromium and nickel and the location where there is an abundance of it.
