4. Compute for his tax: Given: Basic Salary - PHP83,000/month; total monthly contributions - PHP 1500/month; with 5 children. How much is his tax payable?


HERE ARE THE ANSWER;

Taxable income is computed by subtracting the total monthly contributions and the applicable exemptions (e.g. exemptions for the individual and their dependents) from the basic salary.

For an individual with 5 children, the exemptions are as follows:

PHP 50,000 exemption for the individualPHP 8,000 exemption for each dependent, totaling PHP 40,000 for 5 children.

Therefore, the total exemptions for an individual with 5 children are PHP 50,000 + PHP 40,000 = PHP 90,000.

Subtracting the total exemptions from the basic salary gives the taxable income: PHP 83,000 - PHP 90,000 - PHP 1,500 = PHP (8,500).

10. TEENAH Company has a gross income of 3,000,000 as of 3rd quarter of 2020. If the company has a sale of P500,000 for the last quarter and cost of sales of 50%, what is the company’s vat payable for the third quarter?​


Answer:

To find the company's VAT payable for the 3rd quarter, we need to calculate the company's net sales and multiply it by the VAT rate.

First, we need to calculate the company's net sales for the 3rd quarter. We can do this by subtracting the cost of sales from the gross income.

Gross income: P3,000,000

Cost of sales (50% of gross income): P3,000,000 x 50% = P1,500,000

Net sales: P3,000,000 - P1,500,000 = P1,500,000

Then, we need to multiply the net sales by the VAT rate of 12%.

VAT payable = P1,500,000 x 12% = P180,000

Therefore, the company's VAT payable for the 3rd quarter is P180,000.

28. cash amounts to 20,000 , inventory is 30,000 , accounts receivable is 10,000 and notes receivable is 5,000. It also has accounts payable of 5,000 and notes payable of 3,000. Compute the quick ratio.​


Answer:

Quick Ratio is 4.375 (meaning for every peso of Current Liabilities, there is 4.375 quick assets available to pay for it).

Step-by-step explanation:

First, compute for quick assets:

20,000 Cash

10,000 Accts Receivable

 5,000 Notes Receivable

35,000 Quick Assets

Then, compute for Current Liabilities

5,000 Accounts Payable

3,000 Notes Payable

8,000 Current Liabilities

Finally, compute for Quick Ratio (Quick Assets divided by Current Liabilities)

= 35000 / 8000

= 4.375

Note: Inventory is not included in computation of Quick Ratio, since it is not easily convertible to cash.
