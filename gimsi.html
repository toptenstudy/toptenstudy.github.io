7. Analyze each passage and determine the sound device used. Write on the line the letters AS if assonance, AL if alliteration, CO if consonance, ON if onomatopoeia, and CA if cacophony1."Why does the sea sob so, Mother, As it breaks on the Rocky shore?"2."Each building will be a hill, All sharps made sound;"3."That squeaking door will always squeak, For, prithee, don't you see?"All mimsy were the borogoves, And the mome raths outgrabe.4."Twas brillig, and the slithy toves Did gyre and gimble in the wabe:"5."A host, of golden daffodils, Beside the lake, beneath the trees, "​


Answer:

1. AL

This sentence is an alliteration because many words began with the same letter b. “Yes, the molave builds seven thousands bridges in blood.

2. ON

The word that is used as an onomatopoeia here is the word sob. The word sob is meant to denote the sound one makes when someone is crying and heaving loudly.

3. AL

The phrase “. . . all sharps made sound.” in this sentence uses the literary device of alliteration.

4. ON

The onomatopoeia in this sentence is the word squeak. It is the sound where the pitch is high commonly used to describe the sound rodents make.

Both alliteration and onomatopoeia are literary devices used for the sake of emphasis, creativity, entertainment, and many more. Popular examples of alliteration include Peter Parker, Dunkin Donuts, and Mamma Mia. Other examples of onomatopoeia include quack, tweet, and roar.

Other literary devices are simile, metaphor, rhyming, allegory, irony, symbolism, and many more. The use of literary devices adds colors to your everyday speech, writing, etc.
