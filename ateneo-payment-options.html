14. A credit card company offers a deferred payment option for the purchase of any appliance rose plans to buy a smart television set with monthly payment of ₱ 4,000 for 2 years the payment will start at the end of 3 month. How much is the cash price of the TV set if the interest rate is 10% compound monthly.


The cash price of the TV set can be calculated by solving for the present value of the deferred payment option. The present value is the current worth of a future payment, taking into account the time value of money and any applicable interest rate.

To calculate the present value, we need to determine the total amount of the monthly payments and the number of months over which they will be made. In this case, the monthly payment is ₱4,000 and there will be 24 payments (2 years * 12 months/year). The total amount of the payments is therefore ₱4,000 * 24 = ₱96,000.

We can then use the present value formula to calculate the cash price of the TV set:

PV = PMT * ((1 - (1 + r)^-n)/r)

where PV is the present value, PMT is the periodic payment (in this case, the monthly payment of ₱4,000), r is the interest rate (in decimal form), and n is the number of periods (in this case, the number of months).

Substituting the values into the formula, we get:

PV = ₱4,000 * ((1 - (1 + 0.1)^-24)/0.1)

= ₱4,000 * ((1 - (1.1)^-24)/0.1)

= ₱4,000 * ((1 - 0.5405)/0.1)

= ₱4,000 * (0.4595/0.1)

= ₱4,000 * 4.595

= ₱183,800

Therefore, the cash price of the TV set is ₱183,800.

(d) Compute the outstanding balance after t years/months. You will provide the value of t.


Option A: With Regular Payment

Situation:

You have taken out a loan of $50,000 at an annual interest rate of 5% to purchase a new car. You have agreed to make monthly payments of $900 for 5 years to pay off the loan.

Questions:

(a) How many regular payments will be made?

There will be 60 regular payments made (5 years * 12 months/year = 60 months).

(b) Using option B, compute the final smaller amount.

This question does not apply to this situation, as it is based on Option B which involves irregular payments.

(c) Compute the interest on the nth payment. You will provide the value of n.

To compute the interest on the nth payment, we can use the following formula:

Interest = Outstanding Balance * Interest Rate

For example, if n = 30 (the 30th payment), the outstanding balance would be $30,000 (the initial loan amount of $50,000 minus the 30 payments of $900 each), and the interest would be $1,500 (30,000 * 0.05 = 1,500).

(d) Compute the outstanding balance after t years/months. You will provide the value of t.

To compute the outstanding balance after t years/months, we can use the following formula:

Outstanding Balance = Initial Loan Amount - (Payment * Number of Payments)

For example, if t = 3 years (36 months), the outstanding balance would be $18,000 (50,000 - (900 * 36) = 18,000). This means that after making 36 monthly payments of $900 each, the outstanding balance on the loan would be $18,000.


20. Mr. Tan wants to buy a new cellular phone worth P12,000. He has the option of paying it today for P12,000 or buying in instalment where he has to pay a down payment of P5,500 today, and the balance will be paid in two equal payments of P5,500 each for the next two years. Given an interest rate of 9%, which is the better option?


Answer:

It is better for Mr. Tan to pay P12,000 today instead of choosing to pay in instalments because it will save him money in the long run.

Step-by-step explanation:

To answer this question, we need to calculate the total amount of money that Mr. Tan will pay in both options.

If he pays P12,000 today, he will not have to pay any interest. So the total amount he will pay is P12,000.

If he chooses to pay in instalments, he will have to pay a down payment of P5,500 today. The balance of P6,500 will be paid in two equal payments of P3,250 each for the next two years.

To calculate the total amount he will pay in instalments, we need to calculate the interest on the balance of P6,500 for two years at a rate of 9%.

The formula for calculating simple interest is:

I = P * r * t

Where:

- I = Interest

- P = Principal (the amount borrowed)

- r = Rate of interest

- t = Time (in years)

Using this formula, we can calculate the interest on the balance as follows:

I = 6,500 * 0.09 * 2

I = 1,170

So the total amount he will pay in instalments is:

Total amount = Down payment + Balance + Interest

Total amount = 5,500 + 6,500 + 1,170

Total amount = 13,170

Therefore, it is better for Mr. Tan to pay P12,000 today instead of choosing to pay in instalments because it will save him money in the long run.
