B ) what equation will you use to find the number of votes each candidate receive C) how many votes did each candidate receive? D) why should we be wise in choosing the right person or candidate during election? ANSWER ASAP PLS


Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

A) To represent the number of votes received by each candidate, we can use variables. Let's represent the number of votes received by Candidate A as 'a' and the number of votes received by Candidate B as 'b'.

B) To find the number of votes received by each candidate, we can use the equation:

a - b = 2065 (since Candidate A received 2065 more votes than Candidate B)

a + b = 13215 (since there were 13215 votes cast in all)

We can solve these equations simultaneously using a method such as substitution or elimination to find the values of 'a' and 'b'.

C) Solving the above equations, we get:

a = (13215 + 2065) / 2 = 7715

b = (13215 - 2065) / 2 = 5575

Therefore, Candidate A received 7715 votes and Candidate B received 5575 votes.

D) We should be wise in choosing the right person or candidate during an election because the person elected will be responsible for making important decisions that affect our lives and communities. A good leader with integrity, competence, and a commitment to serving the people can bring positive change and progress to the society. On the other hand, a bad leader with selfish motives and a lack of concern for the people can cause harm and damage to the society. Therefore, it is important to carefully evaluate the candidates and make an informed choice based on their qualifications, track record, and values.
