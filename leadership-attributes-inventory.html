14. As an NCO, what leadership attributes and competencies do you believe are necessary to become an effective Army NCO leader and why? 750 words​


As an NCO (Non-Commissioned Officer) in the Army, there are several leadership attributes and competencies that are necessary in order to become an effective leader.

First and foremost, integrity is crucial. NCOs must maintain a high level of ethical and moral standards and must always do what is right, even in the face of adversity. This helps to build trust with subordinates and fosters a positive working environment.

Secondly, NCOs must have strong communication skills. They must be able to effectively communicate both upward and downward, and be able to articulate their thoughts and ideas in a clear and concise manner. This helps to ensure that the message being conveyed is understood and that all members of the team are on the same page.

Thirdly, NCOs must have a strong sense of accountability. They must take ownership of their actions and be willing to accept responsibility for their decisions. This helps to build trust and demonstrates their commitment to the mission and their subordinates.

Fourthly, NCOs must be competent in their field. They must have a deep understanding of the procedures, tactics, and equipment they use in order to effectively lead their team. This helps to build confidence in their subordinates and ensures that they are able to complete their mission effectively.

Fifthly, NCOs must be adaptable and flexible. The Army is constantly evolving, and NCOs must be able to quickly adapt to new situations and changes in the environment. This helps to maintain a high level of mission readiness and ensures that they are able to effectively respond to any challenges that may arise.

Sixthly, NCOs must be able to lead by example. They must be able to demonstrate the desired behavior and attitude, and set the standard for their subordinates. This helps to build a positive culture and instills a sense of pride in their team.

Finally, NCOs must be empathetic and have a deep understanding of human nature. They must be able to connect with their subordinates on a personal level, and understand the challenges they may be facing. This helps to build strong relationships and fosters a sense of unity within the team.

In conclusion, there are several leadership attributes and competencies that are necessary in order to become an effective Army NCO leader. These include integrity, strong communication skills, accountability, competence, adaptability, leading by example, and empathy. By possessing these traits, NCOs can effectively lead their team and contribute to the success of the mission.

Put these all together and you get a huge chasm between the old leadership style of administrating and directing versus the new model of guiding and inspiring.
