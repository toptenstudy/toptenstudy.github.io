3. The ethical school of thought that states that an action is considered right and good if one can get a use out of it is called _____.


Answer:

Utilitarianism

Explanation:

The classic form of results-based ethics is called utilitarianism. This says that the ethically right choice in a given situation is the one that produces the most happiness and the least unhappiness for the largest number of people.

Aristotle's virtue ethics

In my opinion, Aristotle's virtue ethics is a philosophy concerning human happiness. As human beings, we need act morally in order to find pleasure, especially in our social interactions. When we do well, we find happiness

Let's describe more about this ethics

According to Aristotle, virtue is “the state of a thing which constitutes its peculiar excellence and enables it to perform its function well..in man (it is) the activity of reason and of rationally ordered habits.”

Aristotle popularized the virtue ethic, which emphasizes on one's character rather than acts. In general, Plato's theories serve as a basis for Aristotle's ethics of virtue. In this case, Plato argues that Sophia reflects the fundamental human principles necessary to reach the perfection of life. This principle leads people pursue happiness, the prime goal of human life.

Aristotle believed that virtues are positive habits we have that control our emotions. For example, we develop the virtue of courage in reaction to our fearful nature, which keeps us strong in the face of danger.

Human interaction is a fundamental of social life. Virtue will always have a chance to show itself in this social life. Humans will strive to achieve goodness if they do not interact with other humans.

As a consequence, as human beings, we should be principled and respect other people. This will encourage us to always do good in order to achieve complete happiness as humans.

11. thought about natural law ethics

Natural law is a theory in ethics and philosophy that says that human beings possess intrinsic values that govern their reasoning and behavior

Answer:

Natural Law says that everything has a purpose, and that mankind was made by God with a specific design or objective in mind (although it doesn't require belief in God). It says that this purpose can be known through reason. As a result, fulfilling the purpose of our design is the only 'good' for humans.Natural Law theory says that human nature can serve as the objective standard of morality. we do right when our acts express human nature, and wrong when they violate it. ... true, according to natural law theory, only humans have the sort of nature that enables them to be moral agents.Natural law is a theory in ethics and philosophy that says that human beings possess intrinsic values that govern their reasoning and behavior. Natural law maintains that these rules of right and wrong are inherent in people and are not created by society or court judges.Natural law is important because it is applied to moral, political, and ethical systems today. 25. ethical dilemmas arise when it is thought that serious good and bad are bound together in the same activity.


ETHICAL DILLEMMA

An dilemma occurs when the person serious good and harm are connected in one activity, ethical dilemmas occur. An ethical dilemma is an issue that arises during the decision-making process. It is a complicated circumstance in which there appears to be a mental conflict between competing moral obligations, whereby obeying one will violate another.

EXAMPLE OF ETHICAL DILEMMAYou are uncomfortable wearing facemasks  because it makes your glasses fog up and at the same time you have not been feeling ill either. You spend most of your time at home and just occasionally go shopping. You don't reside in a place where wearing a mask is required and you live alone. When you occasionally visit the grocery store,  should wear facemasks accordingly?When considering this dilemma, keep in mind that wearing a facemask is not required by law ( such as, there is no law that applies to your country  or community). Consider whether something is ethical even if it is legal first. 30. why should ethics be taught in school​


Answer:

Ethical discussions not only expose students to contrasting ethical opinions, they also provide an opportunity to understand the reasons behind the differences. As a result, students are able to expand their understanding of ethics, sometimes even altering their own values and ethical decision-making process.

Unethical decisions often result from rationalization and external pressures.
