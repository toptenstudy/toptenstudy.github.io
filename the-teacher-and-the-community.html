In order to achieve professional standards, one must be productive, maintain a professional appearance, take the initiative, maintain effective work habits, manage time effectively, exhibit integrity, provide excellence, be a problem-solver, be resilient, communicate effectively, develop self-awareness, and form relationships with all of their students.

Filipino traits that work against the making of a true Filipino professional teacher.Crab Mentality

This characteristic of the Filipinos is battling for superiority. If someone believes they have no possibility of success, they will bring others down with them. A professional teacher will have coworkers, and you might compete as a professional at some time, but the main thing is to strive without inflicting suffering on others.

Procrastination

If you can wait till tomorrow, why do it now? This phrase is extremely risky. A professional teacher has additional tasks that need to be completed as quickly as possible. If possible, a true professional complete it earlier.

Bahala Habits

This characteristic illustrates the need to continue handling tasks as important even when they become complicated. Every task can be completed; that is how professionals work.

Ningas Cogon

This demonstrates when someone is initially enthusiastic and energized but subsequently loses interest. A skilled instructor should maintain enthusiasm throughout the work or endeavor.

Filipino Time

It's not a good thing that Filipinos are almost always late. Being on time helps a professional teacher set a good example for the students.
