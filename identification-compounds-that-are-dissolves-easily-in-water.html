- Effective germicide (because disinfectants are designed and engineered to safely kill germs and bacteria, and are considered effective when they kill the targeted level of germs and bacteria in a particular substance/ location)

- Non-corrosive (because disinfectants are not corrosive to metal or plastic, meaning they do not negatively impact the quality, strength, or functionality of these items, and are safe for use on variety of surfaces and materials)

Characteristic of detergents:

- capacity to hold materials in suspension (because of their ability to hold dirty materials or stains until they are cleaned away)

- compatible with cleaning compounds (because they can be mixed and used safely with other cleaning solutions and tools for cleaning without negatively impacting their efficacy or safety)

- Ability to remove soil or dirt from surfaces (because they are designed to help make things clean and to be effective at maintaining hygiene and cleanliness of surfaces and items)

- Easily dissolved in water (because they can be dissolved in water and used in variety of cleaning solutions and devices)
