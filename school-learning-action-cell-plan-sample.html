Constant feeling of exhaustion in school posits some various reasons.  To help you out, here are some factors that you should consider to deal with your school exhaustion:

1. Keep track and maintain a healthy sleeping habits.

Most of the teenagers have become so active in school and other activities.  Even their biological disposition has changed over time making their sleeping patterns disrupted.

However, due to a significantly busier lifestyle among teens, their sleep time is compromised to make up with school works, requirements, organizations, family obligations, and maybe, work if any.

2. Create a timetable of activities.

This is where time management enters.  A proper time management and daily plans will help in the organization of one’s activities.  If this is strictly followed, then an improvement will take place.

3. Control the use of gadgets.

Increased amount of time in using gadgets means a decrease in sleep hours.  Instead of spending another hour browsing through the social media, it would be better to use that extra hour for sleep.

23. My Action Plan.Prepare an action plan showing how you live the values of Jesus andhow to be a "sacrament" to others at home, in school, and in the Christiancommunity.I need some help.​


Answer:

Living a sacramental worldview means, quite simply, viewing the world as a sacrament. A redundant definition might be, but often the simplest explanations are the best. If we do truly believe that the Sacraments are moments in time where the invisible grace of God is made visible and tangible then seeing this same grace working constantly in and through our daily lives would only beg that we see the sacramental nature of daily life. This is not to say that every blade of grass is truly the transubstantiated body of Christ, but it does substantiate St. Ignatius’s charge to see God in all things. Furthermore, viewing the world through “sacramentally-tinted glasses” would mean seeing the very world itself as sacramental; it would mean recognizing our lives and everything that they contain as the gift that they are. Indeed, it would mean seeing this world, our fallen world, for what it truly is: a tangible sign of the invisible and salvific grace of God. That being said, the question is not so much what it means to live with a sacramental worldview, but rather how this worldview will change the way we act.
