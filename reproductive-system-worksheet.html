4. Search for an example of a worksheet. Explain the different columns included in the worksheet.


Accounting Worksheet Columns Included:

-Unadjusted Trial Balance

-Adjusting Entries

-Adjusted Trial Balance

-Income Statement

-Balance Sheet

Explanations:

Unadjusted Trial Balance

A column contains all the company’s assets, liability, expenses, and revenue accounts, which are used in a concerned year.

Adjusting Entries

All the accounts of the company, which requires the passing of the adjustment entry will get listed in the adjustment column.

Adjusted Trial Balance

The adjusted trial balance will be prepared by combining the entries of the previous two columns, Unadjusted trial balance, and adjustments.

Income Statement

A column contains the values concerning the expenses and revenue accounts only. In this case, if the value of total revenue exceeds the expense column, then the difference will be the net income of the year of the company as it is earning more revenues in the year than what it is spending on its expenses.

Balance Sheet

A column contains the values with respect to the assets, liabilities, and owner’s capital only.

Answer:

Scrotum's function: Protect and keep the testicles safe and cold because the testicles like a cold temperature.

Urethra's function: Passage-way for the urine and sperm cells to pass outside of the male body by delivering them to the Penis.

Epidydimis' function: A storage for the sperm cells so that they could earn the ability to move (motility) and reach maturity.

Vas Deferens: A passage-way for mature sperm cells and brings them to the urethra.

Prostate Gland: Secretes whitish fluid (semen) to nourish and ensure the survival of the mature sperm cells.

Cowper's Gland: Secretes fluid to lubricate the Urethra and to neutralize the acidity of urine.

Seminal Vesicle: Secretes fructose to give the mature sperm cells some energy.

Penis: An external part known to deliver the sperm cells onto the female reproductive tract (Vagina).

Testes (Testicles): Produces the Male sex hormone (Testosterone) and sperm cells.
