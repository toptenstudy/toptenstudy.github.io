19. A thousand years Select one: a. Soiree b. Rendezvous c. Faux pas d. Millennium


The answer is letter D. Millennium .

Millennium is also called kiloyears that is equal to 1000 years. Often related to a particular dating system, but not always. From the Latin word mille, meaning thousand, and annum/annus meaning year, or ennium, period of years.

Ex. The Third Reich strongly believed that they will reign for a millennium.

Soiree is a fancy evening party of affair that is formal o refined in some way. Typically it is held in a private house. The word comes directly from the French word soir, meaning "evening" or “night”.

Ex. We'll be going to a soiree across the street in about an hour.

Rendezvous is an appointed time or place for meeting or assembly. From Middle French word rendez vous, meaning “present yourselves”.

Ex. During the Civil War the fort was the rendezvous for several military expeditions, notably those of General Benjamin F.

Faux pas is an act or remark that can cause embarrassment in a social situation. A French word which literally means, “false step”.

Ex. Although white shoes are often considered a fashion faux-pas when worn with other types of attire, the wedding dress is where white shoes are an exception.
