5. the object with a mass of 17.77 kg experiences a force of 19.05N.what is the acceleration of the object?(a=f/m)​


Answer:

Using the formula for acceleration, a = f/m, where a is acceleration, f is force, and m is mass, we can calculate the acceleration of the object as follows:

a = f/m

a = 19.05 N / 17.77 kg

a = 1.073 m/s^2

Therefore, the acceleration of the object is 1.073 m/s^2.

29. True or false? You exert a push P on an object and it pushes back on you with a force F. If the object is moving at constant velocity, then F is equal to P, but if the object is being accelerated, then P must be greater than F. Explain your answer.


Answer:

False

Explanation:

The question is not about net force, it is asking specifically about 2 forces. F on P and P on F. These should be equal at all times, the only difference is the friction force of the two objects which allow motion. If F increases, than F on P increases which transfers to the friction force of the pushers feet. If the friction force of the object being pushed cannot be matched than it will accelerate. This is a net non zero force, but not because of a difference in F on P and P on F.
