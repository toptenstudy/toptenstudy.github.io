11. Cu(s)+ NO 3 ^ - (aq) Cu^ 2+ (aq) + N*O_{2}(g)​


The chemical reaction you have written is the reaction between copper (Cu) and nitric acid (HNO3). In this reaction, the copper reacts with the nitric acid to form copper ions (Cu2+) and nitrogen dioxide gas (NO2). The reaction can be written as follows:

Cu(s) + 2HNO3(aq) -> Cu2+(aq) + 2NO2(g) + H2O(l)

In this equation, the reactants are on the left side of the arrow and the products are on the right side. The "s" and "aq" symbols indicate that the copper is in a solid state and the nitric acid is in an aqueous (water-based) solution, respectively. The "g" symbol indicates that the nitrogen dioxide is a gas. The "l" symbol indicates that water is produced as a liquid.

24. 2 AgNO3 + Cu→Cu(NO 3)2 + 2 Ag what kind of chemical reaction is this?


Answer:

Single Replacement Reaction

Explanation:

Answer:

Single replacement

Explanation:

In single replacement, the metal in a compound can be replaced by another metal in free state. The metal in free state must be more reactive than the metal in a compound in order for the reaction to occur, otherwise.

In the chemical equation above, Cu is the metal in the free state and Ag is the metal in the compound AgNO₃. Cu can replace Ag because Cu is more reactive than Ag. Cu replaces Ag in AgNO₃ to form Cu(NO₃)₂ and Ag.

The general formula for this reaction is

A + BC → AC + B
