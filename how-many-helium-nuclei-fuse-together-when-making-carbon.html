1. how many helium nuclei fuse together when making carbon? ​


Answer:

3

Explanation:

At sufficiently high temperatures and densities, a 3-body reaction called the triple alpha process can occur: Two helium nuclei ("alpha particles") fuse to form unstable beryllium.

If another helium nucleus can fuse with the beryllium nucleus before it decays, stable carbon is formed along with a gamma ray. ml

11. how to fuse hydrogen into helium


1. In the first stage two protons combine and one of them converts into a neutron to form a nucleus of the heavy isotope of hydrogen known as deuterium.

2. Next, the deuterium nucleus combines with another proton to form the light helium isotope known as helium-3.

3. Finally, two helium-3 nuclei combine to form helium-4, releasing two protons.
