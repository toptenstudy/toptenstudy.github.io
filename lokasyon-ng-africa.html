12. Gamit ang mapa ng mundo hanapin ang absoluto o tiyak na lokasyon ng sumusunod na mga bansa1.Pilipinas2.Japan3.France 4.Syria5.south africa​


TIYAK/ABSOLUTONG LOKASYON

Ano-ano nga ba ang absoluto o tiyak na lokasyon ng:

Pilipinas?Hapon?Pransya?Sirya?Timog Aprika?

Mas madali nating malalaman ang tiyak na lokasyon ng bawat bansang nasabi sa itaas sa paggamit ng mapa.

Ang mapang ginagamit upang malaman iyon, na tinatawag ding absolute location (https://brainly.ph/question/587228), ay mayroong mga nakalapat na latitud at longhitud na kung saan ay ginagamitan ng 'sistemang grid' (https://brainly.ph/question/245803) o coordinate system sa Ingles.

Nang mas mapadali pa ang pagtukoy ng tiyak na lokasyon, pwedeng hanapin ang kabisera ng isang naturang bansa. Halimbawa, ang Maynila ay ang kabisera ng Pilipinas; kung ano'ng lokasyon (tiyak) ng kabiserang ito, iyon din ang absolute location ng ating bansa.

Kasagutan:

1. Pilipinas - kabisera: Maynila

nasa pagitan ng 4° at 21° Hilagang Latitud (13°H)nasa pagitan ng 116° at 127° Silangang Longhitud (122°S)

2. Japan - kabisera: Tokyo

nasa pagitan ng 20° at 45° Hilagang Latitud (36°H)nasa pagitan ng 122° at 153° Silangang Longhitud (138°S)

3. France - kabisera: Paris

Latitud: 46°HLonghitud: 2°S

4. Syria - kabisera: Damascus

Latitud: 34°HLonghitud: 38°S

5. South Africa

Mga kabisera:

Pretoria (ehekutibo)Cape Town (lehislatibo)Bloemfontein (hudisyal)

Latitud at Longhitud:

Latitud: 29°TLonghitud: 25°S

18. ano ang tiyak na lokasyon ng africa​


Africa is a continent found on planet Earth. Earth exists within the Milky Way Galaxy which is found in a small group of galaxies called the Local Group on the edge of a supercluster named the Virgo Supercluster. Africa is a fairly large continent. It is the second largest continent by land mass. It contains just over 20% of the planet's total land area. Planet Earth is round, so saying that Africa is in the "middle" is a bit goofy; however, most Mercator projection maps will show Africa in the middle of the map. Africa is nearly cut in half by the equator. The continent's most northern point is located at 37°21′ N, and its most southern point is 34°51′15″ S. The distance between those two points is about 5,000 miles. Interestingly, the distance from the continent's most western and eastern points is also nearly 5,000 miles (4,600 miles). The western most point is located at longitude 17°31′13″W, and the eastern most point is located at 51°27′52″ E. Africa's location is changing as well. Due to plate tectonics, the African continent is slowly moving northeast at a rate of about 2.15 cm per year.
