1. Did you talk to a loved one (family member) your sexual orientation or gender identity? Essay


Gender identity refers to an individual's internal sense of themselves as male, female, or non-binary. It is different from biological sex, which is determined by one's physical characteristics, such as chromosomes, hormones, and genitalia. Gender identity is a deeply personal and subjective experience, and it can vary from person to person.

For most people, their gender identity matches their biological sex, and they identify as either male or female. However, for some individuals, their gender identity does not align with their biological sex. This is known as gender dysphoria or transgender identity. Transgender individuals may experience discomfort or distress because their gender identity is not recognized or supported by society.

Non-binary individuals, on the other hand, do not identify as exclusively male or female. They may identify as both, neither, or a combination of both. Non-binary individuals may use gender-neutral pronouns, such as they/them, or other gender-neutral language.

Gender identity is an important aspect of one's identity and can have a significant impact on a person's mental health and well-being. Discrimination and lack of understanding about gender identity can lead to feelings of isolation, anxiety, and depression. Transgender individuals may also face barriers to accessing healthcare, housing, and employment, which can exacerbate these negative effects.

It is important to understand and respect the diversity of gender identity and to support individuals in their self-expression and identity. This can include using preferred pronouns, creating gender-neutral spaces, and advocating for policies and laws that protect the rights of transgender and non-binary individuals.

Gender identity is a complex and nuanced concept that requires open-mindedness, empathy, and understanding. By creating a more inclusive and accepting society, we can help support the well-being and dignity of all individuals, regardless of their gender identity.

The Filipino value system or Filipino values refers to the set of values that a majority of the Filipino have historically held important in their lives. This Philippine values system includes their own unique assemblage of consistent ideologies, moral codes, ethical practices, etiquette and cultural and personal values that are promoted by their society. As with any society though, the values that an individual holds sacred can differ on the basis of religion, upbringing and other factors.

As a general description, the distinct value system of Filipinos is rooted primarily in personal alliance systems, especially those based in kinship, obligation, friendship, religion (particularly Christianity) and commercial relationships.[1]
