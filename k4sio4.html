4. How many grams of K2SO4 are contained In 50.0 mL of 0.200 N solution? How many millimoles of K2SO4 are present? What Is the molarity of this solution? ​


mass = 0.87 gr

millimoles = 5

molarity of solution = 0.1 M

Further explanation

Molarity is a way to express the concentration of the solution  

Molarity shows the number of moles of solute in every 1 liter of solute or mmol in each ml of solution  

 [tex]\rm \boxed{\bold{M=\dfrac{n}{V}}}[/tex]

Where  

M = Molarity  

n = Number of moles of solute  

V = Volume of solution  

Normality = the number of moles equivalent of solute in 1 liter of solution

[tex]\rm \boxed{\bold{ N=\dfrac{eq}{L}}}[/tex]

[tex]\rm eq=\dfrac{mass}{equivalent~weight}[/tex]

Relationship between normality and molarity

N = M x n ⇒ n = valence

1. mass

50.0 mL of 0.200 N solution

K₂SO₄ , there are two potassium ions per sulfate ion, so :

[tex]\rm BE~K_2SO_4=\dfrac{molar~mass}{2}=\dfrac{174}{2}=87[/tex]

mass of K₂SO₄ :

[tex]\rm N=\dfrac{mass/equivalent~weight}{L}\\\\0.2=\dfrac{mass/87}{0.05~L}\\\\mass=\boxed{\bold{0.87~gr}}[/tex]

2. milimoles

equivalent = mol x valence (valence = 2 for K₂SO₄)

[tex]\rm mol=\dfrac{equivalent}{2}=\dfrac{mass/EW}{2}=\dfrac{0.87/87}{2}=\boxed{\bold{0.005~mol=5~mlmol}}[/tex]

3. Molarity

[tex]\rm M=\dfrac{n}{V}=\dfrac{0.005}{0.05}=\boxed{\bold{0.1~M}}[/tex]

or :

[tex]\rm M=\dfrac{Normality}{2}=\dfrac{0.2}{2}=0.1~M[/tex]

5. What mass of solute is needed to prepare 1.00 L of 0.125 M K2SO4 solution? ​


Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of solute (K₂SO₄).

molar mass = (39.10 g/mol × 2) + (32.07 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 4)

molar mass = 174.27 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

moles of solute = molarity × volume of solution (L)

moles of solute = 0.125 M × 1.00 L

moles of solute = 0.125 mol

Step 3: Calculate the mass of solute.

[tex]\text{mass of solute = 0.125 mol K₂SO₄} × \frac{\text{174.27 g K₂SO₄}}{\text{1 mol K₂SO₄}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of solute = 21.8 g}}[/tex]

9. H2SO4+ 2KOH →KSO4+ 2H2O what kind of chemical reaction is this?


Answer:

Neutralization reaction

Explanation:

In neutralization, the reactants are an acid and a base and their products are a salt and water. In the question above, H₂SO₄ is the acid, KOH is the base, and K₂SO₄ is the salt formed by the reaction of H₂SO₄ and KOH. The general formula for this reaction is

acid + base → salt + water

24. aqueous solution of potassium sulfate (K2SO4) has a freezing point of –2.24 ºC. What is its molality? (Kf = 1.86​


Answer:

m = 1.204 molal

Explanation:

Calculate the freezing point depression.

FP solution = FP solvent - ∆Tf

∆Tf = FP solvent - FP solution

∆Tf = 0.00 °C - (-2.24 °C)

∆Tf = 2.24 °C

Solve for the molality.

∆Tf = m • kf

m = ∆Tf / kf

m = 2.24 °C / 1.86 °C/molal

m = 1.204 molal
