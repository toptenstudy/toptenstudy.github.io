7. description of public communication​


Public communication refers to any type of communication that is intended for a large audience. It can be delivered in any medium, including spoken word, written text, and visual media. Public communication can be used to promote a product or service, build community support, or raise awareness about an issue.

Public communication can be divided into two main categories: formal and informal. Formal public communication happens when governments, businesses, and other organizations engage in planned messaging campaigns. These campaigns often use advertising slogans, media coverage, and other forms of persuasion to reach a wide audience. Informal public communication occurs when individuals communicate with friends and family members using personal conversations and social media posts. It is usually less formal than formal public communication, but it can still have a powerful impact on the public's understanding of an issue.

Public communication is the process of conveying information to a designated audience. It can be done in various ways, such as through print media, television, or the internet. When publicizing a message, it is important to choose the right media and format for reaching your target audience.
