Capiz shell is named after one of the province of the Philippines which is Capiz since it is where it flourishes and very abundant. Capiz Shell Crafts are one of the everyday living sources of some of the Filipino people. That's why Philippines are known to be the primary exporter of capiz shell crafts. Capiz comes from the shell of the Placuna placenta mollusk, which is native to the seas of Southeast Asia, specifically Indonesia and the Philippines. The mollusks are edible, which means fisherman can harvest them for their meat and reduce waste by reusing their shells for décor and handicrafts.

Description of Capiz Shells

What is Capiz Shell?

Capiz comes from the shell of the Placuna placenta mollusk, which is native to the seas of Southeast Asia, specifically Indonesia and the Philippines. The mollusks are edible, which means fisherman can harvest them for their meat and reduce waste by reusing their shells for décor and handicrafts.
