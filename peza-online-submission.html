14. what is the meaning of submissiveness​


Answer:

the quality of being submissive.

Answer:

Submissiveness refers to the quality or state of being submissive, which means to be willing to obey or yield to the authority, will or desires of another person or group. It can also imply a passive or meek demeanor, where one may avoid conflict or confrontation by yielding to others' wishes or demands.

Submissiveness can be a trait or behavior that is learned and developed over time, and may be influenced by factors such as cultural and societal norms, upbringing, personality, and personal experiences. Some individuals may see submissiveness as a positive trait that promotes harmony and cooperation in social relationships, while others may view it as a negative trait that encourages exploitation or abuse by those in power. The interpretation of submissiveness can depend on context and the specific circumstances in which it is expressed.

A person who always agrees with their partner's opinions and decisions, even when they have different beliefs.
