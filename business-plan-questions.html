Benchmarking is the practice of comparing a business performance with the best practices in the industry for the purpose of improving. Forecasting is the best planning tool because a business prepares for the future through past data unlike benchmarking which is a performance improvement tool.

Why Benchmarking is a better planning tool?

Benchmarking helps companies focus on capabilities that are critical to building strategic advantage. Increase the rate of organizational learning. Benchmarking brings new ideas into the company and facilitates experience sharing.

23. Activity 1: Answer the following question based on your own understanding (Please use separate paper). 1. What is meant by Business Plan? 2. What are the different parts of Business Plan and give the characteristics of each part? 3. Think of a business you may want to engage in and try to make a simple business plan on your chosen business.​


Answer:

1. A business plan is an essential written document that provides a description and overview of your company's future. 

2 .

-The Executive Summary

The executive summary is the first paragraph investors read.

-The Business Description

The description fills in the details outlined in your summary.

- Descriptions of Products and Services

The descriptions should show why the products and

services are innovative, unique and exciting.

-Defining the Target Market

Your target market must be clearly defined. You need to show detailed thought about who your customers are and that you have identified their characteristics:

Who are the likely customers?

Are they male or female?

What are their ages?

Where do they live?

How many customers are in your target market?

Is the market growing, shrinking, or is it stable?

What are the national and local trends that affect your industry?

-Outline Your Competitive Advantages

In this section, you have to spell out how you intend to forge a share of the market against more established competitors. 

-Describe Your Marketing Strategy

After you identify the weaknesses of your competitors, the marketing plan should describe how you plan to exploit those weaknesses.

-Financial Projections and Profit Plan

The objective of financial projections is to show that you have worked through the numbers and come up with a plan to make a profit.

-Sell Your Management Team

The real heart of any business is its people. Even the best ideas will fail if a company does not have skilled and aggressive employees.
