2. why is it important to conduct a study of chosen within the chosen topic? (family problems po ang topic) ​


Answer:

Conducting a study on family problems is important for several reasons:

Better understanding: A study on family problems can help individuals gain a better understanding of the causes, effects, and potential solutions to various issues that affect families. This can lead to improved relationships, communication, and overall well-being for families.

Addressing social issues: Family problems can have significant impacts on individuals, communities, and society as a whole. By conducting a study on family problems, researchers can identify patterns, trends, and potential solutions that can help address social issues related to family problems, such as domestic violence, child abuse, and divorce.

Developing effective interventions: A study on family problems can help develop effective interventions and strategies to prevent and address various issues that affect families. This can include counseling, education, and other resources that can help individuals and families cope with and overcome challenges.

Informing policy and decision-making: Findings from a study on family problems can inform policy and decision-making related to family law, social welfare, and healthcare. This can lead to more effective and targeted interventions and resources that can improve the lives of families and communities.

Overall, conducting a study on family problems is important to gain a better understanding of the issues that affect families, develop effective interventions, and inform policy and decision-making. This can ultimately lead to healthier, happier families and communities.

12. formulate the research methodology in your chosen topic​


Answer:

The methods section describes actions to be taken to investigate a research problem and the rationale for the application of specific procedures or techniques used to identify, select, process, and analyze information applied to understanding the problem, thereby, allowing the reader to critically evaluate a study’s overall validity and reliability. The methodology section of a research paper answers two main questions: How was the data collected or generated? And, how was it analyzed? The writing should be direct and precise and always written in the past tense.

Kallet, Richard H. "How to Write the Methods Section of a Research Paper." Respiratory Care 49 (October 2004): 1229-1232.

A graphic organizer visually represents ideas, concepts, and relationships between various components. Concept maps and knowledge maps all are types of graphic organizers. You can use any chart or diagram as a graphic organizer to compare facts and depict a story.

A graphic organizer is a visual and graphic display that depicts the relationships between facts, terms, and or ideas within a learning task. Graphic organizers are also sometimes referred to as knowledge maps, concept maps, story maps, cognitive organizers, advance organizers, or concept diagrams.
