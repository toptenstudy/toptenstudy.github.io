1. who proposed the liberal theory of global citizenship​


Explanation:

The concept of global citizenship, or the idea that individuals have responsibilities and rights as members of the global community, has been developed and discussed by a number of scholars and thinkers over the years. However, one of the most well-known proponents of the liberal theory of global citizenship is Martha Nussbaum, an American philosopher and legal scholar who has written extensively on the topic.

In her work, Nussbaum argues that global citizenship should be grounded in a set of universal human capabilities, or the basic abilities and functions that all human beings should be able to fulfill in order to live a minimally decent life. These capabilities include, but are not limited to, the ability to think, to use reason, to communicate, to form and maintain relationships, and to participate in the political process.

Nussbaum contends that the realization of these capabilities is essential for the development of a just and inclusive global society, and that individuals have a responsibility to promote and defend the rights and capabilities of others. She also argues that states have a responsibility to protect and promote the capabilities of their citizens, and to respect the human rights of individuals within and beyond their borders.

Overall, Nussbaum's work has had a significant influence on the way that global citizenship is understood and theorized, and has helped to shape the way that individuals and societies think about their responsibilities to each other and to the global community.

5. How is neo-liberalism ideology related to globalization?


Answer:

Neoliberalism is an ideology that emphasizes free markets, free trade, and minimal government intervention in the economy. It is often associated with the belief that deregulation, privatization, and lower taxes can lead to economic growth and prosperity.

Globalization refers to the increasing interconnectedness and interdependence of the world's economies, cultures, and populations, brought about by the increasing cross-border movement of goods, services, technology, and capital.

Neoliberalism has played a significant role in shaping the global economic system and driving the process of globalization. Neoliberal policies such as free trade and the liberalization of capital markets have facilitated the increased cross-border flow of goods, services, and capital, leading to the integration of global markets and the emergence of transnational corporations.

However, neoliberal globalization has also been criticized for leading to income inequality, environmental degradation, and cultural homogenization. Some argue that the focus on maximizing profit and minimizing regulation has led to negative consequences for workers, communities, and the environment.


6. What are the causes of women oppression in liberal feminist theory


Answer:

Liberal feminists believe that women's oppression is caused by societal norms and cultural beliefs that discriminate against and disadvantage women. These cultural beliefs may include the idea that men are superior to women or that women are not as capable as men. In addition, liberal feminists believe that economic and political systems that discriminate against women also contribute to their oppression. For example, women may be paid less than men for doing the same job, or they may not have the same access to education and employment opportunities as men. Liberal feminists also point to laws and policies that discriminate against women, such as those that restrict women's reproductive rights or limit their ability to own property or inherit from their families. All of these factors contribute to the oppression of women in liberal feminist theory.

Liberalism, political doctrine that takes protecting and enhancing the freedom of the individual to be the central problem of politics. Liberals typically believe that government is necessary to protect individuals from being harmed by others, but they also recognize that government itself can pose a threat to liberty. As the revolutionary American pamphleteer Thomas Paine expressed it in Common Sense (1776), government is at best “a necessary evil.” Laws, judges, and police are needed to secure the individual’s life and liberty, but their coercive power may also be turned against him. The problem, then, is to devise a system that gives government the power necessary to protect individual liberty but also prevents those who govern from abusing that power.
