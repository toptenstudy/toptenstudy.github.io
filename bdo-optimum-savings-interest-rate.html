9. Ben borrowed 3,000,000 from the BDO that charges 12% interest per annum on personal loans. How much will he pay for his loan after 3 years​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P = 3,000,000 \\ & r = 12\% = 0.12 \\ & t = \text{3 years} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the future worth.

[tex]\begin{aligned} F & = P(1 + rt) \\ & = 3,000,000[1 + 0.12(3)] \\ & = 3,000,000(1 + 0.36) \\ & = 3,000,000(1.36) \\ & = \boxed{4,080,000} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, Ben will pay 4,080,000 for his loan after 3 years.

20. GENERAL ANNUITY (FINDING THE FUTURE VALUE) BDO bank pays interest at the rate of 3% compounded quarterly. How much will have in the bank at the end of 6 years if Ms. Rona deposits P5,000 every month?​


Answer:

To find the future value of an annuity, you can use the formula:

future value = payment * (((1 + interest rate/n)^n*t - 1) / (interest rate/n))

Where:

payment is the amount deposited each period (in this case, P5,000 per month)

interest rate is the rate at which the annuity earns interest (in this case, 3% per year)

n is the number of times the interest is compounded per year (in this case, quarterly, so n = 4)

t is the number of years the annuity is invested (in this case, 6 years)

Plugging in the values given in the problem, we get:

future value = 5000 * (((1 + 0.03/4)^(4*6) - 1) / (0.03/4))

= 5000 * (((1.0075^24) - 1) / 0.0075)

= 5000 * ((1.212161552 - 1) / 0.0075)

= 5000 * (0.212161552 / 0.0075)

= 5000 * 28.2948872

= P141,474.44

So at the end of 6 years, Ms. Rona will have P141,474.44 in the bank if she deposits P5,000 every month and the bank pays interest at a rate of 3% compounded quarterly.

At temperatures above 68 degrees Fahrenheit, or 20 degrees Celsius, the rate of photosynthesis decreases because the enzymes do not work as efficiently at this temperature. This is despite the increase of carbon dioxide diffusion into leaves.

Temperature. ... As with any other enzyme-controlled reaction, the rate of photosynthesis is affected by temperature. At low temperatures, the rate of photosynthesis is limited by the number of molecular collisions between enzymes and substrates. At high temperatures, enzymes are denatured .
