15. find the area to the left of -2.179 under a t-distribution with 12 degrees of freedom ​


Answer:

To find the area to the left of -2.179 under a t-distribution with 12 degrees of freedom, we can use a t-distribution table or a statistical software that provides t-distribution calculations.

Using a t-distribution table:

1.Look for the row that corresponds to the degrees of freedom (df) of 12.

2.Look for the column that corresponds to -2.179.

3.The value in the table at the intersection of the row and column represents the area to the left of -2.179 under the t-distribution with 12 degrees of freedom.

Please note that t-distribution tables typically provide values for positive t-scores, so you may need to use the symmetry property of the t-distribution to find the area to the left of a negative t-score.

Alternatively, you can use statistical software such as R, Python, or a calculator with t-distribution functions to calculate the area. For example, using R:

# Load the stats package

library(stats)

# Set the degrees of freedom

df <- 12

# Calculate the area to the left of -2.179

area <- pt(-2.179, df)

area

The result would be the area to the left of -2.179 under a t-distribution with 12 degrees of freedom.
