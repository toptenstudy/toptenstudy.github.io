Fungal infections are common throughout much of the natural world. In humans, fungal infections occur when an invading fungus takes over an area of the body and is too much for the immune system to handle.

Fungi can live in the air, soil, water, and plants. There are also some fungi that live naturally in the human body.

Like many microbes, there are helpful fungi and harmful fungi. When harmful fungi invade the body, they can be difficult to kill, as they can survive in the environment and re-infect the person trying to get better.

- skin changes, including red and possibly cracking or peeling skin

12. Common Poultry Disease (5 disease)Name of DiseaseSign and Symptomsprevention control, treatment​


Answer:

Here are five common poultry diseases, along with their signs and symptoms, and prevention, control, and treatment methods:

Avian Influenza (Bird Flu)

Signs and Symptoms: Sudden death, coughing, sneezing, difficulty breathing, decrease in egg production, and diarrhea

Prevention and Control: Vaccination, biosecurity measures such as limiting contact with other birds and using disinfectants, and culling infected birds

Treatment: There is no specific treatment for avian influenza, infected birds are usually culled to prevent the spread of the disease.

Newcastle Disease

Signs and Symptoms: Respiratory distress, nervous signs such as tremors, paralysis, and circling, greenish diarrhea, and decreased egg production

Prevention and Control: Vaccination, biosecurity measures, such as limiting contact with other birds and using disinfectants, and culling infected birds

Treatment: There is no specific treatment for Newcastle disease. Infected birds are usually culled to prevent the spread of the disease.

Coccidiosis

Signs and Symptoms: Diarrhea, loss of appetite, weight loss, and dehydration

Prevention and Control: Good sanitation and hygiene practices, such as cleaning and disinfecting equipment and limiting overcrowding, and medication, such as coccidiostats, given in feed or water

Treatment: Medication, such as coccidiostats, given in feed or water.

Fowl Pox

Signs and Symptoms: Yellow-white lesions on the skin around the beak, eyes, and comb, decreased egg production, and respiratory distress

Prevention and Control: Vaccination, biosecurity measures, such as limiting contact with other birds and using disinfectants, and culling infected birds

Treatment: There is no specific treatment for fowl pox. Infected birds are usually culled to prevent the spread of the disease.

Infectious Bronchitis

Signs and Symptoms: Sneezing, coughing, nasal discharge, and decreased egg production

Prevention and Control: Vaccination, biosecurity measures, such as limiting contact with other birds and using disinfectants, and culling infected birds

Treatment: There is no specific treatment for infectious bronchitis. Infected birds are usually given supportive care, such as fluids and electrolytes, and sometimes antibiotics to prevent secondary infections.

In summary, these five common poultry diseases can cause significant losses in poultry production if not adequately managed. Prevention through vaccination and biosecurity measures is the most effective way to control the spread of these diseases. Infected birds should be isolated, culled, or given appropriate medication, depending on the disease

Answer:

Infectious diseases are disorders caused by organisms — such as bacteria, viruses, fungi or parasites. Many organisms live in and on our bodies. They're normally harmless or even helpful. But under certain conditions, some organisms may cause disease.

Some infectious diseases can be passed from person to person. Some are transmitted by insects or other animals. And you may get others by consuming contaminated food or water or being exposed to organisms in the environment.

Signs and symptoms vary depending on the organism causing the infection, but often include fever and fatigue. Mild infections may respond to rest and home remedies, while some life-threatening infections may need hospitalization.

Many infectious diseases, such as measles and chickenpox, can be prevented by vaccines. Frequent and thorough hand-washing also helps protect you from most infectious diseases.
