5. PERFORMANCE TASK IN ENGLISH 5 FIRST QUARTER Performance Task 2 Inferring the Meaning of Compound Words Using Context Clues Read some stories or get hold of storybooks. Find and list five examples of each type of compound word. Open Closed Hyphenated US Gelb Danilor​


Answer: When two words are used together to yield a new meaning, a compound is formed. Compound words can be written in three ways: as open compounds (spelled as two words, e.g., ice cream), closed compounds (joined to form a single word, e.g., doorknob), or hyphenated compounds (two words joined by a hyphen, e.g., long-term). Sometimes, more than two words can form a compound (e.g., mother-in-law).

The most common spelling quandary writers face is whether to write compounds as separate words, one word, or hyphenated words.

I. Title: How to Make a Pencil Holder out of Tin Cans

II. Materials Needed:

Clean tin cansPaint (any color of your choice)PaintbrushesScissors

III. Procedure:

First, clean the tin cans thoroughly and remove any labels or stickers.

Next, paint the tin cans using the paint and paintbrushes. Apply a thin layer of paint and let it dry for a few hours. You can apply more layers of paint for a more vibrant color.

Then, use scissors to cut the top part of the tin can off. Be careful not to cut yourself.

Finally, your recycled pencil holder is now ready to use. You can also decorate it with stickers, ribbons or other materials to make it look even more attractive.

Note: Remember to recycle your empty tin cans after use. Not only does it help reduce waste, but it also helps to save our planet!
