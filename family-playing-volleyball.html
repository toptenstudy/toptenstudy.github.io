To find the probability that a student plays basketball or volleyball, we need to add the probabilities of playing basketball and playing volleyball, but we need to subtract the probability of playing both sports once to avoid double-counting.

Let's start by creating a Venn diagram to represent the situation:

From the given information, we know that:

|B| = 35 (the number of students who play basketball)

|V| = 25 (the number of students who play volleyball)

|BV| = 10 (the number of students who play both sports)

To find the probability that a student plays basketball or volleyball, we can use the formula:

P(B or V) = P(B) + P(V) - P(B and V)

where P(B) is the probability of playing basketball, P(V) is the probability of playing volleyball, and P(B and V) is the probability of playing both sports.

We can find P(B) by dividing the number of students who play basketball by the total number of students:

P(B) = |B| / 100 = 35 / 100 = 0.35

Similarly, we can find P(V) by dividing the number of students who play volleyball by the total number of students:

P(V) = |V| / 100 = 25 / 100 = 0.25

To find P(B and V), we can use the formula:

P(B and V) = |BV| / 100 = 10 / 100 = 0.1

Now we can substitute these values into the formula for P(B or V):

P(B or V) = 0.35 + 0.25 - 0.1 = 0.5

Therefore, the probability that a student plays basketball or volleyball is (((0.5, or 50%.)))

18. Suppose out of 50 grade 10 students, 10 play basketball, 20 play volleyball and 5 play basketball and volleyball. What is the probability that a student play basketball or volleyball?​


Answer:

To calculate the probability that a student plays basketball or volleyball, we need to add the probabilities of each event separately, while accounting for the overlap of students who play both sports.

Given the information provided:

Number of students who play basketball (B) = 10

Number of students who play volleyball (V) = 20

Number of students who play both basketball and volleyball (B ∩ V) = 5

To find the probability of a student playing basketball or volleyball (B ∪ V), we can use the following formula:

P(B ∪ V) = P(B) + P(V) - P(B ∩ V)

where P(B) is the probability of playing basketball, P(V) is the probability of playing volleyball, and P(B ∩ V) is the probability of playing both basketball and volleyball.

Plugging in the values:

P(B) = 10/50 = 0.2

P(V) = 20/50 = 0.4

P(B ∩ V) = 5/50 = 0.1

Now we can calculate the probability of playing basketball or volleyball:

P(B ∪ V) = P(B) + P(V) - P(B ∩ V)

= 0.2 + 0.4 - 0.1

= 0.5

So, the probability that a student plays basketball or volleyball is 0.5 or 50%.
