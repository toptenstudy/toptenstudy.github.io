- jew's harp

29. 10. It is a bamboo zither made from a single- a bamboo piece​


Answer:

BAMBOO ZITHER

Explanation:

Since there is no specified region or place for the bamboo zither , I decided to write some of the local name and origin of the instrument for you to choose from.

1. Kolitong

Kolitong is a bamboo polychordal zither of Kalinga. It has six strings that is parallel to the body of the bamboo tube. Strings are from strings 1 to 6 with string one as the lowest and six the highest. The bamboo tube acts as the resonator of the instrument.

2. Pagang

Pagang is a polychordal zither from Palawan by the Palaw-an ethnolinguistic group. It is made from a single bamboo node. The strings are raised using frets underneath the strings. A crack o one side of the bamboo is made to allow the sound to vibrate on the tube's body. The bamboo polychordal zither  is played by plucking the strings.

3. Tangkol and Takumbo
