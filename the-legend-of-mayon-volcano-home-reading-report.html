4. Moral lesson of legend of mayon volcano


The moral lesson of the Legend of Mayon Volcano is indicated below.

But first, let us clearly define the meaning of moral lesson, which is properly known as “moral”. Moral is the lesson of a story. It is supposed to teach a reader, viewer, or listener on how to become a better person after finishing a story.

Regarding the Legend of the Mayon Volcano, the moral that can be captured from the story is to follow rules, respect authorities and respect others’ feelings.

- Pangaronon (the proud one) went to Makusog (the strong one) who is the father of his loved one named Daragang Magayon (the lovely one). Makusog is also the head of a tribe. Pangaronon went to see Makusog to ask permission to marry Daragang Magayon. However, even if Makusog wanted to allow his daughter to marry Pangaronon, he followed the tribal law of forbidding marriage outside of a clan.

- Pangaronon respected Makusog because he is the father of his loved one and the head of a tribe.

- Even if what they wanted did not work out, Makusog’s and Pangaronon’s obedience to rules and authorities should have led to a peaceful community.

- However, Patuga (the eruptive one), who was the most ardent suitor of Daragang Magayon, did not respect authorities and kidnapped Makusog so that Daragang Magayon would be forced to marry him.

- This disobedience of Patuga led to his death, the death of Pangaronon and the death of Daragang Magayon.

- If only Patuga respected authorities and respected the feelings of Daragang Magayon just as how Makusog and Pangaronon showed obedience and respect, Patuga’s actions would not have resulted to the deaths of the characters.

- This tells us to respect laws, authorities, and other people so that we can maintain a harmonious relationship to other people and to the entire community.

That is the Legend of Mayon Volcano’s moral lesson, also known as Daragang Magayon moral lesson. It can also be considered as a reflection about the Legend of Mayon Volcano.

Answer:

In a place called ibalon, there is a maiden called Magayon, who is the daughter of the chief of the tribe named Makusog. Her beauty attracted several men in different places.One of the said men was Pagtuga, who is a warrior and chief of Iraga. He tried to woo Magayon several times but to no avail.

One day, Magayon bathed in her favorite spot called the Yawa river. She suddenly slipped on the rocks and cried for help.

This caught the attention of Pangaronon, who happened to be nearby, and saved her from drowning. They began creating a very close relationship and even asked Makusog for her hand in marriage.

The news spread of Magayon’s marriage, even Pagtuga, who kidnapped Magayon and threatened her to marry him or his father will die.

Pangaronon went to the place and try to take her lover back, hence, a battle ensued. Pangaronon eventually won, but as he was about to embrace Magayon, she was struck with a poisoned arrow.

Pangaronon knelt over his dying lover and eventually got stabbed by one of Pagtuga’s men. Makusog buried his daughter and her lover together after the battle.

Years passed and the land where they buried rose higher and higher, which was eventually called Mayon.
