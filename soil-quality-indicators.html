5. what job does the indicators of air,water, and soil quality do?(Long definition pls?)


Answer:

Scientists use soil quality indicators to evaluate how well soil functions since soil function often cannot be directly measured. Measuring soil quality is an exercise in identifying soil properties that are responsive to management, affect or correlate with environmental outcomes, and are capable of being precisely measured within certain technical and economic constraints. Soil quality indicators may be qualitative (e.g. drainage is fast) or quantitative (infiltration= 2.5 in/hr).

There are three main categories of soil indicators: chemical, physical and biological. Typical soil tests only look at chemical indicators. Soil quality attempts to integrate all three types of indicators. The categories do not neatly align with the various soil functions, so integration is necessary. The table below shows the relationship between indicator type and soil function.

Organic matter, or more specifically soil carbon, transcends all three indicator categories and has the most widely recognized influence on soil quality. Organic matter is tied to all soil functions. It affects other indicators, such as aggregate stability (physical), nutrient retention and availability (chemical), and nutrient cycling (biological); and is itself an indicator of soil quality.

Chemical indicators can give you information about the equilibrium between soil solution (soil water and nutrients) and exchange sites (clay particles, organic matter); plant health; the nutritional requirements of plant and soil animal communities; and levels of soil contaminants and their availability for uptake by animals and plants. Indicators include measures of:

Electrical conductivitySoil nitrateSoil reaction

Biological indicators can tell us about the organisms that form the soil food web that are responsible for decomposition of organic matter and nutrient cycling. Information about the numbers of organisms, both individuals and species, that perform similar jobs or niches, can indicate a soil's ability to function or bounce back after disturbance (resistance and resilience). Indicators include measures of:

EarthwormsParticulate oraganic matterPotentially mineralizible nitrogenRespirationSoil enzymesTotal oraganic carbon

14. Indicators of Air, Water and Soil Quality


Water:
Biological indicators are direct measures of the health of the fauna and flora in the waterway. Commonly used biological indicators in freshwater include various measures of macroinvertebrate or fish diversity, benthic algal growth and benthic oxygen demand.
Soil:
These indicators include (1) soil texture, (2) depth of soils, topsoil or rooting, (3) infiltration, (4) soil bulk density, and (5) water holding capacity.
Air:
Lichens and bryophytes serve as effective bioindicators of air quality because they have no roots, no cuticle, and acquire all their nutrients from direct exposure to the atmosphere. Their high surface area to volume ratio further encourages the interception and accumulation of contaminants from the air.

23. After reading the articles, I need you to answer the following questions to help you process the information. How does these indicators help to test the quality of water, air and soil? Why do we need to know those indicators? How does living organism affect the quality of air, water and soil?​


Answer:

There are various indicators that can be used to test the quality of water, air, and soil. These indicators can help to measure various physical, chemical, and biological properties of these environments that can impact their suitability for different uses. Some of the main indicators that are used to test the quality of water, air, and soil include:

Water quality indicators: Some of the key indicators that are used to test the quality of water include pH, temperature, dissolved oxygen, total dissolved solids, and the presence of various pollutants and contaminants. These indicators can help to determine whether the water is safe to drink, whether it is suitable for use in agriculture or industry, and whether it is able to support the growth of aquatic life.

Air quality indicators: Some of the key indicators that are used to test the quality of air include the concentration of various pollutants, such as particulate matter, ozone, carbon dioxide, and sulfur dioxide. These indicators can help to determine whether the air is safe to breathe and whether it is able to support the growth of plants and other living organisms.

Soil quality indicators: Some of the key indicators that are used to test the quality of soil include pH, nutrient content, organic matter content, and the presence of various pollutants and contaminants. These indicators can help to determine whether the soil is suitable for growing crops or other plants, and whether it is able to support the growth of various types of microorganisms.

We need to know these indicators in order to understand the overall health and quality of our environment and to ensure that it is able to support the needs of living organisms. By understanding the various indicators that are used to test the quality of water, air, and soil, we can take steps to protect and preserve these vital resources for future generations.

Living organisms can have a significant impact on the quality of air, water, and soil. For example, the activities of humans and other animals can lead to the release of pollutants and contaminants into the environment, which can impact the quality of these resources. Similarly, the growth of plants and other organisms can alter the chemical and physical properties of the soil and water, which can impact their suitability for different uses. By understanding the role that living organisms play in the quality of these resources, we can take steps to minimize negative impacts and preserve their health and vitality.
