4. If AD is an angle bisector of LAB and mZDAB= 49°, what is m/LAB? (Sample) answer: 10 degrees)​


If AD is an angle bisector of ∠LAB and m∠DAB = 49°, what is m∠LAB?

The angle bisector divides the angle into two equal parts. That means, in this problem, m∠DAB is equal to m∠LAD.

m∠DAB = m∠LADm∠DAB = 49°m∠LAD = 49°

m∠LAB is the sum of m∠DAB and m∠LAD. Let's put it like this:

m∠LAB = m∠DAB + m∠LAD

Substitute the value/measure of ∠DAB and ∠LAD to the equation above. Solve to find the answer to the original problem.

m∠LAB = m∠DAB + m∠LADm∠LAB = 49° + 49°m∠LAB = 98°

Thus, the answer to the question: “what is m∠LAB?”, is 98°.

15. Research Problem #1: Suppose the researcher wishes to conduct a study on the effect of lab-lab and seaweed meal as supplemental feeds of 20 milkfish cultured in the fish pond. Lab-lab is the control group because this is the natural food of milkfish and seaweed meal is the experimental group. How will you design the experiment? Design Parameters: Type of Research Design: Experimental units/subjects: Treatment/s: Diagram/Illustration:


Type of Research Design: The study will use a randomized complete block design.

Experimental units/subjects: The experimental units will be 20 milkfish that will be cultured in a fish pond.

Treatment/s: The two treatments will be lab-lab (control group) and seaweed meal (experimental group). The milkfish will be randomly assigned to either the control group or the experimental group. The control group will be fed with lab-lab, which is the natural food of milkfish, while the experimental group will be fed with seaweed meal.

The design ensures that the experimental groups have an equal number of milkfish and that the control and experimental groups are balanced.
