6. MTC Corporation's top management wants to measure the company's overall cost of capital. The following data were gathered by their finance department: • The firm's common stock is currently selling for P90.00 per share. The firm expects to pay cash dividends of P7.00 per share next year. The firm's dividends have been growing at an annual rate of 6%, and this growth is expected to continue into the future. The stock must be under-priced by P7.00 per share, and flotation costs are expected to amount to P5.00 per share. The firm can sell new common stock under these terms. Furthermore, the company's finance department identified that the risk-free rate is at six percent; the market return is ten percent; and the firm's beta is 1.9444445. • The firm is in the 40% tax bracket. The firm can raise debt by selling P1,000-par value, 8% coupon interest rate, 20-year bonds on which annual interest payments will be made. To sell the issue, an average discount of P30.00 per bond would have to be given. The firm also must pay flotation costs of P30.00 per bond. • The firm can sell 8% preferred stock at its P95-per-share par value. The cost of issuing and selling the preferred stock is expected to be P5.00 per share. Preferred stock can be sold under these terms. The firm uses the weights shown in the following table, which are based on the target capital structure proportions to calculate its weighted average cost of capital. SOURCE OF CAPITAL AMOUNT Long-term debt 90,000 Preferred stock 60,000 Common stock 150,000 Total 300,000 1. What is the company's after-tax cost of debt? 2. What is the company's cost of preferred stocks? 3. What is the company's cost of retained earnings? 4. What is the company's weighted average cost of capital using retained earnings? 5. What is the company's weighted average cost of capital using new issue common stocks?


Answer:

1. The after-tax cost of debt can be calculated as follows:

Coupon rate = 8%

Discount = P30.00

Flotation cost = P30.00

Net proceeds = P1,000 - P30 - P30 = P940

Effective cost of debt = coupon rate x (1 - tax rate) = 8% x (1 - 0.40) = 4.8%

After-tax cost of debt = effective cost of debt x (1 - discount / net proceeds) = 4.8% x (1 - 30 / 940) = 4.36%

Therefore, the company's after-tax cost of debt is 4.36%.

2. The cost of preferred stocks can be calculated as follows:

Cost of preferred stocks = dividend / net proceeds - flotation cost

Dividend = P95.00 x 8% = P7.60

Flotation cost = P5.00

Net proceeds = P95.00 - P5.00 = P90.00

Cost of preferred stocks = 7.60 / 90 - 5.00 / 90 = 0.0467 or 4.67%

Therefore, the company's cost of preferred stocks is 4.67%.

3. The cost of retained earnings can be calculated using the Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM) as follows:

Cost of retained earnings = risk-free rate + beta x (market return - risk-free rate)

Risk-free rate = 6%

Beta = 1.9444445

Market return = 10%

Cost of retained earnings = 6% + 1.9444445 x (10% - 6%) = 13.78%

Therefore, the company's cost of retained earnings is 13.78%.

4. The weighted average cost of capital using retained earnings can be calculated as follows:

WACC = (cost of debt x weight of debt) + (cost of preferred stocks x weight of preferred stocks) + (cost of retained earnings x weight of common stock)

Weight of debt = 90,000 / 300,000 = 0.3

Weight of preferred stocks = 60,000 / 300,000 = 0.2

Weight of common stock = 150,000 / 300,000 = 0.5

WACC = (4.36% x 0.3) + (4.67% x 0.2) + (13.78% x 0.5) = 9.79%

Therefore, the company's weighted average cost of capital using retained earnings is 9.79%.

5. The weighted average cost of capital using new issue common stocks can be calculated as follows:

Cost of new issue common stocks = (dividend / net proceeds - flotation cost) / (1 - flotation cost / market price)

Dividend = P7.00 x 1.06 = P7.42

Flotation cost = P5.00

Net proceeds = P90.00 - P5.00 = P85.00

Market price = P90.00 + P7.00 / (10% - 6%) - P5.00 = P90.71

Cost of new issue common stocks = (7.42 / 85 - 5.00 / 90.71) / (1 - 5.00 / 90.71) = 14.08%

Therefore, the company's weighted average cost of capital using new issue common stocks is 14.08%.

Weighted Average Capital Cost

The expense of each financial institution (debt and equity) is multiplied by the appropriate weight by market value, and the products are then added to get the total cost of capital, or WACC. The discount rate for potential future cash flows in discounted cash flow analysis is also WACC.

a. When calculating the WACC for capital budgeting, accounts payable is excluded

Accounts payable is the amount of money a business owes its suppliers, whereas accounts receivable is the amount of money owing to the business, generally by clients. When two businesses deal on credit, one of them records an entry to accounts payable while the other documents an entry to collections receivable in their books.

The calculation of WACC includes preferred stock, long-term debt, common stock "raised" through fresh issuance, and common stock "raised" through the reinvestment of earnings.
