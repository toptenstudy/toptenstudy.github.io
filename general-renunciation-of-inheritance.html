19. explain how sex-influenced traits and abnormalities are inherited and pass on to next generation​


Answer:

Sex-linked diseases are passed down through families through one of the X or Y chromosomes. X and Y are sex chromosomes.

Dominant inheritance occurs when an abnormal gene from one parent causes disease, even though the matching gene from the other parent is normal. The abnormal gene dominates.

But in recessive inheritance, both matching genes must be abnormal to cause disease. If only one gene in the pair is abnormal, the disease does not occur or it is mild. Someone who has one abnormal gene (but no symptoms) is called a carrier. Carriers can pass abnormal genes to their children.

The term "sex-linked recessive" most often refers to X-linked recessive.

25. what non-mendelian inheritance generally oocurs in the x-chromosomes of an ndividual​


Answer:

Sex-linked inheritance is a non-mendalian inheritance that generally occurs in the x-chromosomes of an individual.

Explanation:

Sex linked is a trait in which a gene is located on a sex chromosome. In humans, the term generally refers to traits that are influenced by genes on the X-chromosome. This is because the X chromosome is large and contains many more genes than the smaller Y chromosome. In a sex-linked disease, it is usually males who are affected because they have a single copy of X chromosome that carries the mutation. In females, the effect of the mutation may be masked by the second healthy copy of the X chromosome.
