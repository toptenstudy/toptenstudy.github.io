4. give me an example of storyboard graphic organizer.​


Here's an example of a storyboard graphic organizer:

Title: "A Day at the Beach"

Panel 1:

Visual: A family is packing their car with beach gear.

Caption: "Morning"

Dialogue: Mom says, "Everyone ready for a day of fun in the sun?"

Panel 2:

Visual: The family arrives at the beach and sets up their chairs and umbrellas.

Caption: "Late morning"

Dialogue: Dad says, "Let's get our swimsuits on and hit the waves!"

Panel 3:

Visual: The family is playing in the water and building sandcastles.

Caption: "Afternoon"

Dialogue: Child says, "Look at the cool seashells I found!"

Panel 4:

Visual: The family is having a picnic on the beach.

Caption: "Evening"

Dialogue: Mom says, "This was the perfect day. Let's do it again soon!"

By using a storyboard graphic organizer, you can visualize the sequence of events in a story and plan out each panel or scene before creating the final product, whether it be a comic, a video, or a movie.

19. Why is a storyboard considered as a springboard of the story’s plot? Why is it important to highlight significant scenes from the text in a storyboard?


Storyboard and its Importance

The visual representation of a film or video is called a storyboard. A storyboard can consist of drawings or picture that corresponds to a shot in the final video, with information about the direction of the camera, dialogue, and others. It is done to provide a sketch of a video.

We need a storyboard because of the following reasons: To share a vision, not all people are good at understanding ideas presented in words. A storyboard helps people have a clearer understanding of the video.To have easier production. Since the storyboard contains detailed information about the final video, there would be a concrete guide during the production of the video.To save time, a storyboard reduces the chance of having a revision while actually producing the video. Changes that are made during the storyboard creation do not cause much time and money.

Importance of Storyboard

To sum this all up, a storyboard provides cheap and time-savvy planning of a video. It plays an important role in storytelling on how the video will come up. With the storyboard, everyone can have a clearer understanding of the concept they are eyeing to achieve.

24. what are the components of a storyboard​


Answer:

A storyboard is a graphic representation of how your video will unfold, shot by shot.

It’s made up of a number of squares with illustrations or pictures representing each shot, with notes about what’s going on in the scene and what’s being said in the script during that shot. Think of it as sort of a comic book version of your script.                              

Here are the key elements that every storyboard should include:

Shot images: Individual panels featuring 2D drawings to show what's happening actions, characters throughout a video.

Shot number: The number indicating when a shot appears according to a video's shot list.

Action: The primary activity happening in a shot.

Event 1: Departure - A Filipino family is shown at the airport, bidding farewell to their loved ones as they prepare to leave the Philippines for work abroad. There are tears, but also a sense of hope for better opportunities.

Event 2: Arrival - The family arrives in a foreign country and is shown settling into their new home. They encounter language barriers and cultural differences, but they are determined to adapt and make a new life for themselves.

Event 3: Work Challenges - The family members are shown facing challenges at their respective jobs abroad. They work long hours, face discrimination, and struggle with homesickness. Despite these challenges, they persevere to support their families back in the Philippines.

Event 4: Community Support - The family finds solace in the Filipino community abroad. They attend gatherings, celebrate Filipino traditions, and support each other through the ups and downs of living in a foreign land. They form bonds with fellow Filipinos who become like family.

Event 5: Remittances - The family is shown sending remittances back home to support their families in the Philippines. They make sacrifices and send money to pay for education, healthcare, and other needs of their loved ones.

Event 6: Homesickness - The family experiences moments of homesickness, missing their loved ones, and the familiar surroundings of their home country. They reminisce about their life in the Philippines and yearn for the comfort of home.

Event 7: Achievements - Despite the challenges, the family members are shown achieving their goals abroad. They may have acquired new skills, gained promotions, or established successful businesses. Their hard work and determination pay off.

Event 8: Homecoming - The family is shown returning to the Philippines after a period of time abroad.
