7. is shrek a dynamic or static character?


Answer:

The answer to the question if Shrek is a dynamic or static character is that Shrek, a character made under Dreamworks, is a dynamic character.

In literature as well ads film, a dynamic character is defined as someone whose traits change during the story because of the conflicts, or adventures that he encounters. Usually a dynamic character faces different kinds of trials, but through all of these, he takes time to learn from his experiences, mistakes, as well as from the other characters in the story.

Shrek As a Dynamic Character

Shrek started out as a recluse ogre in the story who only minded his own business. Even when he saved Fiona from the dragon in Shrek 1, he did it because of self interest- he wanted people to get out of his swamp.

Over time the series of the Shrek movies, we see Shrek slowly transforming into a caring individual who puts the needs of his loved first. This was possible because of the different villains that he met (Prince Charming, Fairy Godmother etc.) as well as new friends and characters that he met during his journey.

In the end,  Shrek a recluse and selfish ogre,was now transformed into a loving husband to Fiona ,a loving father to their children, and a friend that can always be depended on.

14. Shrek is well-known animation series that has a lot of intertextuality and references to characters from different Disney films.Associate the shrek character with previous stories or Disney character.​


Answer:

Shrek, the main character of the Shrek film franchise, can be associated with several previous stories and Disney characters due to its intertextuality and references. Here are a few examples:

The character of Shrek himself can be seen as a parody of traditional fairy tale characters, particularly the ogre character in "Beauty and the Beast" and the titular character in "Rumpelstiltskin."

The character of Donkey, Shrek's loyal sidekick, can be seen as a reference to the talking animals found in many fairy tales and fables, including the donkey in "The Bremen Town Musicians" and the Cheshire Cat in "Alice's Adventures in Wonderland."

The princess character in the Shrek franchise, Fiona, can be seen as a subversion of the typical fairy tale princess archetype, particularly the character of Cinderella. Like Cinderella, Fiona is trapped in a tower and dreams of a different life, but she is also capable of taking care of herself and fighting back against her oppressors.

Overall, the Shrek franchise is filled with intertextual references to classic fairy tales and Disney films, which adds to its appeal and makes it a beloved animated series for audiences of all ages.
