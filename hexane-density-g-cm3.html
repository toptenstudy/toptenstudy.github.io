[tex]\large {\boxed {\bold {\rho ~ = ~ \frac {m} {V}}}}[/tex]

Answer:

To solve this problem, you can use the concept of density, which is the mass of an object per unit volume. Specifically, you can use the formula:

density = mass / volume

You know the density of the alloy and the volume of the alloy, and you want to find the mass of aluminum in the alloy. So, you can rearrange the formula to:

mass = density x volume

Therefore, you can use this formula to find the mass of aluminum in 30.0 cm3 of the alloy:

mass = 5.0 g/cm3 x 30.0 cm3 = 150 g

So, there is 150 grams of aluminum in 30.0 cm3 of the metal alloy.

28. Hexane is an organic substance used in the manufacture of gasoline. If a car contains 9.22 X 10*L of hexane, what will be the mass of the substance in grams? Note: the density of hexane is 0.6594 g/mL)


Answer:

To find the mass of 9.22 x 10^L of hexane, we need to first convert it into milliliters, then multiply it by the density of hexane (0.6594 g/mL) to find the mass in grams.

First, convert the volume from liters to milliliters: 9.22 x 10^L = 9.22 x 10^L x 1000 = 9,220 mL

Next, multiply the volume in milliliters by the density of hexane to find the mass:

mass = density x volume

mass = 0.6594 g/mL x 9,220 mL

mass = 6,062.948 g

So, 9.22 x 10^L of hexane has a mass of approximately 6,062.948 g.
