Pedro Poveda (born Pedro José Luis Francisco Javier Poveda Castroverde; December 3, 1874 – July 28, 1936) was a Spanish priest, humanitarian, educator and martyr. 9. How did St. Pedro Calungsod model his love for mission and love for neighbors?


St. Pedro Calungsod was a young Catholic missionary and martyr who lived in the 1600s.

He is remembered for his devotion to his faith and his commitment to spreading the Gospel, even in the face of persecution and danger.

St. Pedro Calungsod modeled his love for mission by dedicating himself to serving others, especially those in need.

He worked tirelessly to spread the message of the Catholic Church and to bring the sacraments to those who had never heard of them.

He was not afraid to face danger or to put his own life at risk, in order to fulfill his mission of serving others.

In addition to his love for mission, St. Pedro Calungsod also modeled love for his neighbors.

He was known for his kindness, compassion, and generosity, and he always put the needs of others before his own.

He was a true servant of God, who lived his life in accordance with the teachings of Jesus Christ, and who inspired many others to do the same.

20. what was the most important contribution of st. pedro Calungsod to the catholic church?​


Answer:

While in Guam, Calungsod preached Christianity to the Chamorro people through catechesis, while baptizing infants, children, and adults at the risk and expense of being persecuted and eventually murdered. Through Calungsod and San Vitores's missionary efforts, many native Chamorros converted to Roman Catholicism.

Calungsod was beatified on March 5, 2000, by Pope John Paul II, and canonized by Pope Benedict XVI at Saint Peter's Basilica in Vatican City on October 21, 2012.[5]
