6. how does the three arms of government interrelates?


Explanation:

Separation of powers refers to the division of a state's government into branches, each with separate, independent powers and responsibilities, so that the powers of one branch are not in conflict with those of the other branches. The typical division is into three branches: a legislature, an executive, and a judiciary, which is the trias politica model. It can be contrasted with the fusion of powers in parliamentary and semi-presidential systems, where the executive and legislative branches overlap.

The intention behind a system of separated powers is to prevent the concentration of power by providing for checks and balances. The separation of powers model is often imprecisely and metonymically used interchangeably with the trias politica principle. While the trias politica model is a common type of separation, there are governments that have more or fewer than three branches.

28. Identify the three major functional areas of business organizations and briefly describe how they interrelate


The following are the three major functional areas of a business:Management Production/Operations Marketing

One motivation for segmenting business processes into functional areas is to enable each to operate within its area of competence, increasing overall efficiency and effectiveness.

Management's significant responsibility in business is to supervise the performance of others. The majority of managerial activities can be classified as follows:

Planning: Managers plan by establishing long-term goals for the firm and the short-term strategies necessary to achieve those goals.Arranging: Managers are responsible for organizing an organization's operations in the most efficient manner possible, allowing the business to make the best use of its resources.Managing:  A considerable portion of a manager's time is spent monitoring the business's actions to ensure that it is on the path to meeting its objectives. When individuals or systems deviate from the course, managers are frequently the first to detect and correct.Leading: Managers serve as organizational leaders in both practical and symbolic ways. The manager may be responsible for leading work teams or groups by implementing a new process or developing a new product.

Operations are converting inputs (factors of production) to outputs (goods and services). Processes function similarly to the heart of a firm, pumping forth goods and services in sufficient numbers and of a high enough quality to suit clients' expectations. The operations manager is in charge of the business's day-to-day operations, which might range from ordering raw supplies to scheduling personnel to generate tangible things.

Marketing encompasses everything a business does to ascertain its consumers' needs and produce products and services to fulfill those demands. Additionally, marketing is responsible for advertising goods and services, establishing how goods and services will be delivered, and developing a price strategy to acquire market share while remaining competitive.
