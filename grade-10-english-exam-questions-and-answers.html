26. Suppose a students grade in his final examination in math is 86 out of 100, and the mean grade of the whole class is 75 with a standard deviation of 7 On the other hand, his grade in English final exam is 65 out of 80, and the mean grade of the whole class is 60 with a standard deviation of 5. In which exam did he do better? ​pa answer po plss!


Answer:

To determine which exam the student did better in, we can use the concept of standard scores, also known as z-scores.

The z-score for the math exam is:

z = (x - μ) / σ = (86 - 75) / 7 ≈ 1.57

The z-score for the English exam is:

z = (x - μ) / σ = (65 - 60) / 5 ≈ 1.00

A z-score measures the distance of an observation from the mean in units of standard deviation. A positive z-score indicates that the observation is above the mean, while a negative z-score indicates that the observation is below the mean.

Since the z-score for the math exam is higher than the z-score for the English exam, we can conclude that the student did better in math. This means that the student's score of 86 in the math exam is farther above the mean of the class than their score of 65 in the English exam.
