27. A cylindrical tank can can hold 44 cubic meters of water. If the raduis of the tank is 3.5 meters, how high is the tank?


Answer:

The height of the cylindrical tank is equal to 1.143 m.

Step-by-step explanation:

Step 1: Write the given

A cylindrical tank of volume 44 cubic metersA cylindrical tank of radis 3.5 meters

Step 2: Identify the formula/s to be utilized

The volume of cylinder is equal to (area of base)(height).Rearrange [tex]V_{cylinder}=  A_{base} *height[/tex] in terms of the height of cylindrical tank.[tex]height of cylinder=\frac{V_{cylinder} }{A_{base} }[/tex]The area of circle is equal to [tex]A_{base}= \pi  (radius)^{2}[/tex] or [tex]A_{base}=\frac{\pi }{4}(diameter)^{2}[/tex]

Step 3: Solve for the base area

[tex]A_{base}= \pi  (radius)^{2}[/tex][tex]A_{base}= \pi  (3.5 m)^{2}[/tex]The area of the base is equal to 49π/4 or 38.485 square meters.

Step 4: Find the height of the cylindrical tank

height of tank= 44 cubic meters/38.485 square metersThe height of tank is equal to 1.143 meters.

Therefore, the 44 cubic meters cylindrical tank with 3.5 m radius have a height of 1.143 meters.
