Answer:

Generation of Sugar

Sugarcane juice is used in making table sugar which is extracted from the stem of the sugarcane plant. When tracing the path of sugar molecules found in the stem from where they are produced, the right answer should be from the roots and leaf and then into the stem, so the correct answer is letter D.

Explanation:

When a sugarcane grows, its leaves start to absorb sunlight, which enables it to create its own food via photosynthesis. Then, the sucrose or the sugars are being made with the help of the water from the roots and other nutrients that the plant receives. All of the nutrients will combine to create sugar, which will stay in the plant’s stem.

About succulents and cacti

Basically, succulents are not the name of a family of plants such as cacti. However, succulent is a term for plants that have the character of absorbing and storing water in the main stem. Physically succulents have petals like leaves, Meanwhile, cacti replace leaf tissue in the form of thorns. So all cacti are succulent plants. But not all succulents are cacti.

Common information that we often hear about how to care for cacti and succulents is that we shouldn't water them too much. For me it's not only that, but there are several things that must be considered in caring for cactus and succulent plants such as light, growing media and containers that we use for planting.

Cacti and succulents are plants that require more light intensity than other types of plants. But that doesn't mean cacti and succulents can be placed in areas exposed to direct sunlight. They need time to adapt to new places and environments. It is best to place this plant in a bright area but not exposed to direct sunlight.

Choose the right planting medium and container. A mixture of husks, compost and sand/gravel is the perfect combination for this type of plant. In addition, choose a planting container / pot that has holes for drainage so that water does not stagnate. Watering the planting medium can be done once a week and spraying twice a week.
