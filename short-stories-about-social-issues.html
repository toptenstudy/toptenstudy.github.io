"Political Issue"

“The Finkelstein Five” in Friday Black by Nana Kwame Adjei Brenyah

An act of horrifying violence makes the national news, a white man beheading five black kids with a chain saw in “self-protection,” galvanizing a movement of people standing up to racism. The narrator of this story has every right to be furious and to want justice. But in a world determined to cast him aside, that wants him to keep quiet, that won’t allow true justice, he has to grapple with the meaning and consequences of retributive violence.

6) The answer is B, moral of the story. Formalist criticism is concerned with analyzing the form of a work of literature, such as the literary devices used and the elements of a short story, to better understand the meaning and impact of the text. The moral of the story, however, is not a formal aspect of the text, but rather a thematic one.

7) The answer is A, author's style of writing using literary devices. Marxist criticism focuses on the ways in which literature reflects and reinforces social and economic inequality, particularly in terms of social class. This approach analyzes the social classes portrayed in the story, the author's message about social issues, and the role of these issues in shaping the plot and themes of the text. The author's style of writing is not typically of interest to Marxist critics.

8) The answer is B, reader's individual experience while or after reading a text. Reader-response criticism is concerned with how readers interpret and respond to a text, and the ways in which their personal experiences, biases, and cultural backgrounds influence this interpretation. This approach is less concerned with a "correct" interpretation of the text or the author's intentions, and more focused on understanding how different readers might respond to the same text in different ways.

9) The answer is A, historical. Historical criticism is concerned with understanding a text within the context of its historical period, including the social, cultural, and political factors that may have influenced its creation and reception. In the case of Padre Damaso's character in Jose Rizal's "Noli Me Tangere," historical criticism could be used to examine the historical context of the novel's setting and the role of the Spanish colonial government in the Philippines at the time.

10) The answer is C, feminist. Feminist criticism is concerned with analyzing literature from a gendered perspective, particularly with regard to the ways in which women are represented and how gender roles and power dynamics are depicted in the text. This approach often seeks to uncover and challenge patriarchal assumptions and biases in literature, and to highlight the experiences and perspectives of women in the text.
