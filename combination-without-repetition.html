3. combining two or more set together without repetition of element is called what


Answer:

Union of Sets

Step-by-step explanation:

Combining two or more sets together without repetition of elements is called the union of sets. The union of sets is a fundamental operation in set theory, which is the branch of mathematics that deals with the study of sets and their properties.

In the union of sets, all the elements that are present in any of the given sets are combined into a single set, without duplication of any element. For example, if we have two sets A = {1, 2, 3} and B = {3, 4, 5}, then the union of A and B, denoted by A ∪ B, is the set {1, 2, 3, 4, 5}.

The union of sets is represented by the symbol "∪", and it is defined as follows:

A ∪ B = {x : x ∈ A or x ∈ B}

This means that the union of sets A and B contains all the elements x that belong to set A or to set B, or to both. The union of sets can also be extended to more than two sets by simply combining all the elements of the sets into a single set without duplication.

An arrangement of things in a definite order or the ordered arrangement of distinguishable objects without allowing repetitions among the objects a. Permutation b. Circular permutation c. Repetitions d. Combination​


1. A special case of permutation where the arrangement of things is in a circular pattern is: B. CIRCULAR PERMUTATION.

2. An arrangement of things in a definite order or the ordered arrangement of distinguishable objects without allowing repetitions among the objects is called A. PERMUTATION.

EXPLANATION:

1. CIRCULAR PERMUTATION

Cyclical permutations, also known as circular permutations or circular permutations, are permutations that are arranged according to a certain round. Cyclic permutations can be defined as Ps(n) = (n-1)!, where Ps(n) = cyclic permutations and n = elements (number of elements). Remember that permutations do not pay attention to the order of the objects, so we consider each of the strings to be identical to one another. Furthermore, cyclic permutations will be calculated by assuming one element as the start of a string.

2. PERMUTATION

Permutations are arrangements of part or all of a set in a certain order (the order of elements is concerned).

There are two kinds of permutations:

Repetition is allowed: an example is the pin lock on your device, the number could be 2-4-4-9.Replays are not allowed: for example, the first three racers cross the finish line, it is impossible for one racer to win 1st and 2nd place simultaneously.
