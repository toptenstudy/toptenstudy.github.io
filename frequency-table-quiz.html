27. 2. The following table shows the distribution of marks of some students who took part in Math quiz. Class Interval Frequency (f) Class Mark (x) 4 16 6 10 4 25-30 20-24 15-19 10-14 5-9 a. What is the mark that has the highest frequency? b. What if the frequency of the mark 15 - 19? c. What is the class interval of the data? d. What is the class mark 15-19? e. What is the total number of students? fx f. What is the product of the frequency and class mark of 15-19 (fx)? g. Find the mean of the data?​


Answer:

a. The mark that has the highest frequency is 20-24.

b. The frequency of the mark 15-19 is 10.

c. The class interval of the data is 5.

d. The class mark of 15-19 is (15+19)/2 = 17.

e. The total number of students is 50 (sum of all frequencies).

f. The product of the frequency and class mark of 15-19 is fx = 10*17 = 170.

g. To find the mean, we need to first calculate the midpoint (class mark) of each class interval, then multiply each midpoint by its corresponding frequency, sum up these products, and divide by the total frequency.

Midpoint of 5-9: (5+9)/2 = 7

Midpoint of 10-14: (10+14)/2 = 12

Midpoint of 15-19: (15+19)/2 = 17

Midpoint of 20-24: (20+24)/2 = 22

Midpoint of 25-30: (25+30)/2 = 27.5

Now we can calculate the mean:

mean = (47 + 1012 + 1617 + 2022 + 6*27.5) / 50

mean = 15.7

Therefore, the mean mark of the students is 15.7.
