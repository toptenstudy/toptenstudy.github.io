8. What are the principles of social justice? Is human rights a part of social justice?


Answer:

Access

To serve the people, a healthy society must offer services and resources. These include education, healthcare, shelter, and food. However, in many societies, there’s unequal access. Education is a prime example of the consequences. When only people from a certain class can afford good schools, those with lower-paying jobs have to settle for less. This leads to those kids growing up and being unable to access certain jobs. In an unjust society, somebody’s race and sexuality can also limit services and opportunities. When everyone gets equal access, it helps even out the playing field.

Access

To serve the people, a healthy society must offer services and resources. These include education, healthcare, shelter, and food. However, in many societies, there’s unequal access. Education is a prime example of the consequences. When only people from a certain class can afford good schools, those with lower-paying jobs have to settle for less. This leads to those kids growing up and being unable to access certain jobs. In an unjust society, somebody’s race and sexuality can also limit services and opportunities. When everyone gets equal access, it helps even out the playing field.

Participation

The fourth principle for social justice is participation. Society must allow everyone to voice their concerns and take part in making decisions. If something affects a person’s life, that person needs to be a part of the process. If there’s only a select group calling the shots with others silenced through discrimination, that’s social injustice. This is where access and equity come into play again. Within most societies, participation is reserved for only a few. To increase access, society must remove barriers to participation. For equity, historically-undermined groups should be encouraged to speak.

Human rights

Human rights and social justice are two sides of the same coin. They can’t exist apart from each other. For a society to be just, it must ensure the protection of everyone’s civil, political, economic, cultural, and social rights. These rights include the right to life, the right to free speech, the right to vote, the right to a fair trial, and so on. Governments must be held accountable when they violate these rights or fail to protect them. Human rights might be the most powerful principle for social justice because they’re recognized internationally and enshrined in many treaties.

Social Justice Issues

Social justice issues can occur in relation to practically any aspect of society where inequality can arise as a result of unjust prejudices or policies.

Social Justice Issues

Types of Social Justice Issues

Social justice issues can be delineated into two categories, which are interrelated and often co-dependent: Inter-Social Treatment and Unequal Government Regulation.

Inter-Social Treatment involves treatment of certain groups of people based on personally-held biases and prejudices. These prejudices most often manifest in relation to sociological categories such as:

-Race

-Gender

-Age

-Sexual Orientation

-Religion

-Nationality

-Education

-Mental or Physical Ability

Find out how Social Justice and Environmental Justice are intrinsically interconnected.

Unequal Government Regulation involves laws and regulations that purposefully or otherwise create conditions that obstruct, limit, or deny certain groups equitable access to the same opportunities and resources available to the rest of society. These laws can intentionally (explicitly) or unintentionally (implicitly) create the conditions for social injustice. Areas in which government policy often gives rise to social inequality and injustice include:

-Voting Laws (i.e. redistricting and voter ID)

-Policing Laws (i.e. search and seizure and drug scheduling)

-Environmental Laws (i.e. clean water and air, industrial waste disposal)

-Health Care Laws (i.e. insurance mandates and coverage eligibility)

-Education Laws (i.e. public school segregation and integration)

-Labor Laws (i.e. worker’s rights, occupational health and safety)

30. Values of giving; Respect Justice Social Justice Kindness Friendship Love Loyalty Define each and example each


Answer:

Values of Giving:

Respect - Respect is the act of showing consideration and honor towards others, regardless of their age, gender, race, or religion. An example of respect is listening attentively to someone who is speaking, even if you don't agree with their opinions.

Justice - Justice is the quality of being fair and impartial, ensuring that everyone is treated equally under the law. An example of justice is punishing someone who has committed a crime, regardless of their social status or connections.

Social Justice - Social justice is the concept of promoting fairness, equity, and equality for all members of society, particularly those who are marginalized or disadvantaged. An example of social justice is fighting for the rights of people who have been discriminated against because of their race, gender, or sexual orientation.

Kindness - Kindness is the act of showing compassion, empathy, and goodwill towards others. An example of kindness is helping a stranger carry their groceries, or comforting a friend who is going through a difficult time.

Friendship - Friendship is the relationship between people who share common interests, values, and experiences. An example of friendship is spending time with someone you enjoy being around, sharing your thoughts and feelings with them, and supporting each other through good times and bad.

Love - Love is the intense feeling of affection and care towards someone or something. An example of love is the love between parents and their children, or between romantic partners who share a deep emotional connection.

Loyalty - Loyalty is the act of remaining committed and faithful to someone or something, even in difficult times. An example of loyalty is standing by a friend or family member who has made a mistake, and supporting them as they try to make things right.
