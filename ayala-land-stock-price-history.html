6. Facts about the history making Game stop short and stock price surge that occured during January 2021​


Answer:

In January 2021, there was a significant surge in the stock price of GameStop, a video game retailer. This surge was largely driven by a group of retail investors on the subreddit forum r/wallstreetbets, who coordinated their efforts to buy GameStop stock in an attempt to squeeze out short sellers.

Short sellers are investors who bet that the price of a particular stock will decline. To execute a short sell, they borrow shares of the stock from another investor, sell the borrowed shares on the market, and then buy back the same number of shares at a lower price in the future to return to the original owner. If the price of the stock does decline, the short seller makes a profit. However, if the price of the stock increases, the short seller incurs a loss.

The retail investors on r/wallstreetbets coordinated their efforts to buy GameStop stock en masse, driving up the price and causing losses for the short sellers. This phenomenon, known as a "short squeeze," can occur when the price of a stock rises sharply, causing short sellers to buy back the stock to cover their positions and avoid further losses. This buying can in turn drive the price of the stock even higher.

The GameStop stock price surge received widespread media attention and became a topic of discussion in financial circles. The situation also prompted regulatory scrutiny and raised questions about the role of retail investors and social media in financial markets.

22. The price of a stock from Company Q changes every minute. There is an 8% chance that the stock price goes down by P10, and there is a 20% chance that the stock price goes down by P5. On the other hand, there is a 43% chance that the stock price will not change, a 25% chance that the stock price will increase by P5, and a 4% chance that the stock price will increase by P10. Find the expected value the probability distribution and interpret it


EXPECTED VALUE OF  THE PROBABILITY DISTRIBUTION

The expected value of the probability distribution is -0.15. It means that, on average. The stock price of Company Q should drop by P0.15 every minute. It's crucial to remember that this is only an average and may not accurately predict what will occur at any one moment. The stock price could yet rise or fall by a bigger or lesser margin, stay the same, or do neither.

What is the solution?To calculate the expected value of the probability distribution, we must multiply each potential change in stock price by the corresponding probability, then add all of the results together. Let's list the possible changes in the following order:

P(-10) for a decrease of P10

P(-5) for a decrease of P5

P(0) for no change

P(5) for an increase of P5

P(10) for an increase of P10

Then we can write the probability distribution as:

Stock price changes                     Probability

P(-10)                                                 0.08

P(-5)                                                   0.20

P(0)                                                     0.43

P(5)                                                     0.25

P(10)                                                    0.04

To find the expected value, we can use the formula:

Expected Value = P(-10) * (-10) + P(-5) * (-5) + P(0) * 0 + P(5) * 5 + P(10) * 10

Plugging in the probabilities from the table, we get:

Expected Value = 0.08 * (-10) + 0.20 * (-5) + 0.43 * 0 + 0.25 * 5 + 0.04 * 10

Expected Value = -0.8 - 1 + 1.25 + 0.4

Expected Value = -0.15

29. one away people can earn money from stocks is by A. selling the stock for lower price than the price they paid for the stock B.buying the stock for investment banker C. selling buying the stock for same price as they paid for the stock D.Selling the stock for the higher price than price they for paid for the stock ​


Answer:

D. Selling the stock for a higher price than the price they paid for it is one way people can earn money from stocks. This is commonly referred to as capital gains. When a person buys a stock and its price increases, they can sell the stock for a profit. For example, if someone buys a stock for $10 per share and the price increases to $20 per share, they can sell the stock for a profit of $10 per share.

Option A is not a way to earn money from stocks as it would result in a loss. Option B is not a way for an individual to earn money from stocks as investment bankers typically buy and sell stocks on behalf of institutional clients. Option C is also not a way to earn money from stocks, as it would result in no gain or loss.
