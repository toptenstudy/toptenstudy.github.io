10. discuss the main difference qualitative research Competitive State Anxiety and Self Confidence among the Student-Athletes of Tanzaoutcome result​


Answer:

The main difference between qualitative research and quantitative research is that qualitative research focuses on the subjective experience of a phenomenon and seeks to understand how people interpret, think about, and experience a particular situation, while quantitative research focuses on the measurable, objective aspects of a phenomenon. In the case of Competitive State Anxiety and Self Confidence among the Student-Athletes of Tanza, qualitative research would involve gathering data from interviews and focus groups to better understand how student-athletes perceive and experience their level of competitiveness and confidence, as well as how these qualities affect their performance in various sports. Quantitative research, on the other hand, would involve collecting data such as performance scores and self-ratings of confidence and anxiety to measure objectively the student-athletes’ experience. The outcome of the qualitative research would be a better understanding of the student-athletes’ lived experience with competitiveness and confidence, while the outcome of the quantitative research would be numerical data that can be used to compare the student-athletes’ performance, confidence, and anxiety levels.
