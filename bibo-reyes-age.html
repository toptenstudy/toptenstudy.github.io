13. mrs reyes is 55 years old and her daughter is 25 years old in how many years will be the mother's age be twice as old as her daughter?(with solution)​


Answer:

Let's call the number of years we're looking for "x". At the moment, the mother is 55 years old, and her daughter is 25 years old.

In x years, the mother's age will be 55 + x, and the daughter's age will be 25 + x.

According to the problem, the mother's age will be twice as old as her daughter's age in x years. We can write this as an equation:

55 + x = 2(25 + x)

Now we can solve for x:

55 + x = 50 + 2x

5 = x

Therefore, the answer is that in 5 years the mother's age will be twice as old as her daughter

17. How much should Mr. and Mrs. Reyes deposit if they want to have Php 800,000 that will be used for their son’s college education after graduating senior high school at age 17 years old. If Mr. and Mrs.Reyes will deposit the said amount in a bank that pays 1.2% interest compounded annually at the time when their son’s age is 5 years old?


In the given problem, apply your knowledge in algebraic expression specially in finance specifically in calculation of interest

Given:

Php 800,000, total amount

1.2% interest compounded annually

5 to 17 years old, time duration

Solution:

The formula for compound interest is A = P (1 + r/n)^(nt), where

A is the total money

P is the principal amount,

r is the interest rate in decimal

n is the number of times interest will be compounded

t is the time period.

Solve for t (time)

t = 17 - 5

t = 12 years

Solve for P (Principal Amount)

A = P (1 + r/n)^(nt)

800 000 = P(1 + 0.012/1)^(1)(12)

800 000 = P(1.1539)

P = 800 000/1.1539

P = 693 304.2093

Answer:

Php. 693 304.21, or approximately Php. 700 000

COMPOUND INTEREST

Using the formula for finding the present value P of a compound interest, P=F/{1+r)^t} wherein P is the present value, F is the future value, r is the rate and t is the term, we will get P693,304.21.

P is the present value, F is the future value which is Php800,000, r is the rate which is 1.2% or 0.012, t is the term. Since their sons age are 5 years old and the future age is 17, the term is 17 minus 5 which will give 12 years. Hence, t is 12. By substituting to the formula, P=800,000/{1+0.012)^12}. By computing, the present value P is P693,304.21. Therefore, Mr.  and Mrs. Reyes should deposit P693,304.21.
