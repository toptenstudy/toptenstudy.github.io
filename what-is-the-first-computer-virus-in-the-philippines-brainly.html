17. What is the first virus in the philippines


On May 4th, 2000, the first computer virus was found in the Philippines. Computers were infected and crashed as a result of the virus, sometimes known as "Love Bug" or "Melissa."

The virus quickly spread and did millions of dollars' worth of harm. The introduction of a new taxation structure by the Philippine government sparked the creation of the LOVE Bug virus as a form of protest. The virus was created to delete specified files from a victim's computer, effectively destroying it. Many companies and individuals were consequently obliged to pay for repairs or purchase new computers. The LOVE Bug virus, which is regarded as one of the most damaging computer viruses in history, caused considerable terror and disaster among Filipino users.

The virus spread through infected floppy disks. It would overwrite essential files on a victim’s hard drive, making it difficult or impossible to recover data. This particular virus was incredibly destructive because it targeted computers running Windows 95, which was a new operating system at the time. It causing significant damage to many businesses and individuals.

25. What is the first computer virus in phone?


The first virus computer on the phone is Cabir.

With the spread of smartphones, viral infections are also on the rise. Viruses on smartphones are just as dangerous as viruses and malware that infect your computer.

When is this virus first detected on a smartphone?

According to Kaspersky, the virus was first detected on smartphones in 2005. The virus was given the name "Cabir" and later became the starting point of the viral world, both by virus analysts like Kaspersky and by hackers whose numbers have multiplied to date.

This virus is also known as SymbOS/Cabir, Symbian/Cabir, and EPOC.cabir. This virus was first detected on the Symbian operating system running on Nokia smartphones at the time.

They chose the name Cabir for several reasons. One of the reasons is said to be the dedication to Kaspersky Lab employee Elena Kabirova, who suddenly appeared at Kaspersky Lab during the virus name debate.

One of the biggest Cabir incidents was confirmed in August 2005 at the World Championships in Athletics in Helsinki, Finland.

How to Avoid? 

To prevent this virus, you must disable Bluetooth or change the device mode to hidden.

30. The first computer virus in the philippines is?


The ILOVEYOU virus or love bug is the first computer virus in the Philippines that is sent as an attachment in an email with the word "ILOVEYOU" in the subject line. When opened, the attachment causes the message to be sent again to everyone in the recipient's Microsoft Outlook contact book. More gravely, all JPEG, MP3, and specific other files on the hard drives of all receivers are lost.

The ILOVEYOU virus can spread quickly within a company because Microsoft Outlook is frequently set up as the default email management tool on business networks around the world. In actuality, on May 4, 2000, this is exactly what took place. ILOVEYOU reportedly gained 45 million users in just around 10 days and cost the economy $10 billion in losses.

Although widely referred to as a computer virus, ILOVEYOU is actually a worm. It goes by the names "love letter virus" and "love bug worm." A worm is a sort of malware that can duplicate itself and spread from system to system without human interaction or intervention, in contrast to a virus, which is malicious code that replicates after human intervention. It doesn't even have to connect to any software. Email, and more specifically a malicious email attachment, is how ILOVEYOU operates. The moment the victim opens the attachment, the worm is silently downloaded into their system and begins to propagate over the network.

When a recipient opened their email, the virus sent copies of itself to everyone in their address book. The email had the subject line "ILOVEYOU" and the straightforward message "kindly check the attached LOVELETTER coming from me." These recipients clicked on the email out of curiosity, thinking it was a sincere love letter or an amusing joke, and unintentionally assisted in its propagation.
