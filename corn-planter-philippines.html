30. Situation:Rice fields and corn fields in the Philippine provinces are dried up decreasing the supply of rice and cornEnvironmental Issue:Reason/Explain:​


Answer:

The environmental issue in this situation is likely to be a lack of water for irrigation. Rice and corn are both water-intensive crops that require consistent irrigation in order to thrive. If the rice fields and corn fields in the Philippine provinces are dried up, it may be because there is not enough water available for irrigation.

There are many possible reasons for this lack of water. One possibility is a change in the local climate, such as a decrease in rainfall or an increase in temperature, which could lead to drought conditions. Another possibility is over-extraction of water from local aquifers or rivers, which could reduce the amount of water available for irrigation. Deforestation, soil erosion and poor land management practices may also contribute to this problem.

The decrease in the supply of rice and corn as a result of this environmental issue not only affects the farmers but also the entire population who rely on rice and corn as their staple food. It could also lead to an increase in food prices and potentially food insecurity. It is important for government and local communities to address the underlying environmental issue in order to ensure a sustainable and stable food supply.
