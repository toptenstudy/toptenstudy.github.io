20. What technique in family therapy cast a new light on a problem and provides a different interpretation of a problematic situation


One technique in family therapy that can help to cast a new light on a problem and provide a different interpretation of a problematic situation is reframing. Reframing involves helping the family to look at a problem or situation from a different perspective, often by reframing the way they understand or interpret the problem. This can involve reinterpreting the problem as an opportunity for growth or change, or by considering the problem in the context of larger systemic issues. By looking at the problem in a new way, the family may be able to find new solutions or approaches to addressing the issue.

29. Which technique would most likely be used by forensic scientists? A gene cloning B gene therapy C DNA fingerprinting D karyotyping


Which technique would most likely be used by forensic scientists? A gene cloning B gene therapy C DNA fingerprinting D karyotyping

The answer is letter C. DNA finger printing.  

Here are the explanation.

DNA profiling is a forensic method used for  the detection of people by their DNA characteristics.

In forensics, DNA profiles are very useful because only a small sample of  human material left behind after a crime can be enough to identify someone.

DNA is present in almost every cell in our bodies and  wherever we go, we leave cells behind without even knowing it.  Skin flakes, blood drops, hair and saliva all  contain DNA which can be used to identify us.  In reality, forensics analysis, widely used by police departments and prosecutors worldwide, mostly relies  on these tiny bits of shed DNA to associate suspects with the crimes they commit.
