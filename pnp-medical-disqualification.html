1. two types of disqualification in volleyballtwo types of disqualification in volleyball ​


Answer:

In volleyball, there are two main types of disqualification:

1. Player Disqualification

Committing a flagrant foul, such as striking an opponent, intentionally tripping or pushing an opponent, or engaging in violent behavior.

Receiving multiple yellow or red cards for unsportsmanlike conduct, such as arguing with the referee, using offensive language, or showing disrespect towards opponents, officials, or spectators.

Engaging in illegal actions, such as intentionally touching the net during play, interfering with an opponent's attempt to play the ball, or making illegal substitutions.

2. Team Disqualification:

Engaging in repeated or severe misconduct, such as physical or verbal abuse towards opponents, officials, or spectators, or intentionally damaging the playing environment.

Violating eligibility rules, such as fielding ineligible players, using illegal equipment, or failing to comply with team roster requirements.

27. give 10 disqualification reasons on a competitive Freestyle strokes​? in swimming​


1.False Start-At the start of a race, swimmers must take a stationary position atop the starting blocks.

2.Delays- Any swimmer who intentionally delays the start of a race or who willfully ignores or disobeys the orders of officials may also receive a disqualification.

3.Swimming Conduct- Failing to touch the wall when executing a turn, grabbing the lane markers, using the lane markers for momentum or pushing off the bottom of the pool will all result in a disqualification.

4.Backstroke- When competing in a backstroke race, a swimmer must remain on his or her back and employ a proper double-armed backstroke or an elementary backstroke.

5.Breaststroke-Most breaststroke disqualifications occur on turns as well. Swimmers must touch the wall with both hands simultaneously.

6.Butterfly-As in the breaststroke, butterfly swimmers must also touch the wall on turns with both hands simultaneously to avoid disqualification.

7.Relays- In relay races, swimmers typically draw disqualifications for false starting, which occurs when one swimmer leaves the starting block before his or her teammate touches the wall.
