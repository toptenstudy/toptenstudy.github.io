[tex]\large{\mathcal{SOLUTION:}}[/tex]

1. Given: B = 400 , P = 80 , r = ?

P = B × R80 = 400 × R80/400 = R0.20 = R, 0.20 × 100 = R20% = R

2. Given: P = 15 , R = ? , B = 600

P = B × R15 = 600 × R15/600 = R0.025 = R0.025 × 100 = R2.5% = R

3. Given: R = ? , B = 250 , P = 25

P = B × R25 = 250 × R25/250 = R0.10 = R0.10 × 100 = R10% = R

4. Given: R = ? , B = 1000 , P = 750

P = B × R750 = 1000 × R750/1000 = R0.75 = R0.75 × 100 = R75% = R

5. Given: R = ? , B = 48 , P = 12

P = B × R12 = 48 × R12/48 = R0.25 = R0.25 × 100 = R25% = R

How many gram of glucose is needed to prepare 1000 mL of 12 % glucose solution?

a. 95 grams b. 105 grams c. 113 grams d. 120 grams

Solution:

1000 x 12% = 1000 x 0.12 = 120 grams

Therefore, the correct answer is letter D.
