2. summary of the movie bad genius


Lynn is an intelligent student living with her father. She was accepted into a prestigious school and has earned a scholarship for her exceptional academic achievements. There, she was befriended by the academically challenged, Grace. Lynn began helping Grace cheat in exams. She is then approached by Grace's rich boyfriend, Pat, who offered money in exchange for helping him and his other friends. Although at doubt at first, Lynn finally agreed. She made use of hand signals based on piano pieces and uses them to send answers during exams. Many became interested in Lynn's little business. However, her cheating was revealed by Bank, another smart student. She was rebuked by her father and the school, which resulted to suspension of her scholarship, as well as her chance to apply for an international scholarship at the university level.

Lynn returned to the business when Pat and Grace asked her to help them cheat in the STIC, in which will earn them millions of baht. However, Lynn told them that they could only do it with Bank's help, but the problem is Bank is honest and upright. Bank also comes from a poor family so when Lynn approaches him and offered, Bank finally agreed.

The four of them made preparations for the exam. Lynn and Bank flew to Australia in order to get the exam earlier than the rest. The two of them answered the exams and sent to Pat and Grace and distribute to the clients. However, on the ever of their flight, Bank got furious knowing that it was Pat who ordered the thugs to beat him up. Lynn was shocked and began rethinking about her actions. But since Bank really needed the money, he returns to Lynn and told her to finish what she started.

In Sydney, Lynn and Bank completed the first sections of the test according to plan, but Bank is overcome by anxiety and is caught. Lynn struggles to memorize the final section herself, but finally pulls through. Lynn left the centre early.

When they came back home, Lynn found out that their scheme was a success, but, broken by the experience. She came to visit Bank who has invested his share in improving his mother's laundry business.

Bank offered Lynn a new business with greater number of clients but she turned him down. In the end, Lynn comes clean, confessing to her father, who comforted her and helped her redeem herself by submitting a formal confession to the STIC organisation.

14. What are the Literary Criticism Theories in the movie "Bad Genius"?


Literary criticism (or literary studies) is the study, evaluation, and interpretation of literature. Modern literary criticism is often influenced by literary theory, which is the philosophical discussion of literature's goals and methods.

Bad Genius, known in Thai as Chalard Games Goeng is a 2017 Thai heist thriller film produced by Jor Kwang Films and released by GDH 559. It was directed by Nattawut Poonpiriya, and stars Chutimon Chuengcharoensukying in her acting debut as Lynn, a straight-A student who devises an exams-cheating scheme which eventually rises to international levels.

Here are the literary criticism of critics of the said film:

- Critics praised the film's concept and design, which tackled a familiar, mundane subject and turned it into an exciting caper thriller—something never before seen in Thai cinema. Kong Rithdee observed, "An academic test is the most boring activity on earth. The film's conceit is to turn it into a gladiatorial ring, a place of risk, wit and sublime deception ... and against the odds it works."  While Nation TV's Natthapong Okapanom said, "Bad Genius is a work of craft that will help raise Thai cinema to another level of diversity."

- Praise was also given to the editing and the script—according to A Daymagazine's Phanuphan Veeravaphusit: "Due credit must be given to ... the photography, with its unfamiliar-yet-meaningful angles, and shots taken to second-by-second detail, which, combined with the fast-paced editing, add to the suspense, stirring the viewers' emotions throughout the entire story." Many noted the film's subtle critique of Thai society's inequality issues and problems with its education system—although some found fault with the somewhat moralizing ending. Most agreed, however, that despite any flaws, the film succeeded in providing excellent entertainment value.

- According to Rappler's movie critic, Francis Cruz, Bad Genius isn't a film that grants what really is child's play a sardonic grown-up treatment. While Poonpiriya stylizes the act of answering multiple choice questions to rightfully add more than just a whiff of tension to what essentially are really dull proceedings, the film doesn't stop at simply inflecting itself with gratuitous genre trappings all for the sake of surface-level novelty.  It is an authentic crowd-pleaser that doesn't allow itself to be complacent with its ingenuities. Its confident subversions of the genre are all noteworthy, all grounded on depth rather than shallow artifice.

22. Bad Genius reflection please ​


Answer:

Bad Genius is a 2017 Thai film that tells the story of a gifted high school student named Lynn who uses her intelligence to cheat her way through exams and help her classmates. The film explores themes of greed, ambition, and the pressure to succeed. It also raises questions about the ethics of cheating and the value of true education.

One of the main reflections that can be drawn from the film is the pressure that students face to succeed academically. Throughout the film, Lynn feels compelled to cheat in order to get good grades and gain acceptance from her peers. She believes that this is the only way to succeed in a society that values academic achievement above all else. This reflects the reality of the high pressure and competition that many students face in the pursuit of education and success.

Another reflection that can be drawn from the film is the concept of greed and ambition. As Lynn becomes more involved in cheating, her greed and ambition for money and power become increasingly clear. She becomes willing to sacrifice her integrity and relationships for the sake of her own gain. This raises questions about the true cost of ambition and the importance of maintaining one's values and principles in the face of temptation.

Overall, Bad Genius is a thought-provoking film that highlights the pressure and challenges that students face in their educational pursuits, and the impact of ambition and greed on an individual's choices and actions
