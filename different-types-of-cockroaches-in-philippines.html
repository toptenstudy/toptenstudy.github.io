1. The cockroaches are carriers of different diseases​


Answer:

Yes, cockroaches are known to be carriers of different diseases. They can pick up various types of bacteria, viruses, and other pathogens as they scavenge for food in unsanitary places like garbage cans, sewage systems, and drains.

When cockroaches come into contact with food, dishes, or surfaces in our homes, they can transfer these pathogens to humans. Some of the diseases that cockroaches can transmit include salmonellosis, typhoid fever, cholera, gastroenteritis, and dysentery.

Additionally, cockroach allergens can cause asthma and other respiratory problems in some people, particularly in children. Therefore, it is important to practice good sanitation, eliminate any sources of food and water that may attract cockroaches, and use pest control measures to prevent and control infestations in homes and other buildings

5. How is the nymph different from an adult cockroach?​


Answer:

Nymphs resemble adults in appearance and behavior, although they are smaller in size and do not have wings. Nymphs undergo a series of molts before becoming fully mature adults. After the final molt, nymphs of some cockroaches are equipped with wings.

12. distinguishing characteristics of cockroach​


Answer:

Cockroaches are generalized insects with few special adaptations, and may be among the most primitive living Neopteran insects. They have a relatively small head and a broad, flattened body, and most species are reddish-brown to dark brown. They have large compound eyes, two ocelli, and long, flexible antennae.
