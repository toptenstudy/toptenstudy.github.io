11. How does entrepreneurship stimulate the economy?​


by bringing new products, methods, and production processes to the market and by boosting productivity and competition more broadly.

Explanation:

Entrepreneurship plays a crucial role in stimulating the economy by creating jobs, driving innovation, and promoting competition. Here are some ways in which entrepreneurship can stimulate the economy:

Job creation: Entrepreneurs create jobs by starting new businesses, expanding existing ones, and developing new products and services. By hiring employees, entrepreneurs increase the level of employment in the economy, which in turn can increase consumer spending and boost economic growth.

Innovation: Entrepreneurs are often at the forefront of innovation, developing new products, services, and technologies that can create entirely new markets. These innovations can lead to increased productivity, improved efficiency, and lower costs, all of which can boost economic growth.

Competition: Entrepreneurship promotes competition by introducing new players into existing markets. This can lead to lower prices, increased quality, and more choices for consumers. In turn, this can stimulate demand and create new opportunities for growth.

Tax revenue: As entrepreneurs create new businesses and generate economic activity, they also contribute to tax revenues. This revenue can be used by governments to fund public services and infrastructure, which can further stimulate economic growth.

International trade: Entrepreneurs who create successful businesses can also contribute to international trade, exporting goods and services to other countries. This can help to increase exports and reduce the trade deficit, which can further stimulate economic growth.

Overall, entrepreneurship plays a vital role in stimulating economic growth and creating prosperity. By promoting innovation, competition, and job creation, entrepreneurs can help to create a dynamic and thriving economy.

29. describe the state of entrepreneurship in the philippines


Answer:

MANILA, The Philippines — Prior to the outbreak of COVID-19, the Philippines was on track for both economic growth and a reduction in poverty levels. In the past five years, Filipino growth averaged around 6%, and it was expected to reach 7% this year. This would surpass the growth rates of China, India and Indonesia. Now, with COVID-19, the Philippines’ growth rate is expected to be around 3%. Poverty rates have declined with the growth in the economy. In 2012, 10.2% of the population lived below the $1.90 poverty line. In 2019, only 2.7% lived below the $1.90 poverty line. But, the Philippines’ poor have suffered during the pandemic. Furthermore, slowed economic growth could push more into poverty. The Filipino government has long promoted entrepreneurship as being a major vehicle for lifting people out of poverty.
