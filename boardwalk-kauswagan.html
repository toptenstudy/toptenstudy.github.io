4. In consecutive turns of a monopoly game, stacy first paid 800 for a hotel . Since then , she lost half of her money when she landed on boardwalk. Next she collected 200 for passing go. She the lost half of her remaining money when she landed on illinois avenue. Stacy now has 2500. How much did she have just before she purchased the hotel?


Let's represent her original money as x.

stacy paid 800: x - 800

lost half her money: (x - 800)/2

collected 200: [tex]\frac{x-800}{2} + 200[/tex]

lost half her money (again): [tex]\frac{\frac{x-800}{2} + 200}{2}[/tex]

After her turns, she has 2,500.

[tex]\frac{\frac{x-800}{2} + 200}{2}[/tex] = 2500

[tex]\frac{x-800}{2} + 200[/tex] = 5000

(x - 800)/2 = 4800

x - 800 = 9600

x = 10400

Stacy originally had 10,400.

12. At the beach, 24% of people are on the boardwalk and 23% are on the sand. If the rest are in the water, what percentage of people are in the water?


Answer: 53% are in the water


Step-by-step explanation:


24%+23%= 47%


100%-47%= 53%


Answer:

53% of people are in the water.

Computation:

Add the percent of people on the boardwalk and on the sand. Then, subtract the sum from 100 percent to determine the percent of people in the water.

[tex]people_{on\:boardwalk}+people_{on\:sand}=24\%+23\%=47\%[/tex]

[tex]people_{in\:water}=100\%-47\%=\boxed{53\%}[/tex]
