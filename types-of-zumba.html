3. What are the four types of latin music in zumba


Answer:

What are the four types of latin music in zumba?

The four types of Latin music in zumba are salsa, reggaeton, merengue and cumbi.

Explanation:

Zumba is an exercise fitness program. The word "Zumba" is not really the original name for this program. It was expressly created as a brand name. The original name was "Rumbacize". It's a combination of "rumba", a Spanish word means party and various dances and "Jazzercise", which combines dance, strength, and resistance training with popular music for a full-body workout. Zumba classes are typically about an hour long and are taught by instructors licensed by Zumba Fitness, LLC known as Zin.

Four Types of Latin Music

Salsa

It is a Latin music associated of Cuban dances that were popular in the ballrooms and nightclubs.

Reggaeton

The word reggaeton formed from the word reggae. Its lyrical structure resembles that of hip hop. Although most reggaeton artists recite their lyrics rapping than singing.

Merengue

Merengues are fast arrangements with a 2/4 beat. It is generally composed in two parts. The first part is rhythmically straightforward and is used to introduce the song's melodic and lyrical material. The second part is dominated by improvisation, more complex rhythms, and hard-driving mambo.

Cumbia

The word was derived from the word cumbe which means dance. Cumbia was born of a cultural mix of black and indigenous backgrounds.
