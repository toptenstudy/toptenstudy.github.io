4. "unload the past, reload the future can pile so full"what is the metaphor?pa help po please need ko na po now​


A metaphor is a figure of speech device that compares two unrelated things.

     Metaphor is a figure of speech comparable simile a figure of speech where  a term or phrase actually expressing one kind of thing or idea is used in lieu of another to convey a likeness or analogy between them (as in drowning in money).

"unload the past, reload the future can pile so full"

This line uses the metaphor "unload and reload" because it compares two unconnected ideas.

    The literary device of metaphor aids readers in comprehending, paying attention to, remembering, and acting upon messages. Lips are volcanoes, for example, is a metaphor. A metaphor is a figure of speech in which a word or phrase is applied to an item or an activity to which it is not literally applicable.

Another example of metaphor

" Love is a fruit in season at all times and in reach of every hand.” — Mother Teresa

Despite the fact that love is not a fruit, the comparison's message is clear.

“She was the black sheep of the family.”
