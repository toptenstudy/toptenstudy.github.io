How were these telescope inventions and discoveries supported by government

Governments have played a crucial role in supporting the development and use of telescopes and their associated discoveries. In many cases, they provided financial support for the construction of telescopes, funding for research programs, and subsidies for the development of new technologies.

For example, in the 17th century, the Dutch government provided funding for the construction of the first large refracting telescope, which was used to make important astronomical discoveries. In the 19th and 20th centuries, many governments established national observatories and funded research programs to study the heavens.

Governments have also played a role in supporting space missions and space-based telescopes, which have made significant contributions to our understanding of the universe. These missions have been expensive, and governments have provided the financial and technological resources needed to carry them out.

Overall, government support has been crucial in advancing our understanding of the universe through telescopes and their associated discoveries. It has enabled astronomers and scientists to build larger and more powerful telescopes, launch space missions, and conduct research programs that have expanded our knowledge of the universe and our place in it.
