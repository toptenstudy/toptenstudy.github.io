Classroom management is all efforts aimed at creating an effective and fun teaching and learning atmosphere that can motivate students to study well according to their abilities. It can be said that classroom management is a conscious effort to organize the teaching and learning process systematically. This conscious effort leads to the preparation of teaching materials, the preparation of teaching aids/ infrastructure/ facility, the arrangement of study rooms, the creation of situations/conditions for the teaching and learning process and the timing so that learning goes well and curricular goals can be achieved.

An effectively managed classroom is one that runs smoothly, with minimal confusion and delay, and maximizes student learning. It is impossible for a teacher to organize learning, or for students to work productively, if they do not have guidelines for how they should behave, when and how to move around the room, where to sit, when they can and cannot interrupt the teacher, and the amount acceptable noise.

22. what can a teacher do to best establish classroom routine?


Answer:

It would be easy if all we had to do was tell our students what all of our classroom procedures are on the first day of school. In a perfect world, they would remember the procedures and follow them without fail until the very last day of school. Dream on! They are kids. They will forget.

Make learning the procedures a concrete, hands-on activity throughout the first weeks of school. Begin with the most important procedures: entering the classroom, opening the class, transitions, and dismissal. Then you may add other procedures later, such as putting the heading on papers, turning in homework, sharpening pencils, etc.

In his book The First Days of School: How to Be an Effective Teacher, Dr. Harry K. Wong suggests a three-step process for teaching classroom procedures to students:

Explain classroom procedures clearly.

Rehearse classroom procedures until they become routines.

Reinforce a correct procedure and reteach an incorrect one.
