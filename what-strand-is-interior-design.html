Answer:

T T A C G G T C A C C A

Explanation:

In the DNA base pairing between the two strands of DNA,

   *A (adenine) is pair with T (Thymine) vice versa

   *G (Guanine) is pair with C (Cytosine) vice versa

  A    A     T    G    C    C    A   G   T   G    G    T

  ↓     ↓     ↓    ↓    ↓     ↓    ↓    ↓   ↓   ↓     ↓     ↓

  T     T     A    C   G     G   T    C   A   C    C    A

Here are the list of nitrogenous bases of DNA

   1.Adenine

   2.Thymine

  3. Guanine

   4.Cytosine

These sequence of nitrogenous bases is very important in making or synthesizing a protein needed for the body. Segment of DNA which codes for protein or genetic materials is known gene. The gene sequence is necessary in the body in maintaining its genetic instruction. Problem with miss pairing of the nitrogenous bases would result to some gene mutation which is accompanied by medical or genetic disorders.

11. what makes an RNA strand different from a DNA strand​


Answer:

It differs from DNA chemically in two respects: (1) the nucleotides in RNA are ribonucleotides—that is, they contain the sugar ribose (hence the name ribonucleic acid) rather than deoxyribose; (2) although, like DNA, RNA contains the bases adenine (A), guanine (G), and cytosine (C), it contains the base uracil (U) .

18. What is the DNA complementary strand of the given parental strand ​


Answer:

Each newly synthesized strand of DNA (daughter strand) is made by the addition of a nucleotide that is complementary to the parent strand of DNA. In this way, DNA replication is semi-conservative, meaning that one parent strand is always passed on to the daughter helix of DNA.
