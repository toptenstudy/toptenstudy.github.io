4. Calculate the electromotive force of: Mg(s) + Sn^2+(aq) --> Mg^2+(aq) + Sn(s) [Mg^2+] = 0.045 M; [Sn^2+] = 0.035M​


We'll use the Nernst equation:

[tex]E_{cell}=E^0_{cell}- \frac{0.0591}{n}\cdot log \ {Q}[/tex]

Let's reduce it to half-reaction:

[tex]Mg_{(s)} \rightarrow Mg^{2+}_{(aq)} +2e^- \\Sn^{2+}_{(aq)} + 2e^- \rightarrow Sn_{(s)}[/tex]

[tex]E^0_{cell}=E^0_{cathode}-E^0_{anode}\\E^0_{cell}=0.136V-(-2.372V)=2.508V[/tex]

For solid substances the concentration of it will be equal to 1.

[tex]Q= \frac{[Mg^{2+}_{(aq)}]}{[Sn^{2+}_{(aq)}]} \\Q=\frac{0.045 \ M}{0.035 \ M} = 1.2857[/tex]

Substituting in the Nernst equation

[tex]E_{cell}=2.508 \ V - \frac{0.0591}{2} \ log \ 1.2857 \\E_{cell}=2.5048 \ V[/tex]

8. Private accounting, as a sub-discipline of accounting, includes: Select one: a. Tax consultancy b. Managerial advisory services c. External auditing d. Management Accounting


Answer:

D. MANAGEMENT ACCOUNTING

Explanation:

MANAGEMENT ACCOUNTING

        Management Accounting or Cost Accounting is a branch of accounting that is only concern in internal decision-making of the company or client. Accountants in this field do not have connections with the investors or lenders. In this branch, accountants are responsible for identifying, counting, analyzing, and interpreting of the accounts that would help the company or client's management of operations of their business.

       The accountants must use different tools in order to analyze and interpret the data they gathered in the account's information.

Methods of Management Accounting:Margin Analysis - this method is concern in the increase of the production.Capital Budgeting - in this method, accountants must consider the capital expenditures to make new capital budgeting decisions by calculating the NPV or Net Present Value and the IRR or Internal Rate of Return.

24. 1. Melaica invested P50,000 cash and P20,000 equipment 2. Bought P15,000 equipment from Chelsey company on credit 3. Consultancy services rendered on account P12,000 4. Paid Chelsey Company P5,000 as partial payment of account. 5. Consultancy services rendered for Cash​


Step-by-step explanation:

The given information can be classified into the following accounts:

Cash

Equipment

Accounts Receivable

Accounts Payable

Service Revenue

Using the information given, we can create a summary of the transactions:

Melaica invested P50,000 cash and P20,000 equipment:

Debit: Cash - P50,000

Debit: Equipment - P20,000

Credit: None

Bought P15,000 equipment from Chelsey company on credit:

Debit: Equipment - P15,000

Credit: Accounts Payable - P15,000

Consultancy services rendered on account P12,000:

Debit: Accounts Receivable - P12,000

Credit: Service Revenue - P12,000

Paid Chelsey Company P5,000 as partial payment of account:

Debit: Accounts Payable - P5,000

Credit: Cash - P5,000

Consultancy services rendered for Cash:

Debit: Cash - P12,000

Credit: Service Revenue - P12,000

Therefore, the accounting entries for the transactions are:

Debit: Cash - P45,000

Debit: Equipment - P35,000

Debit: Accounts Receivable - P12,000

Credit: Accounts Payable - P10,000

Credit: Service Revenue - P24,000

These transactions show that Melaica invested P50,000 in cash and P20,000 in equipment, bought P15,000 equipment on credit, rendered consultancy services for P12,000 on account, paid P5,000 as a partial payment of the account, and rendered consultancy services for P12,000 in cash. At the end of these transactions, Melaica has P45,000 in cash, P35,000 in equipment, P12,000 in accounts receivable, and P10,000 in accounts payable, with a total service revenue of P24,000.
