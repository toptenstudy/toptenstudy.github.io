5. Which of the following is an objective of a review engagement? a. Expressing a positive opinion that the financial information is presented in conformity withgenerally accepted accounting principles. b. Expressing a limited assurance to users who have agreed as to procedures that will beperformed by the CPA. c. Reporting whether material modifications should be made to such financial statements tomake them conform with generally accepted accounting principles. d. Reporting that the financial statements, in all materials respects, fairly present thefinancial position and operating results of the client.


An objective of a review engagement is reporting whether material modifications should be made to such financial statements to make them conform with generally accepted accounting principles.

Limited assurance or negative engagements are other names for review engagements. After an accountant has completed an auditing of a company's financial statements, auditors undertake a review engagement. As a result, the auditor offers only a limited level of assurance regarding the correctness of the financial statements. The auditor conducts investigational and analytical review procedures throughout the engagement to give the moderate degree of assurance necessary to produce a negative assurance report.

The auditor details any findings that lead them to feel that one party's claim does not give a true and fair view in a negative assurance report. If the auditor learned anything during the engagement that led them to conclude that the financial statements do not give a true and fair perspective of the company or that they do not adhere to the relevant accounting standards, they are required to disclose it. Since a review engagement gives the target user less certainty than an audit engagement, they are very different from one another.

17. Which of the following is a mechanical aspect of accounting? Select one: a. Designing the accounting system b. Reviewing the effectiveness of the internal control procedures c. Analyzing the transactions preparatory to the recording process d. Preparation of the financial budget


The mechanical aspect of accounting is analyzing the transactions prior to the recording process.

Accounting Recording System is a system created with the aim of helping business actors or companies to manage various accounting processes. In addition to recording, this system is also able to perform other processes such as calculations and making financial reports more quickly and accurately.

The accounting process includes many things related to corporate finance such as collecting, storing, managing, processing, retrieving, and reporting data. To be able to carry out all these processes, of course, it takes time and human resources that are not small. This of course becomes less efficient, because in addition to requiring a lot of time and money, this process is also prone to data errors caused by human error.

Purpose of Accounting Recording System

The Accounting Recording System was created with the main aim of assisting companies in carrying out the accounting recording process more quickly and accurately. In addition, the accounting record system also has various other purposes, including:

1. Minimize operational costs

The process of managing company finances manually requires significant operational costs. The company needs accountants in each of its divisions as well as additional costs for purchasing and depreciation of various equipment. The existence of an Accounting Recording System can reduce these various expenses.

2. Improve accounting management and internal control

Financial statements are one of the company's assets that need to be maintained as evidence of accountability for the financial flows of a company. The accounting record system also aims to improve the effectiveness and security of the company's financial statements.

3. Updating information from existing systems

Over time, financial accounting methods often become less relevant because some aspects such as quality, appearance, accuracy, and other elements are less accurate and outdated. Therefore, companies must always compile and present financial reports that are more relevant and according to company needs. The existence of an accounting record system is useful so that financial management remains relevant and accurate in accordance with the times.

4. Provide information to manage new business activities

A company or a business, especially a new one, of course requires development in their accounting system. It aims to help the company's operations run more smoothly.

26. Which of the following is a mechanical aspect of accounting? Select one: a. Reviewing the effectiveness of the internal control procedures b. Designing the accounting system c. Preparation of a financial budget d. Analyzing the transactions preparatory to the recording process.


D. Analyze preparatory transactions for the recording process

Further explanation:

Accounting mechanical cycle

The Accounting Cycle is specifically defined as an iterative process to identify, analyze, and record every accounting activity in a company. This cycle of accounting activities occurs within one year.

During this period, all principles, rules, methods, and techniques in accounting were used to record all company accounting activities. Generally, this cycle starts at the beginning of the year with the opening

bookkeeping and closing with closing entries.

One of the cycles of the accounting mechanism is:

Analyze preparatory transactions for the recording process

The first step in the company's accounting cycle is to analyze or identify transactions. Analysis of this transaction is in the form of collecting valid and accountable financial data, such as through receipts, invoices, or purchase notes. The collected data is then recorded in detail in the general journal and should not be mistaken because many companies have financial problems due to the identification of bad transactions.

Further explanation:

Reviewing the effectiveness of internal control procedures is a management audit activity.

Management audit evaluates every control that affects the effectiveness and efficiency of the company. Therefore, Internal Control is included in the scope of management audit. The results of the management audit itself are in the form of recommendations for improvements to the deficiencies or weaknesses of the existing Internal Control System. The management audit will evaluate and provide recommendations for improvements to the company's internal control system.

The first step in making accounting information system applications.
