1. What are your expectations inthe course "introduction to Philippine Criminal Justice System?


Answer:

The course Introduction to Philippine Criminal Justice System is about the five pillars of criminal justice in the Philippines – law enforcement, the prosecution, the courts, the corrections, and the community.

Explanation:

Law Enforcement

The deterrent and preventive pillar. In the Philippines, this pillar is the primary responsibility of the Philippine National Police (PNP), the Department of the Interior and Local Government (DILG), and the National Bureau of Investigation (NBI).  

Prosecution

Refers to the process wherein the criminally accused are brought before a court of law to determine if guilty or innocent.  

The Court

The court is a government institution where cases are decided in accordance to existing laws.  

Corrections

Concerned with the custody, supervision, and rehabilitation of criminal offenders. This pillar is related to penology, the study of crime punishment or of the criminal offender.  

Community

Active participation of members in the community, such as the efforts of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD), is also essential in the administration of the Criminal Justice System.

7. Define the criminal justice system of the Philippines?​


The Philippine criminal justice system is composed of five parts or pillars namely, law enforcement, prosecution, judiciary, penology, and the community.

Law enforcement is a term used to define the act of enforcing the law.

Prosecution is the party in criminal proceeding who instituted the criminal action.

The Judiciary of the Philippines consists of the Supreme Court, which is established in the Constitution, and three levels of lower courts, which are established through law by the Congress of the Philippines.

Penology is an attached agency of the Department of the Interior and Local Government mandated to direct, supervise and control the administration and operation of all district, city and municipal jails in the Philippines with pronged tasks of safekeeping and development of its inmates.

Community is a group of people living in the same place.
