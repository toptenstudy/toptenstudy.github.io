5. For every $20​ you earn, you donate $3​ to charity. The equation d=3/20​e​ models the relationship between the amount earned in dollars and the amount donated in dollars. Determine the amount you earned in dollars if you donated $90​ to charity.


Answer:

Determine the amount you earned in dollars if you donated $90 to charity.

The amount earned is $600.

Step-by-step explanation:

To determine the amount earned in dollars if $90 is donated to charity, we have the given equation, d=‌3⁄20‌e.

This equation shows that the two quantities have a proportional relationship. It means that if one quantity increases, the other quantity increases also. The quantities affect each other.

In proportional relationships, equation can be expressed as y=kx, from the problem above it is expressed as d=‌3⁄20‌e. k or ‌3⁄20‌ is the constant proportionality, then the variables are the quantities.

Solution:

Substitute the value of d.

d =‌ 3⁄20‌e

$90 = 3⁄20‌e

$90÷3⁄20‌ = 3⁄20‌e÷3⁄20‌

$600 = e

Divide both sides by 3⁄20‌ to isolate the variable. Always express the answer in lowest term.

Checking:

To check, cross-multiplication. The products should be the same.

3⁄20‌ = ‌90⁄600‌

3 × 600 = 1800

20 × 90 = 1800 ✔
