16. The total mass of 3 sacks of rice is 57.9 kg. Sack A is 2.4 kg heavier than Sack B but 3.6 kg lighter than Sack C. What is the mass of Sack B?


Let A be the Sack A, B be the Sack B, and C be the Sack C.

The total mass of the 3 sacks of rice is 57.9 kg. The equation will be:

A + B + C = 57.9

If Sack A is 2.4 kg heavier than Sack B, then A = 2.4B

If Sack A is 3.6 kg lighter than Sack C, then C = 3.6(2.4B) or 8.64B

Next, find the value of B.

Substitute the value of A and C in the equation. Whereas, A = 2.4B and C = 8.64B.

            A + B + C = 57.9

2.4B + B + 8.64B = 57.9

                12.04B = 57.9

To find the value of B, divide 12.04 on both sides of the equation.

B = 4.81

The mass of Sack B is 4.81 kg.

To check, substitute.

                            A + B + C = 57.9

2.4(4.81) + 4.81 + 8.64(4.81) = 57.9

            11.54 + 4.81 + 41.55 = 57.9

                                    57.9 = 57.9
