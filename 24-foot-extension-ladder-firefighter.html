5. a 20 foot ladder is leaning against a vertical wall if the foot of the ladder is 8 feet from the wall how high does the ladder reach include an illustration senior solution​


Answer:

The ladder reaches at a height of [tex]18.33\text{ ft}[/tex].

To better understand the situation, draw an illustration that represents the problem. See the illustration below.

This means that the height of the wall needs to be solved to know how long does the ladder reach.

Notice that the shape forms a right triangle.

Characteristics of a Right Triangle

1. It is a closed figure with three sides.

2. One of its angles measures [tex]90^o.[/tex]

3. Two angles are acute angles which measure [tex]0^o<\theta<90^o[/tex].

4. The longest leg is called the hypotenuse.

5. The Pythagorean Theorem is useful in solving the lengths of the sides of a right triangle.

The Pythagorean Theorem states that the sum of the square of the two shorter legs of a right triangle is equal to the square of its hypotenuse or

[tex]c^2=a^2+b^2[/tex], where c is the length of the hypotenuse and, a and b are the two shorter legs.

Since the illustration shows a right triangle, to solve the length of the wall, use the Pythagorean Theorem.

Steps in Solving the Wall Length

1. Substitute the values of [tex]c=20\\[/tex] and [tex]a=8[/tex] into the Pythagorean Theorem,

2. Simplify the terms with squares.

3. Subtract 64 from both sides.

4. Take the square root of both sides.

5. Take the positive value, since the problem talks about length and there's no negative length.

                                     [tex]\begin{aligned}c^2&=a^2+b^2\\(20)^2&=(8)^2+b^2\\400&=64+b^2\\400-64&=64-64+b^2\\36&=b^2\\\pm\sqrt{336}&=\sqrt{b^2}\\\sqrt{336}&=b\\18.33&=b\end{aligned}[/tex]

This means that the length of the wall is 18.33 which also the same length that the ladder reaches.
