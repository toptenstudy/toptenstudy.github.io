10. Karen walked across a circular swimming pool and it is 17 feet across the center. How far will Karen swim around the pool?A. 53.38 ftB. 56.28 ftC. 62.40 ftD. 63.38 ft​


GEOMETRY PROBLEM

The answer to the problem is 53.38 feet around the pool. The circular swimming pool's circumference can be determined using the formula C = 2r, where r is the radius of the pool and is a mathematical constant equivalent to around 3.14, and Karen will swim around it in 53.38 ft.

We must find the circumference of the pool in order to calculate how far Karen will swim around it.

The first thing that we will do is to use the radius of the pool, which is half of the diameter, to find its circumference using the formula:

C = 2πr

where C is the circumference, r is the pool's radius, and (pi) is a mathematical constant that roughly equates to 3.14.

The radius is 8.5 feet because the pool's diameter is 17 feet.

When we enter the values, we obtain:

C = 2πr

C = 2 x 3.14 x 8.5

C = 53.38 ft

Karen will swim approximately 53.38 feet around the pool.

13. Ms. Boyett wants to build a circular swimming pool with a fountain in the center. She wants the fountain to be 18.6 feet from the edge of the pool. What would the distance be around the outside of the pool?


Answer: The distance around the outside of the pool would be approximately 233.45 feet.

Step-by-step explanation:

Assuming that the circular swimming pool has a radius of "r" feet, we can use the formula for the circumference of a circle to find the distance around the outside of the pool:

Circumference = 2πr

However, we also need to take into account the distance between the fountain and the edge of the pool. Since the fountain is 18.6 feet from the edge of the pool, its distance from the center of the pool (which is also the center of the circle) would be r - 18.6 feet.

Therefore, the radius of the pool would be:

r = distance from center to edge + distance from center to fountain

r = 18.6 feet + 18.6 feet

r = 37.2 feet

Now, we can plug in the value of r into the formula for the circumference:

Circumference = 2πr

Circumference = 2π(37.2 feet)

Circumference = 233.45 feet (rounded to two decimal places)

Therefore, the distance around the outside of the pool would be approximately 233.45 feet.
