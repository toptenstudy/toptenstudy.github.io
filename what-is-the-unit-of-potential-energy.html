30. 1. how does speed affect the kinetic energy of an object?2. what are the factors that determine the potential energy of an object?3. why is the unit of energy the same as the work done?4. how is the total energy of the system compared to the sum of kinetic energy and potential energy?5. why does the energy of the system changes? remember energy is neither created nor destroyed​


Answer:

1.) It turns out that an object's kinetic energy increases as the square of its speed. A car moving 40 mph has four times as much kinetic energy as one moving 20 mph, while at 60 mph a car carries nine times as much kinetic energy as at 20 mph. Thus a modest increase in speed can cause a large increase in kinetic energy.

2.) Gravitational Potential Energy is determined by three factors: mass, gravity, and height. All three factors are directly proportional to energy.

3.) Because Energy is what's required to perform work on or to heat an object. Energy is transferred to an object and work is performed as a result. Therefore, work and energy, while not synonyms, are consistent with respect to the object.

4.) When the kinetic energy is maximum, the potential energy is zero. This occurs when the velocity is maximum and the mass is at the equilibrium position. The potential energy is maximum when the speed is zero. The total energy is the sum of the kinetic energy plus the potential energy and it is constant.

5.) The total amount of energy and matter in the Universe remains constant, merely changing from one form to another. The First Law of Thermodynamics (Conservation) states that energy is always conserved, it cannot be created or destroyed. In essence, energy can be converted from one form into another.
