24. explain why trucks need bigger engine than smaller cars.(Hint: Law of acceleration​


Answer:

BHP is a function derived of engine torque and engine speed. So, to get high peak power figures, you need a high rpm running engine that can keep a high torque at such a turning speed.

On the other hand, in NA engines, displacement is directly proportional to torque. Torque is a rotational force, which is of course used to move the wheels. Force is equivalent to acceleration, so there you have it: to accelerate, you need torque.

The larger the cargo capacity and weight of a vehicle, the more torque (force) you´ll need to accelerate it properly. But one thing is the engine torque figure, and a very different one is the torque that ends up driving the wheels. Why? Because the torque from the engine is routed through the gears of a the transmission box, and its differentials.

The shorter the final ratio of a given gear, the higher the engine´s torque will be amplified by the gearbox, so you´ll be able to accelerate faster in shorter gears, or be able to tow a larger cargo.

Following the previous paragraph, there you have also why trucks can indeed have a lot of gears in the form of “high” and “low”, which means basically that there´s more than one differential, and in the “low” position, you´ll get your ratios to be shorter thus giving you a higher torque amplification.

Many pick up trucks and all terrain vehicles (at least the most “serious” ones) have a reduction gearbox to do just that. More torque can also help you overcome steeper climbs and other obstacles which otherwise could potentially leave you stranded.
