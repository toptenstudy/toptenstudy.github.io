4. given isosceles right triangle abc with ac as the hypotenuse ,a circle with a center at a and radius ab intersect ac at d. what is the ratio of the area of sector bad to the area of the region bcd​


✒️CIRCLES

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \Large\underline{\mathbb{PROBLEM}:} [/tex]

given isosceles right triangle ABC with ac as the hypotenuse, a circle with a center at a and radius AB intersect AC at D. what is the ratio of the area of sector BAD to the area of the region BCD.

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \Large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER}:} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \Large » \: \rm{\frac{\pi}{\,4 - \pi\,}} \\ [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \Large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

» The area of the region BCD can be calculated if we subtract the area of the right isosceles ∆ABC into the area of sector BAD. Thus the ratio can be represented as:

[tex] \small \rm \frac{Area \: of \: sector \: BAD}{\:(Area \: of \: \Delta ABC) - (Area \: of \: sector \: BAD)\:} \\ [/tex]

• The area of the sector of a circle is the product of the ratio of the measure of the central angle and 360°, and the area of the circle. Let r be the ratio.

[tex] \rm Area = \bigg(\frac{ \small central \: angle}{360 \degree}\bigg) \, \pi r^2 \\ [/tex]

• And the area of the isosceles right triangle is half the area of the square. Let s be the side.

[tex] \rm Area = \frac{\,s^2\,}{2} \\ [/tex]

» Plugging it everything to the ratio, it would look like.

[tex] \rm \frac{ \Big(\frac{ central \: angle}{ 360 \degree}\Big) \, \pi r^2}{\large\frac{\,s^2\,}{2} \normalsize - \Big(\frac{ central \: angle}{360 \degree}\Big) \, \pi r^2} \\ [/tex]Find the values:

» Since ∆ABC is an isosceles right triangle, then both the measure of angle A and angle C is 45°.

[tex] \rm m \angle A = 45 \degree [/tex]

[tex] \rm m \angle C = 45 \degree [/tex]

» Let x be the radius and the leg of the right triangle. Line AB is congruent to Line BC.

[tex] \rm \overline{AB} \cong \overline{BC} [/tex]

[tex] \rm x = \overline{AB} [/tex]

[tex] \rm x = \overline{BC} [/tex]

» Angle BAC is the central angle of the circle. Thus, the measure of angle BAC is 45°. Plug every given to the ratio then solve.

[tex] \rm \frac{ \large \Big(\frac{45 \degree}{ 360 \degree}\Big) \, \pi x^2}{\large\frac{\,x^2\,}{2} \normalsize - \Big(\frac{45 \degree}{360 \degree}\Big) \, \pi x^2} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \rm \frac{ \large \frac{\,1\,}{8} \, \pi x^2}{\large\frac{\,x^2\,}{2} -\frac{\,1\,}{8} \, \pi x^2} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \rm \frac{ \large \frac{\,\pi x^2\,}{8}}{\large\frac{\,4x^2\,}{8} -\frac{\,\pi x^2\,}{8} } \\ [/tex]

[tex] \rm \frac{ \large \frac{\,\pi x^2\,}{8}}{\large\frac{\,4x^2 \, - \, \pi x^2\,}{8}} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \rm \frac{\,\pi x^2\,}{8} \div \frac{\,4x^2 - \pi x^2\,}{8} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \rm \frac{\,\pi x^2\,}{\cancel8} \times \frac{\cancel8}{\,4x^2 - \pi x^2\,} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \rm \frac{\,\pi x^2\,}{1} \times \frac{1}{\,4x^2 - \pi x^2\,} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \rm \frac{\,\pi x^2\,}{\,4x^2 - \pi x^2\,} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \rm \frac{\,\pi \: \cancel{x^2}\,}{\,\cancel{x^2} \: (4 - \pi) \,} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \rm \frac{\,\pi \,}{\,4 - \pi \,} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] The ratio is π / (4 - π)
