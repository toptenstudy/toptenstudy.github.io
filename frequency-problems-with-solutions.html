21. Find the speed of light in this problem. Show your solution.The relationship between wavelength and frequency is A=c/f. Calculate the wavelength oflight that has a frequency of 5.2 x 10¹² s. The speed of light is 3.0 x 10⁸ m/s.​


Answer:

The wavelength is the distance between two crests, and is measured in length units, such as nanometers, centimeters, or meters. Wavelength is represented by the Greek lowercase letter lambda, #lambda#. Frequency is the number of crests that pass a given point within one second. One wave—or cycle—per second is called a Hertz (Hz). 1 Hz = 1/s or #"s"^(-1)#. Frequency is represented by the Greek lowercase letter nu, #nu#.

For any given photon of electromagnetic radiation, the wavelength times the frequency equals the speed of light: #c# = #lambda##nu#.

30. Activity 5: Problem Solver! Direction: Solve for the speed of sound, wavelength, and frequency. Show your solution. 1. A wave has a frequency of 75 Hz and a wavelength of 10m. What is the speed of the wave? 2. The speed of a wave is 80 m/sec. If the wavelength is 1.5 meters, what is the frequency of the wave? 3. A wave has a frequency of 400 Hz is traveling at a speed of 100 m/s. What is the wavelength? ​


Explanation:

1. The speed of a wave can be calculated using the formula:

speed = frequency x wavelength

Given that the frequency is 75 Hz and the wavelength is 10 m, we can plug these values into the formula to get:

speed = 75 Hz x 10 m

speed = 750 m/s

Therefore, the speed of the wave is 750 meters per second.

2. The frequency of a wave can be calculated using the formula:

frequency = speed / wavelength

Given that the speed is 80 m/s and the wavelength is 1.5 m, we can plug these values into the formula to get:

frequency = 80 m/s / 1.5 m

frequency = 53.33 Hz

Therefore, the frequency of the wave is approximately 53.33 Hz.

3. The wavelength of a wave can be calculated using the formula:

wavelength = speed / frequency

Given that the frequency is 400 Hz and the speed is 100 m/s, we can plug these values into the formula to get:

wavelength = 100 m/s / 400 Hz

wavelength = 0.25 meters

Therefore, the wavelength of the wave is 0.25 meters.
