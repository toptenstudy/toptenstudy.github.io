As we face the uncertainties brought about by Covid-19, the importance of learning through the English language is essential in adapting to the needs of the future generations. The pandemic hastened the acceptance of a more digitally inclined educational system and workplace. With technology comes the use of the English language hand in hand with the native language of the Filipinos

The pandemic pushed our government to implement extreme measures such as the enhanced community quarantine and its variations. Together with this, there were enforced limited mobility of people (especially the senior citizens, minors, and those with comorbidities), following of social or physical distancing, the wearing of facemasks and face shields, limited face-to-face interaction, and the promotion of personal health and disinfection.

The most crucial impact of the pandemic is seen through the rising unemployment due to the restrictions in the livelihood of people. Those persons who live through day-to-day jobs such as the jeepney drivers, tricycle drivers, food stall owners or vendors, and contractual employees are the mostly affected. The Filipinos learned to adapt to changes brought about by uncertainties with the use of technology and the support of the family. In learning to cope with the challenges ahead, the academic community also opened its doors to globalizing education through the E-learning modalities, both synchronous and asynchronous.  Schools such as the St. Vincent de Ferrer College of Camarin strengthens its grip in digital education using technology and the English language in this time of crisis.

The families of Vincentian students are encouraged to learn and engage with the English language as we share our experiences of resilience in times of uncertainties. Students are enjoined to share their experiences by sending them to the email address of the year/grade English subject coordinators.

A descriptive paragraph provides a vibrant experience for the reader through vivid language and descriptions of something. Unlike narrative paragraphs, which must include personal thoughts, feelings, and growth, descriptive paragraphs do not need to be personal in nature. Instead, descriptive paragraphs must focus on vividly and objectively describing something to the reader. In order to provide this vivid detail, the writer must use language that appeals to the reader’s five senses: sight, smell, sound, taste, and touch. To appeal to these senses, the writer must use descriptive language, usually in the form of adjectives, that describes the sensations felt by the senses. For instance, examine the differences between the descriptions below:Sentence 1: The tree was tall and green.Sentence 2: The soft and damp pink flowers of the dogwood tree smelled sweet in the cool spring air as the wind whistled through its yellow-green leaves.
