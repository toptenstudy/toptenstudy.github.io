12. what is the difference between basic buffers and acidic buffers​


A basic buffer solution---(more precisely, pH buffer or hydrogen ion buffer) is an aqueous solution consisting of a mixture of a weak acid and its conjugate base, or vice versa. Its pH changes very little when a small amount of strong acid or base is added to it.

An acidic buffer solution--- is simply one which has a pH less than 7. Acidic buffer solutions are commonly made from a weak acid and one of its salts - often a sodium salt. A common example would be a mixture of ethanoic acid and sodium ethanoate in solution
