Answer:

1.mothballs are typically used to repel moths and other insects from drawers or closets, and their strong odor can be noticed in the surrounding area.

2.Mothballs disappear over time due to a process called sublimation, where a solid material transitions directly into a gas without going through the liquid phase. This causes the solid mothballs to gradually shrink and disappear, as they release their gaseous components into the air.

3.No, not all substances sublimate. Only certain substances, such as mothballs and dry ice (solid carbon dioxide), have the ability to sublimate.

4.The process involved when a solid turns into gas is called sublimation. This occurs when the solid substance absorbs enough energy (usually in the form of heat) to break the intermolecular bonds holding the molecules together. The molecules then transition directly into the gas phase without going through the liquid phase.

5.In places with very cold weather, frost may be more likely to form because the air contains less moisture, and therefore, less water vapor. When the temperature drops below freezing, any moisture in the air can condense onto surfaces and freeze, creating frost.

6.The process involved when a gas turns into a solid is called deposition. This occurs when a gas loses energy (usually in the form of heat) and the molecules come together to form a solid substance, skipping the liquid phase. Examples of deposition include the formation of frost on a cold surface or the formation of snowflakes in the atmosphere.
