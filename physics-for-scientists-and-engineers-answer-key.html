10. How are scientists able to realize their objectives in genetic engineering​


Answer:

how are scientists able to realize their objectives in genetic engineering

Answer:

Scientists are able to achieve their objectives in genetic engineering through a combination of techniques and tools, including:

Cloning: Scientists are able to create copies of specific genes or pieces of DNA using the process of cloning. This allows them to manipulate and study the function of specific genes.

Recombinant DNA technology: Scientists use enzymes such as restriction endonucleases and ligases to cut and paste specific genes from one organism into the genome of another. This process allows them to introduce new traits or modify existing ones in an organism.

Genetic mapping: Scientists use techniques such as PCR (polymerase chain reaction) and DNA sequencing to identify specific genes and map out their locations in an organism's genome.

Gene editing: CRISPR-cas9 is a method used to precisely edit the genome of an organism. This can be used to add, delete or modify specific genes and can be used to correct mutations that cause genetic disorders.

Gene therapy: This refers to the use of genetic engineering to treat or prevent disease by introducing new functional genes into an individual's cells.

These techniques and tools allow scientists to study the function of genes and make precise changes to an organism's genome, thereby realizing their objectives in genetic engineering.​

30. what do you think are the objectives of scientists in genetic engineering?​


Genetic engineering

I think their objectives in genetic engineering was the process of inserting new genetic information into existing cells in order to modify a specific organism for the purpose of changing it's characteristics.

Genetic engineering, also called genetic modification, is the direct manipulation of an organism's genes using biotechnology. It is a set of technologies used to change the genetic makeup of cells, including the transfer of genes across and across species boundaries to produce improved organisms.

New DNA is obtained by isolating and copying genetic material from the parent using recombinant DNA methods or artificial DNA synthesis. A vector is usually created and used to insert this DNA into the host organism.

Genetic engineering is used to produce disease-resistant plant seeds. In the plant genome inserted genes that have immunity to certain diseases. The application of genetic engineering to plants is carried out at the time of treatment and seed washing.

Forest biotechnology also uses genetic engineering to breed forest plants through gene technology and genome analysis. The application of genetic engineering is carried out on trees that have broad leaves and needle leaves, such as poplar, bella, cypress and eucalyptus. Forest plant breeding tests were carried out in laboratories, greenhouses and in the forest.
