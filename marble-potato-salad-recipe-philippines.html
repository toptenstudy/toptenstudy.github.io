9. (Each, Many, Everyone, Everybody) like potato salad.


Indefinite Pronouns

Answer:

Pronouns are a used as a substitute for Nouns. When we write a long text like a paragraph or a report, we tend to use the same name very often as a subject of our sentences. And so to avoid repeating those names over and over again we use Pronouns. It also gives a sentence a proper construction and grammar as long as we follow it properly.

To use Pronouns we should understand its Types, like so:

The very common, Personal Pronouns (I, She, He, They, Them, Who, We) And the Pronouns in the selection below, Indefinite (Any, Each, Everybody, Plenty, Few, None, All, Some)

(Each, Many, Everyone, Everybody) like potato salad.

Each- singular Many- Plural Everyone- Singular (Definite) Everybody- Singular (Indefinite)

In this case the sentence didn’t actually defined who likes potato salad like (“Everyone” in this room likes potato salad), it directly just use a pronoun. It is indefinite the first two pronouns “Each & Many” can’t be used since this require a relationship with a noun like (Each boy around here likes potato salad or Many girls likes potato salad). And the pronoun “everyone” also falls down with the same case as “Each”& “Many”.

Therefore we use the pronoun “Everybody”.

"Everybody likes potato salad. "

Botulism is a type of poisoning from food that is caused by the toxin botulinum. It is quite deadly, as the toxin induces paralysis that starts with face, limbs and ends up with the breathing muscles, which leads further to respiratory failure.


Home-canning or improper canning of the potatoes used in the potato salad is the most probable cause for botulism. Unlike commercial or modern canning, the poor preservation or preparation doesn’t kill the bacterium Clostridium Botulinum because the spores are resistant even to processes such as baking.

18. dessert salad recipe


Answer:

Ingredients

1 egg

1/4 cup sugar

1 tablespoon lemon juice

2 teaspoons butter

1 can (8 ounces) pineapple chunks, drained

1/2 cup green grapes

1/2 cup fresh orange pieces

1/2 cup pastel miniature marshmallows

1/2 cup heavy whipping cream

1 tablespoon confectioners' sugar

Explanation:

Directions

In a small saucepan, combine the egg, sugar, lemon juice and butter; cook and stir over low heat for 5-7 minutes or until thickened. Cool. Meanwhile, combine the pineapple, grapes, oranges and marshmallows in a small bowl; set aside. In a small bowl, beat cream and confectioners' sugar until soft peaks form. Fold into fruit mixture. Add cooled dressing. Chill for at least 1 hour.

27. ingridients and procedure of Potato salad​


Answer:

Ingredients:

6 medium white potatoes or Yukon golds about 2 1/2 to 3 pounds, skin on and quartered

3 tablespoons white vinegar

2 celery stalks (ribs) diced

6 green onions diced

5 hard boiled eggs peeled

1 1/2 cups Miracle Whip or mayonnaise

1 tablespoon yellow mustard

1 1/2 teaspoons celery seed

3/4 teaspoon kosher salt

3/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

paprika for garnish

Procedure:

Bring a large pot of cold water to a boil. Reduce the heat to a lightly rolling boil over medium heat. Cook for 20-25 minutes or until the potatoes are easily pierced with a fork or pairing knife. Drain and allow to cool until just able to handle.

Peel the skins from the potatoes and cut into 1/2" to 3/4" square pieces. Transfer the warm potatoes to a large mixing bowl and sprinkle with the white vinegar. Toss the potatoes with the vinegar and set the potatoes aside to cool, about 15-20 minutes.

Add the celery and the green onions to the potato mixture. Chop 4 of the hard boiled eggs and add to the potato mixture.

In a medium bowl, mix the Miracle Whip or mayonnaise, yellow mustard, celery seed and salt and pepper. Fold into the potato mixture and season with more salt and pepper to taste. Slice the last egg into thin slices and place the slices on top of the salad. Sprinkle with paprika if desired. Chill for at least 1 hour or overnight before serving.
