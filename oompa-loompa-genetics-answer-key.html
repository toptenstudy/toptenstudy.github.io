10. 1. What do you think will be the significance of social support for those people that are currently affected by the mutations in DNA such as genetic disorders 2. What do you think will be the key role of biotechnology in managing genetic disorders?


1. What do you think will be the significance of social support for those people that are currently affected by the mutations in DNA such as genetic disorders?

Answer: Social support is likely to be incredibly significant for individuals and families affected by genetic disorders. Having a supportive network of friends, family, and healthcare professionals can provide practical assistance, emotional support, and a sense of community for those who may feel isolated and alone in their struggles. It is important to note that social support is not a substitute for medical treatment, but it can be an important complement to it and can help individuals and families to better manage their condition, improve their quality of life, and reduce their risk of depression and anxiety.

2. What do you think will be the key role of biotechnology in managing genetic disorders?

Answer: Biotechnology plays a crucial role in the management of genetic disorders by providing tools for early diagnosis, treatment, and prevention. For example, genetic testing can be used to identify individuals at risk for certain disorders, while genetic engineering and gene therapy can be used to treat or prevent the onset of genetic disorders. Additionally, biotechnology can be used to develop new drugs and therapies for genetic disorders. Overall, biotechnology has the potential to greatly improve the management of genetic disorders and improve the lives of individuals affected by these conditions.

30. what is genetics? aa a student, why it is so significant to study genetics? (answer in two paragraphs).​


Genetics is a branch of biology concerned with the study of genes, genetic variation, and heredity in organisms. Though heredity had been observed for millennia, Gregor Mendel, Moravian scientist and Augustinian friar working in the 19th century in Brno, was the first to study genetics scientifically.

Genetics can help us to understand why people look the way they do and why some people are more prone to certain diseases than others. Genetics can help health-care professionals to identify certain conditions in babies before they are born using techniques such as prenatal testing
