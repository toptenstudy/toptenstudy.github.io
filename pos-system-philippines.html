6. Write an argumentative essay on "Is Online Education Relevant inthe Philippine System? pakisagot po sana ng maayos,​


Is Online Education Relevant to the Philippine System?

Philippines is not prepared for online learning. According to the Department of Science and Technology, the Philippines ranks 83rd out of 183 countries in terms of digital readiness. This indicates that the Philippines is not technologically sophisticated (DOST).

During the quarantine in most of the Philippines, education officials have advocated using online platforms for the 2020-2021 school year to continue the education of millions of students. Online education offers virtual classes that a student can take at home alone or with a group via phone call or video conference using software such as Messenger, Google Zoom, or Skype. Online education necessitates the use of suitable technology, such as a smartphone that supports video calls and a stable internet connection.

However, the Philippines cannot support online education for the majority of its pupils, therefore the attempt will fail for the majority of the country. Due to poverty in the Philippines, not every family has the means to acquire the necessary equipment for online learning. These families simply cannot adapt to the needs of online learning and will be left behind or forced to find alternative means of education for their children if they are not provided with an alternative. In addition, internet speed in the Philippines is still bad, as our country's more than 7,000 islands make it impossible to build fixed networks. In addition, the process of constructing cell towers is arduous and time-consuming.

However, Online Education allows both the teacher and the student to set their own learning rate, as well as a schedule that accommodates everyone's schedule. In addition, it can assist us in being highly organized, self-motivated, and time-management savvy. Consequently, employing an online educational platform allows for a better work-study balance.

In conclusion, the Philippines requires a clear set of regulations and norms based on an innovative educational system. This necessitates a thorough and serious evaluation of the nation's readiness to offer learning programs with rigorous criteria. This comprises instructor capability, the learner's position and context, and the effectiveness of the learning environment. These are in addition to the more obvious difficulties of internet speed, material cost, and delivery method. The greatest approach to advance is to take a step back and develop a strategy that involves instructors, students, parents, school officials, and technology-based corporations.

11. What do you think does Philippines' education should adopt and improve to Denmark educational system? HELP PO PLEASE ​


The education system in the Philippines is largely based on the American system, with a focus on rote learning and standardized testing. The system is also characterized by a lack of resources and funding, which has resulted in overcrowded classrooms and limited access to quality education for many students.

In contrast, the education system in Denmark is known for its emphasis on creativity, critical thinking, and student-centered learning. The system is also characterized by a high level of government funding and support, which has resulted in smaller class sizes and greater access to resources and technology.

If the Philippines were to adopt and improve aspects of the Danish education system, it could consider implementing more student-centered and creative approaches to learning, reducing the emphasis on rote memorization and standardized testing. It could also seek to increase government funding and support for education, in order to improve access to resources and reduce class sizes.

However, it is important to note that every country has its own unique cultural, social, and economic factors that influence its education system. Therefore, any changes to the education system should be tailored to the specific needs and challenges of the Philippines.

19. What is the most common type of water system used by farmers in the philippines ? Pa Sagot Po​


Explanation:

The most common type of irrigation system used by farmers in the Philippines is surface irrigation. This method involves the use of channels, dikes, and floodgates to deliver water to fields. Some farmers also use sprinkler systems, which involve the use of pumps to deliver water through pipes to sprinklers that disperse the water over the field. Drip irrigation, which involves the use of tubes or hoses to deliver water directly to the root zone of plants, is also becoming more popular in the Philippines.

Public healthcare

Doctors and nursing staff in public hospitals are highly proficient, however public healthcare in the Philippines faces some limitations. Despite having achieved universal healthcare, the Philippines still struggles with unequal access to medical care. As such, the standard of public healthcare in the Philippines generally varies from excellent in urban centres to poor in rural areas. Public healthcare also faces strain both from treating the large number of Filipinos who rely on public healthcare and from the trend of Filipino medical staff migrating to Western countries. This has resulted in understaffing in some hospitals and patients may experience delays in treatment.

Public healthcare in the Philippines is administered by Philhealth, a government owned corporation. Philhealth subsidises a variety of treatments including inpatient care and non-emergency surgeries, although it does not cover all medical treatments and costs.

Enrolling with Philhealth is mandatory for expats who are employed in the Philippines. Philhealth contributions are derived from employers, employee salaries and the state. Expats can voluntarily enrol with Philhealth if they have residency status.
