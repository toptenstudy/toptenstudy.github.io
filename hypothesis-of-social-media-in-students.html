2. how can social media affect the behavior of the students? null hypothesis. alternative hypothesis ​


Answer:

Social Networks Sites (SNSs) are dominating all internet users’ generations, especially the students’ communities. Consequently, academic institutions are increasingly using SNSs which leads to emerge a crucial question regarding the impact of SNSs on students’ academic performance. This research investigates how and to what degree the use of SNSs affects the students’ academic performance. The current research’s data was conducted by using drop and collect surveys on a large population from the University of Jordan. 366 undergraduate students answered the survey from different faculties at the university. In order to study the impact of SNSs on student’s academic performance, the research hypotheses was tested by using descriptive analysis, T-test and ANOVA. Research results showed that there was a significant impact of SNS on the student’s academic performance. Also, there was a significant impact of SNS use per week on the student’s academic performance, whereas no differences found in the impact of use of SNSs on academic performance due to age, academic achievement, and use per day to most used sites. The findings of this research can be used to suggest future strategies in enhancing student’s awareness in efficient time management and better multitasking that can lead to improving study activities and academic achievements.

3. hypothesis about social media: Its Influence to the social behavior of the students pa help naman po thesis namin​


Answer:

The hypothesis about social media and its influence on the social behavior of students suggests that the use of social media platforms has a significant impact on the way students interact with others and behave in social situations. This hypothesis is based on the idea that social media has become an integral part of students' lives, affecting not only their communication habits but also their perceptions of self and others.

The hypothesis suggests that students who use social media regularly may exhibit certain behaviors such as increased aggression, decreased empathy, and a stronger focus on appearance and popularity. On the other hand, students who use social media in moderation or not at all may display more positive social behaviors such as improved communication skills, enhanced emotional intelligence, and a greater sense of community.

It is important to note that this hypothesis is not a blanket statement and may vary depending on the individual and their specific experiences with social media. Further research and analysis are needed to fully understand the relationship between social media and social behavior among students.

10. Does the use of social media have an effect on senior high school students’ attention span in class? Null Hypothesis: ______________________________________________________ ______________________________________________________ ______________________________________________________ Alternative Hypothesis:


Answer:

Null Hypothesis:

Social media use has no appreciable impact on kids in senior high school's ability to pay attention in class.

Alternative Hypothesis:

The alternative hypothesis suggests that there is a significant relationship between social media use and attention span in class among senior high school students. It means that social media use can affect their ability to focus on the class material or pay attention to the teacher during the class.

Explanation:

The null hypothesis is a claim that implies social media use has no discernible impact on senior high school students' ability to pay attention in class. If this theory is shown to be false, it suggests that senior high school students' attention spans in class are related to their usage of social media.

The alternative hypothesis contends that frequent usage of social media can reduce senior high school students' capacity to pay attention in class, which could make it harder for them to focus on the lesson content and the teacher's directions. If statistical research confirms this hypothesis, it shows that there is a substantial correlation between senior high school students' usage of social media and their attention span in class.
