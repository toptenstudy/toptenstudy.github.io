3. The nurse is reviewing antiinfectives prior to caring for a client who is diagnosed with sepsis. what statements are accurate descriptions of antiinfective therapy? select all that apply.


ANTI INFECTIVES - antibiotics and other anti-infection medications.

 

      Any medication that has the ability to stop the spread of an infectious organism or to actually kill the infectious organism is referred to be an anti-infective in general. Antibiotics, antifungals, anthelmintics, antimalarials, antiprotozoals, antituberculosis medications, and antivirals are all included in this category.

Examples of anti-infectives, which fight infection by either preventing it or treating it.

Antibacterial - a chemical that eradicates germs or prevents them from proliferating and spreading illness.Antiviral - Drugs that treat viral infections differ from antibiotics, which treat bacterial illnesses. Antiviral medications for the flu only combat the virus. Antiviral medications used to treat influenza are distinct from those used to treat other infectious disorders like COVID-19.Anti fungal - Antifungals are drugs that either eradicate or inhibit the development of the fungi (plural of fungus) that cause infections. Additionally known as antimycotic agents.Anti parasitic - a group of drugs used for the prevention, control, and treatment of parasitic illnesses caused by protozoa, helminths, and ectoparasites. Multiple pharmacological classes that treat a wide range of parasitic disorders are referred to as antiparasitic medications.

    When selecting an antibiotic, numerous aspects must be considered for the greatest possible success in treating orodental infections:

The agent's antibacterial range and specificity;The level of bacterial resistance to the antibiotic disclosed;Concentrations attained at different locations;The infection's age, kind, and severity; A number of host variables.

11. what is neo natal sepsis​


Neonatal sepsis is a type of neonatal infection and specifically refers to the presence in a newborn baby of a bacterial blood stream infection (BSI) (such as meningitis, pneumonia, pyelonephritis, or gastroenteritis) in the setting of fever. Older textbooks may refer to neonatal sepsis as "sepsis neonatorum".
