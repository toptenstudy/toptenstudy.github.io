2. Limited learning opportunities: If students are only using traditional kitchen appliances, they may miss out on the chance to learn about and use more modern appliances that are commonly found in commercial kitchens. 3. Safety concerns: Traditional kitchen appliances may pose safety risks if they are not used properly. For example, gas stoves require careful attention to prevent fires, and old or poorly maintained appliances may malfunction and cause injury. This can create anxiety among students and distract them from the learning process.

4. Reduced creativity: If students are struggling with traditional appliances, they may feel limited in their ability to experiment and be creative with their cooking. This can result in a lack of enthusiasm for the subject and a reduced sense of ownership over their learning.

To address these challenges, it may be helpful for the school to invest in more modern kitchen appliances that are safer and more efficient to use. Additionally, providing training and support for students in using these appliances can help build their confidence and reduce their anxiety. The struggles of Grade 11 TVL students on still using traditional kitchen appliances can affect the cookery student's learning experience and Standard Operating Procedures (SOP) in several ways:

Inadequate preparation: Traditional kitchen appliances can be slower and less efficient than modern ones, which can lead to delays and incomplete preparation of ingredients. This can impact the overall quality of the finished product, which in turn affects the learning experience of the cookery students.

Limited exposure: Modern kitchen appliances are often equipped with new features and technologies that improve the cooking process, such as timers, temperature controls, and automatic shut-off. By using traditional appliances, students may miss out on learning about these new features, which can hinder their ability to use modern equipment effectively in the future.

Safety concerns: Traditional kitchen appliances can be more dangerous to use than modern ones. For example, open flames from gas stoves can cause fires or burn injuries, while manual food processors and meat grinders can pose a risk of hand injury. The use of outdated equipment can put students at risk and affect their ability to follow proper SOPs.

Limited creativity: Modern kitchen appliances allow for more creative and innovative cooking techniques. By using traditional appliances, students may be limited in their ability to experiment with new methods and recipes, which can limit their learning experience and their ability to develop their culinary skills.

Outdated curriculum: If the curriculum of the cookery program has not been updated to reflect modern cooking techniques and technologies, students may be learning outdated methods that are no longer relevant in the industry. This can affect their ability to find employment or succeed in culinary competitions in the future.

The struggles of Grade 11 TVL students on still using traditional kitchen appliances can negatively affect the cookery student's learning experience and SOPs.
