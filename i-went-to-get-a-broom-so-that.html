24. Do natural broom bristles(broomcorn) still have proteins after transferring it to a broom?​


Answer:

It is not likely that natural broom bristles (also known as broomcorn) would retain proteins after being transferred to a broom.

Explanation:

Protein molecules are typically quite large and complex, and they are not particularly resistant to degradation by physical or chemical means. As such, it is unlikely that the process of transferring broomcorn to a broom would preserve any proteins that may have been present in the bristles. Additionally, the bristles of a broom are typically subjected to a variety of conditions (including regular use, exposure to water, and cleaning agents) that would further break down any proteins that may have been present. It is possible that small amounts of proteins may still be present in the bristles of a natural broom, but the amount would likely be very small and would not have any significant effect on the broom's performance or durability.

27. 3 witches holding a broom and wand =45 3 broom= 12 3 wand =22 so 1 broom + 1 witch without a broom and a wand * 1 wand is=


Number Logic:

The answer is 73.

Solution:

Find the value of the 3 witches with broom and wand on their hands using x.  

3x = 45

3x/3 = 45/3

x = 15

Therefore, each witch with broom and wand on her hand is equal to 15.

Find the value of the wand by using w.

3w = 21

3w/3 = 21/3

w = 7

Therefore, each wand is equivalent to 7.

Now, find the value of the broom using b. Note that there are 4 brooms in the picture which makes

4b = 12

4b/4 = 12/4

b = 3

Therefore, each broom is equivalent to 3.

Using all the values obtained, find the value of a broom + a witch with no broom and wand on her hand x a double wand which makes

3 + 15 (7 + 3) x (2x7)

3 + 15 - 10 x 14

3 + 5 x 14

By MDAS rule:

3 + 70 = 73

Therefore, broom + a witch with no broom and wand x a double wand is equals to 73.
