4. Why is Math considered as the queen of Sciences?​


Answer:

Mathematics is often called the queen of sciences because it provides a foundation for other sciences. Mathematics is the science of patterns and relationships, and as such, it underlies all the sciences, including physics, chemistry, and biology. The ability to model and analyze complex systems using mathematical tools is a crucial component of scientific research, and mathematics is the language that enables scientists to describe, communicate, and understand their findings. In addition, mathematics is important in many other fields, such as engineering, finance, and computer science, making it an essential tool for understanding the modern world.

Mathematics is often referred to as the "queen of sciences" because of its fundamental role in the study of various scientific disciplines. Mathematics provides the language and tools needed to understand and describe the natural world in a precise and logical way. It allows scientists to make predictions, analyze data, and test hypotheses in a rigorous and systematic way.

Mathematics is also considered the foundation of many other scientific disciplines, such as physics, engineering, and computer science. Without mathematics, these fields would not have been able to make the significant advancements and discoveries that they have achieved.

Furthermore, mathematics has practical applications in everyday life, from calculating the cost of groceries to designing buildings and bridges. It is a tool for problem-solving and critical thinking that is essential in many fields, including business, finance, and technology.

Overall, mathematics is considered the "queen of sciences" because of its critical role in the advancement of scientific knowledge and its practical applications in various fields. Its precision, logic, and problem-solving capabilities make it an essential tool in understanding and describing the world around us.

12. what does the shrine of mary,Queen of peace church,look like


The Shrine of Mary, Queen of Peace, Our Lady of EDSA, or more popularly, the EDSA Shrine is a small church of the Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Manila located at the intersection of Ortigas Avenue and Epifanio de los Santos Avenue (EDSA) in Barangay Ugong Norte, Quezon City. The shrine is also called the Archdiocesan Shrine of Mary, Queen of Peace or Mary, Queen of Peace Quasi-Parish, although these names are seldom used. Built in 1989 originally to commemorate the memories of the People Power Revolution and its peaceful outcome, the shrine stands on the site of two peaceful demonstrations that toppled Philippine presidents Ferdinand Marcos (the People Power Revolution or EDSA I) and Joseph Estrada (the EDSA Revolution of 2001 or EDSA II).

19. sino si Queen marie antoinette​


Answer:

Si Reyna Maria Antonieta, o higit na kilala bilang Marie Antoinette, reynang konsorte ng Pransiya at Navarro,  ay isang Reyna ng Pransiya at Arkodukesa ng Austria. Anak siya ng Emperador ng Banal na Imperyong Romano na si Francisco I at ng asawa nitong si Maria Teresa; asawa ni Haring Luis XVI ng Pransiya; at ina ni Luis XVII ng Pransiya. Binitay siya sa pamamagitan ng pagpugot ng ulo gamit ang gilotin noong Himagsikang Pranses at inihimlay kasama ng asawa niya sa Basilika ni San Dionisio sa Paris.


Sa gulang na labing-apat, ipinakasal si Maria Antonieta kay Louis–Auguste, ang Delfin ng Pransiya kung saan siya ay itinanghal bilang Dauphine de France (Delfina ng Pransiya). Noong mamatay ang biyenan niyang si Luis XV noong Mayo 1774, naging hari ng Pransiya ang kanyang asawa bilang Luis XVI at siya naman ang naging reyna ng Pransiya at Navarro. Makalipas ang pitong taon ng pagsasama, isinilang si Maria-Teresa-Carlota ng Pransiya, ang panganay sa kanilang apat na supling.

Noong Pamamayani ng Katatakutan, bago sumiklab ang Himagsikang Pranses, isinibak mula sa trono ang asawa ni Maria Antonieta at ang ipinakulong ang pamilyang maharlika. Nilitis si Maria Antonia, sinampahan ng kataksilan sa bansa at nahatulan ng pagpupugot gamit ang gilotin noong 16 Oktubre 1793, tulad ng paghatol at pagbitay sa kaniyang asawang si Luis siyam na buwan makaraan.

24. Why did Queen Mary I hated her half sister, Queen Elizabeth I? Explain in 4-5 sentences.


Answer:

Queen Mary I, also known as "Bloody Mary," was a Catholic ruler in a predominantly Protestant England, while her half-sister, Queen Elizabeth I, was a Protestant. Mary saw Elizabeth's religious beliefs as a threat to the restoration of Catholicism in England and considered her a potential rival for the throne. Mary also believed that Elizabeth was involved in plotting against her and was responsible for the uprising known as Wyatt's Rebellion. These factors led to Mary's distrust and hatred of Elizabeth, which lasted throughout their respective reigns.
