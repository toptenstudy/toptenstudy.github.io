3. A fast food chain receives 6 customers every hour. What is the probability that the fast food chain will receive 4 customers in one hour? Find the value of the random variable and calculate the probability distribution's mean, variance, and standard deviation.


Answer:

the mean is 6, the variance is 6, and the standard deviation is approximately 2.4495.

Step-by-step explanation:

This problem can be modeled using a Poisson distribution, where lambda (λ) is the average rate of events occurring in a given time period. In this case, λ = 6, since the fast food chain receives an average of 6 customers every hour.

The probability of receiving exactly 4 customers in one hour can be calculated as:

P(X = 4) = (λ^4 * e^(-λ)) / 4!

where X is the random variable representing the number of customers in one hour, e is the mathematical constant approximately equal to

2.71828, and 4! = 4 * 3 * 2 * 1 = 24.

Substituting λ = 6 and X = 4, we get:

P(X = 4) = (6^4 * e^(-6)) / 24 ≈ 0.1338

So the probability of the fast food chain receiving exactly 4 customers in one hour is approximately 0.1338.

To find the mean of the Poisson distribution, we use the formula:

μ = λ

So in this case, the mean is μ = λ = 6.

To find the variance of the Poisson distribution, we use the formula:

σ^2 = λ

So in this case, the variance is σ^2 = λ = 6.

To find the standard deviation of the Poisson distribution, we take the square root of the variance:

σ = sqrt(σ^2) = sqrt(6) ≈ 2.4495
