16. Under which type of debt capital does accident insurance of the employees of a business fall? A. retained capital B. long-term bond C. policyholder float financing D. vendor financing


Answer:

C. policyholder float financing.

Explanation:

Accident insurance for employees of a business typically falls under the category of policyholder float financing. Policyholder float financing refers to the practice where an insurance company uses premiums collected from policyholders to invest and generate income before paying out claims. This type of debt capital is specific to insurance companies and is not classified as retained capital, long-term bond, or vendor financing, which are different forms of debt capital in business finance.
