Period of Time Name of Scientists Contribution to the Periodic Table of Elements

1669 Hennig Brand Discovered phosphorus

1789 Antoine Lavoisier Compiled a list of 33 elements and established the law of conservation of mass

1817 Johann Dobereiner Grouped elements into triads based on their properties

1829 Johann Wolfgang Proposed the concept of valence and introduced the notation of elemental symbols

Döbereiner

1864 John Newlands Proposed the Law of Octaves and arranged elements in order of increasing atomic mass

1869 Dmitri Mendeleev Arranged elements in order of increasing atomic mass and proposed the periodic law. Also predicted the existence of unknown elements and left gaps in his table for them.

1871 Lothar Meyer Independently proposed the periodic law and arranged elements in order of increasing atomic volume

1913 Henry Moseley Used X-ray spectroscopy to determine the atomic number of elements, leading to the modern periodic table arrangement based on atomic number

1945 Glenn T. Seaborg Discovered several new elements and proposed the actinide series, leading to the current form of the periodic table with the f-block.
