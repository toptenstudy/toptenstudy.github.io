9. What is Future perfect progressive report​


Answer:

The future continuous or the future progressive tense represents an action taking place at some time in future and is used to discuss an event that will happen in the future for a specific amount of time. It is also used to tell about a planned future event.

Answer:

The future perfect progressive tense, also known as the future perfect continuous, is a verb tense that describes an action that will have been ongoing for a period of time in the future. It is formed by using the auxiliary verb "will have been" + the present participle of the main verb (verb + "-ing").

A future perfect progressive report would be a written or spoken document that uses this tense to describe an action or situation that will have been ongoing at a specific point in the future. For example, a report might say "By 5 PM tomorrow, the team will have been working on the project for six hours," using the future perfect progressive tense to describe the team's ongoing work.

Such reports are often used in business or technical writing to describe ongoing processes or projects and to indicate the duration of those processes up to a specific point in the future

23. Although technology is viewed as indication of progress, what do you think are some of disadvantages of having invented these sophisticated tools? why are they disadvantageous?​.


Answer:

There are a number of potential disadvantages to the development of sophisticated technology:

1. Cost: Advanced technology can be expensive to develop and produce, which can limit access to it. This can create inequalities and result in only certain groups or individuals being able to afford and benefit from the technology.

2. Job displacement: As technology becomes more advanced, it can potentially displace human workers by automating certain tasks or processes. This can lead to unemployment and a loss of job skills for those who are displaced.

3. Dependence: As we become more reliant on technology, we may become less self-sufficient and more dependent on it. This can be a problem if the technology fails or is not available, as it could disrupt essential services or cause other problems.

4. Privacy and security: The use of sophisticated technology can raise concerns about privacy and security. For example, the collection and use of personal data by technology companies or the government can be a concern.

5. Environmental impact: The production and use of technology can have negative environmental impacts, such as through the use of non-renewable resources, the generation of pollution and waste, and the contribution to climate change.

6. Social impacts: The use of technology can also have social impacts, such as changing the way people interact with each other and altering the balance of power within societies.
