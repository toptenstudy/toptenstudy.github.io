26. Explain this quote "One thing I know is I know nothing"​


Answer:

"One thing I know is I know nothing."

Explanation:

Originally or most famously known to be spoken by Socrates, this phrase has been given many interpretations but most prominent of them has something to do with Epistemology, (the study of the nature, validity, and sources of knowledge) this quotation lets us think and ponder about everything we thought we knew, everything we want know, and the possible ways of acquiring and possessing knowledge and wisdom.

The idea of the quote is in order to gain knowledge, one must submit to humility. To acquire wisdom, we must first be open minded. In my perspective, when you admit that you know nothing is to be open your mind to many possibilities of knowledge. Rather than to be limited by the perception of knowing everything already, you are submitting yourself of possibilities of shutting of further knowledge. In arrogance there is ignorance and in humility there is wisdom attained.
