Title: The Effect of Different Levels of Chicken Manure on Survival and Growth of Pomelo Seedlings

Introduction:

Pomelo (Citrus maxima) is a popular fruit tree in many parts of the world due to its delicious fruit and health benefits. However, like any other plant, pomelo seedlings require proper nutrients and care for optimal growth and survival. One of the important nutrients needed by plants is nitrogen, which is essential for growth and development. Chicken manure is an excellent source of nitrogen, and it also contains other essential nutrients that can promote the growth and health of plants.

Objective:

The objective of this study is to determine the effect of different levels of chicken manure on the survival and growth of pomelo seedlings.

Methodology:

The study will be conducted in a randomized complete block design (RCBD) with four treatments and five replications. The treatments are as follows: T1 (Control), T2 (2% chicken manure), T3 (4% chicken manure), and T4 (6% chicken manure). The pomelo seeds will be germinated in a nursery for 3 weeks before transplanting to plastic pots filled with soil and chicken manure mixtures. The growth parameters such as plant height, stem diameter, and leaf area will be measured weekly for a period of 8 weeks. The data collected will be analyzed using analysis of variance (ANOVA) and means separated using Duncan's multiple range test.

Expected Results:

It is expected that the use of chicken manure as a source of nitrogen will have a positive effect on the growth and survival of pomelo seedlings. It is also expected that the growth and survival rate of the pomelo seedlings will increase as the level of chicken manure increases.

Conclusion:

The results of this study will provide valuable information on the use of chicken manure as a source of nitrogen for pomelo seedlings. The findings will be beneficial to farmers and gardeners who are interested in growing healthy pomelo trees. The study will also highlight the importance of proper fertilization and care for the growth and survival of pomelo seedlings.

25. What do you call a desexed male chicken usually under 8 months old?a. broiler b. capon c. hend. rooster


Answer:

B. Capon

Explanation:

A desexed male chicken, usually under eight months is called a capon. They are usually chemically castrated, meaning via the use of drugs or physical castration, through surgical means.

This practice allows for the meat of the rooster to be more meaty. This practice dates back to the Romans. It was even mentioned by Shakespeare as food for the wealthy, presumably because of the meat quality.

The reduced sex hormones in the body of a caponized rooster results in a less gamey taste--meaning it does not smell like much as a chicken. In other meat types, such as goats, this could be likened to "anggo". A capon has less of the unpleasant smell.
