12. Importance of itinerary planning and development including skeletal, technical, descriptive itinerary?


By ensuring all the important details are included in the itinerary, there'll be no time wasted travelling between places, activities, or accommodations. You can manage clients' expectations with a more realistic view of how much can fit into each day. 2) Clients can better estimate the cost of their trip.﻿

Answer:

Itinerary planning and development is important in ensuring a smooth and successful trip. By creating a skeletal itinerary, travelers can have a general idea of the places they want to visit, activities they want to do, and the duration of their trip. This helps in budgeting and scheduling, as it allows travelers to plan their expenses and time efficiently.

A technical itinerary provides specific details on travel arrangements, such as flight schedules, hotel accommodations, and transportation options. This ensures that travelers have a clear understanding of the logistics involved in their trip, which can help avoid any issues that may arise from miscommunication or misunderstandings.

A descriptive itinerary provides a more detailed account of the places to visit, activities to do, and sights to see during the trip. This helps travelers to visualize their itinerary and set realistic expectations. It also provides an opportunity to research and learn about the local culture and customs, which can enhance the overall travel experience.

Overall, itinerary planning and development is crucial for travelers to maximize their time, stay organized, and ensure a successful and enjoyable trip. A well-planned itinerary helps to minimize stress and allows travelers to focus on the experience and enjoyment of their trip.

How to Get to Boracay

Located off the northwestern coast of Aklan, Boracay Island is very accessible to the beach-going public. If you’re coming from Manila, flying is the best option. All major airlines fly to either of them.

Two airports serve Boracay-bound passengers. Each airport has pros and cons.

Caticlan Airport and Kalibo Airport

Caticlan Airport and Kalibo Airport

Kalibo is farther, requiring around 2 hours of travel time, but fares are much cheaper.

Caticlan is closer, 15 minutes away, but fares are way more expensive.

Whatever you choose, you need to make your way to the Caticlan Jetty Port. Here’s a brief step-by-step guide:

From Kalibo Airport, take the bus or van to Caticlan Jetty Port. Fare: P200.

If you’re a big group, you may also consider arranging a private van transfer that will wait for you at the airport. I have tried this pre-arranged setup before when I was part of a group of 10. Cost was P3800 roundtrip (P1900 one-way, or P180 per person per way).

Upon arrival at the jetty port, proceed to the booths near the entrance to the building and settle the Terminal Fee (P100), Environmental Fee (P75), and Ferry Fare (P25).

Inside the port building, register, proceed to the Passenger Waiting Area, and wait for boarding announcement.

Board the boat or ferry to the island.

You will dock at either Cagban or Tambisaan Port. Either way, there are tricycles waiting here that can take you to your hotel. If your hotel is in the White Beach Area, cost is P100 per ride.
