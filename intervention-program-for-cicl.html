3. summarize our subject course juvenile delinquency and crime prevention by using terminologies,with a minimum 50terms please include, juvenile delinquency RA 9344 intervention,diversion and CICL ​


Answer:

Juvenile delinquency is a term used to describe criminal acts committed by minors under the age of 18. The Republic Act 9344, also known as the Juvenile Justice and Welfare Act, is a Philippine law that outlines the intervention and rehabilitation of children in conflict with the law (CICL).

Intervention refers to the measures taken to address the root causes of juvenile delinquency, which may include family, school, or community-based interventions. Diversion programs aim to prevent juveniles from entering the formal justice system by providing alternative programs such as counseling, community service, or education.

CICL are children who are alleged as, accused of, or recognized as having committed an offense under Philippine law before the age of 18. The law recognizes that CICL are still in the developmental stage and therefore require special care and protection.

Crime prevention refers to measures taken to prevent crime from occurring. It includes situational crime prevention, which involves changing the physical environment to make it less conducive to criminal activity, and social crime prevention, which aims to address the root causes of crime through social and economic interventions.

Other relevant terms include restorative justice, which seeks to repair harm caused by the offense by involving the victim, offender, and community in the justice process. Recidivism refers to the tendency of offenders to re-offend after being released from prison or completing a sentence. Risk factors refer to characteristics or conditions that increase the likelihood of juvenile delinquency, such as poverty, drug abuse, or lack of parental supervision. Protective factors refer to characteristics or conditions that protect against juvenile delinquency, such as positive family relationships, strong social support, and access to education.

8. Directions Explain briefly Are these intervention programs useful to achieve the eight-millennium development goals of the United Nations Why?​?


Answer:

The eight Millennium Development Goals are a set of broad, global goals set by the UN to be reached by the turn of the century. The goals include such things as eradicating poverty and disease, ensuring universal education and literacy, and creating human capital to help the world's poorest countries. Some intervention programs, such as education and healthcare programs, are directly related to achieving the eight Millennium Development Goals. Other intervention programs, such as anti-poverty programs, are not typically specific to one of the eight Millennium Development Goals, but are instead aimed at improving the livelihoods of the poor. Whether an intervention program is useful to achieve the eight Millennium Development Goals is often a matter of debate. Some argue that many intervention programs are ineffective and can actually contribute to poverty and disease, while others argue that many intervention programs can be very effective if properly designed and implemented.

22. What are the issues in the horticulture industry in the Philippines? What government programs/interventions were implemented to address these issues? ​


Answer:

The issues in the horticulture industry in the Philippines include inadequate agricultural support, low access to credit, weak supply chain, limited access to modern farming technology, and pests and diseases. To address these issues, the government implemented various interventions, such as training farmers on good agricultural practices, providing seed and planting material grants, launching crop insurance programs, and building the infrastructure for crop marketing and market linkages.
