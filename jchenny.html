26. 2. Johnny can finish his GRIT module in 8 hours and Jenny can finish the same in 6 hours. How long would it take them both working together to finish their GRIT module?​


WORK PROBLEMS

Answer: Johnny and Jenny can finish the GRIT module in 3.43 hours when they work together.

Step-by-step explanation:

This word problem involves work. Recall the formula for work is:

W = rt

where: W is the work done, r is the rate of work and t is the time it takes to finish the work

Since we already have the formula, we need to identify and analyze the given information.

If Johnny works alone, he can finish the GRIT module in 8 hours.If Jenny works alone, he can finish the GRIT module in 6 hours.

Now the problem is we need to solve for the time it takes for both of them to finish the GRIT module, assuming that they work at the same rate. But first we need to find the hourly work rate of each of them.

Since Johnny can finish the GRIT module in 8 hours, his rate of work is equivalent to:

Johnny's rate = [tex]\frac{1}{8}[/tex]

This means that in one hour, Johnny can finish [tex]\frac{1}{8}[/tex] of the work.

On the other hand, since Jenny can finish the same module in 6 hours, her rate of work can be expressed as:

Jenny's rate = [tex]\frac{1}{6}[/tex]

This means that in one hour, Jenny can finish [tex]\frac{1}{6}[/tex] of the work.

The next step is to represent the unknown time both of them would take to finish the module, with the variable x.

Going back to the formula of work done which was introduced above. Now we will substitute the values to the formula.

[tex]W = rt\\W = (\frac{1}{8})(x)\\W = \frac{x}{8}[/tex]

So this is the work done by Johnny. Solving for Jenny's work done:

[tex]W = rt\\W = (\frac{1}{6})(x)\\W = \frac{x}{6}[/tex]

Now we can make an equation:

[tex]\frac{x}{8} + \frac{x}{6} = 1[/tex]

So since the GRIT module is only one, we will equate the sum of the work done by Johnny and Jenny. Now it is time to solve for the value of x.

[tex]\frac{x}{8}+\frac{x}{6} = 1\\24(\frac{x}{8}+\frac{x}{6} = 1)\\\frac{24x}{8}+\frac{24x}{6} = 24\\3x + 4x = 24\\7x = 24\\\frac{24x}{7}=\frac{24}{7} \\x=\frac{24}{7} \\x= 3.43[/tex]

In the second line, we need to find the Least Common Denominator or the LCD of 6 and 8, which is 24.

In the third line, we distributed 24 to the terms inside the equation.

In the fourth line, we simplify the equation.

In the fifth line, we added the like terms: 3x and 4x which give us the sum of 7x.

In the sixth line, we divide both sides by 7 to isolate x.

Therefore, Johnny and Jenny can finish working their GRIT module together in 24/7 hours or approximately 3.43 hours.
