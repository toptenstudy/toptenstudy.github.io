11. The bakery at Oaktown Grocery Store sells two types of bread, white and wheat. Last week, the store sold 4 loaves of white bread for every 5 loaves of wheat bread. If the store sold 117 loaves of bread in all last week, how many loaves of white bread did they sell?


Answer: The store sold 9 sets of 4 white loaves, or 36 white loaves in total.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let's use algebra to solve this problem.

Let x be the number of sets of 4 white loaves sold, and y be the number of sets of 5 wheat loaves sold.

We know that the store sold a total of 117 loaves, so we can write an equation:

[tex]\sf:\implies 4x + 5y = 117[/tex]

We want to find the number of white loaves sold, or 4x.

We can use the first equation to solve for one of the variables in terms of the other:

[tex]\sf:\implies 4x = 117 - 5y[/tex]

Now we can substitute this expression for 4x into the equation we want to solve:

[tex]\sf 4x = number\: of\: white\: loaves\: sold[/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies 4x = 117 - 5y[/tex]

So the number of white loaves sold is 117 - 5y.

To find y, we can use guess and check or trial and error. We know that y must be a multiple of 4, since 4 white loaves are sold for every 5 wheat loaves.

Trying y = 4, we get:

[tex]\sf:\implies 4x = 117 - 5(4) [/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies 4x = 97 [/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies x = 24.25 [/tex]

Since x must be a whole number, does this not work.

Trying y = 8, we get:

[tex]\sf:\implies 4x = 117 - 5(8) [/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies 4x = 77 [/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies x = 19.25 [/tex]

Again, this does not work.

Trying y = 12, we get:

[tex]\sf:\implies 4x = 117 - 5(12) [/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies 4x = 57 [/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies x = 14.25 [/tex]

This also does not work.

Trying y = 16, we get:

[tex]\sf:\implies 4x = 117 - 5(16) [/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies 4x = 37 [/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies x = 9.25 [/tex]

This works, since x is a whole number.

So the store sold 9 sets of 4 white loaves, or 36 white loaves in total.
