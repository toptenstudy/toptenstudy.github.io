10. explain your own ideas about arnis and disarming technique​


Answer:

Arnis(from the Spanish/Tagalog word for an armor harness) is the national martial art of the Philippines and closely related to the often interchangeably used terms Escrima (Skirmish in Spanish/Tagalog) and Kali(possibly from the Hindu deity Kali, or Calis, a word for sword from the pre-hispanic era in the Philippines, or from the ancient Indonesian word for fencing Tjakalele/Cakalele). It is mostly known for it's impressive fighting techniques and training methods with single baton(Solo Baston), double batons(Doble Baston), knife(Daga), sword(Kris/Bolo/Itak/Espada), staff(Bankaw), pocket stick(Dulo Dulo), chain(Cadena), or Sword and Dagger(Espada Y Daga), but also includes empty hand striking/kicking (Mano Mano/Panantukan/Suntukan), Grappling (Dumong/Buno). The skills are taught with a lot of commonality (Hubud/Lubud) regardless of whether the student is using stick, knife, or empty hand using an angle system, so the system can be learned quickly and used in sparring.
