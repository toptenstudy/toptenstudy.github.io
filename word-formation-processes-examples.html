Igneous, Sedimentary, Metamorphic Rocks

There are 3 types of rocks that make up the lithosphere, there are igneous rocks, sedimentary rocks, and metamorphic rocks

Igneous Rock

Igneous rock is formed from cooled and hardened magma, which is composed mostly of silica (SiO2). Igneous rocks can vary in terms of color, density, mineral composition and texture. The color difference that exists in igneous rocks is caused by the presence of minerals. Rocks that contain many colored minerals are called ultramafic, for example, peridotite, which forms the earth's mantle. Ordinary rock that is dark in color is called mafic, for example basalt rock. Furthermore, rocks that are light in color are called felsic, for example granite.

The difference in texture in igneous rocks is caused by differences in the cooling rate of magma. The rate of cooling of magma, depends on the location of the magma that can occur in the magma chamber, in the magma channel and on the earth's surface. Generally, the deeper the magma, the slower it cools so that the mineral crystals have enough time to grow before the magma hardens, and the rock will have a coarser texture. Here are three types of rocks that fall into the category of igneous rocks.

Types: intrusive, extrusive and hypabyssal

Examples: basalt, granite, rhyolite

Process of formation: solidification and crystallization

Sedimentary Rock

Sedimentary rocks are formed from deposits of rock structures that are easily separated and carried by water, wind, and ice. Over time, these deposits will accumulate and harden, then form into rock. Therefore, the name of the rock is sedimentary rock.

Types: clastic, organic and chemical

Examples: sandstone, limestone

Process of formation: deposition and lithification

Metamorphic Rock

Metamorphic rock is the result of changes from igneous rock and sedimentary rock that occurs as a result of the metamorphosis process. Factors causing rock changes include high pressure, high temperature,  combination of high temperature and pressure and also adding other ingredients.

Types: contact and Regional

Examples: marble, schist, gneiss

Process of formation: metamorphism
