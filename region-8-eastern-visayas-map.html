7. mga bayan sa lalawigan ng Region 8 Eastern visayas​


Answer:

Eastern Visayas (Waray: Sinirangan Kabisay-an; Cebuano: Sidlakang Kabisay-an; Tagalog: Silangang Kabisayaan or Silangang Visayas) is an administrative region in the Philippines, designated as Region VIII. It consists of three main islands, Samar, Leyte and Biliran. The region has six provinces, one independent city and one highly urbanized city [3] namely, Biliran, Leyte, Northern Samar, Samar, Eastern Samar, Southern Leyte, Ormoc and Tacloban. The highly urbanized city of Tacloban is the sole regional center. These provinces and cities occupy the easternmost islands of the Visayas group of islands.

Answer:

The art forms of the people in the Central and Eastern Visayas region of the Philippines are diverse and reflect the cultural traditions of the various ethnic groups that inhabit the region. Some common themes in the art of this region include the use of natural materials, the depiction of animals and nature, and the incorporation of spiritual or religious elements.

One feature that is common in many of the art forms of the Central and Eastern Visayas region is the use of bright, bold colors. Many of the traditional art forms of this region, such as the dye-resist Ikat textiles of the T'boli people, the intricate beadwork of the Mamanwa people, and the colorful basketry of the Mandaya people, feature bright, vibrant colors that are achieved through the use of natural dyes.

Another common feature in the art of this region is the use of geometric shapes and patterns. Many of the traditional art forms of the Central and Eastern Visayas region, such as the woodcarvings of the Maranao people and the weavings of the Kalinga people, feature intricate geometric patterns and designs.

Overall, the art forms of the Central and Eastern Visayas region are characterized by the use of bold, bright colors and geometric patterns and shapes, as well as the incorporation of spiritual and cultural themes.
