4. In most cases, sexual reproduction requires the involvement of both a male and a female individual.

5. Sexual reproduction typically involves the production of specialized reproductive cells, called gametes, which are specifically adapted for fertilization.

6. Gametes are typically produced by a specialized type of cell division called meiosis, which reduces the number of chromosomes in the gametes by half.

7. Fertilization, the process by which gametes fuse to form a new organism, typically occurs in the female's reproductive tract.

8. After fertilization, the resulting zygote undergoes several rounds of cell division to form a multicellular organism.

9. In some species, sexual reproduction may involve complex courtship behaviors and mating rituals.

10. Sexual reproduction is a common form of reproduction in many organisms, including animals, plants, and fungi.

ASEXUAL REPRODUCTION

1. Asexual reproduction involves the production of offspring by a single individual, without the fusion of gametes.

2. Asexual reproduction results in the formation of genetically identical offspring, as the offspring are produced from a single parent and do not involve the combination of genetic material from two individuals.

3. Asexual reproduction does not require the involvement of two individuals, as it can be carried out by a single individual.

4. Asexual reproduction typically does not involve the production of specialized reproductive cells, such as eggs or sperm.

5. Asexual reproduction often occurs through processes such as cell division, budding, or fragmentation.

6. In some cases, asexual reproduction may involve the production of spores, which are specialized cells that can develop into new individuals.

7. Asexual reproduction can be a relatively rapid form of reproduction, as it does not require the time and energy involved in finding and mating with a mate.

8. Asexual reproduction may be advantageous in certain environments or situations, such as when a population is isolated or conditions are unfavourable for sexual reproduction.

9. Asexual reproduction can lead to the rapid growth of a population, as a single individual can produce many offspring in a short period of time.

27. David scored x marks in a Mathematics quiz and his classmates' scores were 5, 16, 6, 10 and 4. If the mean mark of these six students is 10, (a) Find the values of x and the standard deviation. (b) How did David fare in the quiz among his classmates?​


Step-by-step explanation:

To find the value of x, we can use the formula for the mean of a set of numbers:

Mean = (sum of all numbers) / (number of numbers)

We know that the mean of the six students' scores is 10, so we can set up the equation:

10 = (x + 5 + 16 + 6 + 10 + 4) / 6

Simplifying the right side, we get:

10 = (x + 41) / 6

Multiplying both sides by 6, we get:

60 = x + 41

Subtracting 41 from both sides, we get:

x = 19

So David scored 19 marks in the Mathematics quiz.

To find the standard deviation, we can use the formula:

Standard deviation = sqrt(sum of squared differences from the mean / number of numbers)

First, we need to find the differences of each score from the mean, square them, and add them up:

(5 - 10)^2 + (16 - 10)^2 + (6 - 10)^2 + (10 - 10)^2 + (4 - 10)^2 + (19 - 10)^2 = 306

Next, we divide this sum by the number of numbers (6) to get the mean of the squared differences:

306 / 6 = 51

Finally, we take the square root of the mean of the squared differences to get the standard deviation:

sqrt(51) ≈ 7.14

So the standard deviation is approximately 7.14.

(b)

To determine how David fared in the quiz among his classmates, we can compare his score to the other scores. David's score of 19 is higher than all of his classmates' scores, which were 5, 16, 6, 10, and 4. Therefore, David performed the best in the quiz among his classmates.
