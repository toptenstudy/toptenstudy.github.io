3. . 1. A box is pushed along the floor by a force of 40N, making an angle of 450 with the horizontal. Find the horizontal (Fx) and the vertical (Ey) components of the force.​


Answer:

To find the horizontal (Fx) and vertical (Ey) components of the force, we can use the following formulas:

Fx = F * cos(theta)

Ey = F * sin(theta)

where F is the force, theta is the angle between the force and the horizontal (in this case, 45 degrees), and Fx and Ey are the horizontal and vertical components of the force, respectively.

Therefore,

Fx = 40N * cos(45) = 28.28N [horizontal component]

Ey = 40N * sin(45) = 28.28N [vertical component]

So the horizontal component of the force is 28.28N and the vertical component of the force is 28.28N.

6. what is the acceleration of 20kg cart if the net force on it is 40N?


Answer:

To find the acceleration of the cart, we can use Newton's second law of motion, which states that the acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass. The formula is:

acceleration = net force / mass

In this case, the net force on the cart is 40 N, and the mass of the cart is 20 kg. Plugging these values into the formula, we get:

acceleration = 40 N / 20 kg

acceleration = 2 m/s^2

Therefore, the acceleration of the cart is 2 m/s^2.

30. A 5 kg block is pulled across a table by a horizontal force of 40N with frictional force of 8N opposing the motion.Calculate the acceleration of the object


Given:
m = 5kg
F = 40N
Opposing force = 8N

If we can recall F=ma, we can easily calculate the acceleration by substituting the given variables; but in our case, we have an opposing force. So we have to subtract the opposing force from the horizontal force.
F = ma
40N - 8N = 5kg x a
32N = 5kg x a ; divide both sides by 5kg for a to be a sole variable
(32 kg m/s^2) / 5kg = a ; (Newtons are sometimes referred to as kg m/s^2)
6.4 m/s^2 = a ; (kg's are cancelled)

Therefore, our acceleration is 6.4 m/s^2.
