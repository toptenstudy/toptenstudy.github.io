13. How to write a reconsideration email for college plm?


Answer:

1.Address the recipient in a formal manner. Explain the purpose of your letter, and mention your previous request. Explain the reasons behind the rejection or the unfavorable decision you would like to be reconsidered. Ask for a reconsideration of the company’s position.

2.Explain the dispute in detail. List the company’s reasons and arguments for issuing the rejection. Stay concise, clear, and focused, while keeping your emotions out of the matter.

3.List your arguments as to why the establishment’s decision should be reconsidered. Include facts you think are needed to legitimize your appeal. Mention specific times and dates when particular events happened.

4.Add additional evidence or facts that would speak in your favor in this particular case. These might be testimonials from reliable people you know from work, medical certificates, transcripts of records or receipts, and so on.

5.In your conclusion, restate your position and briefly summarize your arguments. Add your contact information, and end by thanking the recipient for their time.

6.Proofread your letter; check all the information for validity, possible mistakes, and inaccuracies.

Teachers and students apply strict health protocols by being obliged to wear masks. When entering the gate, students are required to wash their hands and check body temperature and pass through the designated entry and exit directions.

Students are also advised to keep their distance, use hand sanitizer after handling items, are not allowed to borrow stationery from each other, and are not allowed to leave the room during breaks.

We have been preparing for face-to-face learning for a long time. However, because of our district the number of cases affected by COVID-19 has increased. So face-to-face learning activities are postponed again.

About a month and a half ago, our school implemented a face-to-face simulation of activities by implementing health protocols. Starting from the arrival of students who must pass officers to check body temperature, wash hands before entering class, wear masks. Keep your distance and so on.

However, with various considerations, especially because the pandemic conditions are not yet safe, face-to-face activities have been postponed again. And a few days ago, our school received a notification that in the new school year there will be face-to-face learning. But not all schools can carry out this face-to-face activity. Only schools appointed by the local government are allowed to do so.
