16. Priorities in life during covid 19​


Panic about the Covid-19 coronavirus outbreak occurs in almost every country. The virus that has spread in 118 countries has been the talk of the town since late December 2019 until now.

But behind the outbreak and feelings of panic, there are lessons or positive things that we can learn. 4 Priorities in life during covid 19​:

1. Time with family

During this pandemic, many workers have been laid off due to staff reductions to maintain economic stability and break the chain of transmission of Covid 19.  Students and students are now learning online. Of course, this causes learning patterns to be 180 degrees different from face-to-face schools. Parents also have difficulties as my aunt experienced when her elementary school child studied online. It is not the child who learns, but the mother who does the assignments while the child is busy playing games on the smartphone.

But we can take the positives and this is where we can spend more time with family. Those of us who are still students have more time to help our parents with their work. Those of us who used to gather only for breakfast can now gather for lunch, dinner, or just talk and joke every day. Not just on weekends.

2. Independent and creative

We can be more independent again, because now is a difficult time, everything is difficult, especially the economy and social relationships. We must be confused about how to keep earning income during this pandemic. The thing that comes to mind is 'how can I get an income without distressing my parents? One of the things we can do is utilize social media to sell online.

Many have discovered their new talents and hobbies during this pandemic, such as cooking because they have often seen cooking content on social media and then tried to practice it. Then we can also sell the food we make. those whose hobbies are drawing and writing can also take advantage of their time between learning activities to express their work, there are even websites that can pay for your paintings. So we can be independent and show our creativity while being able to produce something with our efforts.

3. Learn to save money

Consciously or unconsciously during Covid -19, we became more frugal because the large-scale social restrictions (PSBB) that have been implemented by the government make us stay at home and not leave if there is nothing very important. In this way, we can save money that we usually use for traveling or hanging out with friends and use it for more useful things.

There are many online stores now. That makes it easier for us to shop so it will be more wasteful. Yes, many online stores sell various products, even new and unique products that make us want to buy. Even the price of products in online stores is cheaper than buying directly from the store. You can choose to shop at online stores that provide goods at these lower prices.

4. Maintain health

The current situation is very worrying because the transmission of the virus is very fast and can attack anyone. People affected by the virus can be asymptomatic and when tested it turns out that they are positive for the Coronavirus. Especially now that people seem to be tired of this situation and act as if there is no longer a virus, there are still many who go out, hang out, and public transportation is now crowded and there are still those who do not comply with health protocols.

Explanation:

Parallelism is a literary device that involves using similar grammatical structures or repeating words and phrases in a pattern. In this poem, the parallel structure is the repetition of the phrase "I" + verb at the beginning of each line. This creates a sense of balance and rhythm in the poem, emphasizing the importance of balancing different priorities in life.
