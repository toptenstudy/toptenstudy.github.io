Marxist Analysis of "Hitting Budapest"

Write a critical analysis of the story "Hitting Budapest" using Marxist Criticism.

Marxist criticism is a way of interpreting literature that focuses on the economic, social, and political context in which the work was produced. In this approach, literature is seen as a reflection of the society in which it was created and as a means of exploring and critiquing social, economic, and political systems.

"Hitting Budapest" is a short story by Ambedkar that tells the story of a group of men who travel to Budapest on a business trip. The story is set in India, where the men are from, and follows their experiences as they explore the city and interact with the locals.

From a Marxist perspective, the story can be analyzed in terms of the economic and social context in which it was written. For example, the fact that the men are able to afford a business trip to Budapest suggests that they are part of the upper or middle class in India. This class status is likely to have shaped their experiences and perceptions of the city, as well as the way in which they are treated by the locals.

The story also touches on issues of power and inequality. The men are tourists, and as such, they are outsiders who are able to observe and interact with the local culture but are not fully integrated into it. This creates a power dynamic in which the men hold a certain level of privilege and authority over the locals, and the locals are depicted as being somewhat subservient to the tourists.

Overall, "Hitting Budapest" can be seen as a reflection of the social and economic divides that exist within India, as well as the power dynamics that shape interactions between different classes and cultures. By exploring these issues, the story invites readers to consider the ways in which these divides and power dynamics shape our experiences and perceptions of the world around us.

20. what is the feminist criticism of hitting budapest​


Answer:

Feminist criticism of the practice of “hitting Budapest” (i.e. visiting the city in search of sexual encounters with women) is that it objectifies women, commodifies their bodies, and encourages the perpetuation of gender-based stereotypes and power dynamics. It also perpetuates the idea that women are nothing more than sexual objects, and that they should be treated as such. This can be damaging to both men and women, as it reinforces damaging gender roles and expectations. Additionally, this type of “tourism” often exploits women who are economically vulnerable and in need of money.
