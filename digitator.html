[tex] \large \bold{\blue{PROBLEM:}} [/tex] It is a 9- digit number. The first digit is 6 and the last digit is 7. It has a digit with a value of 400 000 and another digit with a value of 800. The rest of the digits are zero.

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{SOLUTION:}} [/tex] Since it is a 9-digit number, then it reached the hundred-millions. Let us compile the clues so that we can identify the number.

[tex] \: [/tex]

• "The first digit is 6 and the last digit is 7."

Indicating that the face value of the hundred-millions is 6 and the face value of ones is 7.

[tex] \: [/tex]

• "It has a digit with a value of 400,000 and another digit with a value of 800."

Indicating that the hundred thousands has a face value of 4 and the hundreds place has a face value of 8.

[tex] \: [/tex]

• "The rest of the digits are zero."

Indicating that all the digits that wasn't mentioned will be zero.

[tex] \: [/tex]

» Thus, the 9-digit number we were finding was:

[tex] \:\:\: \Large \underline{\boxed{\tt \purple{600,\!400,\!807}}} [/tex]

18. In a two-digit number, the tens digit is twice the ones digit. The difference of the ones digit and half the tens digit is 0. An equation created to find the digit in the ones place will have .


Answer:

b = (1/2)a

Step-by-step explanation:

Let us represent the number as ab with a as the tens digit and b as the ones digit. From the given, we will get the following equations,

a = 2b

b - (1/2)a  = 0  or simply b = (1/2)a

Looking at it closely, the two equations are actually identical as when you substitute one value for another,

a = 2[(1/2)(a)

a = a

There is no additional information given. So the equation to find the ones place is b = (1/2)a

25. The sum of a four digit number is 20, if the tens digit is 2 less than the ones digit, the thousands digit exceeds ones digit by 1 and the hundreds digit is 1 less than the tens digit. find the number. ​


Answer:

Let [tex]x[/tex] be the ones digit, and express the other digits in terms of [tex]x[/tex].

tens digit = [tex]x-2[/tex]

hundreds digit = [tex](x-2)-1=x-3[/tex]

thousands digit = [tex]x+1[/tex]

Then, use their sum to solve for x

[tex]x+(x-2)+(x-3)+(x+1)=20[/tex]

[tex]4x-4=20[/tex]

[tex]x=\frac{20+4}{4} =6[/tex]

tens digit = [tex]x-2=4[/tex]

hundreds digit = [tex]x-3=3[/tex]

thousands digit = [tex]x+1=7[/tex]

Therefore, the answer is 7346.
