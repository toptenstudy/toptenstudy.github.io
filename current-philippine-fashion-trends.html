Answer:

Once upon a time, there was a businessman in the Philippines named Juan. Juan owned a small manufacturing company that produced plastic toys for children. He was known for his cheap prices and quick turnaround times, which made him very popular among local retailers.

However, one day, a news report surfaced, exposing the unethical practices of Juan's company. The report showed that his company was dumping toxic waste into nearby rivers, which was causing harm to the environment and nearby communities.

Juan was shocked when he saw the report. He had always believed in the importance of being a responsible corporate citizen, but he had never paid much attention to the impact of his business on the environment.

After reading the report, Juan decided to take action. He immediately halted all production at his company and called for a meeting with his employees. At the meeting, Juan explained the situation and asked his employees for their thoughts on how to rectify the issue.

Together, Juan and his employees came up with a plan to clean up the waste and reduce the company's environmental impact. They installed waste management systems, hired an environmental consultant, and began using biodegradable materials in their products.

Juan's efforts did not go unnoticed. Customers and retailers alike praised his company for taking responsibility and making a positive change. As a result, Juan's business grew even more successful, as more and more people recognized his commitment to ethical business practices.

In the end, Juan learned that being a responsible corporate citizen wasn't just the right thing to do - it was also good for business. By taking action to address ethical issues, he was able to earn the trust and loyalty of his customers and contribute to the betterment of his community.

20. Compare and contrast philippine folk dance and current dance trends


Answer:

Philippine folk dance and current dance trends are two different styles of dance that have their own unique characteristics and qualities.

Explanation:

Origin and History: Philippine folk dance has its roots in the country's indigenous culture and traditions. These dances have been passed down from generation to generation and are deeply ingrained in the country's cultural heritage. Current dance trends, on the other hand, are often influenced by popular culture, music videos, and social media trends.

Music: Philippine folk dance is often accompanied by traditional music such as the "kulintang" and "bamboo poles." Current dance trends, on the other hand, are often accompanied by contemporary pop music or electronic beats.

Costumes: Philippine folk dance costumes are often colorful and intricate, reflecting the traditional attire of different regions in the country. In contrast, current dance trends often involve casual or trendy clothing, such as streetwear or athletic wear.

Movements: Philippine folk dance movements are often precise and coordinated, with a focus on hand gestures and footwork. Current dance trends, on the other hand, often involve more fluid and exaggerated movements that are meant to be visually impressive and attention-grabbing.

Purpose: Philippine folk dance is often performed to celebrate cultural events or religious ceremonies, and are often used to showcase the country's traditional values and customs. Current dance trends, on the other hand, are often performed for entertainment or to express individual creativity.

Despite these differences, both Philippine folk dance and current dance trends can be enjoyed and appreciated for their respective styles and qualities.

27. What do you think is the current trend of Philippine Visual Arts and Sculpture?


Answer:

Gone are the days of “practicality over passion,” which have been preventing many artists, here and abroad, from pursuing a career in the arts. Along with the dawn of a new decade, expect new and exciting creative and business opportunities in the global art scene.

Below, we have listed some of the visual art trends we’re all excited about:

High Tech Art

As the world progresses, technology will always come to play when trying to capture a massive chunk of the appreciative art audience. From 3D films to holographic installations, you can make the engagement with the audience more interactive. Installing 3D VR technology in museums and art galleries, for instance, could attract both new and returning visitors.

Local Art

Art enthusiasts across the globe have been enthralled by the underrated beauty of the art created by native Filipinos, too. The brilliant works of artists Nona Garcia, Andres Barrioquinto, and Leeroy New, among others, have paved the way for Filipino Art to reach the art lovers beyond the Philippines. Expect more and more Filipino artists to hog the headlines this new decade.

Minimalist Art

The idea of a simplistic outlook in life has impacted the Millennials and Generation Z’s in the last few years. This might be due to the rising costs of living in many countries, along with their national government’s inconsistent economic policies. To keep the young minds’ sanity, they have been favoring the beauty of simplistic daily living, including their choice of minimalist art. Leading the pack of the young people’s favorite minimalist artworks are those of Jiro Yoshihara, Frank Stella, Imi Knoebel, and Dan Flavin.

Green Art

The new generation is fighting for Mother Earth. Scout around and notice all sorts of campaign materials about saving the environment and demanding for eco-friendly policies from the government. Advocating for environmental change through art, such as the “plastic whale art installation” made by Greenpeace Philippines that went viral in 2017, is not going anywhere, anytime soon.
