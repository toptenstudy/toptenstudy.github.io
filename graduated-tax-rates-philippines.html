Explanation:

The policy of taxation in the Philippines is governed chiefly by the Constitution of the Philippines and three Republic Acts.

Constitution: Article VI, Section 28 of the Constitution states that "the rule of taxation shall be uniform and equitable" and that "Congress shall evolve a progressive system of taxation".[1]

national law: National Internal Revenue Code—enacted as Republic Act No. 8424 or the Tax Reform Act of 1997[2] and subsequent laws amending it; the law was most recently amended by Republic Act No. 10963 or the Tax Reform for Acceleration and Inclusion Act;[3] and,

local laws: major sources of revenue for the local government units (LGUs) are the taxes collected by virtue of Republic Act No. 7160 or the Local Government Code of 1991,[4] and those sourced from the proceeds collected by virtue of a local ordinance.

Taxes imposed at the national level are collected by the Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR), while those imposed at the local level (i.e., provincial, city, municipal, barangay) are collected by a local treasurer's office.

1) To compute for the commission, we need to multiply the sales amount by the commission rate.

Commission = Sales × Commission Rate
Commission = $600 × 0.05
Commission = $30

Therefore, the commission for the first transaction is $30.

2) To compute for the sales tax, we need to multiply the price of the washing machine by the tax rate.

Sales Tax = Price × Tax Rate
Sales Tax = $9,250 × 0.71
Sales Tax = $6,567.50

To compute for the total cost, we need to add the price and the sales tax.

Total Cost = Price + Sales Tax
Total Cost = $9,250 + $6,567.50 × 100
Total Cost = $15,817.50

Therefore, the sales tax for the second transaction is $6,567.50, and the total cost is $15,817.50.

3) To compute for the tax rate, we need to divide the commission by the sales amount.

Commission Rate = Commission / Sales
Commission Rate = $79 / $790
Commission Rate = 0.1 or 10%

Therefore, the tax rate is 10%.
