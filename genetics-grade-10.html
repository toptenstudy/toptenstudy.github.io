9. why the application of genetic engineering agriculture and medicine is considered as biotechnology minimum 10 sentence​


Genetic engineering involves the manipulation of genetic material to create desired traits in living organisms, which is a key aspect of biotechnology.

Agriculture and medicine are two major fields where genetic engineering is widely applied, making them important areas of biotechnology.

In agriculture, genetic engineering is used to create crops that are resistant to pests, diseases, and environmental stressors, resulting in increased yields and improved food security.

Genetic engineering is also used in agriculture to create crops that have enhanced nutritional value or longer shelf life, which can benefit both farmers and consumers.

In medicine, genetic engineering is used to develop new treatments for genetic disorders, such as cystic fibrosis and sickle cell anemia.

Genetic engineering is also used to create new vaccines and therapies for diseases like cancer and HIV/AIDS.

Biotechnology companies are heavily involved in the development and commercialization of these new treatments, making biotechnology an important part of the healthcare industry.

Genetic engineering is also used to create genetically modified organisms (GMOs), which are organisms that have been modified in a laboratory to have specific traits.

GMOs are widely used in agriculture and have been a topic of controversy due to concerns about their safety and potential impact on the environment.

Overall, genetic engineering has revolutionized agriculture and medicine, making biotechnology a critical field that has the potential to improve human health and well-being, as well as address global challenges such as food security and climate change.

11. what is 10 advantage and disadvantage of genetic engineering and GMOs​


Answer:

Genetically modified organisms or GMOs are a product of a form of scientific farming, where crops are administered with chemicals to increase their sizes and yields. It is becoming more and more common in many types of food we see every day. But due to its concept, it has been a topic in heated debates around the world, with proponents and opponents pushing their arguments. Here are the critical GMOs advantages and disadvantages:

List of Advantages of GMOs

1. They offer more useful knowledge for genetics.

The mapping of GMO genetic material has increased knowledge about genetic alterations and paved the way for the enhancement of genes in crops to make them more beneficial in terms of production and human consumption. For instance, crops can be engineered to resist unfavorable temperatures or produce higher yields, thus providing a greater level of genetic diversity in regions where climate hinders productivity.

2. They allow for more profit.

GMOs have become a very efficient means for farmers to make larger profits, as it allows them to spend less time on resources. It is also economically efficient in a way that these organisms are designed to be pest-resistant, eliminating the need to use pesticides, which means more savings.

3. They add more value to crops.

Another great reason to grow GMO crops is that these plants will get added nutritional value, especially among those that previously lack the necessary vitamins and minerals. As there are regions around the world that heavily rely on rice or corn, plant genes can be added to such crops to boost their nutritional value, which is particularly helpful in malnourished populations. And because GMOs are able to resist pests and other plant diseases, crop yields will be increased without the farmers having to use pesticides.

4. They are known to decrease the prices of food.

Advanced crops and higher yields certainly mean lower costs, which are a benefit that can be passed to the consumers in the form of cheaper food products. This will be very helpful to families who cannot afford to buy supplies for everyday consumption. This also means that starvation will be prevented.

5. They yield products that are found to be safe.

After precise testing and evaluation of GMOs and other related products, they are found to be safe for human consumption. In fact, study shows that they are even safer compared to traditional crops.

29. GIVE 10 GENETIC DISORDERS AND DESCRIBE EACH.​


Answer:

Down syndrome: A genetic disorder caused by the presence of an extra chromosome 21, which leads to physical and intellectual disability, as well as increased risk of certain medical conditions.

Cystic fibrosis: A genetic disorder that affects the lungs, pancreas, and other organs, causing thick, sticky mucus to build up, leading to lung infections and digestive problems.

Huntington's disease: A genetic disorder that affects the brain, causing progressive and irreversible damage, leading to movement disorders, cognitive decline, and psychiatric symptoms.

Sickle cell anemia: A genetic disorder that affects the red blood cells, causing them to become misshapen and break down more easily, leading to anemia and other medical complications.

Hemophilia: A genetic disorder that affects blood clotting, causing excessive bleeding and bruising, even from minor injuries.

Duchenne muscular dystrophy: A genetic disorder that affects muscle function, leading to progressive muscle weakness and wasting, usually beginning in childhood.

Tay-Sachs disease: A genetic disorder that affects the nervous system, causing progressive deterioration of brain function, leading to blindness, deafness, paralysis, and death, usually in early childhood.

Phenylketonuria (PKU): A genetic disorder that affects the body's ability to process an amino acid called phenylalanine, leading to intellectual disability, seizures, and other medical problems if left untreated.

Turner syndrome: A genetic disorder caused by the absence or partial absence of one of the X chromosomes, leading to short stature, infertility, and other medical problems.

Fragile X syndrome: A genetic disorder caused by a mutation in the FMR1 gene, leading to intellectual disability, autism spectrum disorder, and other medical and behavioral problems.
