Solution (Number of Moles of Solute):

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (in L)}}[/tex]

moles of solute = molarity × volume of solution (in L)

moles of solute = 3.8 × [tex]10^{-5}[/tex] M × 0.1000 L

[tex]\boxed{\text{moles of solute} = 3.8 × 10^{-6} \: \text{mol}}[/tex]

Solution (Mass of Solute):

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of solute.

molar mass = (22.99 g/mol × 1) + (12.01 g/mol × 1) + (14.01 g/mol × 1)

molar mass = 49.01 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the mass of solute.

[tex]\text{mass of solute} = 3.8 × 10^{-5} \: \text{mol NaCN} × \frac{\text{49.01 g NaCN}}{\text{1 mol NaCN}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of solute = 0.000186 g}}[/tex]

27. Given that the ka of a monoprotic acid ha is 3.20 x 10-7, and the acid ha concentration is 0.751 m find the ph of the acid


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Summarize the concentrations using the ICE table.

[tex]\begin{array}{lccccc} & \text{HA}(aq) & \rightleftharpoons & \text{H}^+(aq) & + & \text{A}^-(aq) \\ \text{Initial} \: (M): & 0.751 & & 0 & & 0 \\ \text{Change} \: (M): & -x & & +x & & +x \\ \hline \text{Equilibrium} \: (M): & 0.751-x & & x & & x \\ \end{array}[/tex]

Step 2: Write the Ka expression.

[tex]K_{\text{a}} = \dfrac{[\text{H}^+][\text{A}^-]}{[\text{HA}]}[/tex]

Step 3: Substitute the given values to the Ka expression.

[tex]\begin{aligned} 3.20 \times 10^{-7} & = \frac{(x)(x)}{0.751 - x} \\ 3.20 \times 10^{-7} & = \frac{x^2}{0.751 - x} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 4: Apply approximation method to solve for the hydrogen ion concentration at equilibrium.

Note: 0.751 - x ≈ 0.751

[tex]\begin{aligned} 3.20 \times 10^{-7} & = \frac{x^2}{0.751} \\ x^2 & = (3.20 \times 10^{-7})(0.751) \\ x^2 & = 2.4032 \times 10^{-7} \\ x & = \sqrt{2.4032 \times 10^{-7}} \\ x & = 4.90 \times 10^{-4} \: M\end{aligned}[/tex]

Testing the approximation

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{value} & = \frac{[\text{H}^+]}{[\text{HA}]_{\text{initial}}} \times 100\% \\ & = \frac{4.90 \times 10^{-4} \: M}{0.751 \: M} \times 100\% \\ & = 0.0652\% \end{aligned}[/tex]

Since the value is less than 5%, the approximation is valid. Therefore,

[tex][\text{H}^+] = x = 4.90 \times 10^{-4} \: M[/tex]

Step 5: Calculate the pH of the solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{pH} & = -\text{log} \: [\text{H}^+] \\ & = -\text{log} \: (4.90 \times 10^{-4}) \\ & = \boxed{3.310} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the pH of the acid is 3.310.

30. what is the ph of 0.01 M NaOH​


Answer:

So, a 0.01M NaOH solution will have a pH of 12.

First off, since NaOH is a strong base, it will dissociate completely into Na+ and OH-. Thus, we know that we have 0.01 M OH-.

However, we do not know anything about the concentration of H+. Fortunately, we do not need to, as pH + pOH = 14. So, if we find pOH, we can solve for pH. p is a mathematical function equivalent to -log. So, pH actually means -log[H+] (Note that brackets indicate concentration of).

pOH = -log 0.01M OH-

pOH = 2

pH + 2 = 14

pH = 12

This result makes sense, since a solution of strong base should have a high pH.
