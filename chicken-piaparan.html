Given:

ratio of ducks to chicken in the farm is 3 : 5

ducks = 72

Solution: (determining the number of chickens or x)

[tex]3 : 5 = 72 : x\\ (3)(x) = (5)(72)\\ 3x = 360 \\\frac{3x}{3}  = \frac{360}{3} \\ x = 120[/tex])

New number of chickens

[tex]120 - 6 = 114[/tex]


Getting the new ratio of "ducks to chickens"

[tex]72 : 114[/tex]

[tex]\frac{72}{6} : \frac{114}{6} \\ 12 :19[/tex]

30. In a farm the number of chickrns were 100% morebthan the cows. If there were 640 legs altogether,how many chickens were there?


Answer:

160 chickens

Step-by-step explanation:

Let x = no. of chickens y = no. of cow

It is said that chickens are 100% more than cow (that means that the number of chickens is double the number of cows). Thus,

x = 2y (in terms of y, we have y = x/2)

We know that a chicken has 2 legs and a cow has 4. Thus, we can say,

chicken legs + cow legs = 640 legs

which is,

2x + 4(x/2) = 640 2x + 2x = 640 4x = 640 x = 160 Therefore, there are 160 chickens in the farm.
