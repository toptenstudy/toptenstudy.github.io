Maria is a recent college graduate who has been offered a job at a marketing firm. After her successful interview, the HR representative sends her an employment contract to review and sign. Maria carefully reads through the contract and notices a few clauses that she has questions about. She contacts the HR representative and they have a conversation about the clauses, clarifying any misunderstandings. Maria is satisfied with the responses and decides to sign the contract.

Maria brings this to the attention of her supervisor, who investigates and realizes that the employee handbook needs to be updated to reflect the terms of the employment contract.

Answer:

The Spanish colonial period in the Philippines, as the Captaincy General of the Philippines from 1565 to 1898, was the period during which the Philippines were part of the Spanish Empire.

The islands were part of the larger East Indies of Spain. Forty-four years after Ferdinand Magellan landed in the Philippines and died in the 1521 Battle of Mactan, the islands were explored and colonized by the Spanish, beginning with Miguel Lopez de Legazpi's founding of Cebu in 1565. In 1571, Manila was made the capital of the Philippines.

This was the time of King Philip II of Spain's reign, whose name remained attached to the property. The Spanish colonial era ended in 1898 with the Philippine Revolution and the Spanish-American War, which marked the start of the Philippines' American colonialization.
