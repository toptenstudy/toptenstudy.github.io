18. _____7. Describes the blood donation in the countrya. National Blood Services Acta. Cybercrime Prevention Actc. Traditional and Alternative Medicine d. Philippine AIDS Prevention and Control Act ​


Answer:

A

NATIONAL BLOOD SERVICES ACT

Republic Act No. 7719, otherwise called the National Blood Services Act of 1994, elevates intentional blood gift to give adequate stockpile of safe blood and to control blood donation centers. This act expects to teach public mindfulness that blood gift is a compassionate demonstration.

The National Voluntary Blood Services Program (NVBSP) of the Department of Health is focusing on the adolescent as volunteers in its blood gift program this year. As per RA No. 7719, it intends to make public awareness on the significance of blood gift in saving the existences of millions of Filipinos.

Mission:

Blood Safety

Blood Adequacy

Sane Blood Use

Proficiency of Blood Services

Objectives:

The National Voluntary Blood Services Program (NVBSP) expects to accomplish the accompanying:

1. Improvement of a completely intentional blood gift framework;

2. Fortifying of a broadly planned organization of BSF to expand productivity by concentrated testing and handling of blood;

3. Execution of a quality administration framework including of Good Manufacturing Practice GMP and Management Information System (MIS);

4. Fulfillment of greatest use of blood through judicious utilization of blood items and part treatment; and

5. Improvement of a sound, feasible maintainable administration and subsidizing for the broadly planned blood organization.

25. Is organ donation ethical? Is organ donation a social issue? Justify your answer.


Yes, Because


The demand for solid organs far exceeds the number of organs available from deceased donors. Not surprisingly, instead of facing years on the transplantation waiting list, some patients, often with the encouragement of transplantation teams, seek to identify relatives or others who would be willing to donate the needed organ or partial organ directly or, in some cases, through a donor exchange program. Still other donors offer a kidney or a partial organ to patients whose stories have become known to them, perhaps through the media, whereas still others make a nondirected donation of a kidney to the transplantation system for use by any patient who needs it.
