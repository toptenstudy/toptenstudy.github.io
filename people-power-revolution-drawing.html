People Power Revolution

(22 Feb 1986 – 25 Feb 1986)

The People Power Revolution, also known as the EDSA Revolution or the February Revolution, was a series of popular demonstrations in the Philippines, mostly in Metro Manila, from February 22–25, 1986. There was a sustained campaign of civil resistance against regime violence and electoral fraud.

Dates: 22 Feb 1986 – 25 Feb 1986

Goals: Removal of Marcos from power; Installation of Aquino as President

Location: Philippines, primarily Epifanio de los Santos Avenue, Metro Manila

21. What caused the People Power Revolution?


Answer:

On February 22-25, 1986 a series of demonstration, mainly in Manila, brought millions of people to the streets to protest against the regime of President Ferdinand Marcos. The protests were caused by the murder of Benigno Aquino Jr., fraudulent elections, and decades of authoritarian and repressive regime. The mostly non-violent People's Revolution resulted in the ousting of Marcos and a reform to relative democracy. People Power had a ripple effect throughout the region and still inspires human rights defenders today.
