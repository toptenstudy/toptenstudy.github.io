4. Collagen and elastic fibers are common in the epidermis of the skin. True or False


False, collagen and elastic fibers are not commonly found in the epidermis but in the dermis layer of the skin.

Detailed explanation:

The main layers of the skin consist of:

1. Epidermis

This layer is the outermost layer of the skin and serves to protect the body from things outside. The epidermis layer consists of sublayers that function to produce keratin, fat, and keratinocytes which function to protect the body from bacteria, parasites, and viruses.

2. Dermis

This layer is below the epidermis and is the thickest layer because there are blood vessels, nerves, sweat, and oil glands.

The dermis layer consists of:

Elastic fibers help the skin to stretch.Collagen fibers give skin firmness and elasticity.Reticular fibers function as a liaison between collagen fibers with elastin fibers or with other collagen fibers.

3. Hypodermis

This layer is the lowest layer of human skin. In this layer, there is adipose tissue, blood vessels, and a protein in the form of elastin which helps skin tissue return to its original shape after being stretched.

The subcutis is the innermost layer of the skin, and consists of a network of fat and collagen cells. The subcutis is also known as the hypodermis or subcutaneous layer, and functions as both an insulator, conserving the body's heat, and as a shock-absorber, protecting the inner organs.
