16. The final research report draft is written in what order? Explain your answer. (10 points) I. Discussion IV. Results II. Introduction V. Review of Related Literature III. Methods​


The final research report draft is typically written in a sequential order that follows the standard structure for research papers. The first section is usually the Introduction, which serves to provide an overview of the research project, state the research problem or question, and explain the significance of the study.

The second section is the Review of Related Literature or Literature Review, which provides a critical analysis of the existing research and literature related to the research question. This section demonstrates the gap in knowledge that the current research is trying to fill and how the study contributes to the field.

The third section is the Methods, which describes the research design, data collection methods, data analysis procedures, and ethical considerations. This section provides a detailed explanation of how the research was carried out and ensures the study's credibility and research rigor.

The fourth section is the Results, which presents the findings of the research. This section usually includes tables, figures, and statistical analyses to communicate the data collected in the research process.

Finally, the Discussion section ties the research altogether by interpreting and summarizing the results and explaining their significance. This section should also include suggestions for future research and explore the implications of the study's findings.

Therefore, the correct order for the final research report draft is:

1. Introduction

2. Review of Related Literature

3. Methods

4. Results

5. Discussion.

a. Measurement of the fence that surrounds Tina's garden.

b. The given facts are the lengths of the sides of the trapezoid: 5 meters, 7 meters, 10 meters, and 15 meters.

d. The formula for finding the perimeter of a trapezoid is P = a + b + c + d, where a, b, c, and d are the lengths of the sides of the trapezoid.

e. Substituting the given values, we get P = 5 + 7 + 10 + 15 = 37 meters. Therefore, the measurement of the fence that surrounds Tina's garden is 37 meters.

f. The final answer is 37 meters.

2. The perimeter of an isosceles triangle is 55 cm. One of the equal sides is 17 cm. How long is the base?

a. Length of the base of the isosceles triangle.

b. The given facts are the perimeter of the triangle, which is 55 cm, and one of the equal sides, which is 17 cm.

d. The formula for finding the perimeter of a triangle is P = a + b + c, where a, b, and c are the lengths of the sides of the triangle.

e. Since the triangle is isosceles, the other equal side has the same length as the given side, which is 17 cm. Therefore, we can subtract the sum of the two equal sides from the perimeter to get the length of the base: 55 - 2(17) = 21 cm.

f. The final answer is 21 cm.
