1. Who made the bubble wrap​


Answer:

Alfred Fielding and Marc Chavannes

In 1957 two inventors named Alfred Fielding and Marc Chavannes were attempting to create a three-dimensional plastic wallpaper. Although the idea was a failure, they found that what they made could be used as packing material.


2. does a bubble wrap belong to household waste?​


Answer:

No, Bubble Wrap is a form of plastic waste because it is made from Low-density Polyethylene (LDP).

Explanation:

Bubble wrap is completely recyclable, but cannot be accepted curbside or grouped in with the rest of your home and business recycling.
