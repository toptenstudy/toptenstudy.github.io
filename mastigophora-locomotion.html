Answer: Cell locomotion depends on two principal types of movement: the ciliary or flagellar movement and the amoeboid movement. Cilia and flagella of eukaryotic cells are cylindrical organelles, which when animated, propagate waves resulting in the movement of the cells, which are free to move.

14. what locomotion movement in bending?​


Answer:

Bending is not considered a form of locomotion movement because it does not involve the displacement of the body from one place to another. Instead, bending is a movement that occurs at a joint, such as a knee, hip, or spine, where the joint angle changes to bring two body segments closer together or farther apart. Bending can be done in various positions, such as standing, sitting, or lying down, and is an essential movement for many activities of daily living, such as picking up objects from the ground, tying shoes, or reaching for objects on a low shelf.>-<
