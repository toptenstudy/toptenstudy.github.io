6. D.EVALUATEA. Identify and label the parts of the neuron below.​


Neurons (also known as neurones or nerve cells) are the basic components of the brain and nervous system. They are the cells that receive visual data from the outside world, give control signals to our muscles, and convert and relay electrical signals at every possible avenue.

Four parts of Neurons:

Axon ➪Each neuron in your brain has a long cable that runs away from the cell's main body. An axon is a tiny cable that carries electrical impulses from a neuron to other neurons.

.Nucleus ➪ The nucleus of a neuron is a membrane-bound oval structure found in the neuron's soma or body. The nucleolus and chromosomes, which are required for the cell's coded protein creation, are found within it.

Cell body/soma ➪ The major structural feature of the gray matter of the brain and spinal cord, the ganglia, and the retina is the nucleus-containing center region of a neuron, which is exclusive of its axons and dendrites.

Dendrites ➪ The portion of the neuron that receives information. Axons send synaptic inputs to dendrites, and the sum of dendritic inputs determines whether the neuron fires an action potential.
