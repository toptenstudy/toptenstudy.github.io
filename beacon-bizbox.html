6. The candle is beacon of sunshine metaphor meaning


Answer:

The candle is compared to a beacon of sunshine, which is a metaphor for something that gives immense hope and positivity.

Explanation:

A metaphor is a figure of speech that allows us to express a similarity or resemblance of something to another thing or idea, in a way that is not literal. The comparison can be symbolic or relatable.

In the given example, the candle is likened to a beacon of sunshine. A beacon is a visible object - most of the time, light - that serves as a signal of impending danger or celebration. Of course, something as small as a candle can't possibly function as a beacon literally, but the writer might have felt that way because of the situation he/she was in. Perhaps it was a hopeless situation, where something as little as a candle can provide the same security a beacon does. In literary writing, sunshine is often depicted as a symbol of freedom, positivity, and hope. The writer wants to evoke these emotions or concepts using the image of a candle.

21. what is the importance of airport beacon light?


Answer:

In the United States, airport beacons are used to help pilots identify an airport at night. The beacons are operated from dusk till dawn. Sometimes they are turned on if the Ceiling is less than 1,000 feet and/or the ground visibility is less than 3 statute miles (VFR minimums).
