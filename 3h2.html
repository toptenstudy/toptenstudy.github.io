Discussion:

The reactant used up first in a reaction is called the limiting reagent, because the maximum amount of product formed depends on how much of this reactant was originally present. 7. determine the equilibrium constant K at 25°C for the reaction N2(g)+3H2(g)=2NH3(g). (∆G°NH3(g)=-16.6kj/mol​


Given:

[tex]\Delta G_{\text{f}}^{\circ}(\text{NH}_{3}) = -\text{16.6 kJ/mol}[/tex]

[tex]T = \text{25°C + 273 = 298 K}[/tex]

balanced chemical equation: N₂(g) + 3H₂(g) [tex]\rightleftharpoons[/tex] 2NH₃(g)

Required:

[tex]K[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the standard free energy change of reaction based on the balanced chemical equation.

Note: The standard free energy change of formation of any element in its most stable form is zero. Therefore, the standard free energy change of formation of N₂ and H₂ are both zero.

[tex]\Delta G_{\text{rxn}}^{\circ} = \sum n \Delta G_{\text{f}}^{\circ}(\text{products}) - \sum m \Delta G_{\text{f}}^{\circ}(\text{reactants})[/tex]

[tex]\Delta G_{\text{rxn}}^{\circ} = 2\Delta G_{\text{f}}^{\circ}(\text{NH}_{3}) - [\Delta G_{\text{f}}^{\circ}(\text{N}_{2}) + 3\Delta G_{\text{f}}^{\circ}(\text{H}_{2})][/tex]

[tex]\Delta G_{\text{rxn}}^{\circ} = 2(-\text{16.6 kJ/mol}) - [0 + 3(0)][/tex]

[tex]\Delta G_{\text{rxn}}^{\circ} = -\text{33.2 kJ/mol} = -\text{33200 J/mol}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the equilibrium constant for the reaction at 25°C.

[tex]\Delta G_{\text{rxn}}^{\circ} = -RT \: \text{ln} \: K[/tex]

[tex]K = e^{-\frac{\Delta G_{\text{rxn}}^{\circ}}{RT}}[/tex]

[tex]K = e^{-\frac{-\text{33200 J/mol}}{(\text{8.314 J/K • mol})(\text{298 K})}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{K = 6.60 × 10^{5}}[/tex]

26. Determine the value N2(g) + 3H2(g) → 2NH3(g)+ 22,000 cal ΔH =


ΔH = -22 kcalFurther explanation

Delta H reaction (ΔH) is the amount of heat change between the system and its environment

ΔH is positive if requires heat and is said to be endothermic

ΔH is negative if releasing heat and is said to be exothermic

In endothermic reactions, the system will need energy, so the enthalpy of the system increases. The change in the enthalpy is positive.

Difference between product enthalpy (Hp) and reactant enthalpy (Hr) is positive. Can be formulated

ΔH = Hp- Hr> 0 = +

In exothermic reactions, the system releases energy, so the enthalpy of the system decreases. The change in the enthalpy is negative

Difference between product enthalpy (Hp) and reactant enthalpy (Hr) is negative. Can be formulated

ΔH = Hp- Hr <0 = -

Reaction

N₂ (g) + 3H₂ (g) → 2NH₃ (g) + 22,000 cal

the system releases heat

If we look at the law of conservation of energy then

amount of initial heat = amount of final heat

Where :

Hr = N₂ (g) + 3H₂ (g)

Hp = 2NH₃ (g)

So

number of reactant heat (Hr) = number of product heat (Hp) + 22000 cal

Hr = Hp + 22000 cal

Hp- Hr = -22000 cal

Hp-Hr = -22000 cal

ΔH = -22000 cal = -22 kcal
