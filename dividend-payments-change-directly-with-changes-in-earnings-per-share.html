18. The Board of Directors of Gamma Inc., have declared a dividend of P20,000,000. The company has 25,000 shares of preferred stock that pay P60 per share and 90,000 shares of common stock. Calculate the amount of dividends due the preferred shareholders and the dividend per share of common stock.


The total amount of dividends due to the preferred shareholders is:

P60 per share x 25,000 shares = P1,500,000

To calculate the dividend per share of common stock, we need to find out how much of the P20,000,000 will go to the common shareholders. We can do this by subtracting the amount due to the preferred shareholders from the total dividend:

P20,000,000 - P1,500,000 = P18,500,000

Now we can calculate the dividend per share of common stock by dividing the total amount due to the common shareholders by the number of common shares:

P18,500,000 / 90,000 shares = P205.56 per share

Therefore, the amount of dividends due to the preferred shareholders is P1,500,000 and the dividend per share of common stock is P205.56.

27. Theo bought 150 shares of Gokongwei Group of Companies (GGC) at ₱15,000 per share. GGC paid ₱650 per share of stocks last year. Theo decided to hold the stock for the next five years expecting a constant growth of 3.5% in dividend payments. What are the expected amounts of dividends in the next five years? ​


To calculate the expected amounts of dividends in the next five years for Theo's GGC shares, we can use the formula for compound interest:

A = P(1 + r)^n

Where:

A = the future value (the expected amount of dividends)

P = the initial investment (the amount of dividends paid last year)

r = the growth rate (3.5% in this case)

n = the number of years (5 in this case)

So, we can plug in the known values:

A = 650(1 + 0.035)^5

A = 650(1.035)^5

A = 650(1.1937)

A = 781.955

So, the expected amount of dividends in the next five years is approximately ₱781.955 per share. Since Theo has 150 shares, the total expected dividends for the next five years would be 150 x 781.955 = ₱117293.25.
