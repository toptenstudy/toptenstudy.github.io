2. the detectives need more time to inquire about the case


The detectives need more time to inquire about the case

Despite past negative comments, law enforcement won't unfairly arrest you simply to keep you longer for investigation. I'm sure there are exceptions to the maxim, and there are always many that break the rules to their advantage, including the police and prosecutors, but not enough. You may be charged with, or suspected of committing, any offense you have committed. Criminals also violate several rules when committing a felony, and they get branded. You can be jailed or held for violating such minor infringements. Much of the time, it's just a departmental decision not to get you up for a minor offence, but you still have violated the rule and will be put beyond justification.

It's not worth the chance of making allegations merely to keep you for a little longer before the police can make a case. Too dangerous, too criminal. When the detectives apprehend you, they probably have ample evidence to warrant your arrest, and the court will soon decide whether you spend time in prison if you are at risk of flight, or if you should bail out and wait until your trial. Whether you're arrested by a cop whilst committing a crime because they're looking to make a case with you, they're going to interview/interrogate you, detain you held before either a.) the attorney comes and addresses it, or b.) the hold time expires (72 hours?) and eventually releases you. You're in their system right now, your prints and your address, so they can reach you again if they need to. They also figure that you will finally be out on another charge.

We will keep a suspect in jail in the U.K. for 24 hours. In such cases, it can be expanded to 96 for serious crimes. There are a number of options after that time. The police can arrest, release without action, stay in jail, or bail or release under investigation. It's probable (but very rare) to remand someone where there's a good chance of getting enough evidence to charge. It is really common for there to be outstanding lines of investigation requiring the detective to release the suspect when checking them. There is no time limit for persons to be released from investigation except for the limits that apply for the crime.

In cases where detectives are charged after an investigation, they will usually have more evidence to charge them before the arrest takes place.

Case Managers help their patients understand their options concerning the specific situation they are dealing with at the time.
