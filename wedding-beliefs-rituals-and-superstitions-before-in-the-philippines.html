13. Wedding beliefs, rituals and superstition in Barangay Agojo (during)​


Wedding beliefs, rituals and superstition in Barangay Agojo

Similar to other cultures, it is considered unlucky for the bride and groom to see each additional hours before the wedding. According to wedding day superstition, looking at one's partner before exchanging "I dos" will bring bad luck or cause the marriage to fail.

Observe that some of these may appear like convenient justifications for terrible occurrences on the big day. It may be the way of dealing with unanticipated party crashers. It is ultimately your decision whether or not to follow them, but be aware that one of the beliefs, rituals and superstitions may completely contradict another, so don't go crazy over them. They are:

- If you clear the table while someone is still eating, that individual will never marry.

- A bachelor or spinster who agrees to serve as a ninong or ninang during a wedding will never wed.

- Brides should not test their wedding dresses before the big day since they may not hold up.

- It is believed that offering knives and other sharp, pointed objects as wedding gifts will result in a failed marriage.

- It is thought that giving an arin0la (chamberpot) as a wedding present will bring good fortune to both the giver and the brides.

- It is believed that soon-to-be-weds are more accident-prone as their wedding day approaches. Therefore, they should avoid traveling and long drives before their wedding day.

- The groom will be "under-the-saya" if he sits before his wife during the wedding ritual (henpecked husband).

- If a bride's period occurs on the day of her wedding, the pair will be rewarded with a large family.

- Couples must present eggs to Saint Clara to pray for a rain-free wedding day.

- A shower at the wedding would bring prosperity, and the married will be happy.

- The partner whose side the wedding candle is lit last will be submissive.

- If the flame on one of the wedding candles goes out, it signifies that the spouse whose side the candle belongs to will die before the other.

- Rice confetti thrown upon the bride will bring them riches for the rest of their life.

- To avert bad luck, the man must arrive at the church before the bride.

- It is considered unlucky for siblings to wed during the same calendar year.

- This is called "sukob", or sharing one's good fortune with another individual.

- Breaking a plate or glass by accident during a wedding reception gives good luck to the brides.

- Upon approaching the altar, the bride should "accidentally" stomp on - the groom's foot if she wants him to concur with her every wish.

- Pearls are supposed to be the "tears" of oysters. Therefore a bride who wears them on her wedding day will be miserable.

- A bride who wears pearls on her wedding day will never become a gloomy wife, as the pearls serve as a talisman against bad luck and symbolize the tears she would have shed had she not wear the pearls.

- A woman who follows (literally) in the footsteps of the bride will soon wed.

- The more food served at the reception, the more abundant the wedding's blessings will be.

- During the ceremony, dropping the wedding ring, the veil, or a coin in the arrhae portends sadness for the couple.

- "Something ancient" signifies the bride's past, while "something new" depicts the couple's bright future. The bride is expected to take something from a happily married person hoping that some of their excellent fortunes may rub off on her.

26. wedding superstition belief 1 to 5​


1. Something Old: Wearing something old on your wedding day is said to carry the best of luck and represent coherence with the past.

2. A new thing: Wearing a new thing on your wedding day is said to bring the best of luck and represent a fresh start.

3. Something Acquired: Getting something from a cheerfully hitched companion or relative is said to bring the best of luck and represent shared joy.

4. Something Blue: Wearing something blue on your wedding day is said to bring the best of luck and represent reliability, dedication, and love.

5. A Sixpence in Your Shoe: Putting a sixpence coin in your shoe is said to bring the best of luck and represent riches and success in the marriage.
