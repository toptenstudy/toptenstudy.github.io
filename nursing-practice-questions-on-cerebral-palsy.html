1. meaning of cerebral palsy​


Answer:

Cerebral palsy (CP) is a group of disorders that affect a person's ability to move and maintain balance and posture. CP is the most common motor disability in childhood. Cerebral means having to do with the brain. Palsy means weakness or problems with using the muscles.

8. what are the characteristics of children with cerebral palsy?


Answer:

Cerebral palsy is a group of disorders that affect movement and muscle tone or posture. It's caused by damage that occurs to the immature brain as it develops, most often before birth.

Signs and symptoms appear during infancy or preschool years. In general, cerebral palsy causes impaired movement associated with abnormal reflexes, floppiness or rigidity of the limbs and trunk, abnormal posture, involuntary movements, unsteady walking, or some combination of these.

The primary nursing care plan goals for patients with stroke depend on the phase of CVA the client is in. During the acute phase of CVA, efforts should focus on survival needs and prevent further complications. Care revolves around efficient continuing neurologic assessment, support of respiration, continuous monitoring of vital signs, careful positioning to avoid aspiration and contractures, management of GI problems, and monitoring of electrolyte and nutritional status. 23. what is the sign of the listed below1. Cerebral palsy2. adhd3. autism4. dysgraphia5. deafwhat accomodations that we can offer to them in real life situation and to the school accomodations ​


Explanation:

1. Cerebral Palsy: Physical therapy, adaptive equipment, speech therapy, wheelchair accessibility, written modifications, and/or a personal aide.

2. ADHD: Regular breaks, one-on-one instruction, homework reminders and modifications, use of a calculator and/or laptop, reduced work load, and/or seating close to the front of the class.

3. Autism: Sensory integration, quiet or isolated working area, visual aides, social skills lessons, use of a laptop or other assistive technology, and/or flexible deadlines.

4. Dysgraphia: Extra time to complete written assignments, use of word processors, proofreading assistance, handwriting practice, and/or graph paper.

5. Deaf: Use of an interpreter, assistive listening device, modified textbooks, notetaking assistance, visual aids, and/or pre-reading material.

25. If your students have special needs like what listed below what kind of accomodations that we can offer to them?MEANING AND ACCOMODATIONS PLEASE 1. ADHD2. Dysgraphia3. Autism4. Cerebral palsy5. Deaf​


Answer:

1. For students with ADHD, accommodations may include extra time for completing assignments, a quiet workspace away from distractions, and the use of technology to assist with organization and focus.

2. For students with dysgraphia, accommodations may include use of a computer or laptop to complete assignments, use of an adapted keyboard, and additional time for writing assignments.

3. For students with autism, accommodations may include use of visual supports, access to sensory items, and a quiet place for breaks.

4. For students with cerebral palsy, accommodations may include use of voice recognition software, use of an adapted keyboard, and access to wheelchairs and other assistive devices.

5. For students who are deaf, accommodations may include use of a sign language interpreter, use of assistive technology, and use of visual supports such as photographs or videos.
