8. To calculate the average height of the students in PHINMA COC 50 students are randomly selected. The mean height of the sample is 168 cm. The presumption shows a standard deviation of 54 cm. Find the interval estimate of the population mean u using 90% confidence level.​


To find the interval estimate of the population mean u using a 90% confidence level, we can use the following formula:

CI = x̄ ± z* (σ/√n)

where CI is the confidence interval, x̄ is the sample mean, z is the z-score corresponding to the desired level of confidence (0.90 in this case), σ is the population standard deviation, and n is the sample size.

Substituting the given values, we get:

CI = 168 ± 1.645*(54/√50)

CI = 168 ± 13.38

CI = (154.62, 181.38)

Therefore, with 90% confidence, the interval estimate of the population mean height of the students in PHINMA COC is (154.62 cm, 181.38 cm). If we repeat the sampling process many times and calculate the confidence interval each time, we would expect about 90% of those intervals to contain the true population means height.

Explanation:

The median is the middle number in a sorted, ascending or descending list of numbers and can be more descriptive of that data set than the average. It is the point above and below which half (50%) the observed data falls, and so represents the midpoint of the data.

To find the median, you take these steps:

1. Step 1: Arrange the scores in numerical order.

2. Step 2: Count how many scores you have.

3. Step 3: Divide the total scores by 2.

4. Step 4: If you have an odd number of total scores, round up to get the position of the median number.
