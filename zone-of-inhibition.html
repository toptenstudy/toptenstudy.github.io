12. the increase of ____ and ____ inhibit the secretion of gonadotropins​


Answer:

Increase of progesterone and estrogen inhibit the secretion of gonadotropins.

Explanation:

Gonadotropins stimulates the gonads. Gonads are testes in males and ovaries in females. Luteinizing hormone or LH and follicle-stimulating hormone or FSH are classified as gonadotropins and they are important in reproductive system and reproduction.

LH stimulates the secretion of sex steroids from the gonads. In the testes, LH binds to receptors on Leydig cells, stimulating synthesis and secretion of testosterone. In females, LH secretion helps ovulation of mature follicles on the ovary.

FSH increases the number of mature gametes for females. It is also critical for sperm production and sperm cell maturation.

Progesterone and estrogen inhibits gonadotropins secretions and also appear to have direct negative effects on gonadotropins.

30. What is gene inhibition therapy


Answer:

Gene inhibition therapy

Suitable for the treatment of infectious diseases, cancer and inherited disease caused by inappropriate gene activity. The aim is to introduce a gene whose product either: inhibits the expression of another gene. interferes with the activity of the product of another gene.
