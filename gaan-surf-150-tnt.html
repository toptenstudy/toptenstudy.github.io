4. The explosive, Trinitrotoluene (TNT) is composed of 37.0% Carbon, 2.20% hydrogen, 18.5% nitrogen, and 42.3% oxygen.a. The molar mass for TNT is 227 g/mol. b. Determine the empirical formula of TNT​


Finding the Empirical Formula of a compound.

Given

%C = 37.0 %%H = 2.20 %%N = 18.5 %%O = 42.3 %

Let the mass of the compound be 100g, so we derive the percentage per element in the given mass.

Mass of Carbon = 37 g

Mass of Hydrogen = 2.20 g

Mass of Nitrogen = 18.5 g

Mass of Oxygen = 42.3 g

Calculating the number of moles per element.

Molar Mass of Carbon = 12.0107 g/mol

37 g C x ( 1 mol C / 12.0107 g/mol C )

= 3.080586 mol

Molar Mass of Hydrogen = 1.0078 g/mol

2.20 g H x ( 1 mol H / 1.0078 g/mol H )

= 2.182973 mol

Molar Mass of Nitrogen = 14.0067 g/mol

18.5 g N = ( 1 mol N / 14.0067 g/mol )

= 1.320796 mol

Molar Mass of Oxygen = 15.9994 g/mol

42.3 g x ( 1 mol O / 15.9994 g/mol )

= 2.643849 mol

Dividing the number of moles per element by the least number of moles.

Represent the Empirical Formula

CⱼHₖNₗOₘ

j = 3.080586 / 1.320796

j = 2

k = 2.182973 / 1.320796

k = 2

l = 1.320796 / 1.320796

l = 1

m = 2.643849 / 1.320796

m = 2

Empirical Formula = C₂H₂NO₂
