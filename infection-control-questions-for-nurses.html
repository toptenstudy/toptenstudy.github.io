5. 3. What is the relative effectiveness of patient-controlled of antiemetictherapy versus nurse-controlled antiemetic therapy in terms ofmedication consumption and control of nausea and vomiting in patientsof chemotheraphy?​


Answer:

The purpose of this quasi-experimental pilot study was to compare the effect of patient-controlled (PCAE) and nurse administered (NCAE) antiemetic therapy for controlling chemotherapy-induced nausea and vomiting in patients receiving moderate emetogenic chemotherapy. Twenty subjects were randomly assigned to either the PCAE group who received IV antiemetic medication via a patient-controlled pump or the NCAE group who received antiemetic medication via nurse administered minibags. Nausea, vomiting, sedation, and drug consumption were measured. There was no difference in nausea scores between the two groups. Subjects in the PCAE group consumed significantly less medication than subjects in the NCAE group.

20. what are the prevention and control of Nurse Deployment program(NDP) ​


Answer:

The Nurse Deployment Program (NDP) is a government initiative in the Philippines that aims to deploy registered nurses to different areas in the country to address the shortage of healthcare professionals. The following are some prevention and control measures that can be implemented in the NDP:

Adequate training and education: Before deployment, nurses should undergo proper training and education to ensure they are equipped with the necessary skills and knowledge to provide quality healthcare services.

Regular monitoring and evaluation: The Department of Health (DOH) should conduct regular monitoring and evaluation of the NDP to ensure that the program is being implemented effectively and efficiently.

Proper placement: Nurses should be deployed to areas where there is a shortage of healthcare professionals and where their services are most needed. Proper placement ensures that the NDP is effective in addressing the shortage of healthcare professionals.Adequate compensation and benefits: Nurses should be provided with adequate compensation and benefits, such as health insurance and hazard pay, to ensure that they are motivated to participate in the program.

Coordination with local government units: The DOH should coordinate with local government units to ensure that the NDP is integrated with the local health system and that the needs of the community are being met.

Quality assurance: The DOH should implement quality assurance measures to ensure that the services provided by the nurses are of high quality and meet the standards set by the government.

By implementing these prevention and control measures, the NDP can be an effective program that addresses the shortage of healthcare professionals in the Philippines
