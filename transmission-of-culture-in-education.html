14. Transmission of culture is possible without language? True or false​


In linguistics, cultural transmission is the process whereby a language is passed on from one generation to the next in a community. It is also known as cultural learning and socio/cultural transmission.

 

Linguist Tao Gong has identified three primary forms of cultural transmission:

Horizontal transmission, communications among individuals of the same generation; Vertical transmission, in which a member of one generation talks to a biologically-related member of a later generation; Oblique transmission, in which any member of one generation talks to any non-biologically-related member of a later generation.
