16. The+density+of+a+solution+that+is+20.0%+hclo4+is+1.138+g/ml.+calculate+the+molarity+of+the+solution.


Answer:

To calculate the molarity of a solution, you need to know the number of moles of solute and the volume of the solvent.

In this case, the solute is HClO4 and the solvent is the solution, which has a density of 1.138 g/mL.

To find the number of moles of HClO4, you can use the mass percent formula:

Mass of solute (HClO4) = mass percent of solute x mass of solution / 100%

Plugging in the values given in the question, we get:

Mass of HClO4 = 20.0% x 1.138 g / 100% = 0.2276 g

The atomic weight of HClO4 is 97.45 g/mol, so the number of moles of HClO4 is:

Number of moles of HClO4 = 0.2276 g / 97.45 g/mol = 0.002330 moles

To find the volume of the solvent (the solution), you can use the density formula:

Volume = mass / density

Plugging in the values given in the question, we get:

Volume of solution = 1.138 g / 1.138 g/mL = 1 mL

Since the volume of the solvent is in milliliters (mL), we need to convert it to liters (L) by dividing it by 1000:

Volume of solution in liters = 1 mL / 1000 = 0.001 L

Now that we have the number of moles of solute and the volume of solvent, we can use the molarity formula to calculate the molarity of the solution:

Molarity = number of moles of solute / volume of solvent (in liters)

Plugging in the values we calculated above, we get:

Molarity = 0.002330 moles / 0.001 L = 2.33 M

So, the molarity of the solution is 2.33 M.

29. 10. If HF = 2y -9 and AB =y + 2, find HF.​


[tex] \\ \large\color{grey}{ ━━━━━━ ⸙ ━━━━━━ } \\ [/tex]

ANSWER: [tex]\large{\boxed{{\sf HF = 13}}}[/tex]

[tex] \large\color{grey}{━━━━━━ ⸙ ━━━━━━ } \\ [/tex]

SOLUTION:

• Let's say HF = AB. So,

➧ [tex]2y - 9 = y + 2 \\ \\ 2y - y - 9 = y - y + 2 \\ \\ y - 9 + 9 = 2 + 9 \\ \\ y = 11 \\ \\ [/tex]

• Substitute "y" by 11. So,

➧ [tex] \\ HF = 2y - 9 = 2(11) - 9 = 13 \\ \\ [/tex]

[Therefore, the value of HF is 13]
