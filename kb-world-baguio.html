20. Consider the weak bases below and their Kb values: C6H7O- Kb = 1.3 × 10-10. C2H5NH2 Kb = 5.6 × 10-4. C5H5N Kb = 1.7 ×10-9


The Kb value of a base is a measure of its basicity, or the strength with which it can accept protons. The smaller the Kb value, the weaker the base. Therefore, of the three bases you listed, C6H7O- is the weakest base, followed by C5H5N, and C2H5NH2 is the strongest base.

To understand why one base is stronger or weaker than another, it is helpful to consider the factors that contribute to the basicity of a compound. Some factors that can influence the basicity of a compound include the size and charge of the atom or group of atoms that can accept protons, the presence of electronegative atoms in the compound that can stabilize the charge of the protonated species, and the ability of the compound to stabilize the resulting anion through resonance or delocalization of the negative charge.

For example, C6H7O- is a relatively small and neutral molecule, which may make it less able to accept protons compared to larger or more charged molecules. C5H5N is also a small and neutral molecule, but it contains a nitrogen atom, which is more electronegative than carbon and may be more effective at stabilizing the charge of the protonated species. C2H5NH2 is a larger molecule that contains a nitrogen atom, which may make it more able to accept protons compared to the other two bases. Additionally, the nitrogen atom in C2H5NH2 is part of an amine group, which can stabilize the charge of the protonated species through resonance. This may also contribute to the higher basicity of C2H5NH2 compared to the other two bases.
