2. how long will 40000 amount to 51200 if a simple interest rate is at 12% per annum?


Answer:

The principal amount of 40,000 takes 2.33 years to reach 51,200 with a simple interest of 0.12.

Further Explanation

In business math, two basic topics that are discussed are the Simple Interest and the Compound Interest.

Simple Interest is determined by multiplying the principal amount by the interest rate by the time it covers for the loan.

Compound Interest is the results of reinvesting the interest.

Two Basic Interests and Their Formula

1. Simple Interest [tex]FA = P(1+rt)[/tex]

      where FA is the future value

                 P is the principal amount

                 r is the interest rate

                 t is the time in years

2. Compound Interest [tex]FA = P\left(1+\frac{r}{n}\right)^{nt}[/tex]

       where FA is the future value

                  P is the principal

                  r is the rate

                  t is the time in years, and

                  n is the number of times interest applied per time period (quarterly, semi-annually, bimonthly, biyearly and etc).

The problem seeks "how long", thus we need to solve the value of [tex]t[/tex].

Steps in Solving the Problem

1. Substitute the values of [tex]FA = 51,200[/tex], [tex]P=40,000[/tex], and [tex]r=12\% = 0.12[/tex].

2. Distribute [tex]40,000[/tex] on the right side.

3. Apply the inverse operation to solve for [tex]t[/tex].

Solution:

                               [tex]\begin{aligned}51,200&=40,000(1+0.12t)\\51,200&=40,000+4800t\\51,200-40,000&=4800t\\11,200&=4800t\\\frac{11,200}{4800}&=\frac{4800t}{4800}\\2.33&=t\end{aligned}[/tex]

This means that the principal amount of 40,000 will reach 51,200 for 2.33 years with a simple interest rate of 0.12.
