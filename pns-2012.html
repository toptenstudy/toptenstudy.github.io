4. What is CNS and PNS?


Answer:

2 Major divisions of Nervous System:

I. Central Nervous System

- which is made up of Brain and Spinal Cord. It's main purpose is integrating the sensory information that the Peripheral System collects from all over the body, and responding to it by coordinating both conscious and unconscious activity.

II. Peripheral Nervous System

- which consists of nerves that run throughout the body. This system itself is made up of 2 subdivisions:

         A. Somatic Nervous System

   - which contains nerves that carry sensory signals from the body to the Central Nervous System and nerves that carry motor signals from the Central Nervous System to skeletal muscles. This is associated with voluntary movement.

         B. Autonomic Nervous System

  - is sometimes called the Involuntary Nervous System, and it is involved in regulating the internal environment of the body. It carries signal from Central Nervous system to the internal organs. In this way, it is involved in regulating things like digestion and heartbeat, which are generally outside the realm of conscious control. The Autonomic Nervous System can be further subdivided into:

                1.  Sympathetic Nervous System

-plays a large role in stimulating and mobilizing energy resources. It is often described as being involved in fight or flight responses.

         2. Parasympathetic Nervous System

- acts to conserve energy. It is described as being involved in rest and digest responses.

20. what is the meaning pns​


Answer:

PNS (peripheral nervous system): That portion of the nervous system that is outside the brain and spinal cord.

The peripheral nervous system (PNS) is one of the two major divisions of the nervous system. The other is the central nervous system (CNS) which is made up of the brain and spinal cord.

The nerves in the peripheral nervous system (PNS) connect the central nervous system (CNS) to sensory organs (such as the eye and ear), other organs of the body, muscles, blood vessels and glands.

The peripheral nerves include the 12 cranial nerves, the spinal nerves and roots, and what are called the autonomic nerves. The autonomic nerves are concerned with automatic functions of the body. Specifically, autonomic nerves are involved with the regulation of the heart muscle, the tiny muscles lining the walls of blood vessels, and glands.

"Peripheral" in anatomy and medicine (as elsewhere) is the opposite of "central." It means situated away from the center. The word "peripheral" comes from the Greek "peripheria" ("peri-", around or about + "pherein", to bear, carry). An IV (intravenous) catheter is a peripheral line.

22. what does the pns consist of


The PNS consist of all the nerves that run in your body (except brain and spinal cord). It is composed of somatic nervous system and the autonomic nervous system. The components of each division are as follows:

Somatic nervous system - it is the division of the peripheral nervous system that is connected with the voluntary movements in the body. This includes the  cranial nerves and the spinal nerves.

Cranial Nerves - the 12 pairs of nerves found in the head responsible for the movement in the head, sensation, and transmission of signals from stimuli from the environment (e.g sight).Spinal Nerves - all of the nerves connected to the spinal cord. Some of the largest examples are the brachial nerves, lumbar nerves, and the nerves of the limbs.

Autonomic nervous system - it is the division of the PNS that is concerned with the physiological functions of the body. These include nerves that send signals to the heart, bladder, exocrine, digestive, etc.

30. Differentiate of CNS and PNS

While peripheral nervous system or (PNS) contains all nerves that extend outside the brain and the spinal cord. And it has two main divisions, which are the somatic and the autonomic nervous system.
