4. What is the degree of formality in each conversation? Student-Teacher


Degrees of Formality: Student-Teacher

Answer:

In a conversation or you may also regard it as a discussion for a much formal sense, is when two people are exchanging words in a way that they can reach to an understanding or relating to each other. The flow of the conversation depends also in your relationship with the receiver or the other person, like for example if you are to address your boss you use honorifics such as Mister or Sir/ Madam or Mrs .  

The degree of formality is the way you are to speak, the tone, choice of words towards another person.  

The Degrees/ Levels of Formality:

Formal- most applicable when addressing someone of higher status or position may it be in business or in family. Speaking with the sense of formality, engaging respect throughout the conversation. Semi-Formal- applicable if the other person is of close relations to you, the in between of the levels of formality. You can converse with ease but at the same time considering a boundary between you and the other person.  Informal- you can converse using colloquial language/informal. Somewhat without a huge boundary preventing you from opening up or giving our personal ideas and thoughts to someone.

Student-Teacher

The Degree of Formality to be used is Formal. Teacher and student relationships are often formal, for a teacher provides proper guidance and learning in an academic sense to the student and the student is the one who receives knowledge and disciplines that only a teacher can provide. The teacher can also be regarded as second parent of a child, therefore using formalities and formal language is more than acceptable.  

Considering that by comparison the teacher is to be superior to the student.  

For Example:

You were asked by your teacher to stay behind to talk about you academic records. You as a student will use Formal Language, because the one you are conversing to is of superior standing.

Teacher and student conversation:

Student: Good morning teacher!

Teacher: Good morning! How are you today?

Student: I am doing fine! Also, may I ask you a question?

Teacher: Yes, you may.

Student: I can't understand what this topic is. Everything written here is just too difficult to understand!

Teacher: Don't worry! I will explain everything about this topic.

Student: Thank you so much!

Teacher: You're welcome.

Client and doctor consultation:

Client: Doctor, I have had a stomach ache since yesterday.

Doctor: What did you eat yesterday?

Client: Some street food.

Doctor: You may have eaten contaminated food.

Client: What should I do?

Doctor: Drink plenty of water every day. I will also prescribe you some medicine to control diarrhea.

Client: Thank you, doctor.

Employer and employee conversation:

Employee: Good day, sir. May I speak with you?

Employer: Yes, you may.

Employee: I want to talk to you about something.

Employer: Okay, please take a seat. Now, what would you like to talk about?

Employee: I have been working here for the past 5 years and have been loyal to this company ever since, so may I get my salary raised?

Employer: If this is the matter, how much?

Employee: Php 30,550 to Php 45,000 is good for me.

Employer: If that's the case then I will accept. I will add a few responsibilities for your salary raise, is that alright?

Employee: Yes, sir! Thank you very much!

25. script conversation about Informal Feedback in the classroom teacher and student​


Answer:

Teacher: Good morning, class! Today we're going to talk about informal feedback. Can anyone tell me what that means?

Student 1: Is it when the teacher gives us feedback on our work?

Teacher: That's right! Informal feedback is feedback that happens on a day-to-day basis, as opposed to formal feedback, which is usually given through tests or graded assignments. Informal feedback can be verbal or written, and it helps students improve their understanding of a subject.

Student 2: So how does informal feedback work in the classroom?

Teacher: Well, it can happen in a variety of ways. For example, when you ask a question in class, and I give you an answer, that's informal feedback. Or, when you're working on a group project, and I give you suggestions on how to improve, that's also informal feedback.

Student 3: What are some benefits of informal feedback?

Teacher: There are many benefits! For one, it allows you to get feedback on your work in a more timely manner, which means you can improve more quickly. Also, informal feedback is usually less intimidating than formal feedback, so it can help build your confidence in a subject.

Student 4: That makes sense. But how can we make sure we're getting enough informal feedback?

Teacher: Good question. One way is to ask questions in class, participate in group discussions, and seek feedback from your peers. You can also approach me after class and ask for feedback on a particular assignment or project.

Student 5: That sounds helpful. Is there anything we can do to give feedback to our teachers?

Teacher: Definitely! Feedback is a two-way street. If you have suggestions on how I can improve my teaching or if there's something you'd like me to explain in more detail, please let me know. Your feedback can help me become a better teacher and improve the learning experience for everyone.

Student 1: I think that's great. Thanks for explaining informal feedback to us, teacher.

Teacher: You're welcome! Remember, feedback is a powerful tool for learning, so let's keep the conversation going.
