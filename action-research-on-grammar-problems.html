RAISING ACTION: Awiyao's preparations for his wedding with Madulimay, including the traditional dance they need to perform together. Lumnay secretly watches them rehearsing.

27. Using Story Grammar Story grammar is a technique which classifies the elements of a story. It helps you under- out the component which particularly shows the sequence of episodes in a story. stand the structure of a literary text. The chart below shows how story grammar works. Find . O Setting familiarity with tools and machines Time Place Mood Title Characters setting Plot Beginning Rising Action Climax Falling Action/Denouement Resolution/Outcome​


Answer:

Here is the breakdown of story grammar elements:

Title: The name of the story or book.Characters: The people or animals that are involved in the story. They are the ones who take actions, make decisions, and experience the events of the plot.Setting: The time and place where the story takes place. This includes the physical location, as well as the cultural, historical, and social context.Plot: The sequence of events that make up the story.Beginning: The introduction to the story where the characters, setting, and main conflict are presented.Rising Action: The series of events that build tension and increase the stakes in the story.Climax: The turning point in the story where the conflict reaches its highest point and the outcome is uncertain.Falling Action/Denouement: The events that occur after the climax, where the tension begins to decrease, and the story starts to move towards resolution.Resolution/Outcome: The final outcome of the story, where the conflict is resolved and loose ends are tied up. The characters often learn something or experience personal growth as a result of the events of the story.Note that not all stories will have every single element of story grammar, and some elements may overlap or occur in a different order. However, understanding these elements can help you analyze and appreciate the structure of literary texts.
