What is a lightning?A. Lightning is a naturally occurring electrostatic discharge during which two electrically charged regions in the atmosphere or ground temporarily equalize themselves, causing the instantaneous release of as much as one gigajoule of energy.Where does a lightning originate?B. Cloud-to-ground (CG) lightning comes from the sky down, but the part you see comes from the ground up. A typical cloud-to-ground flash lowers a path of negative electricity (that we cannot see) towards the ground in a series of spurts. Objects on the ground generally have a positive charge under a typical thunderstorm.

How 'powerful' is a lightning bolt?C. Cloud-to-ground lightning bolts are a common phenomenon—about 100 strike Earth's surface every single second—yet their power is extraordinary. Each bolt can contain up to one billion volts of electricity

Can lighting's energy be caught stored, and used?D. No it can't be caught. There's no way to chase the storm and we don't know where the lightning will strike. No it can't be stored. The means of storage doesn't exist, and is unlikely to be developed.How many people are killed by lightning per year?
